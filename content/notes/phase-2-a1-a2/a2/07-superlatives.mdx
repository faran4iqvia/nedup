## Introduction

You compare with **groter dan** and **beter** from [A2 Lesson 6](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/06-comparatives). Superlatives take you one step further: **het grootst**, **het best**, **de mooiste stad**, and **de meeste mensen**. After this lesson you can pick the best restaurant, describe the tallest building, and say what you like most in Dutch.

## Quick Review

From [A2 Lesson 6](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/06-comparatives): **er** comparatives (**groter**, **mooier**), irregular **beter / meer / minder / liever**, and **dan**. From [A1 Lesson 11](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/11-adjectives): adjectives before nouns take **e** (**de grote stad**). Superlatives follow the same **e** rule: **de grootste stad**, **het mooiste huis**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The basic rule: adjective + st

Most Dutch superlatives add **st** to the adjective base:

| Base | Superlative (predicate) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| groot | **het grootst** | the biggest |
| klein | **het kleinst** | the smallest |
| mooi | **het mooist** | the most beautiful |
| duur | **het duurst** | the most expensive |
| goedkoop | **het goedkoopst** | the cheapest |
| interessant | **het interessantst** | the most interesting |
| lang | **het langst** | the longest |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De Euromast is **het hoogst** in Rotterdam. | The Euromast is the tallest in Rotterdam. |
| Dit is **het duurst** restaurant van de stad. | This is the most expensive restaurant in the city. |
| **Welke** film vind jij **het interessantst**? | Which film do you find the most interesting? |
| De zomer is **het warmst** in juli. | Summer is warmest in July. |

Use **het** before the superlative in the predicate (after **zijn**, **vinden**, **lijken**).

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  Full comparative and superlative rules live in the Grammar Track: [Comparatives and Superlatives](/notes/grammar/21-comparatives-superlatives). [Lesson 6](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/06-comparatives) covered comparatives. This lesson completes the superlative half.
</Callout>

### Spelling changes before st

| Situation | Rule | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Short vowel + single consonant at end | double consonant + **st** | dun → **dunst**, plat → **platst** |
| Long vowel already in stem | add **st** only | groot → **grootst**, duur → **duurst** |
| Adjective ends in **s** | add **t** only | interessant → **interessantst** |

| Base | Superlative | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| groot | grootst | long **oo** |
| dun | dunst | double **n** |
| plat | platst | double **t** |
| warm | warmst | no doubling |
| oud | oudst | |
| laat | laatst | |

### Irregular superlatives (learn by heart)

| Base | Comparative | Superlative | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| goed | beter | **best** | best |
| veel | meer | **meest** | most |
| weinig | minder | **minst** | least / fewest |
| graag | liever | **liefst** | most preferably |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dit is **het beste** idee. | This is the best idea. |
| **De beste** film van het jaar. | The best film of the year. |
| **De meeste** studenten komen op tijd. | Most students arrive on time. |
| Ik eet **het liefst** pasta. | I most prefer to eat pasta. / I prefer pasta most. |

**Best**, **meest**, **minst**, and **liefst** never take **st**.

### Superlatives before nouns: add e

From [A1 Lesson 11](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/11-adjectives) and [Lesson 6](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/06-comparatives): superlative adjectives before nouns follow the same **e** rule:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **de grootste** stad | the biggest city |
| **het mooiste** huis | the most beautiful house (het word: **e**) |
| **de duurste** jas | the most expensive coat |
| **het beste** voorstel | the best proposal |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Amsterdam is **de grootste** stad van Nederland. | Amsterdam is the biggest city in the Netherlands. |
| **Het kleinste** appartement is ook **het goedkoopst**. | The smallest flat is also the cheapest. |
| Dat is **de interessantste** les van de week. | That is the most interesting lesson of the week. |

With **een + het** word, superlatives stay without **e**: **een mooist huis** is wrong → **een mooi huis** or **het mooiste huis**.

### meest + adjective for long words

Long adjectives often use **meest** + base adjective:

| With **st** | With **meest** | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| interessantst | **meest** interessant | both possible |
| verantwoordelijkst | **meest** verantwoordelijk | **meest** more common |
| belangrijkst | **meest** belangrijk | both used |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dit is **het meest** interessante boek. | This is the most interesting book. |
| Zij is **de meest** ervaren collega. | She is the most experienced colleague. |
| **Het meest** belangrijke punt is de prijs. | The most important point is the price. |

Before a noun, **meest** + adjective + **e**: **de meest interessante film**.

### van alle, in het land, and context phrases

Superlatives often appear with a group or place:

| Pattern | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **de** + superlative + **van** + group | the … of … | **de mooiste** van **alle** steden |
| **het** + superlative + **in** + place | the … in … | **het hoogst** **in** Nederland |
| **bij** + superlative | at its … | **bij** **het best** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Utrecht is **de gezelligste** stad **van** Nederland, **vind** ik. | Utrecht is the cosiest city in the Netherlands, I think. |
| Dit is **het lekkerste** brood **van** de bakker. | This is the tastiest bread from the baker. |
| **Wat** vind jij **het allermooist** **aan** Nederland? | What do you find most beautiful about the Netherlands? |
| **Bij** **het best** moet je **op** **tijd** zijn. | At best you need to be on time. |

**Aller-** before a superlative strengthens it: **het allermooist**, **de allerbeste** (_the very best_).

### Superlatives in opinions

From [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/05-opinions):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **vind** dit **het beste** restaurant **van** de buurt. | I think this is the best restaurant in the neighbourhood. |
| Ik **denk dat** **de nieuwe** film **de interessantste** is. | I think the new film is the most interesting. |
| **Vind** jij **winter** of **zomer** **het fijnst**? | Do you find winter or summer nicest? |

**Fijn** → **fijnst** (superlative of pleasant / fine).

### de meeste vs het meest

| Form | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **de meeste** + noun | most (quantity) | **de meeste** mensen |
| **het meest** + adjective | most (degree) | **het meest** belangrijk |
| **de meest** + adjective + **e** + noun | most + noun | **de meest** populaire film |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De meeste** studenten **hebben** **het** **examen** **gehaald**. | Most students passed the exam. |
| Dat is **het meest** **interessant**. | That is the most interesting. |
| **De minste** moeite kost koffie zetten. | Making coffee takes the least effort. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Superlative **st** is a clear cluster: **groot-st**, **mooi-st**, **interessant-st**. **Best** and **meest** stress the vowel. Drill triplets: **groot / groter / grootst**, **goed / beter / best**, **veel / meer / meest**. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">De beste plekken in Nederland</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam share their favourite spots after a weekend trip.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** Wat was **het hoogtepunt** van het weekend?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Zonder twijfel** de Veluwe. **Het mooiste** bos **van** Nederland, **vind** ik.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Daar ben ik het mee eens**. En **het lekkerste** eten?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Bij** dat restaurant in Arnhem. **Uitstekend** eten, **vooral** de vis. **De beste** maaltijd **van** het hele weekend.
    >
    > **Mila:** Ik **vind** **de gezelligste** stad **Utrecht**. **Kleiner dan** Amsterdam, maar **voor mij de allerbeste**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **De meeste** toeristen gaan naar Amsterdam. **Dat is logisch**, maar Utrecht is **het fijnst** om te wonen.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Wat** vind jij **het minst** leuk aan Nederland?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Het weer** in de winter. Soms is het **het koudst** in februari. Maar **bovenal** vind ik Nederland **het best** van Europa.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Dan** zijn wij het eens. **Ten minste** over het eten!

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** What was the highlight of the weekend?
    >
    > **Sam:** Without doubt the Veluwe. The most beautiful forest in the Netherlands, I think.
    >
    > **Mila:** I agree with that. And the tastiest food?
    >
    > **Sam:** At that restaurant in Arnhem. Excellent food, especially the fish. The best meal of the whole weekend.
    >
    > **Mila:** I find Utrecht the cosiest city. Smaller than Amsterdam, but for me the very best.
    >
    > **Sam:** Most tourists go to Amsterdam. That makes sense, but Utrecht is nicest to live in.
    >
    > **Mila:** What do you find least nice about the Netherlands?
    >
    > **Sam:** The weather in winter. Sometimes it is coldest in February. But above all I find the Netherlands the best in Europe.
    >
    > **Mila:** Then we agree. At least about the food!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
