## Introduction

You already put the verb at the end after **dat** in **Ik denk dat hij komt** from [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/05-opinions). Relative clauses use the **same word order** with **die** and **dat**: **de man die daar staat**, **het boek dat ik lees**, **de stad waar ik woon** comes later. After this lesson you can add detail without starting a new sentence: **Ken je de vrouw die naast ons woont?** · **Het appartement dat wij zoeken is groot**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### What is a relative clause?

A **relative clause** gives extra information about a noun. In English: _the man **who** lives here_. In Dutch: **de man die hier woont**.

| Part | Role | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Antecedent** | the noun you describe | **de man** |
| **Relative pronoun** | links the clauses | **die** |
| **Relative clause** | extra information | **hier woont** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De man die** hier **woont** is aardig. | The man who lives here is kind. |
| **Het huis dat** groot **is** staat te koop. | The house that is big is for sale. |
| **De film die** wij gisteren **zagen** was goed. | The film that we saw yesterday was good. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  Full relative clause rules live in the Grammar Track: [Relative Clauses](/notes/grammar/24-relative-clauses). Advanced relatives (**waar**, **wie**, **hetgeen**) come in B1.
</Callout>

### die for de words, dat for het words

The relative pronoun matches the **article** of the noun it refers to:

| Antecedent | Relative pronoun | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **de** word (singular) | **die** | **de vrouw die** … |
| **de** words (plural) | **die** | **de mensen die** … |
| **het** word (singular) | **dat** | **het boek dat** … |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De collega die** naast mij **zit** is nieuw. | The colleague who sits next to me is new. |
| **Het restaurant dat** wij **kennen** is dicht. | The restaurant that we know is closed. |
| **De buren die** boven ons **wonen** zijn rustig. | The neighbours who live above us are quiet. |
| **Het appartement dat** wij **zoeken** is te duur. | The flat that we are looking for is too expensive. |

❌ **de man dat** … → ✅ **de man die** …

❌ **het huis die** … → ✅ **het huis dat** …

### Verb to the end (like dat clauses)

From [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/phase-2-a1-a2/a2/05-opinions): in subordinate clauses, the **conjugated verb goes last**. Relative clauses follow the same rule:

| Main clause word order | Relative clause word order |
| --- | --- |
| Hij **is** aardig. | de man die aardig **is** |
| Zij **woont** in Utrecht. | de vrouw die in Utrecht **woont** |
| Wij **hebben** het **gezien**. | de film die wij **hebben gezien** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De stad die** ik het **mooist vind** is Utrecht. | The city that I find most beautiful is Utrecht. |
| **Het boek dat** op tafel **ligt** is van mij. | The book that lies on the table is mine. |
| **De mensen die** gisteren **kwamen** waren vriendelijk. | The people who came yesterday were friendly. |

**Word order inside the relative clause:** subject + other elements + **verb** ( + past participle if perfect tense).

### die/dat cannot be dropped

English often drops the relative pronoun: _the man I know_. Dutch **always** keeps **die** or **dat**:

| English (optional who) | Dutch (die required) |
| --- | --- |
| the man I know | **de man die** ik **ken** |
| the book I read | **het boek dat** ik **lees** |
| the people we met | **de mensen die** wij **hebben ontmoet** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ken** je **de vrouw die** gisteren **belde**? | Do you know the woman who called yesterday? |
| **Dat** is **het huis dat** wij **willen** **kopen**. | That is the house that we want to buy. |

### die vs dat: not the same as demonstratives

From [A1 Lesson 9](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/09-demonstratives): **die jas** (_that coat_) points at something. In relative clauses, **die** / **dat** **link** two parts of a sentence:

| Function | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Demonstrative | **Die** jas **is** duur. | That coat is expensive. |
| Relative | De jas **die** duur **is** … | The coat that is expensive … |

| Function | Example | Translation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Conjunction (L5) | Ik denk **dat** hij **komt**. | I think that he is coming. |
| Relative | Het idee **dat** hij **noemde** … | The idea that he mentioned … |

Context tells you which **dat** you see. After a noun, **dat** is usually relative. After a verb like **denken**, **dat** is a conjunction.

### Relative clauses with prepositions (A2 preview)

When a preposition belongs to the relative clause, Dutch often keeps **die** / **dat** and puts the preposition at the end in speech, or uses **waar** + preposition (more common in formal Dutch). At A2, learn these **fixed spoken patterns**:

| Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- |
| … **die** ik **naar** **zoek** | the flat I am looking for |
| … **die** wij **mee** **werken** | the colleagues we work with |
| … **waar** ik **naar** **zoek** | what I am looking for (het huis **waar** ik naar zoek) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Het appartement dat** wij **naar zoeken** is groot. | The flat we are looking for is big. |
| **De collega met wie** ik **werk** is aardig. | The colleague I work with is kind. |
| **De stad waar** ik **woon** is gezellig. | The city where I live is cosy. |

**Waar** and **wie** in relatives are a B1 topic. For now, recognise **waar ik woon** and **met wie ik werk** as common fixed chunks.

| Topic | Relative example |

| Comparatives (L6) | **De stad die** groter **is** … |
| Superlatives (L7) | **Het restaurant dat** het **beste is** … |
| People (L8) | **De man die** vriendelijk **is** … |
| Places (L9) | **Het dorp dat** aan de rivier **ligt** … |
| Prepositions (L10–11) | **De winkel die** om de hoek **ligt** … |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik vind de buurt die** rustig **is** erg **fijn**. | I find the neighbourhood that is quiet very nice. |

| **De trein die** om acht uur **vertrekt** is **vertraagd**. | The train that departs at eight is delayed. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Relative **die** is often unstressed: **de-man-die-hier-woont**. Do not pause before **die**. Drill: **Ken je de vrouw die daar staat?** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Een nieuw appartement zoeken</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam discuss flats they saw online, using relative clauses.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** **Heb** jij **het appartement gezien dat** op de site **staat**?
    >
    > **Sam:** Ja, **het** **ene dat** aan de gracht **ligt**. **De buurt die** rustig **is**, **vind** ik **fijn**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ken** jij **de makelaar die** ons **heeft** **gebeld**?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Nee**, **maar** **de vrouw die** de **rondleiding gaf** was **heel** **aardig**. **Zij** **gaf** **aanvullende** **informatie**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Het huis dat** wij **gisteren** **zagen**, was **te** **klein**. **De** **woonkamer die** **naast** de keuken **ligt**, was **donker**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Dan** **zoeken** wij **verder**. **Het appartement dat** wij **nodig** **hebben** **moet** **een** **balkon** **hebben**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **De** **eigenaar die** **verkoopt** **woont** **boven** ons, **namelijk** **onze** **buurman**!
    >
    > **Sam:** **Dat** is **een** **feit dat** ik **niet** **wist**!

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** Did you see the flat that is on the website?
    >
    > **Sam:** Yes, the one that lies on the canal. I find the neighbourhood that is quiet nice.
    >
    > **Mila:** Do you know the estate agent who called us?
    >
    > **Sam:** No, but the woman who gave the tour was very kind. She gave additional information.
    >
    > **Mila:** The house that we saw yesterday was too small. The living room that lies next to the kitchen was dark.
    >
    > **Sam:** Then we keep looking. The flat that we need must have a balcony.
    >
    > **Mila:** The owner who is selling lives above us, namely our neighbour!
    >
    > **Sam:** That is a fact that I did not know!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
