## Welkom! 👋

Dutchyy is a complete, structured Dutch course written for English speakers. No streaks, no gimmicks, no locked chapters. Just clear lessons that build on each other, from your very first **hallo** to holding real conversations at B1 level.

Every lesson explains the grammar in plain English, warns you about the mistakes English speakers actually make, gives you vocabulary in order of real usefulness, and ends with exercises and a full answer key. You read, you practice, you move on.

## Your Path to B1

The course follows the CEFR levels, the European standard for language ability. It is split into three phases, and each phase takes you fully through the next level before you move on.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Phase 1: A0 to A1

    The foundation. Greetings, the alphabet, numbers, telling time, ordering food, word order, family, shopping, and the verbs you will use in almost every sentence. Nothing later in the course assumes anything that is not taught here.

    Start with [A0: The Absolute Basics](/notes/a0/01-greetings-and-introductions), then continue into [A1: Everyday Dutch](/notes/a1/01-daily-routines).
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Phase 2: A1 to A2

    Expanding your range. The past tenses in depth, giving opinions, comparisons, subordinate clauses, describing people and places, and the famously slippery little word **er**.

    Start with [A2: Past Tense in Depth](/notes/a2/01-past-tense-depth) after the [A1 Review and Test](/notes/a1/review-and-test).
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Phase 3: A2 to B1

    Towards independence. Passives, hypotheticals, indirect speech, nuanced opinions, reading real news articles, Dutch directness, and strategies for handling fast natural speech.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<br />

Alongside the main phases, dedicated **Grammar** and **Pronunciation** tracks are on the roadmap, so the hardest sounds and structures get the standalone attention they deserve.

## What Is in Every Lesson

Every single lesson follows the same structure, so you always know what to expect and nothing important gets skipped.

| Section                          | What it gives you                                                              |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Introduction and quick review    | What you will be able to do, and how it connects to earlier lessons             |
| Grammar explanation              | Plain English explanations with side by side Dutch and English examples         |
| Common mistakes                  | The errors English speakers actually make, with wrong and right versions        |
| Learning tips                    | Memory tricks and shortcuts that genuinely help                                 |
| Core vocabulary                  | Frequency ordered words with example sentences and a running vocabulary count   |
| Example conversation             | A dialogue real Dutch people would actually say, translated line by line        |
| Practice exercises and answers   | Eight to twelve exercises that also recycle older material, with a full key     |

At the end of every level there is a **review and test** chapter with a summary, a combined vocabulary list, and a longer mixed test, so you can prove to yourself that you are ready for the next level.

## How to Get the Most Out of It

1. **Study a little every day.** Twenty focused minutes daily beats a three hour session on Sunday.
2. **Say everything out loud.** Dutch lives in your mouth, not just your eyes. Read every example sentence aloud.
3. **Do the exercises before peeking.** The answer key is at the bottom of each lesson for a reason. Struggle first, check second.
4. **Trust the recycling.** Old grammar and vocabulary keep resurfacing in later lessons on purpose. If something feels familiar, that is the course doing its job.
5. **Use the review tests honestly.** Pass the level test before moving on, and go back to the referenced lesson whenever something feels shaky.
