## Introduction

You know **Ik woon in Utrecht** from [A0 Lesson 9](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live) and **de grote stad** from [A1 Lesson 11](/notes/a1/11-adjectives). A2 adds full place descriptions and a key Dutch pattern: **er is** and **er zijn** (_there is / there are_). After this lesson you can describe Amsterdam's busy centre, a quiet village, and what exists in a neighbourhood: **Er is een mooi park**, **Er zijn veel musea**, **Het uitzicht is prachtig**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Describing a place: zijn + adjective

Use **zijn** + adjective to say what a place is like. No **e** ending in the predicate:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Amsterdam is **groot** en **druk**. | Amsterdam is big and busy. |
| Dit dorp is **klein** en **rustig**. | This village is small and quiet. |
| De buurt is **gezellig**. | The neighbourhood is cosy. |
| Het centrum is **modern** en **levendig**. | The city centre is modern and lively. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De omgeving** is **landelijk** en **schilderachtig**. | The surroundings are rural and picturesque. |
| **Het uitzicht** op **de rivier** is **prachtig**. | The view of the river is beautiful. |
| **De kust** is **bekend** om **de duinen**. | The coast is famous for the dunes. |

Combine traits with **en**, or stack before a noun from [A2 Lesson 8](/notes/a2/08-describing-people): **een drukke, moderne stad**, **een rustige, groene omgeving**.

### City vs countryside

| Dutch | English | Typical features |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **de stad** | city | druk, modern, veel mensen |
| **het dorp** | village | klein, rustig, landelijk |
| **het platteland** | countryside | natuur, stilte, ruimte |
| **de voorstad** | suburb | rustiger dan het centrum |
| **de wijk** / **de buurt** | district / neighbourhood | wonen, winkels, scholen |
| **het centrum** | city centre | winkels, musea, drukte |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **woon** in **de stad**, **maar** ik **werk** op **het platteland**. | I live in the city, but I work in the countryside. |
| **Het dorp** heeft **twee** straten en **één** kerk. | The village has two streets and one church. |
| **De voorstad** is **rustiger** **dan** **het centrum**. | The suburb is quieter than the city centre. |

From [A2 Lesson 6](/notes/a2/06-comparatives): **drukker dan**, **groter dan**, **rustiger dan**. From [A2 Lesson 7](/notes/a2/07-superlatives): **de drukste stad**, **het mooiste uitzicht**.

### er is and er zijn: there is / there are

Dutch uses **er** to say that something **exists** in a place. This is your first step toward the full **er** system in [A2 Lesson 16](/notes/a2/16-er-constructions).

| Pattern | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Er is** + singular | there is | **Er is** een park. |
| **Er zijn** + plural | there are | **Er zijn** veel musea. |
| **Er is geen** + singular | there is no | **Er is geen** station. |
| **Er zijn geen** + plural | there are no | **Er zijn geen** wolkenkrabbers. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Er is** een **mooi** museum **in** het centrum. | There is a beautiful museum in the city centre. |
| **Er zijn** **drie** bruggen **over** de rivier. | There are three bridges over the river. |
| **Er is geen** supermarkt **in** dit dorp. | There is no supermarket in this village. |
| **Zijn er** veel toeristen **in** de zomer? | Are there many tourists in summer? |

**Er** almost always comes **first** in neutral statements. The verb (**is** / **zijn**) agrees with the noun that follows:

| Subject noun | Verb | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| een park (singular) | **is** | **Er is** een park. |
| veel musea (plural) | **zijn** | **Er zijn** veel musea. |
| geen station (singular) | **is** | **Er is geen** station. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  **Er is** and **er zijn** are the existential use of **er**. Full **er** patterns (**er ligt**, **er loopt**, partitive **er**) come in [A2 Lesson 16](/notes/a2/16-er-constructions) and the Grammar Track: [The Word _er_](/notes/grammar/28-er-constructions).
</Callout>

### Place phrase after er is / er zijn

Add **where** with **in**, **bij**, **op**, or **aan**. Preposition detail comes in [A2 Lesson 10](/notes/a2/10-prepositions-place). For now, learn these fixed chunks:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Er is** een markt **in** de buurt. | There is a market in the neighbourhood. |
| **Er zijn** veel cafés **in** het centrum. | There are many cafés in the city centre. |
| **Er is** een strand **aan** de kust. | There is a beach on the coast. |
| **Er is** een kerk **op** het plein. | There is a church on the square. |
| **Er zijn** geen fabrieken **in** dit dorp. | There are no factories in this village. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **In** Amsterdam **zijn er** veel grachten. | In Amsterdam there are many canals. |
| **Op** het platteland **is er** meer stilte. | In the countryside there is more quiet. |

**In + plaats + zijn er** is common when the place comes first for emphasis.

### Questions with er

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Is er** een supermarkt **hier**? | Is there a supermarket here? |
| **Zijn er** veel parken **in** de stad? | Are there many parks in the city? |
| **Is er** **ook** een museum? | Is there a museum too? |
| **Hoeveel** bruggen **zijn er**? | How many bridges are there? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ja**, **er is** een supermarkt **om de hoek**. | Yes, there is a supermarket around the corner. |
| **Nee**, **er zijn geen** wolkenkrabbers **in** Utrecht. | No, there are no skyscrapers in Utrecht. |

### liggen: where a place is situated

**Liggen** (_to lie_) describes where cities, villages, rivers, and regions are **located**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Utrecht **ligt** **in** het midden van Nederland. | Utrecht lies in the middle of the Netherlands. |
| Het dorp **ligt** **aan** de rivier. | The village lies on the river. |
| De kust **ligt** **in** het westen. | The coast lies in the west. |
| Waar **ligt** het station? | Where is the station located? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Amsterdam **ligt** **noordelijker** **dan** Utrecht. | Amsterdam lies further north than Utrecht. |
| **De fabriek** **ligt** **buiten** de stad. | The factory lies outside the city. |

**Liggen** is for flat geography. [A2 Lesson 10](/notes/a2/10-prepositions-place) covers **staan** and **zitten** for objects.

### Opinions about places

From [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/a2/05-opinions):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **vind** **het platteland** **rustiger** **dan** de stad. | I find the countryside quieter than the city. |
| Ik **denk dat** **er** **te** **weinig** parken **zijn**. | I think there are too few parks. |
| **Vind** jij **deze buurt** **gezellig**? | Do you find this neighbourhood cosy? |
| **Volgens mij** is **het uitzicht** **het mooist** **hier**. | In my opinion the view is most beautiful here. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Er** before **is** is often a light schwa: **er-is**, almost one word. Drill: **Er is een park** · **Er zijn twee musea** · **Is er een café?** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Er zijn with a singular noun</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Er zijn** een park in de buurt.

    ✅ **Er is** een park in de buurt.

    **Een park** is singular → **er is**. **Twee parken** or **veel parken** → **er zijn**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping er in existence sentences</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Is** een museum **in** het centrum?

    ✅ **Is er** een museum **in** het centrum?

    Dutch needs **er** for _there is / there are_. Without **er**, the sentence sounds incomplete.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using hebben instead of er zijn</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Heeft** er veel musea in de stad?

    ✅ **Zijn er** veel musea in de stad?

    For neutral _there are_, use **er zijn**. **Hebben** is for possession (**De stad heeft een groot museum**), not existence.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Wrong verb with liggen</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Utrecht **is** **aan** de rivier. (when meaning location on a map)

    ✅ Utrecht **ligt** **in** het midden van Nederland.

    **Liggen** describes where a place sits geographically. **Zijn** + adjective describes what it is like (**Utrecht is gezellig**).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Stad of platteland?</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam compare two weekend destinations.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** **Waar** gaan wij **dit** weekend naartoe? **De stad** of **het platteland**?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Ik vind** **het platteland** **rustiger**. **Er is** daar **meer** natuur.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Dat klopt**. **Maar in** de stad **zijn er** meer musea en restaurants. **Het centrum** is **levendig**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Ken** jij **Giethoorn**? **Een schilderachtig** dorp **met** kanalen.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**! **Er zijn geen** auto's **in** het centrum. **Alleen** boten en fietsen.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Het uitzicht** op **het meer** is **prachtig**. **De ligging** is **perfect** **voor** een rustig weekend.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Is er** **ook** een goed restaurant?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Ja**, **er is** een **klein** restaurant **aan** het water. **Volgens mij** **is** dat **het beste** **van** de regio.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Dan** kiezen wij **het platteland**. **Er is** **te** **weinig** stilte **in** Amsterdam **op** zaterdag!

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** Where are we going this weekend? The city or the countryside?
    >
    > **Sam:** I find the countryside quieter. There is more nature there.
    >
    > **Mila:** True. But in the city there are more museums and restaurants. The city centre is lively.
    >
    > **Sam:** Do you know Giethoorn? A picturesque village with canals.
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes! There are no cars in the centre. Only boats and bikes.
    >
    > **Sam:** The view of the lake is beautiful. The location is perfect for a quiet weekend.
    >
    > **Mila:** Is there a good restaurant too?
    >
    > **Sam:** Yes, there is a small restaurant on the water. In my opinion that is the best in the region.
    >
    > **Mila:** Then we choose the countryside. There is too little quiet in Amsterdam on Saturday!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
