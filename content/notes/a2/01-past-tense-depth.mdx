## Introduction

You can already say **Gisteren werkte ik thuis** and **Wij gingen naar het museum**. A2 starts by organising what you know: Dutch verbs fall into two past tense families. **Zwakke werkwoorden** (weak verbs) add **te** or **de**. **Sterke werkwoorden** (strong verbs) change their vowel: **drinken** → **dronk**, **schrijven** → **schreef**. After this lesson you can spot which type a verb is, predict its past form, and tell a longer story mixing both kinds without guessing wrong endings.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Two families: zwak and sterk

Dutch teachers call verbs **zwak** (weak) or **sterk** (strong). English learners often say **regular** and **irregular**, but Dutch **sterk** verbs follow patterns too. They are not random.

| Type | Dutch name | Past signal | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Weak | zwak werkwoord | **te / de** on the stem | werken → **werkte** |
| Strong | sterk werkwoord | vowel change in the stem | drinken → **dronk** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik werkte** gisteren. | I worked yesterday. (weak) |
| **Ik dronk** koffie. | I drank coffee. (strong) |
| **Hij schreef** een e-mail. | He wrote an email. (strong) |
| **Zij woonde** in Groningen. | She lived in Groningen. (weak) |

**Good news:** if a verb takes **te/de** in the past, it is zwak. If the vowel changes, it is sterk. You already know both types from A1. A2 teaches you to **predict** and **group** them.

### How to recognise a zwak verb

**Step 1:** Remove **en** to find the stem. **werken** → **werk**.

**Step 2:** Check the last letter of the stem against **'t ex kofschip**.

**Step 3:** Add **te/ten** or **de/den**.

| Infinitive | Stem | Past ik | Past wij | Type |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| werken | werk | werkte | werkten | zwak |
| maken | maak | maakte | maakten | zwak |
| wonen | woon | woonde | woonden | zwak |
| luisteren | luister | luisterde | luisterden | zwak |
| antwoorden | antwoord | antwoordde | antwoordden | zwak |

If you can form **werkte**, the verb is zwak. This covers most new verbs you meet.

### How to recognise a sterk verb

Sterk verbs change the vowel in the stem. The plural often changes the vowel again.

| Infinitive | Present ik | Past ik | Past wij | Vowel change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| drinken | drink | dronk | dronken | i → o |
| schrijven | schrijf | schreef | schreven | ij → ee |
| lezen | lees | las | lazen | ee → a |
| nemen | neem | nam | namen | ee → a |
| geven | geef | gaf | gaven | ee → a |
| spreken | spreek | sprak | spraken | ee → a |
| vinden | vind | vond | vonden | i → o |
| blijven | blijf | bleef | bleven | ij → ee |
| krijgen | krijg | kreeg | kregen | ij → ee |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Gisteren dronk** ik drie koppen thee. | Yesterday I drank three cups of tea. |
| **Wij schreven** een brief aan de buurman. | We wrote a letter to the neighbour. |
| **Hij vond** het boek niet. | He did not find the book. |
| **Zij bleven** tot middernacht. | They stayed until midnight. |

### The A1 core: always sterk

These six verbs from [A1 Lesson 19](/notes/a1/19-simple-past-irregular) are sterk. Learn them as a fixed set:

| Infinitive | ik (past) | wij (past) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| zijn | was | waren |
| hebben | had | hadden |
| gaan | ging | gingen |
| komen | kwam | kwamen |
| doen | deed | deden |
| eten | at | aten |

Never add **te/de** to these: ❌ **wasde**, ❌ **hadde**, ❌ **gingde**.

### Mixed stories: weak and sterk together

Real Dutch mixes both types in one paragraph. Time words from A1 still trigger the past:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Gisteren werkte** ik thuis en **dronk** ik veel koffie. | Yesterday I worked at home and drank a lot of coffee. |
| **Toen** ik **kwam**, **maakte** mijn moeder soep. | When I came, my mother made soup. |
| **Vorige week woonden** wij in een hotel en **gingen** wij elke dag naar het museum. | Last week we stayed in a hotel and went to the museum every day. |
| **Eergisteren schreef** ik een e-mail en **kreeg** ik snel antwoord. | The day before yesterday I wrote an email and got a quick reply. |

Word order stays **verb second** in main clauses, exactly as in [A1 Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo): **Gisteren werkte ik**, not **Gisteren ik werkte**.

### Spoken Dutch: past simple vs perfect (preview)

In **spoken** Dutch, people often prefer the **perfect tense** (**Ik heb gewerkt**, **Ik heb gedronken**) over **Ik werkte**, **Ik dronk**. Both are correct.

| Written / story style | Spoken / everyday |
| --- | --- |
| **Gisteren werkte** ik thuis. | **Gisteren heb** ik thuis **gewerkt**. |
| **Ik dronk** koffie. | **Ik heb** koffie **gedronken**. |

At A1 you learned **werkte** because it is clear and common in writing. **Sterk** verbs especially appear in the simple past in stories: **Ik dronk**, **Hij schreef**. You master the perfect tense in [A2 Lesson 2](/notes/a2/02-perfect-tense-intro). For now, know that **zwak** verbs get **ge** + stem + **t/d** in the perfect (**gewerkt**), while **sterk** verbs have special participles (**gedronken**, **geschreven**).

### Tier A: three new sterk verbs (full past tables)

#### drinken (to drink) — sterk: dronk

From [A1 Lesson 6](/notes/a1/06-food-and-drink). Vowel **i** → **o**:

| Pronoun | drinken (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | dronk | I drank |
| jij / je | dronk | you drank |
| u | dronk | you drank (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | dronk | he / she drank |
| wij / we | dronken | we drank |
| jullie | dronken | you drank (plural) |
| zij / ze | dronken | they drank |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **dronk** gisteren geen koffie. | I did not drink coffee yesterday. |
| **Dronk** je wijn op het feest? | Did you drink wine at the party? |
| Hij **dronk** alleen water. | He only drank water. |
| Wij **dronken** samen thee. | We drank tea together. |
| Zij **dronken niet** veel. | They did not drink much. |

#### schrijven (to write) — sterk: schreef

Vowel **ij** → **ee**:

| Pronoun | schrijven (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | schreef | I wrote |
| jij / je | schreef | you wrote |
| u | schreef | you wrote (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | schreef | he / she wrote |
| wij / we | schreven | we wrote |
| jullie | schreven | you wrote (plural) |
| zij / ze | schreven | they wrote |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **schreef** een brief aan mijn ouders. | I wrote a letter to my parents. |
| **Schreef** je het rapport **gisteren**? | Did you write the report yesterday? |
| Zij **schreef** drie e-mails. | She wrote three emails. |
| Wij **schreven** het samen. | We wrote it together. |
| Hij **schreef niet** terug. | He did not write back. |

#### nemen (to take) — sterk: nam

From [A1 Lesson 7](/notes/a1/07-at-the-restaurant). Vowel **ee** → **a**:

| Pronoun | nemen (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | nam | I took |
| jij / je | nam | you took |
| u | nam | you took (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | nam | he / she took |
| wij / we | namen | we took |
| jullie | namen | you took (plural) |
| zij / ze | namen | they took |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **nam** de trein naar Utrecht. | I took the train to Utrecht. |
| **Nam** je soep of pasta? | Did you have soup or pasta? |
| Hij **nam** een taxi. | He took a taxi. |
| Wij **namen** de bus. | We took the bus. |
| Zij **namen geen** dessert. | They did not have dessert. |

### Tier C: zwak verbs you can trust

If a verb is not in the sterk lists above and not one of the six A1 core irregulars, try **te/de** first. These zwak verbs appear often in past stories:
