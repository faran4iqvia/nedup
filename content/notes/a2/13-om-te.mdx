## Introduction

You already send the infinitive to the end with modals (**Ik wil Nederlands leren**) from [A1 Lesson 12](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs) and separable verbs (**Ik sta om zeven uur op**) from [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs). A2 adds **om … te** + infinitive for **purpose**: **Ik ga naar de cursus om Nederlands te leren**, **Ik kom om te helpen**, **Het is te laat om te gaan**. After this lesson you can explain **why** you do something in Dutch.

## Quick Review

From [A1 Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo): verb second; second verb at the end. From [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock): **om acht uur** (_at_ eight o'clock) uses **om** for time, not purpose. From [A2 Lesson 11](/notes/a2/11-prepositions-time): **naar**, **voor**, **na**. From [A2 Lesson 12](/notes/a2/12-relative-clauses): verb-final subordinate patterns. Today **om te** expresses **in order to**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### om … te + infinitive: in order to

Use **om … te** + infinitive to say **why** someone does something, or **what the goal** is:

| Pattern | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **om te** + infinitive | in order to | **om te** leren |
| main clause + **om te** … | do X in order to Y | Ik **ga** naar school **om te** leren. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** naar de bibliotheek **om te** studeren. | I am going to the library to study. |
| Zij **komt** **om te** helpen. | She is coming to help. |
| Wij **werken** hard **om geld te** verdienen. | We work hard to earn money. |
| Hij **leert** Nederlands **om in** Nederland **te** werken. | He learns Dutch to work in the Netherlands. |

**Om te** usually comes at the **end** of the sentence, after the main clause.

  Full **om te** rules, including separable verbs and **te** + adjective patterns, live in the Grammar Track: [Purpose with _om te_](/notes/grammar/25-om-te-infinitive).
</Callout>

### Word order: main clause first, om te last

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** **naar** Amsterdam **om vrienden te** bezoeken. | I am going to Amsterdam to visit friends. |
| **Moet** je **vroeg** **opstaan** **om op** tijd **te** zijn? | Do you have to get up early to be on time? |
| Ik **koop** brood **om** sandwiches **te** maken. | I buy bread to make sandwiches. |
| Zij **sport** **om** fit **te** blijven. | She exercises to stay fit. |

The **conjugated verb** stays in position 2. **Om te** + infinitive closes the sentence, like a modal infinitive.

### om (time) vs om te (purpose)

Do not confuse **om** + clock time with **om te** + infinitive:

| **om** + time | **om te** + infinitive |
| --- | --- |
| **Om** acht uur **ga** ik **naar** werk. | Ik **ga** **naar** werk **om geld te** verdienen. |
| At eight o'clock I go to work. | I go to work to earn money. |
| **Om** half zes **eten** wij. | Wij **eten** vroeg **om** op tijd **te** zijn. |
| We eat at 5:30. | We eat early to be on time. |

From [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock): **om** + time = _at_ (a moment). **Om te** = _in order to_ (a goal).

### te before the infinitive: never forget te

After **om**, Dutch always uses **te** before the infinitive:

| Wrong | Right |
| --- | --- |
| ❌ om **werken** | ✅ om **te** werken |
| ❌ om **leren** | ✅ om **te** leren |
| ❌ om **kopen** | ✅ om **te** kopen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **bel** je **om** een afspraak **te** maken. | I am calling you to make an appointment. |
| **Wat** **doe** je **om** gezond **te** blijven? | What do you do to stay healthy? |

### Separable verbs: te in the middle

From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs): with separable verbs, **te** sits **between** the prefix and the verb stem in **om te** phrases:

| Infinitive | om te form |
| --- | --- |
| opstaan | om op **te** staan |
| meekomen | om mee **te** komen |
| uitgaan | om uit **te** gaan |
| aankomen | om aan **te** komen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **sta** vroeg op **om op** tijd **te** zijn. | I get up early to be on time. |
| **Kom** je mee **om** een film **te** kijken? | Are you coming along to watch a film? |
| Wij **gaan** uit **om** vrienden **te** ontmoeten. | We go out to meet friends. |

In main clauses the prefix still goes to the end (**Ik sta op**). In **om te** phrases, **te** splits the infinitive: **op + te + staan**.

### te + adjective + om te: too … to

A second pattern uses **te** + adjective + **om te** (_too … to_):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben te** moe **om te** gaan. | I am too tired to go. |
| Het **is te** laat **om te** bellen. | It is too late to call. |
| De jas **is te** duur **om te** kopen. | The coat is too expensive to buy. |
| Het **is te** koud **om te** zwemmen. | It is too cold to swim. |

From [A1 Lesson 16](/notes/a1/16-weather): **Het is te koud om te wandelen**. Same pattern.

| Structure | Example |
| --- | --- |
| **te** + adjective + **om te** + infinitive | te moe **om te** sporten |
| **het** + **te** + adjective + **om te** … | Het is te warm **om te** werken |

### iets, niets, wat + om te

Use **om te** after a noun to describe **purpose or function**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Iets om te** eten | Something to eat |
| **Niets om te** doen | Nothing to do |
| **Wat** **om te** drinken? | Something to drink? |
| **Een reden om te** blijven | A reason to stay |
| **Tijd om te** gaan | Time to go |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Heb** je **iets om te** eten? | Do you have something to eat? |
| **Is** er **nog** **iets om te** bespreken? | Is there still something to discuss? |
| Dit **is** **een goed** moment **om te** beginnen. | This is a good moment to start. |

### Purpose (om te) vs reason (omdat) preview

| Purpose | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| **om te** + infinitive | **omdat** + clause |
| Ik ga naar de winkel **om brood te kopen**. | Ik ga naar de winkel **omdat** ik brood **nodig heb**. |
| I go to buy bread. (goal) | I go because I need bread. (cause) |

**Omdat** clauses go deeper in [A2 Lesson 14](/notes/a2/14-subordinate-want-omdat). For now: **om te** = goal forward; **omdat** = explanation backward.

### Combining with modals and perfect tense

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** naar Nederland **gaan om te** studeren. | I want to go to the Netherlands to study. |
| Je **moet** oefenen **om beter te** worden. | You have to practise to get better. |
| Ik **heb** hard **gewerkt om geld te** verdienen. | I worked hard to earn money. |
| **Kun** je langzaam **praten om me te** helpen? | Can you speak slowly to help me? |

Modal or perfect verb in position 2; **om te** still at the end.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Om te** is often one flow: **om-te-leren**, **om-geld-te-verdienen**. Do not stress **te** heavily. Drill: **Ik ga sporten om fit te blijven.** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Waarom leer je Nederlands?</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam talk about goals and motivation after a lesson.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** **Waarom** **ben** jij **begonnen** **met** Nederlands? **Wat** is je **doel**?
    >
    > **Sam:** Ik **wil** **in** Nederland **werken**. **Daarvoor** **moet** ik **de taal** **leren**. **Mijn** **ambitie** is **om** **volgend** jaar **te** solliciteren.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Dat** is **een** **duidelijk** **doel**. **Hoe** **blijf** je **gemotiveerd**?
    >
    > **Sam:** Ik **oefen** **elke** dag **om beter te** worden. Soms **ben** ik **te** moe **om te** studeren, **maar** **dan** **lees** ik **iets** **lichts** **om** **toch** **bezig** **te** blijven.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Slim**. Ik **ga** **vaak** **naar** het café **om** met vrienden **Nederlands te** spreken. **Dat** is **nuttig** **én** **gezellig**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Goed** **idee**. **Zullen** we **volgende** week **samen** **gaan** **om te** oefenen?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**! Ik **stel** **voor** **om** **op** woensdag **te** beginnen. **Om** zes uur **is** **goed** **voor** mij.

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** Why did you start Dutch? What is your goal?
    >
    > **Sam:** I want to work in the Netherlands. For that I have to learn the language. My ambition is to apply for jobs next year.
    >
    > **Mila:** That is a clear goal. How do you stay motivated?
    >
    > **Sam:** I practise every day to get better. Sometimes I am too tired to study, but then I read something light to still stay active.
    >
    > **Mila:** Clever. I often go to the café to speak Dutch with friends. That is useful and cosy.
    >
    > **Sam:** Good idea. Shall we go together next week to practise?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes! I propose starting on Wednesday. Six o'clock is good for me.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
