## Introduction

You know **de afspraak** from [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock), **afspreken** and **afzeggen** from [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs), and **Zullen we afspreken?** from [A2 Lesson 4](/notes/a2/04-modals-in-depth). A2 adds polite scheduling: **Zou u een afspraak kunnen inplannen?**, **Past het u dinsdag om tien uur?**, and formal email openings. After this lesson you can book a doctor's visit, reschedule a meeting, and write a professional message in Dutch.

## Quick Review

From [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock): **Hoe laat is de afspraak?**, **op tijd**, **te laat**. From [A2 Lesson 11](/notes/a2/11-prepositions-time): **op maandag**, **om** tien uur, **van … tot …**, **uiterlijk**. From [A2 Lesson 13](/notes/a2/13-om-te): **Ik bel je om een afspraak te maken**. From [A2 Lesson 15](/notes/a2/15-indirect-objects): **Stuur me een bevestiging**. Today you put it all together for real appointments.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### afspraak maken and inplannen

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** **graag** een **afspraak** **maken**. | I would like to make an appointment. |
| **Kunt** u **een** **tijd** **inplannen**? | Can you schedule a time? |
| **Wanneer** **zijn** jullie **beschikbaar**? | When are you available? |
| **Zullen** we **vrijdag** **afspreken**? | Shall we arrange Friday? |

**Inplannen** = to schedule something in a calendar. **Afspraak maken** = to create an appointment. **Afspreken** (separable) = to agree on a time: **Wij spreken dinsdag af**.

From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs): **Ik spreek** met de dokter **af**. With a modal, the prefix stays on the infinitive: **Ik wil** vrijdag **afspreken**.

### Proposing and confirming times

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Hoe** **zit** het **met** dinsdag **om** tien uur? | How about Tuesday at ten o'clock? |
| **Past** het **u** **op** donderdag? | Does Thursday suit you? |
| **Het** **komt** me **goed** **uit**. | It suits me well. |
| **Het** **komt** me **niet** **uit**. | It does not suit me. |
| **Dat** **tijdslot** is **bezet**. | That time slot is taken. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **stel** **voor**: **woensdag** **om** half twee. | I suggest: Wednesday at 1:30. |
| **Is** **dat** **passend** **voor** u? | Is that suitable for you? |
| **Prima**, **dan** **spreken** we **dat** **af**. | Fine, then we will arrange that. |
| **Ik** **stuur** u **een** **bevestigingsmail**. | I will send you a confirmation email. |

From [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock): **half twee** = 1:30. From [A2 Lesson 11](/notes/a2/11-prepositions-time): **op** + day, **om** + clock time.

### Zou u … kunnen? Polite requests

**Zou** + pronoun + verb is softer than a bare command. With **kunnen**, it asks politely if something is possible:

| Direct | Polite |
| --- | --- |
| **Bel** me **terug**. | **Zou** u **mij** **kunnen** **terugbellen**? |
| **Verzet** de afspraak. | **Zou** u **de** **afspraak** **kunnen** **verzetten**? |
| **Stuur** een **bevestiging**. | **Zou** u **een** **bevestiging** **kunnen** **sturen**? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zou** u **een** **afspraak** **voor** **volgende** **week** **kunnen** **inplannen**? | Could you schedule an appointment for next week? |
| **Zou** het **mogelijk** **zijn** **om** **vroeger** **te** **komen**? | Would it be possible to come earlier? |
| **Zouden** jullie **de** **afspraak** **kunnen** **bevestigen**? | Could you confirm the appointment? |

Informal: **Zou je … kunnen?** · Formal: **Zou u … kunnen?**

From [A2 Lesson 4](/notes/a2/04-modals-in-depth): **zou / zouden**. Full **als** conditionals come in [A2 Lesson 20](/notes/a2/20-conditional-als).

### Cancelling and rescheduling

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **moet** de afspraak **afzeggen**. | I have to cancel the appointment. |
| **Kunnen** we **de** **afspraak** **verzetten**? | Can we reschedule the appointment? |
| Ik **kan** **helaas** **niet** **komen**. | Unfortunately I cannot come. |
| **Is** **er** **nog** een **plek** **op** de **wachtlijst**? | Is there still a spot on the waiting list? |

From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs): **afzeggen** → **Ik zeg** de afspraak **af**. **Verzetten** = move to another time.

### On the phone

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Met** wie **spreek** ik? | Who am I speaking to? |
| **U** **spreekt** **met** Mila. | You are speaking with Mila. |
| **Een** **momentje** **alstublieft**. | One moment, please. |
| **Bent** u **aan** de **lijn**? | Are you on the line? |
| **Ik** **verbind** u **door**. | I will put you through. |
| **Kunt** u **later** **terugbellen**? | Can you call back later? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Goedemorgen**, **praktijk** De Vries, **waarmee** **kan** ik **u** **helpen**? | Good morning, De Vries practice, how can I help you? |
| **Ik** **wil** **graag** een **afspraak** **maken** **voor** een **consult**. | I would like to make an appointment for a consultation. |
| **Neemt** u **alstublieft** **op** **na** de **toon**. | Please pick up after the tone. (voicemail) |

**Opnemen** = to answer or pick up the phone. **Terugbellen** = to call back.

### Formal email structure

| Part | Dutch | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Opening | **Geachte** mevrouw Jansen, | Dear Ms Jansen, |
| Opening (male) | **Geachte** heer Bakker, | Dear Mr Bakker, |
| Opening (neutral) | **Geachte** heer/mevrouw, | Dear Sir/Madam, |
| Reason | **Ik** **schrijf** u **om** een **afspraak** **te** **maken**. | I am writing to make an appointment. |
| Proposal | **Zou** **dinsdag** **om** tien uur **u** **schikken**? | Would Tuesday at ten o'clock suit you? |
| Closing | **Met** **vriendelijke** **groet**, | Kind regards, |
| Name | Mila de Vries | |

Informal email: **Hoi Sam,** or **Beste Sam,** · **Groetjes,** / **Hartelijke groet,**

| Formal | Informal |
| --- | --- |
| **Geachte** mevrouw … | **Beste** … / **Hoi** … |
| **Met vriendelijke groet** | **Groetjes** / **Tot snel** |
| **Zou u … kunnen?** | **Kun je …?** / **Zou je … kunnen?** |

### Useful appointment chunks

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wat** **zijn** uw **openingstijden**? | What are your opening hours? |
| **Hoe** **lang** **duurt** het **consult**? | How long does the consultation last? |
| **Ik** **krijg** **een** **herinnering**, **toch**? | I will get a reminder, right? |
| **De** **afspraak** **staat** **in** mijn **agenda**. | The appointment is in my calendar. |
| **Tot** **dinsdag** **om** tien uur. | See you Tuesday at ten o'clock. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  Separable **afspreken** and **afzeggen** are in [Separable Verbs](/notes/grammar/15-separable-verbs). Polite **zou** requests are expanded in [A2 Lesson 20: Conditional with _als_](/notes/a2/20-conditional-als).
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Alstublieft** flows as **als-tu-blieft**. In phone phrases, stress **AF-spraak**, **terug-BEL-len**, **be-ves-tiging**. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

  <CardContent>

  </CardContent>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">

    ❌ **Kun** je me helpen? (to a doctor's reception with **u**)

    ✅ **Zou** u **mij** **kunnen** **helpen**? / **Kunt** u **mij** **helpen**?

    With formal **u**, use **Kunt u** or **Zou u … kunnen?**
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Een afspraak bij de dokter</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila calls the doctor's office to book a consultation.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Receptionist:** **Goedemorgen**, **huisartsenpraktijk** De Vries. **Waarmee** **kan** ik **u** **helpen**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Goedemorgen**. **Ik** **wil** **graag** een **afspraak** **maken** **voor** een **consult**.
    >
    > **Receptionist:** **Natuurlijk**. **Wat** **is** uw **beschikbaarheid** **volgende** **week**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Zou** **dinsdag** **om** tien uur **mogelijk** **zijn**?
    >
    > **Receptionist:** **Een** **momentje** **alstublieft** … **Nee**, **dat** **tijdslot** is **bezet**. **Past** **woensdag** **om** half elf **u**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**, **dat** **komt** me **goed** **uit**.
    >
    > **Receptionist:** **Prima**. **Ik** **plan** het **in**. **U** **krijgt** een **bevestigingsmail** **en** een **herinnering** **per** **sms**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Dank** u **wel**. **Moet** ik **iets** **meenemen** **naar** het **consult**?
    >
    > **Receptionist:** **Neem** uw **identiteitsbewijs** **mee**. **Tot** **woensdag** **om** half elf.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Tot** **woensdag**. **Fijne** **dag**!

    **In English:**

    > **Receptionist:** Good morning, De Vries GP practice. How can I help you?
    >
    > **Mila:** Good morning. I would like to make an appointment for a consultation.
    >
    > **Receptionist:** Of course. What is your availability next week?
    >
    > **Mila:** Would Tuesday at ten o'clock be possible?
    >
    > **Receptionist:** One moment please … No, that time slot is taken. Does Wednesday at 10:30 suit you?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, that suits me well.
    >
    > **Receptionist:** Fine. I will schedule it. You will get a confirmation email and a reminder by text.
    >
    > **Mila:** Thank you. Should I bring anything to the consultation?
    >
    > **Receptionist:** Bring your ID. See you Wednesday at 10:30.
    >
    > **Mila:** See you Wednesday. Have a nice day!
