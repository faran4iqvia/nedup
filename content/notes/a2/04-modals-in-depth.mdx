## Introduction

You know **Ik wil koffie drinken** and **Kun je me helpen?** A2 takes modals further: full **zullen** for promises and suggestions, **Ik heb moeten werken** with two infinitives at the end, and the subtle differences between **mogen**, **moeten**, and **kunnen**. After this lesson you can handle modals in everyday speech, past narratives, and perfect tense sentences without breaking word order.

## Quick Review

From [A1 Lesson 12](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs): **willen**, **kunnen**, **moeten**, **mogen** with one infinitive at the end. From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs): modals keep separable verbs as **one word** (**Ik wil opstaan**). From [A2 Lesson 3](/notes/a2/03-perfect-irregular): perfect tense preview **Ik heb moeten werken**. Today **zullen** joins the set and the double infinitive becomes a core skill.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The five modals at A2

| Modal | Core meaning | ik form |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **willen** | want | wil |
| **kunnen** | can / be able to | kan |
| **moeten** | must / have to | moet |
| **mogen** | may / be allowed to | mag |
| **zullen** | shall / will | zal |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** morgen **rusten**. | I want to rest tomorrow. |
| **Kun** je me **helpen**? | Can you help me? |
| Je **moet** een ticket **kopen**. | You have to buy a ticket. |
| **Mag** ik hier **roken**? | May I smoke here? |
| Ik **zal** je morgen **bellen**. | I will call you tomorrow. |

Modals are **irregular**. The infinitive sits at the **end** in main clauses. **Niet** goes before the infinitive: **Ik wil niet werken**.

### zullen: will, shall, and soft predictions

**zullen** expresses future intention, promises, suggestions, and educated guesses.

| Pronoun | zullen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | zal | I will / shall |
| jij / je | zult / zal | you will |
| u | zult / zal | you will (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | zal | he / she will |
| wij / jullie / zij | zullen | we / you / they will |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zal** je **helpen**. | I will help you. (promise) |
| **Zullen** we **gaan**? | Shall we go? |
| **Zul** je **komen**? | Will you come? |
| Dat **zal** wel **goedkomen**. | That will probably work out. |
| Hij **zal** wel **moe** zijn. | He is probably tired. |

**Zullen we …?** is the standard way to suggest a plan: **Zullen we koffie drinken?** · **Zullen we vanavond uitgaan?**

**Zal / zullen + wel** softens a guess, a bit like _probably_ in English: **Het zal wel regenen**.

### Nuances: mogen, moeten, kunnen

#### mogen = permission or prohibition

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Mag** ik **binnenkomen**? | May I come in? |
| Je **mag** hier niet **roken**. | You may not smoke here. |
| **Mogen** wij **fotograferen**? | Are we allowed to take photos? |
| Kinderen **mogen** niet te laat **blijven**. | Children are not allowed to stay late. |

#### moeten = obligation or strong expectation

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **moet** morgen **werken**. | I have to work tomorrow. |
| Je **moet** een paspoort **hebben**. | You need a passport. |
| Het **moet** goed **zijn**. | It must be okay. / It has to be fine. |
| **Moet** je nu **gaan**? | Do you have to go now? |

**Moeten** can mean _supposed to_ as well as _must_: **Je moet dit proberen** = You should try this.

#### hoeven = need to (usually with niet)

**Hoeven** almost always appears in the negative: **niet hoeven** = do not need to.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **hoef** morgen niet te **werken**. | I do not need to work tomorrow. |
| **Hoeft** u nu te **gaan**? | Do you need to go now? |
| Wij **hoeven** niet te **betalen**. | We do not need to pay. |

Past: **hoefde / hoefden**. Perfect: **Ik heb niet hoeven te werken** (advanced; recognition only at A2).

#### kunnen = ability or possibility

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **kan** Nederlands **spreken**. | I can speak Dutch. (ability) |
| **Kan** het **regenen**? | Can it rain? / Might it rain? |
| Dat **kan** **kloppen**. | That could be right. |
| Ik **kan** morgen niet **komen**. | I cannot come tomorrow. |

### Modals + separable verbs

The separable verb stays **one word** at the end:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** vroeg **opstaan**. | I want to get up early. |
| **Moet** je **meenemen**? | Do you have to bring it along? |
| Wij **zullen** vanavond **uitgaan**. | We will go out tonight. |
| **Mag** ik **meespreken**? | May I join the conversation? |

From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs): no split with modals. **opstaan**, not **sta … op**.

### Double infinitive in the perfect tense

When a modal appears inside the perfect tense, Dutch puts **two infinitives** at the end:

**hebben + modal infinitive + main verb infinitive**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **heb** **moeten** **werken**. | I had to work. / I have had to work. |
| Ik **heb** **willen** **komen**. | I wanted to come. |
| Ik **heb** **kunnen** **slapen**. | I was able to sleep. |
| Ik **heb** niet **mogen** **roken**. | I was not allowed to smoke. |
| Wij **hebben** **willen** **uitgaan**. | We wanted to go out. |

| Present | Perfect with modal |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **moet** werken. | Ik **heb** **moeten** **werken**. |
| **Kon** je **komen**? | **Heb** je **kunnen** **komen**? |
| Zij **wil** **opstaan**. | Zij **heeft** **willen** **opstaan**. |

**No past participle** on the main verb when a modal infinitive is present. ❌ **Ik heb moeten gewerkt**. ✅ **Ik heb moeten werken**.

### Modals in the simple past

| Infinitive | ik (past) | wij (past) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| willen | wilde / wou | wilden / wouden |
| kunnen | kon | konden |
| moeten | moest | moesten |
| mogen | mocht | mochten |
| zullen | zou | zouden |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **moest** gisteren **werken**. | I had to work yesterday. |
| **Kon** je **komen**? | Could you come? |
| Zij **wilde** niet **uitgaan**. | She did not want to go out. |
| Wij **mochten** niet **roken**. | We were not allowed to smoke. |
| Ik **zou** je **bellen**. | I would call you. / I was going to call you. |

**Zou / zouden** also appear in conditionals later ([A2 Lesson 20](/notes/a2/20-conditional-als)). For now, read **Ik zou** as _I would_ or _I was going to_.

### zullen vs gaan for the future

Both express future meaning. **Gaan** + infinitive is more concrete and near ([B1 preview](/notes/b1/04-future-with-zullen)):

| Dutch | English | Nuance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** morgen **werken**. | I am going to work tomorrow. | concrete plan |
| Ik **zal** je **bellen**. | I will call you. | promise |
| **Zullen** we **afspreken**? | Shall we make plans? | suggestion |
| Het **gaat** **regenen**. | It is going to rain. | immediate / visible |

At A2, recognise both. Use **zullen** for suggestions and promises, **gaan** for fixed near future plans.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Unstressed modals in fast speech: _ik-MOET-werken_, _kun-je-KOM-en_. **Zullen** in **Zullen we gaan?** has clear stress on **Zul**. Drill five modal sentences without pausing before the final infinitive. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Plannen voor het weekend</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Noor and Daan discuss work rules and weekend plans.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Noor:** Hoi Daan! **Moet** jij **morgen** **werken**?
    >
    > **Daan:** Ja, helaas. Ik **moet** naar een **vergadering** en ik **moet** de **deadline** **halen**. Jij?
    >
    > **Noor:** Ik **hoef** niet te **werken**. **Zullen** we **zaterdag** iets **afspreken**?
    >
    > **Daan:** Goed **voorstel**! **Zullen** we naar de film **gaan**? Ik **heb** **vrijdagavond** al **willen** **uitgaan**, maar ik **moest** **thuisblijven**.
    >
    > **Noor:** Jammer. **Mag** je **zaterdag** wel **uitgaan**?
    >
    > **Daan:** Ja, dat **mag**. Mijn ouders **zullen** wel **thuis** zijn, maar dat **kan** **geen** kwaad.
    >
    > **Noor:** **Zullen** we om zeven uur **afspreken**? Dan **kunnen** we daarna iets **eten**.
    >
    > **Daan:** Prima. **Mag** ik je **opbellen** als ik later **ben**?
    >
    > **Noor:** Natuurlijk. Ik **zal** je **bericht** **sturen** als ik er ben.
    >
    > **Daan:** **Hopelijk** **kan** het **regenen** niet. Ik **heb** **vorige week** **moeten** **binnenblijven** door het weer.
    >
    > **Noor:** Dat **zal** wel **goedkomen**, hoor. **Zullen** we **vrijdag** nog even **bellen**?

    **In English:**

    > **Noor:** Hi Daan! Do you have to work tomorrow?
    >
    > **Daan:** Yes, unfortunately. I have to go to a meeting and I have to meet the deadline. You?
    >
    > **Noor:** I do not have to work. Shall we make plans for Saturday?
    >
    > **Daan:** Good idea! Shall we go to the cinema? I wanted to go out on Friday evening, but I had to stay home.
    >
    > **Noor:** Too bad. Are you allowed to go out on Saturday?
    >
    > **Daan:** Yes, I am. My parents will probably be home, but that is fine.
    >
    > **Noor:** Shall we meet at seven o'clock? Then we can eat something afterwards.
    >
    > **Daan:** Great. May I call you if I am late?
    >
    > **Noor:** Of course. I will send you a message when I am there.
    >
    > **Daan:** Hopefully it cannot rain. I had to stay indoors last week because of the weather.
    >
    > **Noor:** It will probably work out. Shall we call again on Friday?
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
