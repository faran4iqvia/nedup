## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Talking about your job: three patterns

Dutch uses three main patterns to say what you do:

| Pattern | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **werken als** + profession | your role | Ik **werk als** verkoper. |
| **werken bij** + organisation | your employer | Ik **werk bij** een bank. |
| **zijn** + profession | who you are professionally | Ik **ben** verpleegkundige. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wat** **doe** je **voor** **werk**? | What do you do for work? |
| **Waar** **werk** je? | Where do you work? |
| **Wat** **is** je **functie**? | What is your position? |
| Ik **werk als** programmeur **bij** een startup. | I work as a programmer at a startup. |
| Zij **is** manager **op** de **personeelsafdeling**. | She is a manager in the HR department. |

### werken als + profession (no article)

After **werken als**, Dutch uses the profession **without** **de** / **het**:

| Correct | Wrong |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk als** **accountant**. | Ik werk als **de** accountant. |
| Hij **werkt als** **ingenieur**. | Hij werkt als **een** ingenieur. |
| **Werk** jij **als** **verkoper**? | |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Mijn broer **werkt als** **kok** **in** een restaurant. | My brother works as a cook in a restaurant. |
| **Werk** je **als** **stagiair** **of** **fulltime**? | Do you work as an intern or full-time? |
| Na haar **stage** **werkt** zij **als** **ontwerper**. | After her internship she works as a designer. |

### werken bij + company / organisation

**Bij** means **at** or **for** an employer:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk bij** Philips. | I work at Philips. |
| Zij **werkt bij** een **ziekenhuis**. | She works at a hospital. |
| **Waar** **werk** je **precies**? **Bij** de **gemeente**. | Where exactly do you work? At the municipality. |
| **Hoeveel** mensen **werken** **bij** jullie? | How many people work at your company? |

You can combine **als** and **bij**: **Ik werk als consultant bij een adviesbureau.**

### zijn + profession

**Zijn** + profession also drops the article in most job talk:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben** **accountant**. | I am an accountant. |
| Hij **is** **baas** **van** het **team**. | He is the boss of the team. |
| **Ben** jij **verantwoordelijk** **voor** de planning? | Are you responsible for the planning? |

**Werk als** focuses on the role. **Ben** focuses on identity: **Ik ben lerares**, **Ik werk als lerares op een basisschool**.

### Where you work: op, bij, thuis

| Pattern | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **op** kantoor | Ik **werk** **op** kantoor. | I work at the office. |
| **thuis** | Ik **werk** **thuis** **op** vrijdag. | I work at home on Friday. |
| **bij** de klant | Hij **werkt** **bij** de klant. | He works at the client's site. |
| **op** de afdeling | Zij **zit** **op** de IT-afdeling. | She is in the IT department. |

From [A1 Lesson 1](/notes/a1/01-daily-routines): **thuiswerken**. From [A2 Lesson 10](/notes/a2/10-prepositions-place): **op** and **bij** for location.

### verantwoordelijk voor: duties

To describe duties, use **verantwoordelijk voor** + noun:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben** **verantwoordelijk** **voor** het **team**. | I am responsible for the team. |
| Zij **is** **verantwoordelijk** **voor** de **planning**. | She is responsible for the planning. |
| **Wie** **is** **verantwoordelijk** **voor** deze **taak**? | Who is responsible for this task? |
| Wij **zijn** **allemaal** **verantwoordelijk** **voor** de **kwaliteit**. | We are all responsible for the quality. |

### Fulltime, parttime, and hours

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk** **fulltime**. | I work full-time. |
| Zij **werkt** **parttime**, **vier** dagen **per** week. | She works part-time, four days per week. |
| Mijn **werktijd** is **van** negen **tot** vijf. | My working hours are from nine to five. |
| **Moet** je **overwerken**? | Do you have to work overtime? |
| **Neem** je **pauze** **om** twaalf uur? | Do you take a break at twelve o'clock? |

### Applying for jobs: solliciteren

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zoek** **werk**. | I am looking for work. |
| Ik **solliciteer** **op** een **vacature**. | I am applying for a vacancy. |
| **Heb** je je **cv** **al** **gestuurd**? | Have you sent your CV already? |
| **Wanneer** **is** je **sollicitatiegesprek**? | When is your job interview? |
| Zij **is** **aangenomen**! | She was hired! |
| Hij **neemt** **ontslag**. | He is resigning. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De werkgever** **zoekt** een **nieuwe** **werknemer**. | The employer is looking for a new employee. |
| **Er** **is** een **vacature** **op** de **personeelsafdeling**. | There is a vacancy in the HR department. |
| **Ben** je **in** **dienst** **bij** een **bedrijf**? | Are you employed at a company? |

### Questions colleagues ask

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wat** **doe** je **hier** **precies**? | What exactly do you do here? |
| **Hoe** **lang** **werk** je **al** **hier**? | How long have you worked here already? |
| **Op** **welke** **afdeling** **zit** je? | Which department are you in? |
| **Vind** je je **werk** **leuk**? | Do you like your work? |
| **Hoe** **is** je **nieuwe** **baas**? | How is your new boss? |

From [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/a2/05-opinions): **Ik vind mijn werk interessant**. From [A2 Lesson 12](/notes/a2/12-relative-clauses): **De collega die naast mij zit is nieuw**.

### kennen vs weten: people and facts

English uses _know_ for both. Dutch splits it:

| Verb | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **kennen** | know a person, place, or thing (familiarity) | Ik **ken** mijn collega goed. |
| **weten** | know a fact (information) | Ik **weet** waar de vergaderzaal is. |

| Pronoun | kennen | weten |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | ken | weet |
| jij / je | kent | weet |
| hij / zij / het | kent | weet |
| wij / jullie / zij | kennen | weten |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ken** je de **nieuwe** **manager**? | Do you know the new manager? |
| **Weet** je **waar** de **receptie** **is**? | Do you know where reception is? |
| Ik **ken** hem **van** de **vorige** **baan**. | I know him from the previous job. |
| Ik **weet** **niet** **of** hij **vandaag** **komt**. | I do not know whether he is coming today. |
| **Weten** jullie **al** **wie** **verantwoordelijk** **is**? | Do you already know who is responsible? |

**Weet je het?** / **Weet je dat?** = Do you know? · **Ik weet het niet** = I do not know.

Practise **kennen** vs **weten** in **Section B** of the exercises below — right after the core **werken als** patterns.

### Perfect tense: gekend and geweten

| Verb | Past participle | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| kennen | **gekend** | Ik heb hem jaren gekend. |
| weten | **geweten** | Ik heb het niet geweten. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Heb je **geweten** dat er een **vacature** **is**? | Did you know there is a vacancy? |
| Ik heb die **collega** **nog** **niet** **gekend**. | I have not met that colleague yet. |

### zeggen and geven at work

You learned **zeggen** and **geven** in A1. At work they appear constantly:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zeg** het **aan** de **manager**. | Tell the manager. |
| **Geef** mij **het** **rapport**, **alsjeblieft**. | Give me the report, please. |
| Hij **zegt** dat de **vergadering** **uitvalt**. | He says the meeting is cancelled. |
| Kun je mij de **link** **geven**? | Can you give me the link? |
| **Wat** **zei** de **baas** **gisteren**? | What did the boss say yesterday? |

From [A2 Lesson 15](/notes/a2/15-indirect-objects): **Geef het me** · **Zeg het haar**.

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  **Kennen** (familiarity) vs **weten** (facts) is essential A2 Dutch. **Werken als** without an article and **werken bij** for employers are practised here. Modal verbs at work (**moeten**, **kunnen**) are in [Modal Verbs in Depth](/notes/grammar/20-modal-verbs-depth).
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Werkgever**, **werknemer**, and **vergaderzaal** stress the first syllable. **Sollicitatie** is **so-li-ci-ta-tie**. Say **Ik werk als …** as one smooth phrase. See [Word Stress](/notes/pronunciation/03-word-stress).
</Callout>

  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **Baan** = job (general): **Ik zoek een baan.**

    **Functie** = position / role: **Mijn functie is projectmanager.**

    Both are useful; **functie** is more formal and specific.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using weten for people</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Weet** je mijn collega?

    ✅ **Ken** je mijn collega?

    **Kennen** = familiarity with a person. **Weten** = knowing a fact: **Weet je waar hij werkt?**
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Een nieuwe collega op kantoor</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila welcomes Sam on his first day at a new company.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** Hoi Sam, **welkom**! **Wat** **doe** je **voor** **werk** **hier** **precies**?
    >
    > **Sam:** Dank je! Ik **werk als** **projectmanager** **bij** dit **bedrijf**. **Vandaag** **is** mijn **eerste** **werkdag**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Leuk**! **Op** **welke** **afdeling** **zit** je?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Op** de **afdeling** **marketing**. Ik **ben** **verantwoordelijk** **voor** **twee** **projecten** **en** **het** **team** **van** **vijf** **personen**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Werk** je **fulltime** **of** **parttime**?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Fulltime**, **van** negen **tot** vijf. **Op** vrijdag **werk** ik **thuis**. **Is** **er** een **vergaderzaal** **vrij** **om** tien uur?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**, **er** **is** **een** **vrije** **vergaderzaal** **op** de **tweede** **verdieping**. **Neem** **eerst** **even** **pauze** **en** **meld** je **bij** de **receptie**. **Onze** **baas** **wil** je **even** **ontmoeten**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Prima**. **Hoe** **lang** **werk** jij **al** **hier**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Al** **drie** **jaar**. **Ik** **werk** **als** **ontwerper**. **Ken** je **onze** **baas** **al**? **Zij** **is** **heel** **direct**, **maar** **eerlijk**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Nee**, **nog** **niet**. **Weet** jij **waar** **haar** **kantoor** **is**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**, **op** de **eerste** **verdieping**. **Ik** **geef** je **het** **nummer** **straks**. **Volgens** mij **ga** je het **hier** **leuk** **vinden**!

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** Hi Sam, welcome! What exactly do you do for work here?
    >
    > **Sam:** Thanks! I work as a project manager at this company. Today is my first workday.
    >
    > **Mila:** Nice! Which department are you in?
    >
    > **Sam:** In the marketing department. I am responsible for two projects and the team of five people.
    >
    > **Mila:** Do you work full-time or part-time?
    >
    > **Sam:** Full-time, from nine to five. On Friday I work from home. Is there a meeting room free at ten o'clock?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, there is one on the second floor. Take a break first and report to reception. Our boss wants to meet you briefly.
    >
    > **Sam:** Fine. How long have you worked here already?
    >
    > **Mila:** Already three years. I work as a designer. Do you know our boss already? She is very direct, but honest.
    >
    > **Sam:** No, not yet. Do you know where her office is?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, on the first floor. I will give you the number later. In my opinion you will enjoy it here!
  </CardContent>
