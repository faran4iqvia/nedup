## Introduction

You met **in de keuken**, **op de tafel**, and **naast het bed** in [A1 Lesson 13](/notes/a1/13-housing), and **Er is een kerk op het plein** in [A2 Lesson 9](/notes/a2/09-describing-places). A2 teaches prepositions of **static location** in depth: when to use **in**, **op**, **aan**, **bij**, and **naast**, plus **staan**, **liggen**, and **zitten**. After this lesson you can say exactly where a building, person, or object is: **Het museum ligt in het centrum**, **De bank staat naast de ingang**, **Ik wacht bij het station**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Static location: no movement

This lesson covers **where something is**, not **where it goes**. Movement prepositions (**naar**, **van**, **door**) come in [A2 Lesson 11](/notes/a2/11-prepositions-time).

| Static (this lesson) | Movement (Lesson 11) |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben in** de winkel. | Ik **ga naar** de winkel. |
| Het boek **ligt op** de tafel. | Ik **leg** het boek **op** de tafel. |
| Wij **zitten bij** het raam. | Wij **lopen naar** het raam. |

Ask **Waar …?** for location. Answer with **preposition + place**.

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  Full preposition tables live in the Grammar Track: [Prepositions of Place](/notes/grammar/22-prepositions-place). Movement and time prepositions come in [Grammar 23](/notes/grammar/23-prepositions-time-movement) with [A2 Lesson 11](/notes/a2/11-prepositions-time).
</Callout>

### in: inside, in cities and countries

Use **in** when something is **inside** an enclosed space, or **in** a city, region, or country:

| Use | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| room / building | **in** de keuken | in the kitchen |
| city / country | **in** Amsterdam | in Amsterdam |
| area / district | **in** het centrum | in the city centre |
| corner (inside) | **in** de hoek | in the corner |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De kat **zit in** de woonkamer. | The cat is in the living room. |
| Het museum **ligt in** het centrum. | The museum is in the city centre. |
| **Er zijn** veel grachten **in** Amsterdam. | There are many canals in Amsterdam. |
| De sleutels **liggen in** de la. | The keys are in the drawer. |

**In** = enclosed or within boundaries. Cities and countries take **in**: **in Nederland**, **in de buurt**, **in het park**.

### op: on a surface, on a floor, on a square

Use **op** when something rests **on top of** a surface, or **on** an open platform like a square or floor level:

| Use | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| surface | **op** de tafel | on the table |
| floor level | **op** de begane grond | on the ground floor |
| open platform | **op** het plein | on the square |
| island / open land | **op** het platteland | in the countryside |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het boek **ligt op** de tafel. | The book is on the table. |
| De markt **is op** het plein. | The market is on the square. |
| Ik **woon op** de tweede verdieping. | I live on the second floor. |
| **Er staat** een monument **op** het plein. | There is a monument on the square. |

❌ **in** het plein → ✅ **op** het plein. A square is an open surface, not a closed box.

### aan: at the edge, contact, water

Use **aan** when something is **at** or **on** the edge of something, especially water, rivers, coasts, and contact points:

| Use | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| water / coast | **aan** de kust | on the coast |
| river / canal | **aan** de gracht | on the canal |
| contact / attached | **aan** de deur | on the door (knocker, sign) |
| side of | **aan** de linkerkant | on the left side |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het dorp **ligt aan** de rivier. | The village lies on the river. |
| **Er is** een strand **aan** de kust. | There is a beach on the coast. |
| Het huis **staat aan** de gracht. | The house stands on the canal. |
| De klok **hangt aan** de muur. | The clock hangs on the wall. |

**Aan** often pairs with **liggen** and **staan** for geography: **aan de kust**, **aan het water**, **aan de overkant**.

### bij: near, at a landmark, at someone's place

Use **bij** for **near** or **at** a reference point. Also **at someone's home**:

| Use | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| near a place | **bij** het station | near the station |
| at someone's place | **bij** mij thuis | at my place |
| with / at an institution | **bij** de ingang | at the entrance |
| company | **bij** de apotheek | at the pharmacy |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wacht** je **bij** het station? | Are you waiting at the station? |
| De bushalte **is bij** de supermarkt. | The bus stop is near the supermarket. |
| **Kom** je **bij** ons eten? | Are you coming to eat at our place? |
| Het café **ligt bij** de brug. | The café is near the bridge. |

**Bij** = close to, not necessarily inside. **In het station** = inside the building. **Bij het station** = near it, outside or at the entrance.

### naast: right next to

**Naast** means **directly beside**, with nothing in between:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De bank **staat naast** de ingang. | The bench stands next to the entrance. |
| **Woon** je **naast** de buren? | Do you live next to the neighbours? |
| Het park **ligt naast** de school. | The park is next to the school. |
| **Zit** je **naast** het raam? | Are you sitting next to the window? |

From [A1 Lesson 13](/notes/a1/13-housing): **naast het bed**, **naast ons**.

### tussen, onder, boven, voor, achter, tegen

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **tussen** | between | **tussen** de twee gebouwen |
| **onder** | under | **onder** de brug |
| **boven** | above / over | **boven** de winkel |
| **voor** | in front of | **voor** het stadhuis |
| **achter** | behind | **achter** de kerk |
| **tegen** | against | **tegen** de muur |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De tafel **staat tussen** de stoelen. | The table stands between the chairs. |
| De boot **ligt onder** de brug. | The boat is under the bridge. |
| De slaapkamer **is boven**, de keuken **beneden**. | The bedroom is upstairs, the kitchen downstairs. |
| **Tussen** Amsterdam **en** Utrecht **liggen** veel steden. | Between Amsterdam and Utrecht lie many cities. |

**Tussen X en Y** is a fixed pattern for between two points.

### staan, liggen, zitten + preposition

From [A1 Lesson 13](/notes/a1/13-housing). At A2, combine them deliberately with prepositions:

| Verb | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **staan** | upright / tall objects | De lamp **staat op** de tafel. |
| **liggen** | flat / geography | Het dorp **ligt aan** de rivier. |
| **zitten** | inside / seated | Wij **zitten in** de tuin. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Waar staat** de supermarkt? | Where is the supermarket (located)? |
| **Waar ligt** het station? | Where is the station situated? |
| **Waar zit** de sleutel? | Where is the key (inside something)? |
| De fiets **staat voor** het huis. | The bike stands in front of the house. |

**Waar is …?** works everywhere. **Waar staat/ligt …?** sounds more natural for objects and places.

### Fixed chunks to learn as units

| Chunk | English |
| --- | --- |
| **in** het centrum | in the city centre |
| **op** het plein | on the square |
| **aan** de kust / rivier / gracht | on the coast / river / canal |
| **bij** het station / de ingang | at / near the station / entrance |
| **naast** de kerk | next to the church |
| **om de hoek** | around the corner |
| **aan de overkant** | on the other side |
| **tegenover** het plein | opposite the square |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De bakkerij **is om de hoek**. | The bakery is around the corner. |
| Het park **ligt aan de overkant** van de rivier. | The park is on the other side of the river. |
| De halte **staat tegenover** het stadhuis. | The stop is opposite the town hall. |
| **Is er** een café **in de buurt**? | Is there a café nearby? |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Prepositions are unstressed in fast speech: **in-het-centrum**, **op-het-plein**, **bij-het-station**. Drill full phrases, not isolated words. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

  <CardHeader>

  <CardContent>

  </CardContent>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">bij instead of in for cities</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik woon **bij** Amsterdam. (when you live in the city)

    ✅ Ik woon **in** Amsterdam.

    **Bij** = near. **In** = inside the city boundaries. **Bij Amsterdam** means near Amsterdam, outside the city.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping staan/liggen/zitten</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Het boek **is op** de tafel. (understandable, but less natural)

    ✅ Het boek **ligt op** de tafel.

    Dutch prefers **liggen/staan/zitten** for where objects are positioned.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">aan vs op for walls</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Het schilderij hangt **op** de muur.

    ✅ Het schilderij hangt **aan** de muur. / **aan** de muur.

    Something **attached** to a vertical surface often takes **aan**. A poster **op** de muur is also heard. **Aan** is safer for hanging objects.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Waar is het café?</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Sam asks Mila for directions to a meeting spot near the station.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Sam:** Hoi Mila, **waar** moet ik zijn? **Is** het café **in** het station?
    >
    > **Mila:** Nee, **niet in** het station. **Bij** het station, **aan** de overkant van de hoofdstraat.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Aan** welke kant? Links of rechts?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Aan** de linkerkant, **naast** de ingang van de parkeergarage. **Het hoekpand** met **het grote** terras.
    >
    > **Sam:** Oké. **Staat** het terras **op** het plein of **in** een zijstraat?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Op** het plein, **in** het midden, **tegenover** het stadhuis. **Je kunt** niet missen.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Is** er **ergens** een pinautomaat **in de buurt**?
    >
    > **Mila:** Ja, **bij** de uitgang van het station, **naast** de lift. **Om** vijf uur **zit** ik **bij** het raam.
    >
    > **Sam:** Prima. **Dan** zie ik je daar!

    **In English:**

    > **Sam:** Hi Mila, where should I be? Is the café in the station?
    >
    > **Mila:** No, not in the station. Near the station, on the other side of the main street.
    >
    > **Sam:** On which side? Left or right?
    >
    > **Mila:** On the left side, next to the entrance of the car park. The corner building with the big terrace.
    >
    > **Sam:** OK. Is the terrace on the square or in a side street?
    >
    > **Mila:** On the square, in the middle, opposite the town hall. You cannot miss it.
    >
    > **Sam:** Is there an ATM somewhere nearby?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, at the exit of the station, next to the lift. At five o'clock I will be sitting by the window.
    >
    > **Sam:** Fine. Then I will see you there!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
