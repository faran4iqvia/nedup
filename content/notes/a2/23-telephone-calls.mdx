## Introduction

The phone is where many learners freeze: no face, no gestures, and fast Dutch. But phone calls are also highly predictable — the same dozen phrases come up every time. This lesson teaches the conventions Dutch people actually use, starting with the one that surprises every English speaker: you answer the phone by saying your **name**, not "hello". After this you can open a call, ask for someone, be put through, leave a message, and rescue a bad connection. The [Rescheduling by Phone scenario](/notes/scenarios/a2-phone-reschedule) is the drill once you finish here.

## Quick Review

From [Lesson 4](/notes/a2/04-modals-in-depth) you know **mag** and **kan** for polite requests (**Mag ik …?**, **Kunt u …?**). From [Lesson 14](/notes/a2/14-subordinate-want-omdat) you know **omdat** sends the verb to the end — useful for **Ik bel omdat …**. From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs) you know separable verbs: **terugbellen** and **doorverbinden** split exactly the same way.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Answering: you say your name

In the Netherlands you pick up the phone and say **Met** + your name. Answering with just "Hallo?" sounds oddly incomplete:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Met Anna. | Anna speaking. |
| Met Anna de Vries. | Anna de Vries speaking. |
| Met de familie Jansen. | The Jansen family. |
| U spreekt met de receptie. | You've reached reception. |

### Opening the call (the caller)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Goedemorgen, u spreekt met Tom Bakker. | Good morning, this is Tom Bakker. |
| Hallo, met Tom. | Hi, it's Tom. |
| Ik bel over mijn afspraak. | I'm calling about my appointment. |
| Ik bel omdat ik een vraag heb. | I'm calling because I have a question. |

### Asking for someone / being put through

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Mag ik mevrouw De Wit spreken? | May I speak to Ms De Wit? |
| Kunt u mij doorverbinden? | Can you put me through? |
| Een moment, ik verbind u door. | One moment, I'll put you through. |
| Blijft u aan de lijn. | Please stay on the line. |

**Doorverbinden** is separable: **ik verbind u door**, not ~~ik doorverbind u~~.

### When the person is not available

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hij is er niet. | He's not here. |
| Zij is in gesprek. | She's on another call. |
| Kan zij u terugbellen? | Can she call you back? |
| Wilt u een boodschap achterlaten? | Would you like to leave a message? |
| Ik bel later wel terug. | I'll call back later. |

**Terugbellen** is also separable: **ik bel u terug**.

### Bad connection and wrong number

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik kan u slecht verstaan. | I can barely hear you. |
| U valt weg. | You're breaking up. |
| De verbinding is slecht. | The connection is bad. |
| Sorry, verkeerd verbonden. | Sorry, wrong number. |

### Ending the call

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Bedankt voor het bellen. | Thanks for calling. |
| Ik hoor het graag. | I look forward to hearing from you. |
| Fijne dag nog! | Have a nice day! |
| Tot ziens. | Goodbye. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  Phone calls lean on modal requests (**Mag ik … spreken?**, **Kunt u …?**) from [Lesson 4](/notes/a2/04-modals-in-depth) and on separable verbs (**doorverbinden**, **terugbellen**, **ophangen**) from [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs). See [Separable Verbs](/notes/grammar/15-separable-verbs) if the split still feels shaky.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  The Dutch phone-opener **Met …** is said with a short, clear **e** and a crisp **t**. Practise your own line — **Met** + your name — until it is instant, because you say it every single time you answer. Phone openers need crisp rhythm: [Intonation and Rhythm](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm) and [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Answering with "Dit is Tom"</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Hallo, dit is Tom. · ❌ Ik ben Tom.

    ✅ **Met Tom.** / **U spreekt met Tom.**

    Dutch does not calque "this is Tom" on the phone. The fixed opener is **Met …**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Not splitting doorverbinden / terugbellen</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik doorverbind u. · ❌ Ik terugbel u.

    ✅ Ik verbind u **door**. · ✅ Ik bel u **terug**.

    These are separable verbs: the prefix goes to the end of the sentence.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Word order in "Ik bel omdat ..."</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik bel omdat ik heb een vraag.

    ✅ Ik bel omdat ik een vraag **heb**.

    After **omdat** the verb moves to the end, just as in [Lesson 14](/notes/a2/14-subordinate-want-omdat).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying "verkeerd nummer" for a wrong number</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Sorry, verkeerd nummer.

    ✅ Sorry, **verkeerd verbonden**.

    The fixed Dutch phrase is **verkeerd verbonden** (wrongly connected), not a literal "wrong number".
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Learning Tips

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Script your opening line</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Before any real call, write your first two sentences: **Goedemorgen, u spreekt met … Ik bel over …** Having the opening ready removes the worst of the phone panic.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Keep a rescue phrase ready</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    When the line is bad or the Dutch is too fast, you are not stuck: **Sorry, ik kan u slecht verstaan. Kunt u dat herhalen?** (from [A0 Lesson 14](/notes/a0/14-getting-by-survival-phrases)). One rescue phrase turns a failed call into a successful one.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Calling a company and being put through</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>A customer phones about an invoice and leaves a message.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Receptie:** Bakker & Co, u spreekt met Sanne. Goedemiddag.
    >
    > **Klant:** Goedemiddag, u spreekt met Peter Vos. Mag ik meneer De Wit spreken?
    >
    > **Receptie:** Een moment, ik verbind u door. … Sorry, hij is in gesprek. Kan hij u terugbellen?
    >
    > **Klant:** Ja graag. Ik bel over een factuur, factuurnummer 2043.
    >
    > **Receptie:** Ik noteer het. Op welk nummer kan hij u bereiken?
    >
    > **Klant:** Op 06 … sorry, u valt een beetje weg. Kunt u mij horen?
    >
    > **Receptie:** Nu wel. Zegt u het maar.
    >
    > **Klant:** 06-12345678. Bedankt alvast!
    >
    > **Receptie:** Ik geef de boodschap door. Fijne dag nog!
    >
    > **Klant:** U ook, tot ziens.

    **In English:**

    > **Reception:** Bakker & Co, Sanne speaking. Good afternoon.
    >
    > **Customer:** Good afternoon, this is Peter Vos. May I speak to Mr De Wit?
    >
    > **Reception:** One moment, I'll put you through. … Sorry, he's on another call. Can he call you back?
    >
    > **Customer:** Yes please. I'm calling about an invoice, invoice number 2043.
    >
    > **Reception:** I'll note it down. On what number can he reach you?
    >
    > **Customer:** On 06 … sorry, you're breaking up a bit. Can you hear me?
    >
    > **Reception:** Now I can. Go ahead.
    >
    > **Customer:** 06-12345678. Thanks in advance!
    >
    > **Reception:** I'll pass the message on. Have a nice day!
    >
    > **Customer:** You too, goodbye.

    Notice **u spreekt met …**, **ik verbind u door**, **hij is in gesprek**, **u valt weg**, and **Ik bel over …** — the backbone of a real Dutch call.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
