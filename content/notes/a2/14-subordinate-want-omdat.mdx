## Introduction

You met **want** in [A1 Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo) (**Ik ben moe, want ik werk veel**) and **dat** clauses in [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/a2/05-opinions) (**Ik denk dat hij komt**). A2 adds **omdat** for **because** with the verb at the end: **Ik blijf thuis omdat ik ziek ben**. After this lesson you can explain causes in spoken Dutch with **want** and **omdat**, plus **daarom** for _that is why_. Extra connectors like **doordat** and **aangezien** appear in this lesson for recognition only.

## Quick Review

From [A1 Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo): **en**, **maar**, **want** connect two main clauses; verb stays in position 2 in both halves. From [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/a2/05-opinions): **dat** clause → verb last (**Ik vind dat het goed is**). From [A2 Lesson 12](/notes/a2/12-relative-clauses): **die/dat** clauses → verb last. From [A2 Lesson 13](/notes/a2/13-om-te): **om te** = purpose; **omdat** = reason. Today **want** and **omdat** complete the because system.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Subordinate clauses: verb to the end

A **subordinate clause** ( **bijzin** ) depends on a **main clause** ( **hoofdzin** ). In Dutch, the conjugated verb goes to the **end** of the subordinate clause:

| Main clause (V2) | Subordinate clause (verb last) |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **blijf** thuis | **omdat** ik ziek **ben** |
| Hij **komt** niet | **omdat** hij moe **is** |
| Wij **gaan** later | **omdat** de trein vertraagd **is** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **blijf** thuis, **omdat** ik ziek **ben**. | I am staying home because I am sick. |
| **Omdat** ik ziek **ben**, **blijf** ik thuis. | Because I am sick, I am staying home. |
| **Denk** je **dat** hij **komt**? | Do you think he is coming? |

You already know this pattern from **dat** and **die/dat**. **Omdat** follows the same rule.

### omdat: because (subordinate)

Use **omdat** to give a **reason**. The verb goes **last** in the **omdat** clause:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **eet** niet, **omdat** ik **geen** honger **heb**. | I am not eating because I am not hungry. |
| **Omdat** het **regent**, **neem** ik **een** paraplu **mee**. | Because it is raining, I take an umbrella. |
| Zij **is** blij, **omdat** zij **geslaagd** **is**. | She is happy because she passed. |
| **Waarom** **blijf** je thuis? **Omdat** ik ziek **ben**. | Why are you staying home? Because I am sick. |

**Omdat** works in **spoken and written** Dutch. It sounds complete and neutral.

### want: because (two main clauses)

From [A1 Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo): **want** also means **because**, but it connects two **main** clauses. **Both** verbs stay in position 2:

| with **omdat** (verb last) | with **want** (V2 + V2) |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **blijf** thuis, **omdat** ik ziek **ben**. | Ik **blijf** thuis, **want** ik **ben** ziek. |
| **Omdat** hij moe **is**, **gaat** hij **naar** bed. | Hij **is** moe, **want** hij **werkt** veel. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **koop** brood, **want** ik **heb** honger. | I am buying bread because I am hungry. |
| **Kom** je niet, **want** je **bent** ziek? | You are not coming because you are sick? |
| Het **is** **gezellig**, **want** iedereen **is** er. | It is cosy because everyone is here. |

**Want** feels conversational: you add a reason on the spot, like _you see, …_ in English.

### want vs omdat: when to use which?

| | **want** | **omdat** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Clause type | two main clauses | subordinate clause |
| Verb position | V2 in both halves | verb last after **omdat** |
| Register | spoken, informal | spoken + written |
| Comma | often before **want** | often before **omdat** |
| Example | Ik **rust**, **want** ik **ben** moe. | Ik **rust**, **omdat** ik moe **ben**. |

| Tip | Use |
| --- | --- |
| Quick chat, giving a reason | **want** |
| Fuller explanation, writing, exams | **omdat** |
| Verb already at end pattern | **omdat** (matches **dat**, **die**) |

Both are correct. **Want** is not slang. **Omdat** is not only formal. In daily talk, Dutch people use **both** all the time.

### omdat clause first: inversion in the main clause

When **omdat** starts the sentence, the subordinate clause is one block. The **main clause** still has V2:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Omdat** ik ziek **ben**, **blijf** ik thuis. | Because I am sick, I stay home. |
| **Omdat** de trein vertraagd **is**, **komen** wij later. | Because the train is delayed, we arrive later. |
| **Omdat** het **morgen** **regent**, **neem** ik **een** jas **mee**. | Because it will rain tomorrow, I take a coat. |

**Omdat** … **ben/is** , **verb** subject … Main verb in position 2 after the comma.

### Purpose (om te) vs reason (omdat) vs reason (want)

From [A2 Lesson 13](/notes/a2/13-om-te):

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **om te** + infinitive | in order to (purpose) | Ik **ga** naar de winkel **om brood te kopen**. |
| **omdat** + clause | because (reason) | Ik **ga** naar de winkel, **omdat** ik brood **nodig heb**. |
| **want** + main clause | because (reason) | Ik **ga** naar de winkel, **want** ik **heb** honger. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **leer** Nederlands **om** in Nederland **te** werken. | I learn Dutch to work in the Netherlands. (goal) |
| Ik **leer** Nederlands, **omdat** ik **in** Nederland **woon**. | I learn Dutch because I live in the Netherlands. (cause) |
| Ik **leer** Nederlands, **want** het **is** **nuttig**. | I learn Dutch because it is useful. (reason, chat) |

### daarom: therefore (main clause)

**Daarom** starts a **main** clause (V2) — it is not a subordinate connector:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben** ziek. **Daarom** **blijf** ik thuis. | I am sick. That is why I am staying home. |
| Het **regent**. **Daarom** **neem** ik **een** paraplu. | It is raining. That is why I take an umbrella. |
| Zij **is** **geslaagd**. **Daarom** **vieren** wij **vanavond**. | She passed. That is why we are celebrating tonight. |

<Callout title="Recognition only (do not drill yet)" type="info">
  You will **see** these in dialogues and vocab lists. Treat them like extra flavours of _because_ / _unless_ until B1:

  | Word | Meaning | Example |
  | --- | --- | --- |
  | **doordat** | because (due to the fact) | **Doordat** het **regende**, **bleven** wij thuis. |
  | **aangezien** | since / given that | **Aangezien** je **druk** **bent**, **gaan** wij **later**. |
  | **tenzij** | unless | Ik **ga**, **tenzij** het **regent**. |

  **Doordat** and **aangezien** send the verb to the end, like **omdat**. Focus your exercises on **want**, **omdat**, **daarom**, and **om te** vs **omdat**.
</Callout>

### Combining with modals and perfect tense in omdat clauses

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **blijf** thuis, **omdat** ik **moet** **werken**. | I am staying home because I have to work. |
| Hij **is** blij, **omdat** hij **heeft** **geslaagd**. | He is happy because he passed. |
| **Omdat** ik **niet** **kon** **komen**, **heb** ik **gebeld**. | Because I could not come, I called. |
| Zij **eet** **niet**, **want** zij **wil** **afvallen**. | She is not eating because she wants to lose weight. |

Inside **omdat**: modal or auxiliary before the main verb at the end (**omdat ik moet werken**, **omdat hij heeft geslaagd**).

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  In fast speech, **omdat ik** sounds like **om-dat-ik**, one flow. The verb at the end still needs a clear stress: **omdat ik ziek BEN**. Drill: **Ik blijf thuis, omdat ik ziek ben.** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Waarom ben je niet op kantoor?</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Sam explains to Mila why he worked from home.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** Hoi Sam, **waarom** **was** je **gisteren** **niet** op kantoor?
    >
    > **Sam:** Ik **werkte** thuis, **want** ik **was** **niet** **zo** **goed**. **Omdat** ik **koorts** **had**, **moest** ik **binnenblijven**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ah**, **logisch**. **Ben** je **nu** **beter**?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Ja**, **dank** je. **Doordat** ik **gisteren** **heb** **gerust**, **voel** ik me **vandaag** **beter**. **Daarom** **ben** ik **weer** **aan** het **werk**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Goed** zo. **Waarom** **belde** je **niet**?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Omdat** ik **dacht** **dat** het **niet** **ernstig** **was**. **Achteraf** **was** dat **geen** **goed** **idee**, **want** jullie **wisten** **niets**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Geen** probleem. **Aangezien** je **nu** **beter** **bent**, **kunnen** wij **vanmiddag** **samen** **werken** **aan** het **project**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Prima**. Ik **kom** **om** twee uur, **tenzij** de **vergadering** **langer** **duurt**.

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** Hi Sam, why were you not at the office yesterday?
    >
    > **Sam:** I worked from home because I was not feeling well. Because I had a fever, I had to stay inside.
    >
    > **Mila:** Ah, logical. Are you better now?
    >
    > **Sam:** Yes, thank you. Because I rested yesterday, I feel better today. That is why I am back at work.
    >
    > **Mila:** Good. Why did you not call?
    >
    > **Sam:** Because I thought it was not serious. In hindsight that was not a good idea, because you knew nothing.
    >
    > **Mila:** No problem. Since you are better now, we can work on the project together this afternoon.
    >
    > **Sam:** Fine. I will come at two o'clock, unless the meeting lasts longer.

    **Doordat**, **aangezien**, and **tenzij** in this dialogue are **recognition-only** connectors from the callout above — focus on **want**, **omdat**, and **daarom** in your own sentences for now.
