## Introduction

You know **Ik ga naar het station** from [A1 Lesson 15](/notes/a1/15-transport) and **Om acht uur** / **van negen tot vijf** from [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock). This lesson covers **movement** (**naar, van, door, uit**) and **time** (**om, op, in, van … tot, sinds, tijdens, na, voor**). After today you can plan trips, schedules, and deadlines in natural Dutch.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Movement vs static place

| Type | Question | Prepositions | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Static (L10) | Waar is …? | in, op, aan, bij | Ik **ben in** de winkel. |
| Movement (today) | Waar ga je heen? | naar, van, door, uit | Ik **ga naar** de winkel. |

Movement pairs with verbs like **gaan**, **komen**, **rijden**, **lopen**, **reizen**, **vertrekken**.

</Callout>

### naar: to / towards (destination)

Use **naar** for movement **towards** a place:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ga **naar** Amsterdam. | I am going to Amsterdam. |
| Wij reizen **naar** Rotterdam. | We travel to Rotterdam. |
| **Kom** je **naar** het feest? | Are you coming to the party? |
| De trein rijdt **naar** Utrecht. | The train goes to Utrecht. |

| Rule | Example |
| --- | --- |
| cities / countries (no article) | **naar** Amsterdam, **naar** Nederland |
| with article | **naar** **de** winkel, **naar** **het** station |
| someone's place | **naar** huis, **naar** de docent |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ga **naar** huis **om** zes uur. | I am going home at six o'clock. |
| **Waar** ga je **naar** toe? | Where are you going (to)? |
| Zij fietst **naar** haar werk. | She cycles to her work. |

**Naar toe** often appears at the end of questions: **Waar ga je naartoe?** In speech, **naartoe** merges: **Waar ga je heen?** is equally common.

### van: from (origin)

Use **van** for movement **away from** a starting point:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik kom **van** het werk. | I am coming from work. |
| De trein vertrekt **van** Amsterdam Centraal. | The train departs from Amsterdam Central. |
| Wij komen **uit** België, **maar** wij wonen **in** Nederland. | We come from Belgium, but we live in the Netherlands. |
| **Van** hier **naar** het station is het tien minuten. | From here to the station is ten minutes. |

**Van** + place = from. **Van … naar …** = from … to …:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Van** maandag **tot** vrijdag werk ik. | From Monday to Friday I work. |
| **Van** Utrecht **naar** Amsterdam duurt het een half uur. | From Utrecht to Amsterdam takes half an hour. |

### door, langs, uit: through, along, out of

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **door** | through | **door** de tunnel |
| **langs** | along / past | **langs** de rivier lopen |
| **uit** | out of / from (origin) | **uit** Nederland komen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wij lopen **door** het park **naar** het museum. | We walk through the park to the museum. |
| De bus rijdt **langs** de kust. | The bus drives along the coast. |
| Hij komt **uit** Frankrijk. | He comes from France. |
| Ik ga **de deur uit** **naar** buiten. | I go out the door outside. |

**Uit** for origin countries: **Ik kom uit Engeland**. **Van** for starting point of a trip: **De trein vertrekt van Utrecht**.

### met and te voet: how you travel

From [A1 Lesson 15](/notes/a1/15-transport):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **met** de trein / bus / metro / fiets | by train / bus / metro / bike |
| **te voet** | on foot |
| **met** de auto | by car |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ga **met** de trein **naar** Den Haag. | I go to The Hague by train. |
| **Ga** jij **te voet** of **met** de bus? | Are you going on foot or by bus? |

### om: at (clock time)

From [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock). **Om** marks **when on the clock** something happens:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Om** acht uur begin ik. | I start at eight o'clock. |
| De vergadering is **om** half tien. | The meeting is at 9:30. |
| **Om** kwart over drie kom ik. | I come at 3:15. |
| **Om** hoe laat vertrek je? | At what time do you depart? |

Also: **om de hoek** (around the corner) is a **place** chunk from [Lesson 10](/notes/a2/10-prepositions-place), not time.

### op: days and dates

Use **op** for **days** and **calendar dates**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **op** maandag | on Monday |
| **op** vrijdag | on Friday |
| **op** het weekend | on the weekend |
| **op** de eerste van januari | on the first of January |
| **op** 18 april | on 18 April |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Op** zaterdag ga ik **naar** de markt. | On Saturday I go to the market. |
| Het feest is **op** de twintigste van maart. | The party is on the twentieth of March. |
| **Op** maandag **om** negen uur. | On Monday at nine o'clock. |

❌ **in** maandag → ✅ **op** maandag.

### in: months, seasons, years, parts of the day

Use **in** for **longer time periods** and **parts of the day** (with **de**):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **in** mei | in May |
| **in** de zomer / winter / lente / herfst | in summer / winter / spring / autumn |
| **in** 2024 | in 2024 |
| **in** de ochtend / middag / avond | in the morning / afternoon / evening |
| **in** het weekend | in the weekend (regional; **op** het weekend also heard) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **In** de zomer reizen wij **naar** de kust. | In summer we travel to the coast. |
| Ik werk **in** de ochtend **thuis**. | I work at home in the morning. |
| **In** januari is het **koud**. | In January it is cold. |

**'s ochtends / 's avonds / 's nachts** are fixed short forms: **Ik sport 's ochtends**.

### van … tot … and tot: from … to / until

| Pattern | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **van … tot …** | from … to … (time span) | **van** negen **tot** vijf |
| **tot** | until | **tot** vrijdag |
| **tot** + time | until (a moment) | **tot** acht uur |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik werk **van** maandag **tot** vrijdag. | I work from Monday to Friday. |
| De winkel is open **van** negen **tot** zes. | The shop is open from nine to six. |
| **Tot** morgen! | Until tomorrow! |
| Blijf **tot** half zes. | Stay until 5:30. |

**Van … tot …** covers both time and place: **van Amsterdam tot Utrecht**.

### sinds: since (then until now)

**Sinds** marks a starting point that still continues to the present:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik woon **sinds** 2020 **in** Utrecht. | I have lived in Utrecht since 2020. |
| **Sinds** maandag ben ik ziek. | I have been sick since Monday. |
| **Sinds** wanneer spreek je Nederlands? | Since when do you speak Dutch? |
| **Sinds** gisteren regent het. | It has been raining since yesterday. |

Use the **present tense** in Dutch after **sinds** when the situation continues: **Ik woon hier sinds januari** (not \*woonde). English uses present perfect; Dutch often uses present.

From [A2 Lesson 2](/notes/a2/02-perfect-tense-intro): perfect tense also expresses duration (**Ik heb sinds maandag hoofdpijn**). At A2, both **sinds + present** and **sinds + perfect** appear.

### tijdens, voor, na, vanaf

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **tijdens** | during | **tijdens** de les |
| **gedurende** | during (formal) | **gedurende** de pauze |
| **voor** | before | **voor** het eten |
| **na** | after | **na** de vergadering |
| **vanaf** | from (starting point forward) | **vanaf** morgen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Tijdens** de rit lees ik. | During the ride I read. |
| **Voor** het werk drink ik koffie. | Before work I drink coffee. |
| **Na** de les ga ik **naar** huis. | After the lesson I go home. |
| **Vanaf** volgende week werk ik thuis. | From next week I work from home. |

### omstreeks, rond, tegen, uiterlijk: approximate and deadline time

| Word | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **omstreeks** | around (approximate) | **omstreeks** drie uur |
| **rond** | around | **rond** een uur |
| **tegen** | by / towards (time) | **tegen** acht uur klaar |
| **uiterlijk** | at the latest | **uiterlijk** vrijdag |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ben **omstreeks** half zes bij je. | I will be at your place around 5:30. |
| **Rond** negen uur begint de film. | The film starts around nine o'clock. |
| Stuur het **uiterlijk** morgen. | Send it by tomorrow at the latest. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Time prepositions are often unstressed: **om-acht-uur**, **op-maandag**, **van-negen-tot-vijf**. Drill full phrases, not isolated words. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Een reis plannen</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam plan a trip from Amsterdam to Brussel with a stopover.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** **Wanneer** vertrek jij **naar** Brussel?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Op** vrijdag **om** half acht **van** Amsterdam Centraal. **Vanaf** half negen heb ik een **tussenstop** **in** Utrecht.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Hoe lang** duurt de **rit**?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Van** Amsterdam **tot** Utrecht een **kwartier**. **Tijdens** de **overstaptijd** drink ik koffie. **Daarna** ga ik **door** **naar** Brussel.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Kom** je **op** tijd aan?
    >
    > **Sam:** **Normaal** wel. **Sinds** vorige maand rijden de treinen soms **vertraagd**. **Uiterlijk** om elf uur ben ik er, denk ik.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Na** de **rit** ga je **naar** het hotel?
    >
    > **Sam:** Ja. **Vanaf** het station loop ik **te voet** **naar** het centrum. Het hotel ligt **bij** het plein.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Stuur** me een bericht **rond** elf uur!

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** When do you depart for Brussels?
    >
    > **Sam:** On Friday at 7:30 from Amsterdam Central. From 8:30 I have a stopover in Utrecht.
    >
    > **Mila:** How long does the ride take?
    >
    > **Sam:** From Amsterdam to Utrecht, a quarter of an hour. During the transfer time I drink coffee. After that I continue on to Brussels.
    >
    > **Mila:** Will you arrive on time?
    >
    > **Sam:** Normally yes. Since last month the trains are sometimes delayed. I think I will be there by eleven o'clock at the latest.
    >
    > **Mila:** After the ride are you going to the hotel?
    >
    > **Sam:** Yes. From the station I walk to the city centre on foot. The hotel is near the square.
    >
    > **Mila:** Send me a message around eleven o'clock!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
