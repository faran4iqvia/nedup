## Introduction

Welcome to A1. You finished A0 with **250 words**, the clock, basic questions, and the verbs **zijn** and **hebben**. Now Dutch becomes useful in a new way: you can describe a full day, from waking up to going to sleep. After this lesson you can tell someone when you work, what you usually do in the morning, and ask **Hoe laat sta je op?** like a real Dutch colleague or neighbour would.

## Quick Review

In [A0 Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock) you learned **Hoe laat is het?**, **om acht uur**, **van negen tot vijf**, and the Dutch **half** rule. In [A0 Lesson 8](/notes/a0/08-days-months-and-dates) you met **maandag** through **zondag** and **het weekend**. In [Lesson 5](/notes/a0/05-hebben-and-everyday-things) you already said **Ik heb haast** and **Ik heb honger**. Today we connect time expressions to actions: not just _when_, but _what you do when_.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Two new core verbs: werken and doen

**Werken** means to work. **Doen** means to do. Together they cover a huge part of daily conversation.

**Werken** follows the regular pattern you have seen with **wonen** in [Lesson 9](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live):

| Pronoun | werken | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | werk | I work |
| jij / je | werkt | you work |
| u | werkt | you work (formal) |
| hij / zij | werkt | he / she works |
| wij / we | werken | we work |
| jullie | werken | you work (plural) |
| zij | werken | they work |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik werk in Rotterdam. | I work in Rotterdam. |
| Waar werk jij? | Where do you work? |
| Zij werkt van negen tot vijf. | She works from nine to five. |
| Wij werken op maandag. | We work on Monday. |

**Doen** is irregular but very common. Learn these forms as a set:

| Pronoun | doen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | doe | I do |
| jij / je | doet | you do |
| u | doet | you do (formal) |
| hij / zij | doet | he / she does |
| wij / we | doen | we do |
| jullie | doen | you do (plural) |
| zij | doen | they do |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wat doe je vandaag? | What are you doing today? |
| Ik doe niets. | I am doing nothing. |
| Wat doet hij op zaterdag? | What does he do on Saturday? |
| Wij doen sport op vrijdag. | We exercise on Friday. |

Notice the **t** drops in questions with **jij**: **Wat doe je?** not **Wat doet je?** Same rule as **Hoe heet je?** from [Lesson 1](/notes/a0/01-greetings-and-introductions).

### Routine verbs: slapen, douchen, koken, sporten

These verbs describe what happens in a normal day:

| Verb | ik | jij | hij/zij | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| slapen | slaap | slaapt | slaapt | to sleep |
| douchen | douche | doucht | doucht | to shower |
| koken | kook | kookt | kookt | to cook |
| sporten | sport | sport | sport | to exercise |
| rusten | rust | rust | rust | to rest |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik slaap om elf uur. | I sleep at eleven o'clock. |
| Douche jij 's ochtends? | Do you shower in the morning? |
| Hij kookt het avondeten. | He cooks dinner. |
| Wij sporten in het weekend. | We exercise at the weekend. |

**Ontbijten** (to have breakfast) and **lunchen** (to have lunch) are also routine verbs. You already know **het ontbijt** and **de lunch** from [Lesson 10](/notes/a0/10-ordering-food-and-drink).

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ontbijt om half acht. | I have breakfast at 7:30. |
| Zij luncht om twaalf uur. | She has lunch at twelve o'clock. |
| Wij eten om zes uur. | We eat at six o'clock. |

### Opstaan: your first separable verb (as a phrase)

**Opstaan** means to get up. The verb splits in normal sentences: **op** moves to the end.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik sta om zeven uur op. | I get up at seven o'clock. |
| Sta jij vroeg op? | Do you get up early? |
| Hij staat om half zeven op. | He gets up at 6:30. |
| Wij staan om acht uur op. | We get up at eight o'clock. |

You do not need the full separable verb rules yet. For now, learn **Ik sta om … op** as one pattern — the **op** at the end is a preview of [Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs).

### Time + routine: om, van ... tot ..., 's ochtends

Connect activities to clock time with **om** (at) and **van ... tot ...** (from ... to):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik sta om zeven uur op. | I get up at seven o'clock. |
| Ik werk van negen tot vijf. | I work from nine to five. |
| De pauze is om twaalf uur. | The break is at twelve o'clock. |
| 's Ochtends ontbijt ik. | In the morning I have breakfast. |
| 's Avonds kijk ik tv. | In the evening I watch TV. |

**'s Ochtends** and **'s avonds** mean in the morning and in the evening. They often come at the start of the sentence, which is natural in Dutch.

### Frequency words: altijd, meestal, soms, nooit

| Dutch | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| altijd | always | Ik sta altijd vroeg op. |
| meestal | usually | Ik werk meestal thuis. |
| soms | sometimes | Soms sport ik. |
| nooit | never | Ik kook nooit 's ochtends. |
| elke dag | every day | Elke dag loop ik naar werk. |

Frequency words usually sit after the verb: **Ik werk meestal van huis.**

### Sequence words: eerst, dan, daarna

Tell a story step by step:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Eerst douche ik. | First I shower. |
| Dan ontbijt ik. | Then I have breakfast. |
| Daarna werk ik. | After that I work. |
| Later rust ik. | Later I rest. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Eerst sta ik op. Dan douche ik. Daarna ontbijt ik. | First I get up. Then I shower. After that I have breakfast. |
| Ik werk van negen tot vijf. Daarna kook ik. | I work from nine to five. After that I cook. |

### Useful questions about routines

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoe laat sta je op? | What time do you get up? |
| Hoe laat ga je slapen? | What time do you go to sleep? |
| Wat doe je op maandag? | What do you do on Monday? |
| Werk je op zaterdag? | Do you work on Saturday? |
| Heb je vandaag tijd? | Do you have time today? |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  Dutch daily routines sound flat to English ears until you get the rhythm right. Short sentences like **Ik sta op. Ik douche. Ik ontbijt.** should flow with equal stress on each verb. Read [Sentence Intonation and Rhythm](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm) alongside this lesson and read your own routine aloud three times without pausing between sentences.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Putting op in the wrong place</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik opsta om zeven uur.

    ✅ Ik sta om zeven uur op.

    **Opstaan** splits: the main part **sta** comes early, **op** goes to the end. English keeps "get up" together; Dutch spreads the verb apart.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using doet with jij in questions</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Wat doet je vandaag?

    ✅ Wat doe je vandaag?

    With **jij**, the **t** disappears in questions, just like **ben jij** and **heet je** from A0.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying ik ben moe after sport</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik ben moe na sport. (sounds like moe is your identity)

    ✅ Ik ben moe. / Ik ben erg moe vandaag.

    **Moe** works with **zijn**, which is correct here. The mistake is adding **na sport** without a clear structure. Better: **Na het sporten ben ik moe** (after exercising I am tired). At A1, keep it simple: **Ik ben moe.**
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting van ... tot ... for work hours</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik werk negen tot vijf.

    ✅ Ik werk van negen tot vijf.

    Dutch needs **van** at the start of a from ... to ... range, just like in [Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Monday morning at the office</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Lisa and Mark compare their daily routines over coffee.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Lisa:** Hoi Mark! Hoe laat sta jij meestal op?
    >
    > **Mark:** Meestal om half zeven. En jij?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ik sta om zeven uur op. Eerst douche ik, dan ontbijt ik.
    >
    > **Mark:** Ik ook. Daarna werk ik van negen tot vijf, toch?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ja, maar op vrijdag werk ik thuis. Wat doe jij in je vrije tijd?
    >
    > **Mark:** Ik sport soms 's avonds. En jij?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ik kook graag. 's Avonds kook ik meestal. Maar vandaag ben ik moe, hoor.
    >
    > **Mark:** Ha! Dan rust jij vanavond. Ik ook, denk ik.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Prima. Nou, tot straks op de vergadering!
    >
    > **Mark:** Tot straks!

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** Hi Mark! What time do you usually get up?
    >
    > **Mark:** Usually at 6:30. And you?
    >
    > **Lisa:** I get up at seven o'clock. First I shower, then I have breakfast.
    >
    > **Mark:** Me too. After that I work from nine to five, right?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Yes, but on Friday I work from home. What do you do in your free time?
    >
    > **Mark:** I sometimes exercise in the evening. And you?
    >
    > **Lisa:** I like cooking. In the evening I usually cook. But today I am tired, you know.
    >
    > **Mark:** Ha! Then you rest tonight. Me too, I think.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Great. Well, see you soon at the meeting!
    >
    > **Mark:** See you soon!

    Notice how **meestal**, **soms**, and **eerst ... dan ...** turn a list of facts into a natural story. **Thuiswerken** is one word in Dutch. **Graag** from A0 softens **Ik kook graag** into I like cooking.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
