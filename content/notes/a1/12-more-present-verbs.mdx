## Introduction

You already use **ik werk**, **wat doe je?**, and **wij eten pasta** in daily sentences. You also know **Ik wil graag** from A0. Dutch verbs look chaotic at first because **werken** and **doen** do not follow the same pattern, and **eten** becomes **eet**. This lesson pulls the present tense together with **one fixed structure per verb**: full conjugation tables, modal verbs (**willen**, **kunnen**, **moeten**, **mogen**), clear pattern labels, and enough examples to drill **ik**, **je** questions, **hij/zij**, **wij**, and **niet**. After today you can conjugate the eight core A1 verbs and a solid Tier B set without guessing.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Present tense at a glance

Before each verb block, use this map. Every Dutch present form fits one of these rows:

| Pattern | ik / hij | jij + je question | wij / jullie / zij | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Regular** | stem / stem + **t** | stem + **t** drops before **je** | infinitive | werk, werkt, **werk** je, werken |
| **Irregular stem** | special stem | same stem before **je** | infinitive | doe, doet, **doe** je, doen |
| **zijn / hebben** | fully irregular | **ben** je / **heb** je | zijn / hebben | ben, is, **ben** je, zijn |

**Three rules to hold onto:**

1. **ik** uses the stem (or irregular stem).
2. **hij / zij / u** uses stem + **t** (or irregular form).
3. **wij / jullie / zij** uses the full infinitive.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk**. Hij **werkt**. Wij **werken**. | I work. He works. We work. |
| Ik **doe** niets. Zij **doet** sport. Wij **doen** de boodschappen. | I do nothing. She exercises. We do the groceries. |
| **Werk** je vandaag? | Are you working today? |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  The full regular conjugation system lives in the Grammar Track: [Present Tense: Regular Verbs](/notes/grammar/04-present-tense-regular). This lesson is your A1 verb hub with tables and examples.
</Callout>

### The jij rule: t drops before je

When **jij** or **je** comes **right after** the verb, the **t** disappears. This applies to **every** verb in this lesson.

| With t (jij before verb) | Without t (verb before je) |
| --- | --- |
| jij **werkt** | **werk** je |
| jij **doet** | **doe** je |
| jij **ziet** | **zie** je |
| jij **bent** | **ben** je |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Werk** je op vrijdag? | Do you work on Friday? |
| **Ga** je naar huis? | Are you going home? |
| **Heb** je tijd? | Do you have time? |
| **Ben** je klaar? | Are you ready? |

With **u**, the **t** always stays: **Werkt u vandaag?** · **Hebt u een vraag?**

### Tier A: eight core verbs (full tables)

Learn these eight by heart. Each block has the **full conjugation table** and **five example sentences**.

#### werken (to work) — regular

| Pronoun | werken | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | werk | I work |
| jij / je | werkt | you work |
| u | werkt | you work (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | werkt | he / she works |
| wij / we | werken | we work |
| jullie | werken | you work (plural) |
| zij / ze | werken | they work |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk** in Utrecht. | I work in Utrecht. |
| **Werk** je op vrijdag? | Do you work on Friday? |
| Zij **werkt** van negen tot vijf. | She works from nine to five. |
| Wij **werken** samen. | We work together. |
| Ik **werk** vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. |

### Modal verbs: willen, kunnen, moeten, mogen

You already met **Ik wil graag** in [A0 Lesson 10](/notes/a0/10-ordering-food-and-drink). Modals change the meaning of the main verb. The **infinitive always goes at the end** of the sentence.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** koffie **drinken**. | I want to drink coffee. |
| **Kun** je me **helpen**? | Can you help me? |
| Ik **moet** naar de dokter **gaan**. | I have to go to the doctor. |
| **Mag** ik hier **zitten**? | May I sit here? |

**willen** (to want) — irregular stem **wil**

| Pronoun | willen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | wil | I want |
| jij / je | wilt | you want |
| u | wilt | you want (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | wil | he / she wants |
| wij / jullie / zij | willen | we / you / they want |

**kunnen** (can / to be able to) — irregular stem **kan**

| Pronoun | kunnen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | kan | I can |
| jij / je | kunt / kan | you can |
| u | kunt / kan | you can (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | kan | he / she can |
| wij / jullie / zij | kunnen | we / you / they can |

**moeten** (must / to have to) — irregular stem **moet**

| Pronoun | moeten | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | moet | I must |
| jij / je | moet | you must |
| u | moet | you must (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | moet | he / she must |
| wij / jullie / zij | moeten | we / you / they must |

**mogen** (may / to be allowed to) — irregular stem **mag**

| Pronoun | mogen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | mag | I may |
| jij / je | mag | you may |
| u | mag | you may (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | mag | he / she may |
| wij / jullie / zij | mogen | we / you / they may |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil niet** werken vandaag. | I do not want to work today. |
| **Kan** je Nederlands **spreken**? | Can you speak Dutch? |
| Wij **moeten** morgen **vertrekken**. | We have to leave tomorrow. |
| **Mag** ik een vraag **stellen**? | May I ask a question? |
| **Wil** je mee **komen**? | Do you want to come along? |

**zullen** (will / shall) appears in later lessons. For now, recognise **Ik zal** and **We zullen**.

### gaan + infinitive (near future)

**Gaan** often points to the near future. The second verb stays at the end:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** vandaag **sporten**. | I am going to exercise today. |
| **Ga** je vanavond **koken**? | Are you going to cook tonight? |
| Wij **gaan** naar de winkel **lopen**. | We are going to walk to the shop. |
| Zij **gaan** **niet** **werken** morgen. | They are not going to work tomorrow. |

You met this pattern in [Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo). **Gaan** is the verb in position 2. The infinitive closes the sentence.

### Verbs with negation and word order

From [Lesson 3](/notes/a1/03-negation):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zie** het **niet**. | I do not see it. |
| Wij **gaan** **nergens** heen. | We are not going anywhere. |
| Hij **geeft** **geen** cadeau. | He does not give a present. |
| **Vandaag** **maak** ik **geen** soep. | Today I am not making soup. |
| **Heb** je **geen** tijd? | Do you not have time? |

The conjugated verb stays in position 2. **Niet** and **geen** wrap around the rest as you learned before.

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

    > **Lisa:** **Zie** je mijn bril? Ik **zoek** hem overal.
    >
    > **Tom:** Ja, **die** is op de tafel. Ik **geef** hem aan je.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Dank je. **Kom** je mee?
    >
    > **Tom:** Ja. Eerst **maak** ik koffie. Dan **lopen** we samen.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Prima. **Spreek** je Nederlands op de markt?
    >
    > **Tom:** Ik **probeer** het. De verkoper **helpt** altijd.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Mooi. **Vertel** me later hoe het **gesprek** gaat.

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** What are you doing today?
    >
    > **Tom:** I am going to the market. I will get bread and buy vegetables.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Do you see my glasses? I am looking for them everywhere.
    >
    > **Tom:** Yes, they are on the table. I will give them to you.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Thank you. Are you coming with me?
    >
    > **Tom:** Yes. First I will make coffee. Then we walk together.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Fine. Do you speak Dutch at the market?
    >
    > **Tom:** I try. The seller always helps.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Nice. Tell me later how the conversation goes.
