## Introduction

In [Lesson 4](/notes/a1/04-family) you met **mijn moeder**, **zijn broer**, and **haar dochter**. Now you learn the complete possessive system: every form for _my, your, his, her, our, their_, plus the Dutch trick with **ons** and **onze**, and the spoken pattern **van mij** / **die van jou**. After this lesson you can say what belongs to you, ask **Wiens fiets is dit?**, and talk about passports, phones, and keys without pointing awkwardly.

## Quick Review

You already use **hebben** for possession in the sense of having people in your life: **Ik heb geen kinderen**. Possessive pronouns are different: they mark **whose** something is. **Mijn telefoon** means the phone belongs to me. Combine with [Lesson 3](/notes/a1/03-negation): **Dat is niet mijn tas**. And with [Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo): **Waar is jouw fiets?** keeps the verb in position 2.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The full possessive table

| English | Before noun | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| my | mijn | mijn fiets |
| your (informal) | jouw / je | jouw auto / je auto |
| your (formal) | uw | uw paspoort |
| his / its | zijn | zijn telefoon |
| her | haar | haar tas |
| our | ons / onze | ons huis / onze fiets |
| your (plural) | jullie | jullie sleutels |
| their | hun | hun kinderen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dit is **mijn** moeder. | This is my mother. |
| Is dat **jouw** broer? | Is that your brother? |
| **Zijn** opa woont in Utrecht. | His grandfather lives in Utrecht. |
| **Haar** dochter is vier jaar. | Her daughter is four years old. |
| **Ons** gezin is klein. | Our household is small. |
| **Hun** bruiloft is in juni. | Their wedding is in June. |
| Heeft **u** **uw** rijbewijs? | Do you have your driver's licence? |

**Mijn, jouw, zijn, haar, jullie, hun** and **uw** keep the same form before every noun. Only **ons** changes.

### The ons / onze rule (the one change)

Dutch treats **our** differently depending on **de** or **het**:

| Article | Possessive | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **het** | **ons** | **ons** huis | our house |
| **het** | **ons** | **ons** kind | our child |
| **de** | **onze** | **onze** fiets | our bicycle |
| **de** | **onze** | **onze** ouders | our parents |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ons huis is groot. | Our house is big. |
| Onze auto is oud. | Our car is old. |
| Ons gezin heeft vier personen. | Our household has four people. |
| Onze familie woont in Nederland. | Our family lives in the Netherlands. |

**Memory trick:** **ons** goes with **het** words (both short). **onze** goes with **de** words.

| Wrong | Correct |
| --- | --- |
| ❌ onze huis | ✅ **ons** huis |
| ❌ ons fiets | ✅ **onze** fiets |

**Mijn, jouw, zijn, haar, hun** do not change: **mijn huis**, **mijn fiets**, **hun huis**, **hun fiets**.

### zijn and haar: possessive vs pronoun

| Word | As subject pronoun | As possessive |
| --- | --- | --- |
| hij / zijn | **Hij** is moe. | **Zijn** broer is jong. |
| zij / haar | **Zij** woont hier. | **Haar** zus is getrouwd. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Hij** is mijn broer. | He is my brother. |
| **Zijn** vader is zestig. | His father is sixty. |
| **Zij** is mijn nicht. | She is my cousin. |
| **Haar** moeder kookt goed. | Her mother cooks well. |

The possessive **zijn** has no **i** accent shift: it sounds like the verb **zijn** (to be) but sits before a noun.

### jouw vs je, formal uw

| Form | When | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **jouw** | informal, slightly emphatic | Is dit jouw tas? |
| **je** | informal, everyday | Waar is je telefoon? |
| **uw** | formal with **u** | Heeft u uw paspoort bij u? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Is dit je fiets? | Is this your bicycle? |
| Jouw broer is aardig. | Your brother is nice. |
| Mevrouw, is dit uw tas? | Madam, is this your bag? |

Both **je** and **jouw** mean _your_. **Je** is more common in speech.

### van mij, van jou, van hem: another way to show ownership

Dutch often uses **van** + pronoun, especially when pointing at something:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dat is van mij. | That is mine. / That belongs to me. |
| Is dit van jou? | Is this yours? |
| De fiets is van hem. | The bicycle is his. |
| Het huis is van haar. | The house is hers. |
| De auto is van ons. | The car is ours. |
| De sleutels zijn van hun. | The keys are theirs. |

| van + | English |
| --- | --- |
| mij | me / mine |
| jou | you / yours |
| hem | him / his |
| haar | her / hers |
| ons | us / ours |
| jullie | you (plural) / yours |
| hun | them / theirs |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wiens tas is dit? **Van mij.** | Whose bag is this? Mine. |
| Is dat jullie auto? Nee, **van hem**. | Is that your car? No, his. |

### die van mij / die van jou

When comparing things, Dutch uses **die** (that one) + **van**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Mijn fiets is rood. **Die van jou** is blauw. | My bike is red. Yours is blue. |
| Ik pak **mijn** tas, niet **die van haar**. | I take my bag, not hers. |
| **Die van ons** is groter. | Ours is bigger. |

### Wiens? and Wie zijn ...?

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wiens fiets is dit? | Whose bicycle is this? |
| Wiens telefoon is dat? | Whose phone is that? |
| Wiens kinderen zijn dat? | Whose children are those? |
| Wie is dat? | Who is that? |
| Van wie is dit? | Whose is this? (literally: of whom is this) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wiens tas is dit? Het is mijn tas. | Whose bag is this? It is my bag. |
| Van wie is deze fiets? Van Tom. | Whose bicycle is this? Tom's. |

In modern Dutch, **Wiens** is standard for _whose_. **Van wie is dit?** is also very common.

### eigen: own

**Eigen** means _own_ and comes after the possessive:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| mijn eigen kamer | my own room |
| zijn eigen auto | his own car |
| ons eigen huis | our own house |
| een eigen idee | an idea of one's own |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb mijn eigen fiets. | I have my own bicycle. |
| Zij heeft haar eigen bedrijf. | She has her own business. (preview) |

### Possessives with objects you know

Connect to A0 vocabulary:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| mijn telefoon | my phone |
| jouw sleutels | your keys |
| zijn auto | his car |
| haar tas | her bag |
| ons huis | our house |
| jullie fietsen | your bicycles |
| hun portemonnee | their wallet |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |

### New possession words

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| het paspoort | passport |
| het rijbewijs | driver's licence |
| de identiteitskaart | identity card |
| het horloge | watch |
| de bril | glasses |

| de eigenaar | owner |
| privé | private |

### Possessives in questions and negation

Word order stays the same. Possessives sit before the noun.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Is dit **jouw** fiets? | Is this your bicycle? |
| **Nee**, dat is **niet mijn** fiets. | No, that is not my bicycle. |
| **Heb** je **je** sleutels **niet**? | Do you not have your keys? |
| **Vandaag** neem ik **mijn** auto **niet**. | Today I do not take my car. |
| Ik heb **geen** eigen auto. | I do not have my own car. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dat is niet van mij. | That is not mine. |
| Die tas is niet van jou. | That bag is not yours. |

### van + name (review from Lesson 4)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de fiets **van** Tom | Tom's bicycle |
| de moeder **van** Anna | Anna's mother |
| het huis **van** mijn ouders | my parents' house |

Both patterns work: **Toms fiets** (informal written) and **de fiets van Tom** (very common in speech). At A1, prefer **van**: **de telefoon van Lisa**.

  </CardContent>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">

    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting de/het after possessive</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ mijn fiets is kapot (if you wrote _mijnfiets_ as one word)

    ✅ **mijn fiets** is kapot

    The possessive and noun stay separate: **mijn** + **fiets**, **ons** + **huis**.
  </CardContent>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lost and found at the station</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mark and Sanne sort out whose bag is whose.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mark:** Sanne! **Wiens** tas is dit? Op **mijn** stoel.
    >
    > **Sanne:** Hm. Niet **van mij**. Misschien **van haar**? **Die van** Lisa?
    >
    > **Mark:** En **wiens** jas is dit? **Jouw** jas, toch?
    >
    > **Sanne:** Ja, **mijn** jas. Maar **mijn** telefoon zit **niet** in **mijn** tas!
    >
    > **Mark:** **Je** telefoon is hier. Op de tafel.
    >
    > **Sanne:** Ah! En deze sleutels? **Van jou**?
    >
    > **Mark:** Nee, **niet van mij**. **Van hem**, denk ik. **Zijn** sleutels zien er zo uit.
    >
    > **Sanne:** Oké. **Onze** spullen zijn klaar. **Jullie** moeten om half tien weg, toch?
    >
    > **Mark:** Ja. **Hun** afspraak is om half tien. Kom, we geven de tas van Lisa terug.

    **In English:**

    > **Mark:** Sanne! Whose bag is this? On my seat.
    >
    > **Sanne:** Hm. Not mine. Maybe hers? Lisa's?
    >
    > **Mark:** And whose coat is this? Your coat, right?
    >
    > **Sanne:** Yes, my coat. But my phone is not in my bag!
    >
    > **Mark:** Your phone is here. On the table.
    >
    > **Sanne:** Ah! And these keys? Yours?
    >
    > **Mark:** No, not mine. His, I think. His keys look like this.
    >
    > **Sanne:** Okay. Our things are ready. You need to leave at half past nine, right?
    >
    > **Mark:** Yes. Their appointment is at half past nine. Come, we will give Lisa her bag back.

    **Wiens**, **van mij/jou/hem**, **die van**, and possessives before nouns all appear in one real scene. **Denk ik** means _I think_.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
