## Introduction

Your phone dies, or someone stops you on the street and asks the way. Either way you need directions. This lesson teaches you to ask **Hoe kom ik bij het station?**, to understand answers like **Ga rechtdoor en sla dan linksaf**, and to give simple directions yourself using the **imperative** (the command form). You met the imperative briefly in [Lesson 15: Transport](/notes/a1/15-transport); here you drill it for street directions. It builds on transport vocabulary and prepares you for deeper prepositions of place in A2. There is also a short [Asking Directions scenario](/notes/scenarios/a1-asking-directions) to rehearse once you finish here.

## Quick Review

In [Lesson 15](/notes/a1/15-transport) you learned **het station**, **de bus**, **de fiets**, and **lopen**. From A0 you know the question word **waar** ([A0 Lesson 6](/notes/a0/06-basic-questions)) and the prepositions **naast**, **bij**, and **op**. From [A0 Lesson 14](/notes/a0/14-getting-by-survival-phrases), **Kunt u dat herhalen?** is exactly what you say when directions come too fast.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Asking the way

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Pardon, mag ik u iets vragen? | Excuse me, may I ask you something? |
| Waar is het station? | Where is the station? |
| Hoe kom ik bij de supermarkt? | How do I get to the supermarket? |
| Is het ver? | Is it far? |
| Is het dichtbij? | Is it nearby? |
| Kunt u het op de kaart laten zien? | Can you show it on the map? |

Use **bij** (not naar) after **Hoe kom ik â€¦**: **Hoe kom ik bij het museum?**

### The imperative: giving a direction

To tell someone what to do, use the **imperative** â€” usually just the verb stem, with no pronoun:

| Verb | Imperative | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| gaan | Ga rechtdoor. | Go straight ahead. |
| lopen | Loop door. | Keep walking. |
| slaan | Sla linksaf. | Turn left. |
| nemen | Neem de eerste straat. | Take the first street. |
| oversteken | Steek de straat over. | Cross the street. |

Separable verbs still split in the imperative: **Steek** de straat **over**, **Sla** linksaf.

### Direction words

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| rechtdoor | straight ahead |
| links / linksaf | left / to the left |
| rechts / rechtsaf | right / to the right |
| terug | back |
| de eerste / tweede straat | the first / second street |
| het kruispunt | crossroads |
| de rotonde | roundabout |
| het stoplicht | traffic light |
| de hoek / om de hoek | corner / around the corner |
| het zebrapad | pedestrian crossing |

### Where things are (prepositions of place)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| naast | next to |
| tegenover | opposite |
| tussen | between |
| achter | behind |
| voor | in front of |
| bij het stoplicht | at the traffic light |
| aan je linkerhand | on your left-hand side |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het is naast de bank. | It is next to the bank. |
| De apotheek is tegenover de kerk. | The pharmacy is opposite the church. |
| Het museum is aan je rechterhand. | The museum is on your right. |

## Learning Tips

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>

  </CardHeader>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Left hand, right hand</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Tie **links** to your left hand and **rechts** to your right, physically, while you say them. The body memory stops the classic leftâ€“right swap under pressure.
  </CardContent>
