## Introduction

In [A0 Lesson 7](/notes/a0/07-numbers-21-100-and-money) you learned **Hoeveel kost het?**, compound numbers like **vijfentwintig**, and **pinnen** vs **contant**. In [Lesson 6](/notes/a1/06-food-and-drink) you **koopt** groceries at **de supermarkt**. Now you shop everywhere: the market, clothing stores, and the kassa. You learn quantities (**een kilo**, **een liter**), sizes, discounts, and the phrases sellers use when you browse or buy.

After this lesson you can ask the price, say something is too expensive, try clothes on, and pay like a local.

## Quick Review

You know **Hoeveel kost deze tas?** and **Dat is te duur** from A0. From [Lesson 7](/notes/a1/07-at-the-restaurant): **Ik neem het** works in shops too. From [Lesson 3](/notes/a1/03-negation): **Ik koop geen vlees**. From [Lesson 5](/notes/a1/05-possessives): **Is dit jouw jas?**

| --- | --- |

### kopen and verkopen

**Kopen** you met in [Lesson 6](/notes/a1/06-food-and-drink). Full present tense:

| Pronoun | kopen | verkopen |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | koop | verkoop |
| jij | koopt | verkoopt |
| u | koopt | verkoopt |
| hij/zij | koopt | verkoopt |
| wij | kopen | verkopen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik koop drie appels. | I buy three apples. |
| Koopt u dit vaak? | Do you buy this often? |
| De winkel verkoopt brood. | The shop sells bread. |
| Wij kopen cadeaus. | We buy presents. |

Questions: **Koop je dit?** (t drops with **jij**)

### Asking about prices

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoeveel kost het? | How much does it cost? |
| Hoeveel kost deze tas? | How much does this bag cost? |
| Hoeveel kosten de appels? | How much do the apples cost? |
| Wat kost dat? | What does that cost? |
| Hoe duur is het? | How expensive is it? |
| Wat is de prijs? | What is the price? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het kost twaalf euro vijftig. | It costs twelve euros fifty. |
| De appels kosten twee euro. | The apples cost two euros. |
| Dat is vijfentwintig euro. | That is twenty five euros. |
| Is er korting? | Is there a discount? |

Remember Dutch price speech from A0: **€ 4,50** = **vier euro vijftig**.

### Too expensive, cheap, discounts

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| duur | expensive |
| goedkoop | cheap |
| te duur | too expensive |
| goedkoper | cheaper |
| duurder | more expensive |
| de korting | discount |
| de aanbieding | special offer / sale item |
| de uitverkoop | sale |
| gratis | free |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dat is te duur. | That is too expensive. |
| Heb je iets goedkopers? | Do you have something cheaper? |
| De aanbieding is vandaag. | The special offer is today. |
| Ik koop het in de uitverkoop. | I buy it in the sale. |

Full comparatives come in A2. For now, learn **goedkoper** and **duurder** as shopping words.

### Quantities and packaging

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| een kilo | a kilo |
| een pond | 500 grams (half kilo) |
| een liter | a litre |
| een fles | a bottle |
| een pak | a pack / carton |
| een doos | a box |
| een stuk | a piece |
| een zak | a bag (of flour, crisps) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik wil een kilo appels. | I want a kilo of apples. |
| Twee liter melk, alstublieft. | Two litres of milk, please. |
| Een fles water. | A bottle of water. |
| Drie pakken kaas. | Three packs of cheese. |

At the market, sellers often ask: **Hoeveel wilt u?** (How much would you like?)

### deze and die in the shop (recognise only)

You will master **deze** and **die** in [Lesson 9](/notes/a1/09-demonstratives). Until then, use them only as **fixed shop phrases** — do not worry about the full near/far rules yet:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **deze** tas | this bag (near you) |
| **die** jas | that coat (farther away) |
| **dit** shirt | this shirt |
| **dat** boek | that book |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoeveel kost **deze** trui? | How much does this sweater cost? |
| **Die** schoenen zijn mooi. | Those shoes are beautiful. |
| Ik neem **dit**. | I will take this. |

### Clothing: sizes and trying on

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de maat | size |
| passen | to fit / to try on |
| te groot | too big |
| te klein | too small |
| de paskamer | fitting room |
| Mag ik passen? | May I try it on? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Welke maat heeft u? | What size are you? |
| Ik heb maat veertig. | I am size forty. |
| De jas is te groot. | The coat is too big. |
| Mag ik even passen? | May I try it on for a moment? |
| Het past goed. | It fits well. |

**Passen** does double duty: _to try on_ and _to fit_.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik kijk alleen. | I am just looking. |
| Ik neem het. | I will take it. |
| Ik neem deze. | I will take this one. |
| Nee, bedankt. | No, thank you. |
| Ik denk erover. | I will think about it. |
| Dat is alles. | That is everything. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Kan ik u helpen? | Can I help you? |
| Nee, ik kijk alleen, dank u. | No, I am just looking, thank you. |
| Oké, ik neem beide. | Okay, I will take both. |

### Paying at the kassa

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| betalen | to pay |
| contant | cash |
| pinnen | pay by card |
| het wisselgeld | change |
| het bonnetje | receipt |
| de pinpas | debit card |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Waar kan ik betalen? | Where can I pay? |
| Kan ik pinnen? | Can I pay by card? |
| Ik betaal contant. | I pay cash. |
| Hier is uw wisselgeld. | Here is your change. |
| Bonnetje mee? | Receipt with you? (want a receipt?) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dat is dan twaalf euro vijftig. | That will be twelve euros fifty then. |
| Alstublieft, uw bonnetje. | Here is your receipt. |

### Word order and negation while shopping

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Vandaag** koop ik geen brood. | Today I am not buying bread. |
| Ik koop **deze** niet. | I am not buying this one. |
| **Die** jas is **te duur**. | That coat is too expensive. |
| **Hoeveel** kosten **jullie** appels? | How much do your apples cost? |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  **kost** vs **kosten**: singular item → **Hoeveel kost het?**; plural → **Hoeveel kosten de appels?** For **deze/die** rules, see [Demonstratives](/notes/grammar/11-demonstratives) after [Lesson 9](/notes/a1/09-demonstratives).
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  Compound prices like **vijfentwintig euro** and **tachtig euro vijftig** need clear **en** in the middle. Say **Hoeveel kost deze tas?** and **Dat is te duur** ten times — shopkeepers use these every day.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">English number order in prices</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ twintig vijf euro (for € 25)

    ✅ **vijfentwintig** euro

    Dutch compounds flip the unit before the ten: **vijf + en + twintig**, not tens first like English.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting plural verb with plural prices</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Hoeveel kost de appels?

    ✅ Hoeveel **kosten** de appels?

    **Het** costs **kost**. Plural subjects take **kosten**: De schoenen **kosten** tachtig euro.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using groot for expensive</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ De tas is te groot. (when meaning expensive)

    ✅ De tas is te **duur**.

    **Groot** means big. **Duur** means expensive. **Te groot** = too big (size).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying passen only for size</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik pas de jas in de winkel. (ambiguous)

    ✅ **Mag ik de jas passen?** / **De jas past goed.**

    Use **Mag ik passen?** for trying on. Use **Het past** for fit.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saturday at the market and clothing store</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Raj buys fruit, then tries on a jacket.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Verkoper:** Goedemorgen! **Hoeveel** appels wilt u?
    >
    > **Raj:** Goedemorgen. **Een kilo**, alstublieft. **Hoeveel kost** het?
    >
    > **Verkoper:** Dat is twee euro vijftig.
    >
    > **Raj:** Prima. En **deze** bananen?
    >
    > **Verkoper:** De bananen **kosten** een euro twintig per kilo.
    >
    > **Raj:** **Ik neem** ook **een pond**. **Dat is alles**.
    >
    > **Verkoper:** **Dat is dan** drie euro veertig. **Contant** of **pinnen**?
    >
    > **Raj:** Ik **pin** het. Dank u.
    >
    > _(in de kledingwinkel)_
    >
    > **Verkoopster:** Kan ik u helpen?
    >
    > **Raj:** **Ik kijk alleen**, dank u. Oh, **hoeveel kost deze jas**?
    >
    > **Verkoopster:** Die jas **kost** negenenzestig euro. **In de uitverkoop**.
    >
    > **Raj:** **Mag ik passen**? **Heeft u maat M**?
    >
    > **Verkoopster:** Natuurlijk. De **paskamer** is daar.
    >
    > **Raj:** **De jas past** goed. **Ik neem hem**. Kan ik bij de **kassa** **pinnen**?
    >
    > **Verkoopster:** Ja hoor. **Bonnetje mee**?
    >
    > **Raj:** Ja, graag!

    **In English:**

    > **Seller:** Good morning! How many apples would you like?
    >
    > **Raj:** Good morning. A kilo, please. How much does it cost?
    >
    > **Seller:** That is two euros fifty.
    >
    > **Raj:** Great. And these bananas?
    >
    > **Seller:** The bananas cost one euro twenty per kilo.
    >
    > **Raj:** I will also take half a kilo. That is everything.
    >
    > **Seller:** That will be three euros forty then. Cash or card?
    >
    > **Raj:** I will pay by card. Thank you.
    >
    > _(in the clothing store)_
    >
    > **Saleswoman:** Can I help you?
    >
    > **Raj:** I am just looking, thank you. Oh, how much does this coat cost?
    >
    > **Saleswoman:** That coat costs sixty nine euros. On sale.
    >
    > **Raj:** May I try it on? Do you have size M?
    >
    > **Saleswoman:** Of course. The fitting room is over there.
    >
    > **Raj:** The coat fits well. I will take it. Can I pay by card at the checkout?
    >
    > **Saleswoman:** Yes. Want a receipt?
    >
    > **Raj:** Yes, please!

    **Hoeveel kost** vs **kosten**, **een kilo** / **een pond**, **ik kijk alleen**, **Mag ik passen?**, and **Dat is dan** at checkout are daily shopping Dutch.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
