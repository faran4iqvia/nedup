## Introduction

Knowing vocabulary is one thing; turning it into a plan with someone is another. This lesson gives you the Dutch for suggesting, inviting, accepting, and politely declining: **Zullen we koffie drinken?**, **Heb je zin om mee te gaan?**, **Laten we gaan!**, and **Zullen we iets afspreken?**. These are the phrases that turn a colleague or classmate into a friend. It builds on your hobbies vocabulary from [Lesson 14](/notes/a1/14-hobbies) and previews the verb **zullen**, which you will meet fully in B1.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Zullen we …? — Shall we …?

**Zullen we …?** is the standard way to suggest doing something together. The main verb goes to the **end**, in the infinitive:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Zullen we koffie drinken? | Shall we drink coffee? |
| Zullen we naar de film gaan? | Shall we go to the cinema? |
| Zullen we vanavond koken? | Shall we cook tonight? |
| Zal ik je helpen? | Shall I help you? |

Use **Zal ik …?** (Shall I …?) when you offer to do something yourself.

### Heb je zin in / om te … — Do you feel like …?

**Zin** means the desire or mood to do something. It comes in two shapes:

| Structure | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zin in** + noun | for a thing | Heb je zin in koffie? |
| **zin om** + te + verb | for an action | Heb je zin om te wandelen? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb zin in pizza. | I feel like pizza. |
| Heb je zin om mee te gaan? | Do you feel like coming along? |
| Ik heb geen zin. | I don't feel like it. |

Note the separable verb in **mee te gaan**: **te** slots between **mee** and **gaan**.

### Laten we … — Let's …

**Laten we** plus the infinitive at the end means "Let's …". No **te**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Laten we gaan! | Let's go! |
| Laten we een pizza bestellen. | Let's order a pizza. |
| Laten we afspreken. | Let's arrange to meet. |

### Inviting, accepting, declining

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ga je mee? | Are you coming along? |
| Wil je meegaan? | Do you want to come along? |
| Ja, leuk! / Ja, gezellig! | Yes, nice! / Yes, lovely! |
| Goed idee! | Good idea! |
| Nee, ik kan niet. | No, I can't. |
| Ik heb al een afspraak. | I already have an appointment. |
| Misschien een andere keer. | Maybe another time. |

### Arranging the details: afspreken

**Afspreken** means to arrange to meet; **de afspraak** is the arrangement or appointment:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Zullen we iets afspreken? | Shall we arrange something? |
| Hoe laat spreken we af? | What time shall we meet? |
| Waar spreken we af? | Where shall we meet? |
| We spreken om acht uur af. | We'll meet at eight o'clock. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  All three openers — **Zullen we …?**, **Laten we …**, **Heb je zin om te …** — push the main verb to the end of the sentence, the V2/end-verb pattern from [Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo). **Zullen** is doing suggestion work here; its full role as the future tense arrives in [B1 Lesson 4](/notes/b1/04-future-with-zullen).
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Gezellig** — the untranslatable Dutch word for cosy/convivial — has two soft **g** sounds and is worth mastering early: **ge-ZEL-lig**. **Zullen** starts with a **z** (voiced, like the English z), not an s.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Mixing up zin in and zin om te</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik heb zin om koffie. · ❌ Ik heb zin in gaan.

    ✅ Ik heb zin **in** koffie (a thing). · ✅ Ik heb zin **om** te gaan (an action).

    **Zin in** takes a noun; **zin om** takes **te** plus a verb. Choose by what follows.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Leaving the verb in the middle</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Zullen we gaan naar de bioscoop?

    ✅ Zullen we naar de bioscoop **gaan**?

    The main verb goes to the very end, just like in Lesson 2. **Gaan** lands after **naar de bioscoop**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Adding te after Laten we</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Laten we te gaan.

    ✅ Laten we gaan.

    **Laten we** is followed by a plain infinitive — no **te**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying Ga je meegaan?</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ga je meegaan?

    ✅ **Ga je mee?**

    **Meegaan** is separable. In this short question the **mee** already sits at the end and the verb is just **ga** — you do not repeat the whole infinitive.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Making weekend plans</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Two friends arrange to go out on Saturday.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Sofie:** Hé Tom, heb je zin om zaterdag iets te doen?
    >
    > **Tom:** Ja, leuk! Wat wil je doen?
    >
    > **Sofie:** Zullen we naar de bioscoop gaan? Er is een nieuwe film.
    >
    > **Tom:** Goed idee! Hoe laat spreken we af?
    >
    > **Sofie:** Zullen we om zeven uur afspreken, bij het station?
    >
    > **Tom:** Prima. Zal ik de kaartjes kopen?
    >
    > **Sofie:** Graag! En daarna zin in pizza?
    >
    > **Tom:** Altijd zin in pizza. Gezellig!
    >
    > **Sofie:** Top, tot zaterdag dan!
    >
    > **Tom:** Tot zaterdag!

    **In English:**

    > **Sofie:** Hey Tom, do you feel like doing something on Saturday?
    >
    > **Tom:** Yes, nice! What do you want to do?
    >
    > **Sofie:** Shall we go to the cinema? There's a new film.
    >
    > **Tom:** Good idea! What time shall we meet?
    >
    > **Sofie:** Shall we meet at seven, at the station?
    >
    > **Tom:** Fine. Shall I buy the tickets?
    >
    > **Sofie:** Please do! And pizza afterwards?
    >
    > **Tom:** Always up for pizza. Lovely!
    >
    > **Sofie:** Great, see you Saturday then!
    >
    > **Tom:** See you Saturday!

    Notice **zin om … te doen**, **Zullen we … gaan?** with the verb at the end, **Zal ik …?** for an offer, and **zin in pizza** (a thing) versus **zin om te doen** (an action).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
