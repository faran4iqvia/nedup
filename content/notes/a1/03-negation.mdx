## Introduction

You have been saying **nee**, **ik heb geen tijd**, and **ik weet het niet** since A0. Now you learn the full system behind Dutch negation. Dutch does not use **niet** for everything. It chooses between **geen** and **niet**, and **niet** has a specific place in the sentence that depends on what you want to deny. Get this right and you sound precise. Get it wrong and people still understand you, but your Dutch feels "almost there."

After this lesson you can deny verbs, nouns, adjectives, and whole plans, and you know where **niet** belongs inside the verb second frame from [Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo).

## Quick Review

In [A0 Lesson 5](/notes/a0/05-hebben-and-everyday-things) you saw **Ik heb geen tijd** and **Ik heb mijn sleutels niet**. In [Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo) you placed **niet** before the infinitive: **Ik wil vandaag niet werken**. The verb stayed in position 2; **niet** lived in the middle or at the end. Today we make those patterns explicit and complete.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Two words, two jobs: geen and niet

| Word | Negates | English equivalent |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **geen** | nouns without **de**, **het**, **mijn**, **die**, and so on | no / not a / not any |
| **niet** | verbs, adjectives, adverbs, definite nouns, whole sentences | not |

Simple decision:

| Are you denying a noun? | Is the noun indefinite (no the/my/this)? | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Yes | Yes | **geen** |
| Yes | No (has **de**, **het**, **mijn**, **die**, and so on) | **niet** after that noun phrase |
| No (denying verb/adjective/sentence) | — | **niet** |

| Dutch | English | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik heb **geen** fiets. | I do not have a bicycle. | indefinite noun |
| Ik heb **geen** tijd. | I have no time. | indefinite noun |
| Ik drink **geen** koffie. | I do not drink coffee. | general noun without article |
| Ik drink **de** koffie **niet**. | I do not drink the coffee. | definite noun → **niet** |
| Ik heb mijn sleutels **niet**. | I do not have my keys. | possessive = definite → **niet** |
| Ik werk **niet** vandaag. | I am not working today. | denying the action |
| Zij is **niet** moe. | She is not tired. | denying adjective |

### Geen in detail

**Geen** replaces **een** or the zero article. It means _not a_ or _no_.

| Affirmative | Negative with geen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik heb **een** fiets. | Ik heb **geen** fiets. | I do not have a bicycle. |
| Hij is **een** docent. | Hij is **geen** docent. | He is not a teacher. |
| Wij hebben **tijd**. | Wij hebben **geen** tijd. | We have no time. |
| Zij eet **vlees**. | Zij eet **geen** vlees. | She does not eat meat. |
| Dat is **een** probleem. | Dat is **geen** probleem. | That is not a problem. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb geen honger. | I am not hungry. |
| Heb jij geen dorst? | Are you not thirsty? |
| Wij hebben geen auto. | We do not have a car. |
| Is er geen brood? | Is there no bread? |
| Hij drinkt geen bier. | He does not drink beer. |

**Geen** also works with **wat** and **veel**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb **geen** idee. | I have no idea. |
| Er is **geen** probleem. | There is no problem. |
| Ik heb **weinig** tijd, maar **geen** geld. | I have little time but no money. |

When the noun already has **de**, **het**, **mijn**, **jouw**, **die**, or **deze**, use **niet**, not **geen**:

| Wrong | Correct | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ❌ Ik heb geen mijn telefoon. | ✅ Ik heb mijn telefoon **niet**. | I do not have my phone. |
| ❌ Ik eet geen de kaas. | ✅ Ik eet de kaas **niet**. | I do not eat the cheese. |
| ❌ Zij drinkt geen het water. | ✅ Zij drinkt het water **niet**. | She does not drink the water. |

### Niet in detail: what it can deny

**Niet** can negate:

1. The **verb** (whole action)
2. An **adjective** or **adverb**
3. A **definite noun phrase**
4. The **whole sentence** (default: **niet** at the end)

| Type | Dutch | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Verb | Ik **werk** vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. |
| Adjective | Hij is **niet** moe. | He is not tired. |
| Adverb | Zij kookt **niet** goed. | She does not cook well. |
| Definite noun | Ik eet het brood **niet**. | I do not eat the bread. |
| Whole sentence | Ik sta om zeven uur op **niet**. | _(awkward)_ → better: **Ik sta niet om zeven uur op.** |

### Where does niet go? The placement rules

This is the hardest part for English speakers. English puts **not** right after the auxiliary: _I do not work._ Dutch puts **niet** based on **what** you deny.

**Rule 1: Denying the whole action → niet at the end** (default)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik werk vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. |
| Lisa kookt **niet**. | Lisa is not cooking. |
| Wij sporten op maandag **niet**. | We do not exercise on Monday. |
| Hij woont in Amsterdam **niet**. | He does not live in Amsterdam. |

**Rule 2: Denying one word (adjective, adverb, time, place) → niet right before that word**

| Dutch | English | What is denied |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik ben **niet** moe. | I am not tired. | the adjective |
| Zij werkt **niet** hard. | She does not work hard. | the adverb |
| Ik werk **niet** vandaag. | I am not working today. | the time word |
| Hij eet **niet** thuis. | He does not eat at home. | the place |
| Dat is **niet** goed. | That is not good. | the adjective |

Compare the difference:

| Dutch | English | Nuance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik werk vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. | I work other days, just not today. |
| Ik werk **niet** vandaag. | I am not working **today**. | emphasis on today (maybe I work tomorrow). |

At A1, both are correct. The second stresses the denied element.

**Rule 3: Definite noun phrase → niet after that phrase**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb mijn telefoon **niet**. | I do not have my phone. |
| Jij hebt de sleutels **niet**. | You do not have the keys. |
| Ik eet de soep **niet**. | I do not eat the soup. |
| Zij drinkt de koffie **niet**. | She does not drink the coffee. |

**Rule 4: Two verbs → niet before the verb at the end**

When position 2 has one verb and the infinitive or participle sits at the end, **niet** goes **before** the end verb:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik wil vandaag **niet** werken. | I do not want to work today. |
| Hij kan morgen **niet** komen. | He cannot come tomorrow. |
| Zij moet **niet** sporten. | She must not exercise. |
| Ik ga vanavond **niet** koken. | I am not going to cook tonight. |
| Ik heb gisteren **niet** gewerkt. | I did not work yesterday. |

| Position 2 | Middle | End |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** | vandaag **niet** | **werken** |
| Ik **heb** | gisteren **niet** | **gewerkt** |
| Ik **sta** | om zeven uur **niet** | **op** |

For separable verbs, **niet** usually goes before the prefix at the end:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik sta **niet** op. | I do not get up. |
| Ik sta om zeven uur **niet** op. | I do not get up at seven o'clock. |
| Hij doet de tv **niet** aan. | He does not turn the TV on. (preview) |

**Rule 5: Inversion and questions → same rules, verb still in position 2**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Vandaag** werk ik **niet**. | Today I do not work. |
| **Morgen** wil ik **niet** sporten. | Tomorrow I do not want to exercise. |
| Werk je vandaag **niet**? | Are you not working today? |
| Heb je **geen** tijd? | Do you have no time? |
| Waarom eet je het brood **niet**? | Why do you not eat the bread? |

Negation does **not** change the V2 rule from Lesson 2. Only the position of **niet** inside the sentence needs care.

### Niet vs geen: side by side

| Situation | geen | niet |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik heb \_\_\_ fiets. | **geen** (indefinite) | — |
| Ik heb mijn fiets \_\_\_. | — | **niet** |
| Ik heb \_\_\_ tijd. | **geen** | — |
| Ik ben \_\_\_ moe. | — | **niet** |
| Ik werk vandaag \_\_\_. | — | **niet** |
| Ik drink \_\_\_ koffie. | **geen** (general) | — |
| Ik drink de koffie \_\_\_. | — | **niet** |
| Dat is \_\_\_ probleem. | **geen** | — |
| Dat is \_\_\_ goed. | — | **niet** |

### Negative pronouns: niets, niemand, nergens

Dutch has negative words that work like **geen** but for pronouns:

| Affirmative | Negative | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **iets** | **niets** | something → nothing |
| **iemand** | **niemand** | somebody → nobody |
| **ergens** | **nergens** | somewhere → nowhere |
| **altijd** | **nooit** | always → never |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik zie **niemand**. | I see nobody. |
| Hij doet **niets** vandaag. | He is doing nothing today. |
| Wij gaan **nergens** heen. | We are not going anywhere. |
| Zij sport **nooit**. | She never exercises. |
| Ik heb **niets** gegeten. | I have eaten nothing. |

**Nooit** you know from [Lesson 1](/notes/a1/01-daily-routines). **Niets** appeared in **Ik doe niets**. Now they sit in a complete family.

### Wel: the positive answer to niet

Dutch often uses **wel** to stress the positive when someone expected a negative:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ben **niet** moe. | I am not tired. |
| Ik ben **wel** moe. | I **am** tired (contrary to what you thought). |
| Ik werk vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. |
| Ik werk vandaag **wel**. | I **am** working today. |
| Heb je geen tijd? | Do you have no time? |
| Jawel, ik heb **wel** tijd! | Yes I do, I **do** have time! |

**Wel** does not mean "well" here. It means _indeed_ or _actually yes_.

### Common combinations

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **nog niet** | not yet |
| **ook niet** | not either |
| **helemaal niet** | not at all |
| **helemaal geen** | no ... at all |
| **nog steeds niet** | still not |
| **liever niet** | prefer not to |
| **zeker niet** | certainly not |
| **waarschijnlijk niet** | probably not |
| **geen probleem** | no problem |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ben **nog niet** klaar. | I am not ready yet. |
| Ik sport **ook niet**. | I do not exercise either. |
| Dat is **helemaal niet** goed. | That is not good at all. |
| Ik heb **helemaal geen** tijd. | I have no time at all. |
| Kom je? **Waarschijnlijk niet**. | Are you coming? Probably not. |

### Negation in past and perfect (same placement)

The **niet** rules do not change in other tenses. Only the verb form in position 2 changes.

| Present | Past | Perfect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik werk **niet**. | Ik werkte **niet**. | Ik heb **niet** gewerkt. |
| Ik heb **geen** tijd. | Ik had **geen** tijd. | Ik heb **geen** tijd gehad. |
| Zij is **niet** moe. | Zij was **niet** moe. | Zij is **niet** moe geweest. |
| Hij wil **niet** koken. | Hij wilde **niet** koken. | Hij heeft **niet** willen koken. (advanced, recognition only) |

Focus on present for now. Notice the pattern: **niet** before the participle at the end in perfect tense.

  </CardContent>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Turning down dinner plans</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Lisa invites Tom. He says no politely, with several negation patterns.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Lisa:** Hoi Tom! **Wil** je vanavond bij mij eten?
    >
    > **Tom:** Leuk idee, maar **helaas niet**. Ik heb **geen** tijd.
    >
    > **Lisa:** **Geen** tijd? Werk je **niet** vandaag?
    >
    > **Tom:** Jawel, ik werk **wel**, maar ik ben **nog niet** klaar. Ik moet **nog** werken.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ah. Heb je **geen** honger dan?
    >
    > **Tom:** **Wel** honger, maar ik eet **'s avonds liever niet** thuis.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Oké. **Waarschijnlijk niet** vanavond dus?
    >
    > **Tom:** Nee, **vandaag niet**. **Morgen** heb ik **wel** tijd. **Geen** probleem?
    >
    > **Lisa:** **Geen** probleem! Tot morgen dan.
    >
    > **Tom:** Tot morgen!

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** Hi Tom! Do you want to eat at my place tonight?
    >
    > **Tom:** Nice idea, but unfortunately not. I have no time.
    >
    > **Lisa:** No time? Are you not working today?
    >
    > **Tom:** Yes I am, but I am not finished yet. I still have to work.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ah. Are you not hungry then?
    >
    > **Tom:** I am hungry, but I prefer not to eat at home in the evening.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Okay. So probably not tonight?
    >
    > **Tom:** No, not today. Tomorrow I do have time. No problem?
    >
    > **Lisa:** No problem! See you tomorrow then.
    >
    > **Tom:** See you tomorrow!

    Spot **geen tijd**, **niet vandaag**, **jawel ... wel**, **'s avonds liever niet**, **geen probleem**, and **nog niet**. Tom denies the plan without denying everything.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
