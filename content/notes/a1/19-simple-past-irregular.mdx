## Introduction

In [Lesson 18](/notes/a1/18-simple-past-regular) you learned **werkte**, **woonde**, and the **te/de** rule. But the verbs Dutch uses most often in the past do **not** follow that pattern: **Ik was**, **Ik had**, **Ik ging**, **Ik kwam**, **Ik deed**, **Ik at**. These are **onregelmatige** (irregular) forms. After this lesson you can combine regular and irregular past in one story: **Gisteren was ik ziek. Ik at soep en ging vroeg naar bed.**

## Quick Review

From [Lesson 18](/notes/a1/18-simple-past-regular): regular past with **te/de/ten/den**, time words (**gisteren**, **vorige week**, **toen**), and verb second order (**Gisteren werkte ik**). From [Lesson 12](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs): present forms of **zijn**, **hebben**, **gaan**, **komen**, **doen**, **eten**, **zien**, **geven**, and **nemen**. From [A0 Lesson 5](/notes/a0/05-hebben-and-everyday-things): **Ik heb honger** and **Ik heb het koud**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Irregular past at a glance

Irregular verbs in Dutch have **their own past forms**. You cannot build them with **te/de**. The good news: **singular persons share one form**, just like regular past.

| Person | Pattern | Example: gaan | Example: hebben |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik / jij / u / hij / zij / het | unique past form | **ging** | **had** |
| wij / jullie / zij | often stem + **en** | **gingen** | **hadden** |

**Three rules to hold onto:**

1. Learn irregular past forms **as blocks**, not from a formula.
2. **Singular** = one form for **ik**, **jij**, **hij** (like regular past).
3. **Plural** often adds **en**: **ging** → **gingen**, **had** → **hadden**.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was** thuis. | I was home. |
| **Had** je tijd? | Did you have time? |
| Hij **ging** naar de winkel. | He went to the shop. |
| Wij **kwamen** laat. | We came late. |
| Zij **deden** de boodschappen. | They did the groceries. |

### Regular vs irregular in one story

Dutch mixes both types naturally. Regular verbs keep **te/de**; irregular verbs use their own forms:

| Dutch | Verb type | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Gisteren was** ik moe. | irregular **zijn** | Yesterday I was tired. |
| Ik **werkte** thuis. | regular **werken** | I worked at home. |
| Ik **had** geen tijd. | irregular **hebben** | I had no time. |
| Ik **at** pasta. | irregular **eten** | I ate pasta. |
| Ik **ging** vroeg naar bed. | irregular **gaan** | I went to bed early. |
| Ik **luisterde** naar muziek. | regular **luisteren** | I listened to music. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Toen** ik jong **was**, **woonde** ik in Engeland. | When I was young, I lived in England. |
| **Vorige week ging** ik naar de dokter. | Last week I went to the doctor. |
| **Gisteren gaf** hij mij een **cadeau**. | Yesterday he gave me a present. |

### Past tense questions with irregular verbs

Questions flip word order, same as [Lesson 18](/notes/a1/18-simple-past-regular):

| Statement | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Je **was** ziek. | **Was** je ziek? |
| Hij **had** koorts. | **Had** hij koorts? |
| Wij **gingen** naar huis. | **Gingen** jullie naar huis? |
| Zij **at** brood. | **At** zij brood? |

**Niet** and **geen** stay in the same positions as before:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was gisteren niet** thuis. | I was not home yesterday. |
| Hij **had geen** tijd. | He had no time. |
| Wij **gingen niet** naar het feest. | We did not go to the party. |

### Tier A: six core irregular verbs (full tables)

These six verbs appear in almost every past tense conversation. Learn each table by heart.

#### zijn (to be) — past: was / waren

From [A0 Lesson 4](/notes/a0/04-pronouns-and-zijn):

| Pronoun | zijn (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | was | I was |
| jij / je | was | you were |
| u | was | you were (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | was | he / she / it was |
| wij / we | waren | we were |
| jullie | waren | you were (plural) |
| zij / ze | waren | they were |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was** gisteren ziek. | I was sick yesterday. |
| **Was** je op het feest? | Were you at the party? |
| Hij **was** te laat. | He was late. |
| Wij **waren** in Amsterdam. | We were in Amsterdam. |
| Zij **was niet** thuis. | She was not home. |

#### hebben (to have) — past: had / hadden

From [A0 Lesson 5](/notes/a0/05-hebben-and-everyday-things):

| Pronoun | hebben (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | had | I had |
| jij / je | had | you had |
| u | had | you had (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | had | he / she / it had |
| wij / we | hadden | we had |
| jullie | hadden | you had (plural) |
| zij / ze | hadden | they had |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **had** hoofdpijn. | I had a headache. |
| **Had** je een goed weekend? | Did you have a good weekend? |
| Hij **had** geen tijd. | He had no time. |
| Wij **hadden** een leuke dag. | We had a nice day. |
| Ik **had geen** honger. | I was not hungry. |

#### gaan (to go) — past: ging / gingen

| Pronoun | gaan (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | ging | I went |
| jij / je | ging | you went |
| u | ging | you went (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | ging | he / she went |
| wij / we | gingen | we went |
| jullie | gingen | you went (plural) |
| zij / ze | gingen | they went |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ging** naar de supermarkt. | I went to the supermarket. |
| **Ging** je met de trein? | Did you go by train? |
| Zij **ging** naar de dokter. | She went to the doctor. |
| Wij **gingen** naar het museum. | We went to the museum. |
| Wij **gingen niet** naar huis. | We did not go home. |

#### komen (to come) — past: kwam / kwamen

| Pronoun | komen (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | kwam | I came |
| jij / je | kwam | you came |
| u | kwam | you came (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | kwam | he / she came |
| wij / we | kwamen | we came |
| jullie | kwamen | you came (plural) |
| zij / ze | kwamen | they came |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **kwam** om acht uur. | I came at eight o'clock. |
| **Kwam** je op tijd? | Did you come on time? |
| Hij **kwam** uit het ziekenhuis. | He came from the hospital. |
| Wij **kwamen** samen. | We came together. |
| Zij **kwam niet** vandaag. | She did not come today. |

#### doen (to do) — past: deed / deden

| Pronoun | doen (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | deed | I did |
| jij / je | deed | you did |
| u | deed | you did (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | deed | he / she did |
| wij / we | deden | we did |
| jullie | deden | you did (plural) |
| zij / ze | deden | they did |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wat **deed** je gisteren? | What did you do yesterday? |
| Ik **deed** de boodschappen. | I did the grocery shopping. |
| Hij **deed** niets. | He did nothing. |
| Wij **deden** sport. | We exercised. |
| **Deed** je mee? | Did you join in? |

#### eten (to eat) — past: at / aten

| Pronoun | eten (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | at | I ate |
| jij / je | at | you ate |
| u | at | you ate (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | at | he / she ate |
| wij / we | aten | we ate |
| jullie | aten | you ate (plural) |
| zij / ze | aten | they ate |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **at** brood met kaas. | I ate bread with cheese. |
| **At** je vlees? | Did you eat meat? |
| Hij **at** in het restaurant. | He ate in the restaurant. |
| Wij **aten** pasta. | We ate pasta. |
| Ik **at geen** vis. | I did not eat fish. |

**At** looks like English _at_, but here it means _ate_. Learn **ik at** as one block.

### Tier B: high frequency irregular past (grouped tables)

These verbs are irregular but very common. Learn Tier A first, then add these in groups.

| Pronoun | zien | geven | nemen | drinken | kopen |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik / jij / hij | zag | gaf | nam | dronk | kocht |
| wij / jullie / zij | zagen | gaven | namen | dronken | kochten |

| Verb | Example 1 | Example 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zien** | Ik **zag** de trein. <br /> _I saw the train._ | **Zag** je dat? <br /> _Did you see that?_ |
| **geven** | Hij **gaf** mij een cadeau. <br /> _He gave me a present._ | **Gaf** je haar het boek? <br /> _Did you give her the book?_ |
| **nemen** | Ik **nam** de trein. <br /> _I took the train._ | **Nam** je soep? <br /> _Did you have soup?_ |
| **drinken** | Wij **dronken** koffie. <br /> _We drank coffee._ | **Dronk** je water? <br /> _Did you drink water?_ |
| **kopen** | Zij **kocht** brood. <br /> _She bought bread._ | **Kochten** jullie cadeaus? <br /> _Did you buy presents?_ |

| Pronoun | spreken | zeggen | helpen | blijven | zoeken |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik / jij / hij | sprak | zei | hielp | bleef | zocht |
| wij / jullie / zij | spraken | zeiden | hielpen | bleven | zochten |

| Verb | Example 1 | Example 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **spreken** | Ik **sprak** met de dokter. <br /> _I spoke with the doctor._ | **Sprak** je Nederlands? <br /> _Did you speak Dutch?_ |
| **zeggen** | Hij **zei** hallo. <br /> _He said hello._ | Wat **zei** je? <br /> _What did you say?_ |
| **helpen** | Zij **hielp** mij. <br /> _She helped me._ | **Hielp** je je moeder? <br /> _Did you help your mother?_ |
| **blijven** | Wij **bleven** thuis. <br /> _We stayed home._ | **Bleef** je lang? <br /> _Did you stay long?_ |
| **zoeken** | Ik **zocht** mijn sleutels. <br /> _I looked for my keys._ | **Zocht** je het boek? <br /> _Were you looking for the book?_ |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Was**, **had**, and **ging** are short and unstressed in fast speech: _ik was thuis_, _hij had tijd_. **At** (ate) sounds like Dutch short **a**, not English _at_. Drill **ik at**, **wij aten** out loud. See [Sentence Intonation](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using te/de on irregular verbs</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik **wasde** ziek. / Ik **hadde** tijd. / Ik **gingde** naar huis.

    ✅ Ik **was** ziek. / Ik **had** tijd. / Ik **ging** naar huis.

    **Zijn**, **hebben**, and **gaan** never take **te/de**. Learn **was**, **had**, **ging** as fixed forms.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Confusing at with English at</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik **at** om acht uur. (read as English _at_)

    ✅ Ik **at** om acht uur. = I **ate** at eight o'clock.

    Dutch **at** is the past of **eten**. Say it as one Dutch word, not English _at_.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using present with gisteren</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Gisteren ben** ik moe.

    ✅ **Gisteren was** ik moe.

    Time words like **gisteren** and **toen** need the past form, even with irregular verbs.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Wrong plural: was / had with wij</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Wij **was** thuis. / Wij **had** een feest.

    ✅ Wij **waren** thuis. / Wij **hadden** een feest.

    **Was** and **had** are singular. **Wij** takes **waren** and **hadden**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
