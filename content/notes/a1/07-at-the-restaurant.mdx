## Introduction

In [A0 Lesson 10](/notes/a0/10-ordering-food-and-drink) you ordered **een broodje kaas** and asked for **de rekening**. In [Lesson 6](/notes/a1/06-food-and-drink) you learned ingredients, **de** and **het**, and **eten** in full sentences. Now you step into a real restaurant: reserve a table, read a menu with starters and mains, ask for recommendations, mention allergies, and pay politely. This is one of the most rewarding places to use Dutch, because a few good phrases earn an immediate smile.

## Quick Review

You know **Mag ik de menukaart?**, **Ik wil graag**, **alstublieft**, and **Hoeveel kost het?** from A0. From [Lesson 3](/notes/a1/03-negation): **Ik drink geen alcohol**. From [Lesson 5](/notes/a1/05-possessives): **ons eten**. Today those tools move from a quick café stop to a full sit down meal.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Restaurant, café, terrace: where are you?

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| het restaurant | restaurant (full service) |
| het café | café (drinks, light food) |
| het eetcafé | café with meals |
| het terras | terrace / outdoor seating |
| de brasserie | brasserie |
| de snackbar | snack bar |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wij gaan naar een restaurant. | We are going to a restaurant. |
| Zullen we op het terras zitten? | Shall we sit on the terrace? |
| Het café is vol. | The café is full. |

### People who work there

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de ober | waiter |
| de serveerster | waitress |
| de bediening | service / staff |
| de kok | cook / chef |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De ober komt zo. | The waiter will come shortly. |
| De bediening is vriendelijk. | The service is friendly. |

In modern Dutch, **ober** and **serveerster** are still common. **De bediening** works for staff in general.

### Reserving a table

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| reserveren | to reserve / book |
| de reservering | reservation |
| een tafel voor twee | a table for two |
| op naam | under a name |
| om acht uur | at eight o'clock |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik wil graag reserveren. | I would like to make a reservation. |
| Een tafel voor drie, alstublieft. | A table for three, please. |
| Op naam Bakker, om zeven uur. | Under the name Bakker, at seven o'clock. |
| Hebben jullie een reservering? | Do you have a reservation? |
| Nee, we hebben niet gereserveerd. | No, we did not reserve. |

Phone or in person, the pattern is short: number of people + time + name.

### Reading the menu: courses

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de menukaart / het menu | menu |
| het voorgerecht | starter / appetizer |
| het hoofdgerecht | main course |
| het nagerecht | dessert course |
| het dessert | dessert |
| de specialiteit | specialty |
| de dagsoep | soup of the day |
| het dagschotel | dish of the day |
| de drank | drink / beverage |
| het drankje | (a) drink |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Als voorgerecht neem ik de soep. | As a starter I will have the soup. |
| Wat is het hoofdgerecht? | What is the main course? |
| Het nagerecht is ijs. | The dessert is ice cream. |
| De specialiteit van het huis. | The house specialty. |

### Polite ordering: the A1 toolkit

Dutch politeness at the table uses **graag**, **alstublieft**, **mag ik**, and **zou graag**:

| Dutch | English | Level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Een koffie, alstublieft. | A coffee, please. | neutral |
| Ik wil graag de soep. | I would like the soup. | polite |
| Mag ik de menukaart? | May I have the menu? | polite |
| Ik zou graag reserveren. | I would like to reserve. | more formal |
| Zou ik mogen bestellen? | May I order? | formal |
| Wat raadt u aan? | What do you recommend? | formal with **u** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Voor mij de pasta, alstublieft. | For me the pasta, please. |
| Ik neem het dagschotel. | I will take the dish of the day. |
| Nog iets te drinken? | Anything else to drink? |
| Nee, dat was het. | No, that is all. |

**Ik neem** means _I will take_ and is very common when ordering:

| Pronoun | nemen |
| --- | --- |
| ik | neem |
| jij | neemt |
| u | neemt |
| hij/zij | neemt |
| wij | nemen |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik neem de vis. | I will take the fish. |
| Neemt u ook een voorgerecht? | Will you also have a starter? |
| Wij nemen twee koffies. | We will take two coffees. |

In questions with **jij**, the **t** drops: **Neem je de soep?**

### Asking and answering about food

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wat raadt u aan? | What do you recommend? |
| Wat is lekker? | What is tasty? |
| Is dit vegetarisch? | Is this vegetarian? |
| Ik ben allergisch voor noten. | I am allergic to nuts. |
| Zonder ui, alstublieft. | Without onion, please. |
| Hoeveel kost het hoofdgerecht? | How much is the main course? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik eet geen vlees. | I do not eat meat. |
| Ik drink geen alcohol. | I do not drink alcohol. |
| Is de soep glutenvrij? | Is the soup gluten free? |

### During the meal

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Smakelijk! / Eet smakelijk! | Enjoy your meal! |
| Proost! | Cheers! |
| Het smaakt goed. | It tastes good. |
| Het was erg lekker. | It was very tasty. |
| Nog water, alstublieft. | More water, please. |
| Mag ik extra brood? | May I have extra bread? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De bediening is traag. | The service is slow. |
| Het eten is koud. | The food is cold. |
| Alles is prima. | Everything is fine. |

### Paying the bill

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de rekening | bill / check |
| betalen | to pay |
| contant | cash |
| pinnen | pay by card |
| de fooi | tip |
| het bonnetje | receipt |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De rekening, alstublieft. | The bill, please. |
| Mag ik betalen? | May I pay? |
| Kan ik pinnen? | Can I pay by card? |
| Samen of apart? | Together or separate? |
| Samen, alstublieft. | Together, please. |
| Houd de rest. | Keep the change. (tip) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het was erg lekker. De rekening, alstublieft. | It was very tasty. The bill, please. |
| Betaalt u contant of met de pin? | Are you paying cash or by card? |

### Formal **u** at the table

In restaurants, staff often use **u**:

| Informal (jij) | Formal (u) |
| --- | --- |
| Wat wil je eten? | Wat wilt **u** eten? |
| Neem je soep? | Neemt **u** soep? |
| Heb je een reservering? | Heeft **u** een reservering? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wilt u nog iets? | Would you like anything else? |
| Heeft u al gekozen? | Have you chosen already? |

You can answer with **ik** forms even when they use **u**: **Ik neem de pasta, alstublieft.**

### Word order with ordering (Lesson 2 review)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Vandaag** neem ik de vis. | Today I will take the fish. |
| **Als voorgerecht** neem ik de soep. | As a starter I will take the soup. |
| **Mag ik** de rekening? | May I have the bill? |

With two verbs, **niet** before the infinitive if you add a modal later: **Ik wil niet betalen** (rare at dinner, but the rule holds).

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  **nemen** follows regular present-tense rules (same **t**-drop as **werken**). For formal **u** at the table and polite requests, see [Modal Verbs: Introduction](/notes/grammar/06-modal-verbs-intro) — you master **willen**, **kunnen**, **mogen** in [Lesson 12](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs).
</Callout>

<Callout title="Modal preview" type="info">
  **Ik zou graag** and **Zou ik mogen** are formal polite chunks — **zou** and **mogen** get full tables in [Lesson 12](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs). Until then, learn them as fixed phrases: **Ik zou graag reserveren**, **Mag ik de rekening?**
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Allergie** and **allergisch** have Dutch **g** and **sch** at the end. **Specialiteit** stresses the third syllable: spe-cia-li-**TEIT**. Practise: **Ik ben allergisch voor noten. Zonder ui, alstublieft.**
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using willen without graag in nice restaurants</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik wil de soep. (sounds blunt)

    ✅ Ik wil **graag** de soep. / **Zou ik** de soep **mogen**?

    **Willen** alone can sound demanding. Add **graag** or use **mag ik** / **zou ik mogen**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Asking for menu with brief</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Mag ik het brief?

    ✅ Mag ik **de menukaart**?

    **Brief** means letter. **Menukaart** or **menu** is the restaurant menu.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting alstublieft at the end</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ De rekening. (can sound impatient)

    ✅ De rekening, **alstublieft**.

    Dutch politeness often puts **alstublieft** at the end of a request, especially with **u**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying allergisch verkeerd</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik heb allergie voor noten.

    ✅ Ik ben **allergisch voor** noten.

    Allergies use **zijn** + **allergisch voor**, not **hebben allergie**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dinner for two in Den Haag</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Lisa and Tom order a full meal with the waiter.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Ober:** Goedenavond! Heeft u gereserveerd?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ja, op naam De Vries. Een tafel voor twee.
    >
    > **Ober:** Natuurlijk. Volgt u mij maar. Hier op het terras.
    >
    > **Tom:** Dank u. Mag ik de menukaart?
    >
    > **Ober:** Natuurlijk. De **specialiteit** vandaag is vis. De **dagsoep** is groentesoep.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Wat **raadt u aan**?
    >
    > **Ober:** Het **dagschotel** is erg **lekker**. En de pasta ook.
    >
    > **Tom:** Ik ben **allergisch voor** noten. Zit er noten in de pasta?
    >
    > **Ober:** Nee hoor. **Geen** noten.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Prima. **Als voorgerecht** neem ik de dagsoep. **Als hoofdgerecht** de pasta.
    >
    > **Tom:** **Voor mij** het **dagschotel**, alstublieft. En een glas water.
    >
    > **Ober:** En **te drinken**? Wijn?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Een glas witte wijn, graag.
    >
    > **Tom:** Ik drink **geen** alcohol. Alleen water.
    >
    > **Ober:** **Smakelijk!**
    >
    > _(later)_
    >
    > **Lisa:** Het was **heerlijk**. **De rekening**, alstublieft.
    >
    > **Ober:** Natuurlijk. Samen of apart?
    >
    > **Tom:** **Samen**, alstublieft. Kan ik **pinnen**?
    >
    > **Ober:** Ja hoor. Alstublieft, uw bonnetje.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Dank u wel. Het was **gezellig**!

    **In English:**

    > **Waiter:** Good evening! Do you have a reservation?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Yes, under the name De Vries. A table for two.
    >
    > **Waiter:** Of course. Follow me. Here on the terrace.
    >
    > **Tom:** Thank you. May I have the menu?
    >
    > **Waiter:** Of course. Today's specialty is fish. The soup of the day is vegetable soup.
    >
    > **Lisa:** What do you recommend?
    >
    > **Waiter:** The dish of the day is very tasty. And the pasta too.
    >
    > **Tom:** I am allergic to nuts. Are there nuts in the pasta?
    >
    > **Waiter:** No. No nuts.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Great. As a starter I will have the soup of the day. As a main course the pasta.
    >
    > **Tom:** For me the dish of the day, please. And a glass of water.
    >
    > **Waiter:** And to drink? Wine?
    >
    > **Lisa:** A glass of white wine, please.
    >
    > **Tom:** I do not drink alcohol. Only water.
    >
    > **Waiter:** Enjoy your meal!
    >
    > _(later)_
    >
    > **Lisa:** It was delicious. The bill, please.
    >
    > **Waiter:** Of course. Together or separate?
    >
    > **Tom:** Together, please. Can I pay by card?
    >
    > **Waiter:** Yes. Here is your receipt.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Thank you very much. It was lovely!

    **Op naam**, **als voorgerecht**, **voor mij**, **geen alcohol**, and **samen betalen** are all high frequency restaurant Dutch.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
