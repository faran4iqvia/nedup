## Introduction

You already met a few colours inside [Lesson 11: Adjectives](/notes/a1/11-adjectives), but clothing and colour deserve a lesson of their own: they come up every time you get dressed, shop, or describe someone. After this lesson you can say **Ik draag een blauwe trui**, ask **Heeft u deze broek in maat 40?**, and describe what someone is wearing at a glance. You will also recycle the adjective endings from Lesson 11 and the separable verbs from [Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs). For a quick lookup table, see [Word Hub → Clothing](/notes/word-hub/nouns#clothing-and-accessories) and [Colours](/notes/word-hub/adjectives#colours).

## Quick Review

In [Lesson 11](/notes/a1/11-adjectives) you learned that an adjective before a **de**-word takes **-e** (**de rode jas**, **een rode jas**), and a **het**-word with **een** takes no ending (**een groen shirt**), but with **het** it does (**het groene shirt**). In [Lesson 8](/notes/a1/08-shopping) you learned **Hoeveel kost het?** and **passen**. In [Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs) you learned separable verbs like **aantrekken**. All three come back here.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The colours

| Dutch | English | Dutch | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| rood | red | bruin | brown |
| oranje | orange | grijs | grey |
| geel | yellow | zwart | black |
| groen | green | wit | white |
| blauw | blue | roze | pink |
| paars | purple | beige | beige |

Make a colour lighter or darker with **licht-** and **donker-**: **lichtblauw** (light blue), **donkergroen** (dark green). These stay one word.

<Callout title="Capital letters: colours stay lowercase">
  Unlike nationalities and languages (**Nederlands**, **Frans**) from [A0 Lesson 13](/notes/a0/13-nationalities-and-languages), colours are written in **lowercase**: **een rode jas**, not een Rode jas.
</Callout>

### Colours as adjectives (a Lesson 11 recap)

Colours behave like any adjective. Before the noun they usually take **-e**:

| Dutch | English | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de **rode** jas | the red coat | de-word → -e |
| een **blauwe** trui | a blue sweater | de-word → -e |
| het **witte** shirt | the white shirt | het-word with **het** → -e |
| een **wit** shirt | a white shirt | het-word with **een** → no ending |

### The clothing words

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de kleding / de kleren | clothing / clothes |
| de jas | coat |
| de trui | sweater / jumper |
| het shirt / het T-shirt | shirt / T-shirt |
| de broek | trousers (singular in Dutch!) |
| de spijkerbroek | jeans |
| de jurk | dress |
| de rok | skirt |
| de schoenen (de schoen) | shoes (shoe) |
| de sokken (de sok) | socks (sock) |
| de muts | woolly hat |
| de sjaal | scarf |
| de bril | glasses |

### The verb dragen (to wear) and aanhebben

**Dragen** means to wear. It is regular in the present:

| Pronoun | dragen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | draag | I wear |
| jij / je | draagt | you wear |
| hij / zij | draagt | he / she wears |
| wij / we | dragen | we wear |

You can also use the separable verb **aanhebben** (to have on), which splits like the verbs in Lesson 21:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Zij draagt een rode jas. | She is wearing a red coat. |
| Hij heeft een blauwe trui aan. | He has a blue sweater on. |
| Ik trek mijn schoenen aan. | I put my shoes on. (**aantrekken**) |
| Ik trek mijn jas uit. | I take my coat off. (**uittrekken**) |

### Sizes and trying on (shopping recap)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de maat | size |
| Welke maat heeft u? | What size do you take? |
| Heeft u dit in maat 40? | Do you have this in size 40? |
| Kan ik dit passen? | Can I try this on? |
| Het past niet. | It doesn't fit. |
| Het is te groot / te klein. | It is too big / too small. |
| de paskamer | fitting room |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  The adjective endings here are exactly the rule from [Lesson 11](/notes/a1/11-adjectives); **aantrekken** and **uittrekken** split exactly like the separable verbs in [Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs). Nothing new to memorise structurally — you are applying rules you already have to a new topic.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Grijs** and **geel** both start with the soft Dutch **g** from [Consonant Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds). **Trui** has the tricky **ui** diphthong from [Vowel Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) — round your lips and glide. **Kleren** has the stress on the first syllable: **KLE**-ren.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Making broek plural</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Mijn broeken zijn blauw. (for one pair)

    ✅ Mijn broek is blauw.

    English "trousers" is plural, but Dutch **de broek** is singular — one item. The same goes for **de spijkerbroek** (jeans).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting the -e on the colour</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ een rood jas

    ✅ een **rode** jas

    **Jas** is a de-word, so the adjective takes **-e**, even with **een**. Only het-words with **een** stay uninflected: **een wit shirt**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using dragen for "carry" and getting confused</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **Dragen** means both to wear and to carry. For clothes it is fine: **Ik draag een jas**. When you mean "carry a bag", context makes it clear, but you can also say **Ik heb een jas aan** to be unambiguous about wearing.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Capitalising a colour</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ een Blauwe trui

    ✅ een blauwe trui

    Colours are lowercase. Only nationalities and languages take a capital in Dutch.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Learning Tips

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Describe what you are wearing right now</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Look down and say it: **Ik draag een … en een …**. Add a colour to each item. Do it every morning for a week and clothing plus colour becomes automatic.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Pair each colour with a real object</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Colours stick better attached to things: **de rode brievenbus** (the red postbox), **de oranje trui** (the orange sweater — very Dutch on King's Day). Build your own colour–object pairs.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Buying a coat</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>A customer tries on a coat with help from a shop assistant.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Klant:** Hallo, ik zoek een warme jas voor de winter.
    >
    > **Verkoper:** Wat voor kleur wilt u?
    >
    > **Klant:** Iets donkers. Misschien donkerblauw of zwart.
    >
    > **Verkoper:** Deze blauwe jas is heel warm. Welke maat heeft u?
    >
    > **Klant:** Maat 40, denk ik. Kan ik hem passen?
    >
    > **Verkoper:** Natuurlijk. De paskamer is daar.
    >
    > **Klant:** Hmm, hij is een beetje te groot. Heeft u maat 38?
    >
    > **Verkoper:** Ja, hier. En deze past perfect!
    >
    > **Klant:** Mooi! Ik neem de blauwe. Hoeveel kost hij?

    **In English:**

    > **Customer:** Hello, I'm looking for a warm coat for the winter.
    >
    > **Assistant:** What colour would you like?
    >
    > **Customer:** Something dark. Maybe dark blue or black.
    >
    > **Assistant:** This blue coat is very warm. What size do you take?
    >
    > **Customer:** Size 40, I think. Can I try it on?
    >
    > **Assistant:** Of course. The fitting room is over there.
    >
    > **Customer:** Hmm, it's a bit too big. Do you have size 38?
    >
    > **Assistant:** Yes, here. And this one fits perfectly!
    >
    > **Customer:** Nice! I'll take the blue one. How much does it cost?

    Notice **de blauwe jas** and **de blauwe** (dropping the noun once it is clear), **te groot**, and **passen** from shopping. **Hem** refers back to **de jas** (a de-word).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
