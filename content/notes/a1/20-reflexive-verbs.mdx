## Introduction

You say **Ik douche** and **Ik sta om zeven uur op** from [Lesson 1](/notes/a1/01-daily-routines). Many Dutch verbs add a **reflexive pronoun** when the action happens to **yourself**: **Ik was me**, **Ik kleed me aan**, **Ik voel me goed**. The little word **me**, **je**, or **zich** is not optional. After this lesson you can describe a full morning routine on your own body, from washing to getting dressed.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### What is a reflexive verb?

A **reflexive verb** describes something you do **to yourself**. English often drops the word _myself_: _I wash_, _I get dressed_. Dutch keeps the reflexive pronoun:

| Dutch | Literally | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik **was me**. | I wash myself. | I wash / I am washing. |
| Ik **kleed me aan**. | I dress myself. | I get dressed. |
| Ik **voel me** goed. | I feel myself good. | I feel good. |
| Hij **scheert zich**. | He shaves himself. | He shaves. |

Without the reflexive pronoun, the meaning changes or the sentence sounds wrong:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was me**. | I wash myself. (morning routine) |
| Ik **was mijn handen**. | I wash my hands. (no reflexive needed) |
| Ik **was** de auto. | I wash the car. (not reflexive at all) |

### Reflexive pronouns at a glance

| Subject | Reflexive pronoun | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | **me** / **mij** | Ik was **me**. |
| jij / je | **je** / **jou** | Was **je** al? |
| u | **zich** | Wast u **zich**? |
| hij / zij / het | **zich** | Zij kleedt **zich** aan. |
| wij / we | **ons** | Wij kammen **ons**. |
| jullie | **jullie** | Kleden **jullie** je aan? |
| zij / ze | **zich** | Zij haasten **zich**. |

**Me** and **je** are most common in speech. **Mij** and **jou** work too, especially for emphasis: **Ik was mij**. At A1, use **me** and **je** as your default.

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  For the full reflexive system, verbs that are always reflexive, and verbs that can be reflexive or not, see [Reflexive Verbs and _zich_](/notes/grammar/14-reflexive-verbs) in the Grammar Track.
</Callout>

### Where the reflexive pronoun goes

In a main clause, the reflexive pronoun usually comes **after the conjugated verb**, before other objects:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was me** 's ochtends. | I wash in the morning. |
| **Voel** je **je** beter? | Do you feel better? |
| Hij **kleedt zich** snel aan. | He gets dressed quickly. |
| Wij **haasten ons** altijd. | We always hurry. |

With **niet** from [Lesson 3](/notes/a1/03-negation):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was me niet** elke dag. | I do not wash every day. |
| Zij **voelt zich niet** goed. | She does not feel well. |
| Wij **haasten ons niet**. | We are not in a hurry. |

### douchen vs zich wassen

**Douchen** (to shower) from [Lesson 1](/notes/a1/01-daily-routines) is usually used **without** **zich**: **Ik douche 's ochtends**. That is normal Dutch.

**Zich wassen** means to wash yourself (face, body, hands in a general routine sense). Both appear in daily talk:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **douche** om zeven uur. | I shower at seven o'clock. |
| Ik **was me** en **kleed me aan**. | I wash and get dressed. |
| Eerst **was** ik **me**, daarna **douche** ik. | First I wash, then I shower. |

### Reflexive + separable verbs (preview)

**Zich aankleden** and **zich uitkleden** are **reflexive** and **separable**. The prefix **aan** / **uit** jumps to the end, just like **opstaan** from Lesson 1:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **kleed me aan**. | I get dressed. |
| **Kleed** je **je aan**? | Are you getting dressed? |
| Hij **kleedt zich** uit. | He gets undressed. |
| Wij **kleden ons** aan. | We get dressed. |

Full separable verb rules come in [Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs). For now, learn **Ik kleed me aan** as one chunk.

### Tier A: three core reflexive verbs (full tables)

Each block has the **verb + reflexive pronoun** and **five example sentences**.

#### zich wassen (to wash oneself)

| Pronoun | wassen | reflexive | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik | was | me | I wash myself |
| jij / je | wast | je | you wash yourself |
| u | wast | zich | you wash yourself (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | wast | zich | he / she washes |
| wij / we | wassen | ons | we wash ourselves |
| jullie | wassen | jullie | you wash yourselves |
| zij / ze | wassen | zich | they wash themselves |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was me** 's ochtends. | I wash in the morning. |
| **Was** je **je** al? | Have you washed yet? |
| Hij **wast zich** in de badkamer. | He washes in the bathroom. |
| Wij **wassen ons** voor het ontbijt. | We wash before breakfast. |
| Ik **was me niet** gisteren. | I did not wash yesterday. |

#### zich aankleden (to get dressed) — separable

| Pronoun | aankleden | reflexive | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik | kleed … aan | me | I get dressed |
| jij / je | kleedt … aan | je | you get dressed |
| u | kleedt … aan | zich | you get dressed (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | kleedt … aan | zich | he / she gets dressed |
| wij / we | kleden … aan | ons | we get dressed |
| jullie | kleden … aan | jullie | you get dressed (plural) |
| zij / ze | kleden … aan | zich | they get dressed |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **kleed me aan** na het douchen. | I get dressed after showering. |
| **Kleed** je **je** snel aan? | Are you getting dressed quickly? |
| Zij **kleedt zich** warm aan. | She dresses warmly. |
| Wij **kleden ons** om zeven uur aan. | We get dressed at seven o'clock. |
| Hij **kleedt zich niet** snel aan. | He does not get dressed quickly. |

#### zich voelen (to feel) — health and mood

You met **Ik voel me niet goed** in [Lesson 17](/notes/a1/17-body-health):

| Pronoun | voelen | reflexive | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik | voel | me | I feel |
| jij / je | voelt | je | you feel |
| u | voelt | zich | you feel (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | voelt | zich | he / she feels |
| wij / we | voelen | ons | we feel |
| jullie | voelen | jullie | you feel (plural) |
| zij / ze | voelen | zich | they feel |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **voel me** goed vandaag. | I feel good today. |
| **Voel** je **je** beter? | Do you feel better? |
| Hij **voelt zich** moe. | He feels tired. |
| Wij **voelen ons** gezond. | We feel healthy. |
| Ik **voel me niet** zo lekker. | I do not feel so well. |

**Voelen** without reflexive means to feel physically with your senses: **Ik voel de warmte**. With **me/je/zich**, it means how you feel inside.

### Tier B: daily reflexive verbs (grouped table)

| Pronoun | zich uitkleden | zich scheren | zich kammen | zich haasten | zich vervelen |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik | kleed me uit | scheer me | kam me | haast me | verveel me |
| jij / je | kleedt je uit | scheert je | kamt je | haast je | verveelt je |
| hij / zij | kleedt zich uit | scheert zich | kamt zich | haast zich | verveelt zich |
| wij / zij | kleden ons/… uit | scheren ons/… | kammen ons/… | haasten ons/… | vervelen ons/… |

| Verb | Example 1 | Example 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zich uitkleden** | Ik **kleed me uit** 's avonds. <br /> _I get undressed in the evening._ | **Kleed** je **je** uit? <br /> _Are you getting undressed?_ |
| **zich scheren** | Hij **scheert zich** elke ochtend. <br /> _He shaves every morning._ | **Scheer** je **je** vandaag? <br /> _Are you shaving today?_ |
| **zich kammen** | Zij **kamt zich** voor de spiegel. <br /> _She combs her hair in front of the mirror._ | **Kam** je **je** elke dag? <br /> _Do you comb your hair every day?_ |
| **zich haasten** | Wij **haasten ons** voor het werk. <br /> _We hurry for work._ | **Haast** je **je**! <br /> _Hurry up!_ |
| **zich vervelen** | Ik **verveel me** thuis. <br /> _I am bored at home._ | **Verveel** je **je** niet? <br /> _Are you not bored?_ |

### A full morning routine in Dutch

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **sta** om zeven uur **op**. | I get up at seven o'clock. |
| Ik **was me** en **poets mijn tanden**. | I wash and brush my teeth. |
| Ik **douche** en **kleed me aan**. | I shower and get dressed. |
| Ik **kam me** en **scheer me**. | I comb my hair and shave. |
| Ik **haast me** voor het ontbijt. | I hurry before breakfast. |
| Ik **voel me** goed. | I feel good. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Me** and **je** are short and unstressed: _ik-WAS-me_, _VOEL-je-je_. Do not stress **me** like English _me_. Say the whole morning chain in one flow. See [Sentence Intonation](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping the reflexive pronoun</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik **was**. / Ik **kleed aan**. / Ik **voel** goed.

    ✅ Ik **was me**. / Ik **kleed me aan**. / Ik **voel me** goed.

    English drops _myself_ easily. Dutch needs **me**, **je**, or **zich** with these verbs.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using me with hij or zij</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Hij **wast me**. / Zij **kleedt me** aan.

    ✅ Hij **wast zich**. / Zij **kleedt zich** aan.

    **Me** and **je** match **ik** and **jij**. For **hij**, **zij**, and **u**, use **zich**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Putting aan before me</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik **kleed aan me**.

    ✅ Ik **kleed me aan**.

    **Aan** is separable: it goes to the end. Reflexive **me** sits after the verb stem: **kleed me … aan**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Confusing zich wassen with wassen de auto</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik **was** (= I washed, but incomplete)

    ✅ Ik **was me** (= I washed myself) · Ik **was de auto** (= I washed the car)

    **Zich wassen** always needs the reflexive. Plain **wassen** takes an object: **de auto**, **de kleding**, **mijn handen**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
