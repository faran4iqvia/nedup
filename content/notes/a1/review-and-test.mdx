## Introduction

You have completed all twenty one A1 lessons, from daily routines to separable verbs. This chapter brings everything together: a topic by topic summary, a combined vocabulary reference, a mixed test, and a clear picture of what you can do at A1 level. Take your time, say answers aloud, and revisit any lesson where you hesitated. Passing most of this test means you are ready for A2.

## Summary by Topic

### Daily routines, word order, and negation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 1 to 3</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    You describe a full day with **werken**, **doen**, **opstaan**, **douchen**, and frequency words (**altijd**, **meestal**, **soms**, **nooit**). Dutch uses **verb second** in main clauses: **Vandaag werk ik thuis**. **Niet** negates verbs and adjectives; **geen** negates nouns without **de/het**: **Ik heb geen tijd**. With separable verbs, **niet** goes before the prefix: **Ik sta niet op**.

    **Key patterns:** Ik sta om zeven uur op. · Vandaag werk ik niet. · Ik heb geen auto.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Family, possessives, and descriptions

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 4, 5, and 11</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    You talk about family with **hebben** (**Ik heb twee broers**) and **zijn** (**Mijn moeder is lerares**). Possessives: **mijn, jouw, zijn, haar, ons/onze, jullie, hun**. Adjectives and colours: **de grote stad**, **het witte huis**, **de rode auto**, predicative **De soep is lekker**.

    **Key patterns:** Mijn ouders wonen in Utrecht. · Ons huis is klein. · De groene bomen zijn mooi.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Food, shopping, and demonstratives

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 6 to 9</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    You shop, order food, and use **deze/die** (near) and **dit/dat** (far) with correct plural agreement: **deze appels**, **die stoelen**. Restaurant phrases: **Mag ik …?**, **de rekening**, **Alsjeblieft**. Shopping: **Hoeveel kost het?**, **Ik kijk alleen**.

    **Key patterns:** Deze broek is te duur. · Dat is mijn tas. · Ik wil graag de soep.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Plurals and present tense verbs

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 10 and 12</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Plural patterns: **en** (**de stoel → de stoelen**), **s** (**de tafel → de tafels**), vowel change (**man → mannen**, **stad → steden**). Core verbs in full: **werken, doen, eten, zien, gaan, komen, hebben, zijn**. Modals: **willen, kunnen, moeten, mogen** with infinitive at the end (**Ik wil koffie drinken**). **Jij** questions drop **t**: **Werk je?**, **Kun je me helpen?**

    **Key patterns:** De kinderen spelen buiten. · Ik moet werken. · Wij maken soep.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Home, hobbies, and transport

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 13 to 15</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Housing: **de keuken**, **het bed**, **staan/liggen/zitten** for position. Hobbies: **houden van**, **leuk vinden**, **Ik sport in het weekend**. Transport: **met de trein**, **Hoe kom ik bij …?**, **Ga rechtdoor** (imperative), **Kun je me helpen?** (object pronoun), separable **overstappen**.

    **Key patterns:** De lamp staat op de tafel. · Ik houd van lezen. · Sla links af bij het plein.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Weather and health

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 16 and 17</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Impersonal **het**: **Het regent**, **Het is warm**. Sensations with **hebben**: **Ik heb het koud**, **Ik heb hoofdpijn**. Health: **Ik ben ziek/verkouden**, **Mijn keel doet pijn**, **de dokter**, **de apotheek**.

    **Key patterns:** Hoe is het weer? · Ik heb koorts. · Waar is de apotheek?
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Simple past tense

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 18 and 19</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Regular past: **te/de/ten/den** (**werkte**, **woonden**, **maakte**). Irregular past: **was/waren**, **had/hadden**, **ging/gingen**, **kwam/kwamen**, **deed/deden**, **at/aten**, plus **zag**, **gaf**, **nam**, **kocht**. Time words: **gisteren**, **vorige week**, **toen**, **vroeger**.

    **Key patterns:** Gisteren werkte ik thuis. · Wij gingen naar het museum. · Had je tijd?
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Reflexive and separable verbs

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 20 and 21</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Reflexive pronouns: **me, je, zich, ons, jullie**. **Ik was me**, **Ik voel me goed**, **Ik kleed me aan**. Separable verbs split: **sta** … **op**, **neem** … **mee**, **ga** … **uit**. With modals, prefix stays attached: **Ik wil opstaan**, **Ik kan meekomen**.

    **Key patterns:** Voel je je beter? · Ik neem een paraplu mee. · Wij gaan vanavond uit.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## What You Can Do at A1 (CEFR)

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Can do statements</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    At A1 level, you can:

    - **Understand and use** familiar everyday expressions and very frequent phrases concerning yourself, your family, shopping, local geography, and employment
    - **Introduce yourself** and others, ask and answer questions about personal details, daily routine, and immediate needs
    - **Interact** in a simple way on familiar topics if the other person speaks slowly and clearly
    - **Describe** where you live, people you know, and things you have using simple connected sentences
    - **Handle** short social exchanges: weather, health, transport, hobbies, food, and shopping
    - **Talk** about the past in simple terms: **gisteren werkte ik**, **wij gingen uit**
    - **Use** reflexive and separable verbs in everyday routines

    **You are ready for A2 when** you can pass most of this test (roughly 80% or more) and hold a slow conversation about your day, last weekend, and plans without long pauses on basic grammar. If several sections felt hard, revisit those lessons before starting A2.

    **Strengthen weak spots:** [Grammar Review and Practice](/notes/grammar/review-and-practice) (Part A) · [Pronunciation Review and Practice](/notes/pronunciation/review-and-practice) (Parts A to C)

    **Next step:** [Phase 2 A2 lessons](/notes/a2) begin with past tense in depth and the perfect tense (**hebben/zijn** + past participle). Topics you have previewed but will master in A2 include comparatives, **er is/er zijn**, and subordinate clauses. You have built a solid foundation across **1,000 words** and twenty one A1 lessons plus your A0 base.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
