## About Dutch verbs

**~105 verbs** · Regular (**weak**) verbs form the past with **-te / -de** and the participle with **ge- … -t / -d** — the choice follows the **'t kofschip** rule ([see the Grammar Track](/notes/grammar/19-past-tense-weak-strong)). Irregular (**strong**) verbs change their vowel instead, so those are listed with their full past and participle. The other groups — separable, reflexive, and modal — behave in special ways worth learning as sets.

## Weak verbs (pattern)

Most Dutch verbs are **weak**. Past: stem + **-te** or **-de** · Participle: **ge-** + stem + **-t** or **-d**.

| Infinitive | English | Past (ik) | Participle |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| werken | to work | werkte | gewerkt |
| wonen | to live | woonde | gewoond |
| maken | to make | maakte | gemaakt |
| leren | to learn | leerde | geleerd |
| wachten | to wait | wachtte | gewacht |
| betalen | to pay | betaalde | betaald |
| bellen | to call | belde | gebeld |
| reserveren | to book | reserveerde | gereserveerd |

<Callout title="'t kofschip" type="info">
  After voiceless consonants (**t, k, f, s, ch, p**): **-te** / **-t** (**werkte**, **gewerkt**). After voiced sounds: **-de** / **-d** (**woonde**, **gewoond**).
</Callout>

## Modal verbs

The five modals push the main verb to the end as an infinitive.

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| kunnen | can / to be able | kan | kon |
| moeten | must / to have to | moet | moest |
| mogen | may / to be allowed | mag | mocht |
| willen | to want | wil | wilde / wou |
| zullen | shall / will | zal | zou |
