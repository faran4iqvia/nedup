## About Dutch nouns

**~231 words** · Every noun is listed **with its article** — memorise **de** or **het** as part of the word. The **plural** is given too, because Dutch plurals split mainly between **-en** (the majority) and **-s** (words ending in **-el, -em, -en, -er, -je**, and most loanwords). A dash (—) means the noun is normally uncountable.

<Callout title="Quick rules of thumb" type="info">
  All **plurals** take **de** (**de huizen**, **de kinderen**). All
  **diminutives** (the **-je** form) take **het**: **het huisje**, **het
  kopje**. When in doubt about an article, check the [Grammar Track: Articles de
  and het](/notes/grammar/09-articles-de-het).
</Callout>
