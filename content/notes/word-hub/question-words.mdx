## About Dutch question words

A question word starts the sentence and the verb comes straight after it: **Waar woon je?**, **Hoe heet je?**. Two pairs need care: **welke / welk** (which agrees with de/het) and the split pattern **waar … naartoe / vandaan**. See [Question Formation](/notes/grammar/05-question-formation).

## The core question words

| Dutch | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| wie | who | **Wie** ben jij? |
| wat | what | **Wat** doe je? |
| waar | where | **Waar** woon je? |
| wanneer | when | **Wanneer** kom je? |
| waarom | why | **Waarom** ben je laat? |
| hoe | how | **Hoe** gaat het? |
| hoeveel | how much / many | **Hoeveel** kost het? |
| hoe laat | what time | **Hoe laat** is het? |

## Which and what kind

| Dutch | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| welke | which (de-word / plural) | **Welke** trein neem je? |
| welk | which (het-word) | **Welk** boek lees je? |
| wat voor (een) | what kind of | **Wat voor** muziek hou je van? |

## Direction and origin

Dutch often splits these around the verb.

| Dutch | English | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| waar … naartoe | where to | **Waar** ga je **naartoe**? |
| waar … vandaan | where from | **Waar** kom je **vandaan**? |
| met wie | with whom | **Met wie** ga je? |
| hoe lang | how long | **Hoe lang** blijf je? |
| hoe vaak | how often | **Hoe vaak** sport je? |
