## Welcome to the Word Hub 🗂️

The Word Hub is a **reference dictionary**, not a lesson. Instead of learning words in the order you meet them, here they are sorted by **word type** so you can look things up, revise a whole category at once, or hunt for the exact word you need.

Every page is a set of clean tables: **Dutch on the left, English on the right**, with the extra detail that actually matters in Dutch — the article for nouns, the past forms for verbs, an example for prepositions.

<Callout title="Learn nouns with their article" type="info">
  Dutch nouns are either **de**-words or **het**-words, and there is no reliable
  rule for guessing which. So every noun in the Word Hub is listed **with its
  article** — always memorise **het huis**, never just **huis**. About two
  thirds of all nouns are **de**-words.
</Callout>

## How to use it

1. **Look up, don't cram.** Use it like a dictionary — find the word you need for a sentence or a [Scenario](/notes/scenarios/00-introduction).
2. **Revise by category.** Reading a whole table of colours or prepositions at once builds a mental map the lessons spread out over weeks.
3. **Always say the article.** For nouns, read **de** or **het** out loud every time. It is the single hardest thing to fix later.
4. **Cross-check with the lessons.** When a word feels shaky, the [Grammar Track](/notes/grammar) explains the rules behind the forms you see here.
5. **Sounds wrong?** Check vowel and stress in the [Pronunciation Track](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) and hear single words on [Forvo](https://forvo.com/languages/nl/).
