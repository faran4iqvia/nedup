## Introduction

You are flying to the Netherlands (or another Schengen country) and may meet a **grensbewaking** / **douane** officer at the airport. In practice, many officers switch to English — but preparing Dutch answers helps you stay calm, catch key words, and respond if they start in Dutch.

This scenario assumes **A1** Dutch: present tense, short answers, formal **u**, and vocabulary you already met in [A1 Lesson 5: Possessives](/notes/a1/05-possessives) (**uw paspoort**), [A0 Lesson 9: Where Do You Live](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live) (**ik kom uit …**), [A1 Lesson 4: Family](/notes/a1/04-family), [A1 Lesson 13: Housing](/notes/a1/13-housing), and [A1 Lesson 15: Transport](/notes/a1/15-transport).

<Callout title="Before you travel" type="info">
  Officers usually ask **three to six** questions, not all fifteen. Learn the full list anyway — then nothing surprises you. Keep **paspoort**, **boarding pass**, and **retourticket** (or proof of onward travel) easy to reach. If you travel with children, every child needs their **own paspoort** (baby included).
</Callout>

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Register: always formal with the officer

At the border, use **u** / **uw**, not **jij** / **je**:

| ❌ Informal | ✅ Formal |
| --- | --- |
| **Heb je** je paspoort? | **Heeft u** uw paspoort? |
| **Waar kom je** vandaan? | **Waar komt u** vandaan? |
| **Hoe lang blijf je?** | **Hoe lang blijft u?** |

Short polite replies: **Ja, natuurlijk.** · **Alstublieft.** · **Dank u wel.**

### Sentence patterns immigration uses

Border questions are short **V2** sentences (verb in second position). Your answers follow four patterns:

**Pattern 1 — Yes / no + detail**

| Officer | You |
| --- | --- |
| **Heeft u een retourticket?** | **Ja**, hier is mijn retourticket. |
| **Reist u alleen?** | **Nee**, met mijn gezin. |

**Pattern 2 — Ik + verb + rest** (statements)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik kom** uit India. | I come from India. |
| **Ik ga** naar Amsterdam. | I am going to Amsterdam. |
| **Ik verblijf** in een hotel. | I am staying in a hotel. |
| **Ik ben** hier op vakantie. | I am here on holiday. |

**Pattern 3 — Purpose with voor / op**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik ben hier voor** vakantie. | I am here for a holiday. |
| **Ik ben hier op** zakenreis. | I am here on a business trip. |
| **Ik bezoek** familie in Utrecht. | I am visiting family in Utrecht. |

**Pattern 4 — With family (wij / mijn)**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wij reizen samen.** | We are travelling together. |
| **Dit is mijn zoon**, hij is zes jaar. | This is my son, he is six. |
| **Hier zijn onze paspoorten.** | Here are our passports. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  Formal **u**-questions and possessives are in [Possessive Pronouns](/notes/grammar/10-possessive-pronouns). For **ik kom uit** and **wonen**, see [Where Do You Live?](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live) and A1 housing vocabulary in [Housing](/notes/a1/13-housing).
</Callout>

## The 15 questions (and how to answer)

Study **solo** answers first, then the **family** column. English is under each Dutch line.

| # | Officer asks | Solo traveler answer | With family / child |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Uw paspoort, alstublieft.** <br /> _Your passport, please._ | **Alstublieft. Hier is mijn paspoort.** <br /> _Here you are. Here is my passport._ | **Hier zijn onze paspoorten.** <br /> _Here are our passports._ |
| 2 | **Waar komt u vandaan?** <br /> _Where are you from?_ | **Ik kom uit India.** / **Ik kom uit het Verenigd Koninkrijk.** <br /> _I am from India._ / _I am from the United Kingdom._ | **Wij komen uit …** <br /> _We are from …_ |
| 3 | **Wat is het doel van uw reis?** <br /> _What is the purpose of your trip?_ | **Toerisme.** / **Ik ben hier op vakantie.** <br /> _Tourism._ / _I am here on holiday._ | **Familiebezoek.** / **Vakantie met mijn gezin.** <br /> _Visiting family._ / _Holiday with my family._ |
| 4 | **Hoe lang blijft u?** <br /> _How long are you staying?_ | **Twee weken.** / **Tot 15 augustus.** <br /> _Two weeks._ / _Until 15 August._ | **Drie weken met mijn gezin.** <br /> _Three weeks with my family._ |
| 5 | **Waar verblijft u?** <br /> _Where are you staying?_ | **In een hotel in Amsterdam.** / **Bij vrienden in Utrecht.** <br /> _In a hotel in Amsterdam._ / _With friends in Utrecht._ | **In een appartement bij familie.** <br /> _In a flat with family._ |
| 6 | **Heeft u een retourticket?** <br /> _Do you have a return ticket?_ | **Ja, hier is mijn retourticket.** <br /> _Yes, here is my return ticket._ | **Ja, voor ons allemaal.** <br /> _Yes, for all of us._ |
| 7 | **Reist u alleen?** <br /> _Are you travelling alone?_ | **Ja, ik reis alleen.** <br /> _Yes, I am travelling alone._ | **Nee, met mijn vrouw en mijn zoon.** <br /> _No, with my wife and my son._ |
| 8 | **Hoeveel bagage heeft u?** <br /> _How much luggage do you have?_ | **Eén koffer en één handbagage.** <br /> _One suitcase and one piece of hand luggage._ | **Twee koffers en een kinderwagen.** <br /> _Two suitcases and a pram._ |
| 9 | **Brengt u voedsel of planten mee?** <br /> _Are you bringing food or plants?_ | **Nee, niets.** <br /> _No, nothing._ | **Nee, alleen babyvoeding voor mijn kind.** <br /> _No, only baby food for my child._ |
| 10 | **Heeft u genoeg geld voor uw verblijf?** <br /> _Do you have enough money for your stay?_ | **Ja, ik heb een creditcard en contant geld.** <br /> _Yes, I have a credit card and cash._ | **Ja, wij hebben genoeg geld.** <br /> _Yes, we have enough money._ |
| 11 | **Gaat u hier werken?** <br /> _Are you going to work here?_ | **Nee, ik werk niet in Nederland.** <br /> _No, I am not working in the Netherlands._ | **Nee, wij zijn op vakantie.** <br /> _No, we are on holiday._ |
| 12 | **Was u hier eerder?** <br /> _Have you been here before?_ | **Nee, dit is mijn eerste keer.** <br /> _No, this is my first time._ | **Ja, vorig jaar ook.** <br /> _Yes, last year as well._ |
| 13 | **Spreekt u Nederlands?** <br /> _Do you speak Dutch?_ | **Een beetje. Ik leer Nederlands.** <br /> _A little. I am learning Dutch._ | **Een beetje. Mijn kind spreekt Engels.** <br /> _A little. My child speaks English._ |
| 14 | **Heeft uw kind een eigen paspoort?** <br /> _Does your child have their own passport?_ | — | **Ja, hier is het paspoort van mijn dochter.** <br /> _Yes, here is my daughter's passport._ |
| 15 | **Reist uw gezin samen?** / **Is dit uw kind?** <br /> _Is your family travelling together?_ / _Is this your child?_ | — | **Ja, dit is mijn zoon. Wij reizen samen.** <br /> _Yes, this is my son. We are travelling together._ |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Drill these aloud: **UW pas-poort alstublieft** · **WAAR komt u van-DAAN** · **ik kom UIT …** · **twee WE-ken**. Stress content words; keep **u** short and clear. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech) when phrases feel too fast.
</Callout>
