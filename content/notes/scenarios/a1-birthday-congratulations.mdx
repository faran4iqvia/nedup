## Introduction

In the Netherlands you say **Gefeliciteerd met je/uw verjaardag!** — often to the person *and* their family. This scenario covers [Family](/notes/a1/04-family) and basic [Culture](/notes/a2/18-culture-traditions) phrases at **A1** level.

<Callout title="Dutch birthday culture" type="info">
  At office birthdays, colleagues may sit in a circle and eat **taart** together. **Gefeliciteerd** to everyone connected to the birthday person is normal — do not find it strange!
</Callout>

## Key phrases

| # | Them | You |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Vandaag ben ik jarig!** | **Gefeliciteerd!** |
| 2 | **Hoe oud word je?** | **Ik word dertig.** |
| 3 | **Wil je een stuk taart?** | **Ja, graag!** |
| 4 | **Proost!** | **Proost!** |
| 5 | **Bedankt voor het cadeau!** | **Graag gedaan!** |
| 6 | **Gefeliciteerd met je moeder!** | *(at Dutch family party)* |

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader><CardTitle className="text-lg">Op kantoor</CardTitle></CardHeader>
  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Collega:** Goedemorgen! Vandaag ben ik jarig — ik trakteer op taart in de keuken.
    >
    > **U:** Gefeliciteerd! Hoe oud word je?
    >
    > **Collega:** Vijfendertig. Neem een stuk!
    >
    > **U:** Dank u wel, gefeliciteerd! … Lekker taart.
    >
    > **Collega:** Dank je! Om vier uur komen nog collega's.
    >
    > **U:** Leuk! Nog een keer gefeliciteerd!

    **In English:**

    > **Colleague:** Good morning! Today is my birthday — I am treating everyone to cake in the kitchen.
    >
    > **You:** Congratulations! How old are you turning?
    >
    > **Colleague:** Thirty-five. Have a piece!
    >
    > **You:** Thank you, congratulations! … Tasty cake.
    >
    > **Colleague:** Thanks! At four o'clock more colleagues are coming.
    >
    > **You:** Nice! Congratulations again!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
