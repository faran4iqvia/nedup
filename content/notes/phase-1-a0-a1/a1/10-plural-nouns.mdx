## Introduction

You already say **de appels**, **de eieren**, and **de kinderen** in shopping and family sentences. Now you learn **why** Dutch plurals look the way they do: the **en** ending, the **s** ending, vowel changes, and a handful of irregular sets. After this lesson you can form plurals confidently, pair them with **deze** and **die** from [Lesson 9](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/09-demonstratives), and fix mistakes like **dit eieren** or **Hoeveel kost de schoenen?**

## Quick Review

From [Lesson 9](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/09-demonstratives): plural nouns always use **de**, so demonstratives become **deze** / **die**, never **dit** / **dat**. From [Lesson 6](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/06-food-and-drink) you met **de appels**, **de tomaten**, and **de eieren**. From [Lesson 4](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/04-family): **de broers**, **de zussen**, **de kinderen**. From [Lesson 8](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/08-shopping): **De schoenen kosten** tachtig euro. Today those pieces become one clear system.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### One rule first: plural = always de

| Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- |
| **het** ei → **de** eieren | eggs |
| **het** kind → **de** kinderen | children |
| **de** appel → **de** appels | apples |
| **de** stad → **de** steden | cities |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De** eieren zijn vers. | The eggs are fresh. |
| **Deze** eieren zijn goedkoop. | These eggs are cheap. |
| **Die** huizen zijn groot. | Those houses are big. |

**Het** disappears in the plural. Every plural noun takes **de**, including words that were **het** in the singular.

### Four patterns at a glance

Dutch plurals are not fully predictable from the singular. At A1, group them like this:

| Pattern | How it forms | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1. + en** | add **en** (sometimes with consonant doubling) | stoelen, vragen, weken, mannen |
| **2. + s** | add **s** or **'s** | kamers, tafels, appels, auto's |
| **3. vowel change** | inner vowel shifts, then usually **en** | steden, huizen, broden, bedrijven |
| **4. + eren** | add **eren** on a small set | kinderen, eieren |

One noun = one pattern. **De kamer** takes **s**, not **en**. **De vraag** takes **en**, not **s**. When in doubt, check a dictionary: **de stoel (stoelen)**.

### Pattern 1: add en (most common)

This is the default plural ending in Dutch. If you are guessing at A1, try **en** first, but always check the dictionary.

| Singular | Plural | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de stoel | de stoelen | chairs |
| de boom | de bomen | trees |
| de student | de studenten | students |
| de les | de lessen | lessons |
| de week | de weken | weeks |
| de maand | de maanden | months |
| de vrouw | de vrouwen | women |
| de broek | de broeken | trousers |
| de vraag | de vragen | questions |
| de trein | de treinen | trains |
| het woord | de woorden | words |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| In de kamer staan twee stoelen. | There are two chairs in the room. |
| De studenten hebben vragen. | The students have questions. |
| Ik werk veertig uur per week, maar deze weken meer. | I work forty hours a week, but more these weeks. |

**Spelling tip:** when a short vowel sits in a closed syllable, Dutch often doubles the consonant before **en**:

| Singular | Plural | What happens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de pen | de pennen | double **n** + en |
| de man | de mannen | double **n** + en |
| de bus | de bussen | double **s** + en |
| de kat | de katten | double **t** + en |
| de trap | de trappen | double **p** + en |
| de zus | de zussen | double **s** + en |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Drie mannen wachten op de bus. | Three men are waiting for the bus. |
| De katten slapen op de stoelen. | The cats sleep on the chairs. |

### Pattern 2: add s (or 's)

Many words take **s** instead of **en**. Common groups:

- words ending in **el**, **em**, **er**, **en**, **je**, or an unstressed final syllable
- many short words and loanwords
- words that already end in a vowel often take **'s** (apostrophe + s)

| Singular | Plural | Ending | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| de kamer | de kamers | + s | rooms |
| de tafel | de tafels | + s | tables |
| de broer | de broers | + s | brothers |
| de vogel | de vogels | + s | birds |
| de appel | de appels | + s | apples |
| de foto | de foto's | + 's | photos |
| de auto | de auto's | + 's | cars |
| de oma | de oma's | + 's | grandmothers |
| het museum | de museums | + s | museums |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Mijn ouders hebben twee auto's. | My parents have two cars. |
| De foto's zijn mooi. | The photos are beautiful. |
| De tafels in het restaurant zijn vol. | The tables in the restaurant are full. |

**Apostrophe rule at A1:**

| Ends in | Example |
| --- | --- |
| vowel (a, o, u, e) | de auto → de **auto's**, de foto → de **foto's** |
| consonant | de tafel → de **tafels**, de kamer → de **kamers** |

The last letter alone is not enough. **Vraag** ends in a consonant but takes **vragen**, not **vraags**. Learn frequent pairs as chunks.

### Pattern 3: vowel change in the stem

Some common nouns change an inner vowel before **en**. The ending is still usually **en**, but the stem itself shifts.

| Singular | Plural | Change | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| de stad | de steden | a → e | cities |
| de straat | de straten | aa → a | streets |
| het huis | de huizen | ui → u | houses |
| de neef | de neven | ee → e | cousins (male) |
| het bedrijf | de bedrijven | ij → i, f → v | companies |
| het brood | de broden | oo → o | loaves |
| de brief | de brieven | ie → ie (long) | letters |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Veel steden hebben een markt. | Many cities have a market. |
| De huizen in deze straat zijn oud. | The houses in this street are old. |
| Twee bedrijven werken in dit gebouw. | Two companies work in this building. |

You met **de stad** and **de straat** in [A0 Lesson 9](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a0/09-where-do-you-live). The plural forms **steden**, **straten**, and **huizen** are high frequency.

**Not vowel change:** **man → mannen** and **vrouw → vrouwen** only double a consonant or add **en**. The vowel stays the same. Those belong in Pattern 1.

### Pattern 4: eren and other irregular sets

A small group of very common words uses **eren**, or a shape you should memorise early.

| Singular | Plural | Pattern | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| het kind | de kinderen | + eren | children |
| het ei | de eieren | + eren | eggs |
| de nicht | de nichten | + en | cousins (female) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De kinderen spelen in de tuin. | The children play in the garden. |
| Zes eieren, alstublieft. | Six eggs, please. |
| Mijn nichten wonen in België. | My cousins live in Belgium. |

Family plurals from [Lesson 4](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/04-family) fit across these patterns:

| Singular | Plural | Pattern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de broer | de broers | + s |
| de zus | de zussen | double s + en |
| het kind | de kinderen | + eren |
| de neef | de neven | vowel change + en |
| de nicht | de nichten | + en |
| de dochter | de dochters | + s |
| de tante | de tantes | + s |
| de oom | de ooms | + s |

### Food plurals you already use

| Singular | Plural | Pattern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de appel | de appels | + s |
| de banaan | de bananen | + en |
| de tomaat | de tomaten | + en |
| de aardappel | de aardappelen | + en |
| het brood | de broden | vowel change + en |
| het ei | de eieren | + eren |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik koop vijf bananen en drie tomaten. | I buy five bananas and three tomatoes. |
| De broden van de bakkerij zijn vers. | The loaves from the bakery are fresh. |
| Deze aardappelen zijn te duur. | These potatoes are too expensive. |

### Plural subjects and verbs

Plural nouns need plural verb forms where Dutch distinguishes them:

| Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- |
| De schoen **kost** tachtig euro. | De schoenen **kosten** tachtig euro. |
| Het ei **is** vers. | De eieren **zijn** vers. |
| De kat **slaapt**. | De katten **slapen**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoeveel **kosten** deze truien? | How much do these sweaters cost? |
| De honden **zijn** lief. | The dogs are sweet. |
| Waar **zijn** mijn sleutels? | Where are my keys? |

From [Lesson 3](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/03-negation): **Ik koop geen appels** (not **geen appel** when you mean apples in general).

### A practical strategy at A1

You cannot guess every plural from the singular ending alone. **Kamer** looks like **stoel**, but **kamers** takes **s** while **stoelen** takes **en**. Learn **high frequency pairs** as chunks: **het huis → de huizen**, **de stad → de steden**, **het kind → de kinderen**, **de kamer → de kamers**. When you learn a new noun, learn the plural in the same moment. Dictionaries show: **de stoel (stoelen)**.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  The **en** plural ending is often a schwa sound, like the **e** in **de** or **een**, not a loud English "en." Say **stoelen**, **mannen**, **kinderen** with a soft ending. See [Vowel Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) for short vs long vowels, which also explain spelling changes like **pen → pennen**.
</Callout>

  </CardContent>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>

  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ De appels **kost** twee euro.

    ✅ De appels **kosten** twee euro.

    English "cost" looks the same for one apple or many. Dutch uses **kost** vs **kosten**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Wrong apostrophe with s plurals</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ twee autos / twee foto's for **tafel**

    ✅ twee **auto's**, twee **foto's**, maar twee **tafels**

    Use **'s** after a vowel (**auto's**). No apostrophe when the word ends in a consonant (**tafels**).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

  <CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Lisa:** Kijk! **Deze** kamers zijn groot. **Die** ramen zijn ook mooi.
    >
    > **Tom:** Ja, en **de** stoelen en **de** tafels blijven van **de** eigenaar.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Twee stoelen zijn genoeg. **De** katten zijn niet in **de** kamer, hoor.
    >
    > **Tom:** Geen katten hier. En geen honden in **het** appartement.
    >
    > **Lisa:** **In dat geval** is **de** tuin perfect. **De** bomen zijn mooi.
    >
    > **Tom:** Hoeveel **kosten** **de** lampen in **die** kamer?
    >
    > **Lisa:** **Die** lampen? **Die** **kosten** samen veertig euro.
    >
    > **Tom:** Prima. En **de** foto's aan **de** muur?
    >
    > **Lisa:** **Die** foto's blijven. **De** straten op **de** foto's zijn in Amsterdam.
    >
    > **Tom:** Mooi. **Deze** weken gaan we vaak naar **de** museums, denk ik.

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** Look! These rooms are big. Those windows are beautiful too.
    >
    > **Tom:** Yes, and the chairs and tables stay from the owner.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Two chairs are enough. The cats are not in the room, you know.
    >
    > **Tom:** No cats here. And no dogs in the apartment.
    >
    > **Lisa:** In that case the garden is perfect. The trees are beautiful.
    >
    > **Tom:** How much do the lamps in that room cost?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Those lamps? They cost forty euros together.
    >
    > **Tom:** Fine. And the photos on the wall?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Those photos stay. The streets in the photos are in Amsterdam.
    >
    > **Tom:** Nice. These weeks we will go to the museums often, I think.
