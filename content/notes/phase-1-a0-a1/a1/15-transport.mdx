## Introduction

You can say **Ik ga naar de supermarkt** and **Ik kom uit Engeland**. Now you learn the full transport picture: **de trein**, **de metro**, **het station**, and **Hoe kom ik bij het museum?** After this lesson you can buy a ticket, find a platform, ask for directions, and explain how you travel to work or school.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Transport at a glance

| Dutch | English | Article |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de trein | train | de |
| de bus | bus | de |
| de metro | metro / subway | de |
| de tram | tram | de |
| de fiets | bicycle | de |
| de auto | car | de |
| het vliegtuig | aeroplane | het |
| de boot | boat | de |
| het openbaar vervoer | public transport | het |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ga **met de trein**. | I go by train. |
| Wij reizen **met de bus**. | We travel by bus. |
| Hij fietst **naar** het werk. | He cycles to work. |
| Zij gaat **te voet**. | She goes on foot. |

**Met** + transport means by: **met de metro**, **met de tram**, **met de fiets**.

### Stations, stops, and tickets

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| het station | station |
| de halte | stop (bus/tram) |
| het perron | platform |
| het spoor | track / platform |
| het kaartje | ticket |
| de OV-chipkaart | public transport card (NL) |
| de route | route |
| de dienstregeling | timetable / schedule |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Waar is **het station**? | Where is the station? |
| De bus stopt bij **de halte**. | The bus stops at the stop. |
| Het **kaartje** kost drie euro. | The ticket costs three euros. |
| Ik reis met **het openbaar vervoer**. | I travel by public transport. |

### gaan and komen in transport sentences

You know both verbs from [Lesson 12](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/12-more-present-verbs). They are essential for travel talk:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** naar het station. | I am going to the station. |
| **Ga** je met de metro? | Are you taking the metro? |
| De trein **komt** om acht uur. | The train comes at eight o'clock. |
| Wij **komen** aan om tien uur. | We arrive at ten o'clock. |
| **Kom** je ook **met de fiets**? | Are you coming by bike too? |

**Naar** + place = to / towards: **naar Amsterdam**, **naar het museum**, **naar de halte**.

### rijden (to drive / ride) — Tier A

**Rijden** covers driving a car, riding a bike, or being on a moving vehicle:

| Pronoun | rijden | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | rijd | I drive / ride |
| jij / je | rijdt | you drive / ride |
| u | rijdt | you drive (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | rijdt | he / she drives |
| wij / we | rijden | we drive / ride |
| jullie | rijden | you drive (plural) |
| zij / ze | rijden | they drive |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **rijd** met de auto. | I drive by car. |
| **Rijd** je met de fiets? | Do you ride a bike? |
| De bus **rijdt** naar het centrum. | The bus drives to the city centre. |
| Wij **rijden** naar Rotterdam. | We drive to Rotterdam. |
| Ik **rijd niet** in de file. | I do not drive in the traffic jam. |

### nemen, overstappen, and reizen — Tier B

| Pronoun | nemen | overstappen | reizen |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik | neem | stap over | reis |
| jij / je | neemt | stapt over | reist |
| hij / zij / het | neemt | stapt over | reist |
| wij / jullie / zij | nemen | stappen over | reizen |

| Verb | Example 1 | Example 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **nemen** | Ik **neem** de trein. <br /> _I take the train._ | **Neem** je de bus? <br /> _Do you take the bus?_ |
| **overstappen** | Wij **stappen over** in Utrecht. <br /> _We transfer in Utrecht._ | **Stap** je over op de metro? <br /> _Do you transfer to the metro?_ |
| **reizen** | Ik **reis** elke dag. <br /> _I travel every day._ | **Reis** je graag met de trein? <br /> _Do you like travelling by train?_ |

**Overstappen** is separable: **Ik stap over** / **Wij stappen over in Amsterdam**.

### Asking for directions

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Waar is …? | Where is …? |
| Hoe kom ik bij …? | How do I get to …? |
| Hoe ga ik naar …? | How do I go to …? |
| Is het ver? | Is it far? |
| Hoe ver is het? | How far is it? |
| Kun je me helpen? | Can you help me? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Waar is** het station? | Where is the station? |
| **Hoe kom ik bij** het museum? | How do I get to the museum? |
| **Hoe ga ik naar** de bibliotheek? | How do I go to the library? |
| Is het **dichtbij**? | Is it nearby? |

### Direction words

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| links | left |
| rechts | right |
| rechtdoor | straight ahead |
| hier | here |
| daar | there |
| terug | back |
| de hoek | corner |
| de brug | bridge |
| het plein | square |
| het centrum | city centre |
| de richting | direction |
| de bestemming | destination |
| tegenover | opposite / across from |
| dichtbij | nearby / close |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ga **rechtdoor**. | Go straight ahead. |
| Sla **links** af. | Turn left. |
| Sla **rechts** af. | Turn right. |
| Het station is **om de hoek**. | The station is around the corner. |
| De halte is **tegenover** het plein. | The stop is opposite the square. |

**Linksaf** and **rechtsaf** mean turn left and turn right. Very common in directions.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |

### Combining with time and word order

From [Lesson 2](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/02-word-order-svo):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Om acht uur** ga ik met de trein. | At eight o'clock I take the train. |
| **Vandaag** neem ik de bus. | Today I take the bus. |
| **Morgen** reis ik naar Den Haag. | Tomorrow I travel to The Hague. |
| De metro **komt** om kwart over drie. | The metro comes at quarter past three. |

### Object pronouns: me, je, hem, haar

When the person receiving the action is a pronoun, Dutch uses short forms:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **me** | me |
| **je** | you (informal) |
| **hem** | him / it (de word) |
| **haar** | her / it (de word) |
| **ons** | us |
| **jullie** | you (plural) |
| **hen** / **ze** | them |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Kun je **me** helpen? | Can you help me? |
| Ik zie **hem** op het perron. | I see him on the platform. |
| Geef **haar** het ticket. | Give her the ticket. |
| Bel **ons** als je klaar bent. | Call us when you are ready. |

The pronoun usually comes **before** the main verb in statements, or **right after** the verb in questions: **Help je me?**

### Imperative: giving directions

To give a command or direction, use the **verb stem** alone. No subject.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ga** rechtdoor. | Go straight ahead. |
| **Sla** links af. | Turn left. |
| **Neem** de eerste straat rechts. | Take the first street on the right. |
| **Wacht** bij het stoplicht. | Wait at the traffic light. |
| **Volg** de borden naar het station. | Follow the signs to the station. |

With **u**, add **t**: **Gaat u rechtdoor.** In informal speech, the stem alone is standard: **Kom mee!** · **Neem de bus!**

<Callout title="Grammar Focus" type="info">
  Object pronoun order and more imperative forms are covered in the Grammar Track as you advance. For A1, practise **me**, **hem**, and **haar** in transport and direction sentences.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  **Rijden**, **rechtdoor**, and **rechts** all have that Dutch **r**. **OV-chipkaart** is said **o-vie-chipkaart**. Say **het station**, **de halte**, **het perron** clearly. See [Consonant Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using in instead of met for transport</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik ga **in** de trein. (when meaning by train as method)

    ✅ Ik ga **met de trein**.

    Use **met** for the mode of travel. **In de trein** means physically inside the train (**Ik zit in de trein**).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting naar with destinations</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik ga **het station**.

    ✅ Ik ga **naar** het station.

    Movement towards a place needs **naar**: **naar huis**, **naar het museum**, **naar Amsterdam**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Translating How do I get as Hoe is ik</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Hoe is ik bij het museum?

    ✅ **Hoe kom ik bij** het museum? / **Hoe ga ik naar** het museum?

    Dutch uses **komen bij** or **gaan naar** for getting to a place.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Wrong separable verb order with overstappen</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik **overstap** in Utrecht.

    ✅ Ik **stap over** in Utrecht.

    **Over** goes to the end: **Wij stappen over op het station**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Onderweg naar het museum</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Lisa:** Excuseer, **hoe kom ik bij** het museum?
    >
    > **Tom:** **Het museum**? **Ga rechtdoor**. Dan **sla je linksaf** bij **de brug**.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Is het ver?
    >
    > **Tom:** Nee, **te voet** is het vijf minuten. Of **neem** de tram.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Waar is **de halte**?
    >
    > **Tom:** **Om de hoek**, **tegenover** **het plein**. **Neem** tram drie.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Heb ik een **kaartje** nodig?
    >
    > **Tom:** Ja. Of gebruik je **OV-chipkaart**. Die tram **komt** om kwart over twee.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Dank u wel! **Ik stap over** bij **het centrum**, toch?
    >
    > **Tom:** Nee, tram drie gaat **rechtstreeks** naar **het museum**. **Goede reis**!

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** Excuse me, how do I get to the museum?
    >
    > **Tom:** The museum? Go straight ahead. Then turn left at the bridge.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Is it far?
    >
    > **Tom:** No, on foot it is five minutes. Or take the tram.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Where is the stop?
    >
    > **Tom:** Around the corner, opposite the square. Take tram three.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Do I need a ticket?
    >
    > **Tom:** Yes. Or use your OV-chip card. That tram comes at quarter past two.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Thank you! I transfer at the city centre, right?
    >
    > **Tom:** No, tram three goes directly to the museum. Have a good trip!
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
