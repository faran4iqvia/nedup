## Introduction

In [A0 Lesson 10](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a0/10-ordering-food-and-drink) you ordered **een koffie**, **een broodje kaas**, and asked for **de rekening**. That was your first taste of Dutch food language. Now you go deeper: more ingredients, supermarket words, the full present tense of **eten** and **drinken**, and the **de** vs **het** pattern that runs through every Dutch noun. After this lesson you can talk about what you eat at home, what you buy at the supermarket, and whether you drink **de melk** or **het water**.

You know **ontbijt**, **lunch**, and **diner** from A0, plus **lekker**, **smakelijk**, and **graag**. ## Grammar and Language Explanation

### de and het: why articles matter

Every Dutch noun is either a **de** word or a **het** word. The article affects:

| What | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Saying _the_ | **de** appel vs **het** ei |
| Saying _a_ | **een** appel vs **een** ei (same **een**, but you must know which) |
| Possessive **our** | **onze** appels vs **ons** ei |
| Later: adjectives | **de** grote appel vs **het** grote ei |

There is no perfect rule for de vs het. Learn each food word **with** its article as one chunk: **de kaas**, not just _kaas_.

### eten and drinken: full present tense

You met these verbs in A0. Here is the complete pattern:

| Pronoun | eten | drinken |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | eet | drink |
| jij / je | eet | drinkt |
| u | eet | drinkt |
| hij / zij / het | eet | drinkt |
| wij / we | eten | drinken |
| jullie | eten | drinken |
| zij / ze | eten | drinken |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **eet** brood met kaas. | I eat bread with cheese. |
| Jij **drinkt** geen melk. | You do not drink milk. |
| Zij **eten** pasta vanavond. | They eat pasta tonight. |
| Wij **drinken** koffie na het eten. | We drink coffee after the meal. |

Questions follow verb second from [Lesson 2](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/02-word-order-svo):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Eet** je vlees? | Do you eat meat? |
| **Drink** je koffie? | Do you drink coffee? |
| Wat **eet** jij vanavond? | What are you eating tonight? |

### de boodschappen doen

**Boodschappen** means groceries or errands. **De boodschappen doen** means to do the grocery shopping.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik doe de boodschappen. | I do the grocery shopping. |
| Wij gaan naar de supermarkt. | We go to the supermarket. |
| Wat staat op het lijstje? | What is on the list? (preview) |
| Ik heb brood, melk en eieren nodig. | I need bread, milk, and eggs. |

### Articles in real sentences (possessives + negation)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ons** ontbijt is simpel. | Our breakfast is simple. |
| **Onze** boodschappen zijn duur. | Our groceries are expensive. |
| Ik drink **geen** melk. | I do not drink milk. |
| Ik eet **het** brood **niet**. | I do not eat the bread. |
| Heb je **geen** honger? | Are you not hungry? |

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping de or het</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik drink melk. (when you mean milk in general, still ok sometimes)

    ✅ Ik drink **melk**. / Ik drink **de** melk. / Ik drink **geen** melk.

    For learners, attach the article when learning: **de melk**, **het brood**. **Geen** replaces **een**, not **de/het**: **geen melk**, not **geen de melk**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">

    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using onze with het eten</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ onze eten

    ✅ **ons** eten

    **Het eten** is a **het** word: **ons eten**, **ons ontbijt**, **ons brood**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">

    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Confusing eten (verb) and het eten (noun)</CardTitle>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Het eten eet lekker.

    ✅ **Het eten** is lekker. / **Wij eten** pasta.

  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saturday morning at the supermarket</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Anna and Mark shop together with a list.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Anna:** Goedemorgen! Heb je **het lijstje**?
    >
    > **Mark:** Ja. **Brood**, **melk**, **eieren**, **appels** en **groente**.
    >
    > **Anna:** Oké. Jij pakt **het brood** en **de melk**. Ik pak **de groente**.
    >
    > **Mark:** Drink jij **geen** melk meer?
    >
    > **Anna:** Nee, alleen **het sap**. **Ons** ontbijt is nu **fruit** en **brood met jam**.
    >
    > **Mark:** Lekker! Ik koop ook **de hagelslag**. Typisch Nederlands, hoor.
    >
    > **Anna:** Haha, ja. Eet jij **vlees**?
    >
    > **Mark:** Ja, maar vanavond **eten** wij **vegetarisch**. **Pasta** met **tomaten** en **ui**.
    >
    > **Anna:** Prima. Hebben we **peper** en **zout** thuis?
    >
    > **Mark:** **Nee**, die koop ik ook. Kom, naar de kassa.

    **In English:**

    > **Anna:** Good morning! Do you have the list?
    >
    > **Mark:** Yes. Bread, milk, eggs, apples, and vegetables.
    >
    > **Anna:** Okay. You grab the bread and the milk. I will grab the vegetables.
    >
    > **Mark:** Do you not drink milk anymore?
    >
    > **Anna:** No, only juice. Our breakfast is now fruit and bread with jam.
    >
    > **Mark:** Nice! I will also buy the hagelslag. Typically Dutch, you know.
    >
    > **Anna:** Haha, yes. Do you eat meat?
    >
    > **Mark:** Yes, but tonight we eat vegetarian. Pasta with tomatoes and onion.
    >
    > **Anna:** Great. Do we have pepper and salt at home?
    >
    > **Mark:** No, I will buy those too. Come, let's go to the checkout.

    **Het lijstje**, **ons ontbijt**, **geen melk**, and article pairs **het brood / de melk / de groente** all appear naturally.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
