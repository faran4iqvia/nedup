## Introduction

You can say **Ik werk** and **Wij wonen in Utrecht** in the present. To talk about **gisteren**, **vorige week**, or **vroeger**, Dutch uses the **verleden tijd** (simple past). Regular verbs add **te** or **de**: **Ik werkte**, **Hij woonde**, **Wij maakten**. After this lesson you can tell a short story about what happened yesterday, last weekend, or long ago, using verbs you already know from [Lesson 12](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/12-more-present-verbs).

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Simple past at a glance

Regular Dutch verbs in the past use **one form for all singular persons** and **one form for all plural persons**. Unlike the present, **ik**, **jij**, and **hij** all share the same past form.

| Person | Ending | Example: werken | Example: wonen |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik / jij / u / hij / zij / het | **te** or **de** | werk**te** | woon**de** |
| wij / jullie / zij | **ten** or **den** | werk**ten** | woon**den** |

**Three rules to hold onto:**

1. Find the **stem** (same as present: remove **en** from the infinitive → **werken** → **werk**).
2. Add **te/ten** or **de/den** using the **'t ex kofschip** rule below.
3. **Singular** = **te** or **de**. **Plural** = **ten** or **den**.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werkte** gisteren. | I worked yesterday. |
| **Werkte** je laat? | Did you work late? |
| Hij **woonde** in Amsterdam. | He lived in Amsterdam. |
| Wij **woonden** in een klein huis. | We lived in a small house. |
| Zij **maakten** soep. | They made soup. |

</Callout>

### te or de? The 't ex kofschip rule

Look at the **last letter of the stem**:

| Stem ends in… | Add | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **t, k, f, s, ch, p** (voiceless) | **te / ten** | werk → werk**te**, maak → maak**te**, gebruik → gebruik**te** |
| **anything else** (vowels, l, n, m, r…) | **de / den** | woon → woon**de**, luister → luister**de**, antwoord → antwoord**de** |

Remember **'t ex kofschip** from spelling lessons: the voiceless consonants take **te**.

| Infinitive | Stem | Past singular | Past plural |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| werken | werk | werkte | werkten |
| maken | maak | maakte | maakten |
| wonen | woon | woonde | woonden |
| luisteren | luister | luisterde | luisterden |
| wachten | wacht | wachtte | wachtten |
| gebruiken | gebruik | gebruikte | gebruikten |

**Spelling note:** when the stem already ends in **t** or **d**, Dutch doubles it: **wacht** → **wachtte**, **antwoord** → **antwoordde**.

### Past tense questions and negation

Questions flip word order, just like the present. **Niet** stays in the same position as you learned in [Lesson 3](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/03-negation):

| Statement | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Je **werkte** gisteren. | **Werkte** je gisteren? |
| Hij **woonde** in Rotterdam. | **Woonde** hij in Rotterdam? |
| Wij **maakten** pasta. | **Maakten** jullie pasta? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Werkte** je **gisteren**? | Did you work yesterday? |
| Ik **werkte gisteren niet**. | I did not work yesterday. |
| Wij **woonden niet** in Utrecht. | We did not live in Utrecht. |
| **Luisterde** je naar muziek? | Did you listen to music? |

There is **no t drop** in the past for **je** questions. The past ending is **te** or **de**, not a separate **t** on the stem: **Werkte** je?, not **Werk** je? (present).

### Time expressions with the past

Use these time words with past verbs to talk about when something happened:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| gisteren | yesterday |
| gisterenavond | yesterday evening |
| gisterochtend | yesterday morning |
| eergisteren | the day before yesterday |
| vorige week | last week |
| vorig jaar | last year |
| afgelopen week | this past week |
| onlangs | recently |
| vroeger | in the past / used to |
| lang geleden | long ago |
| toen | when / then (past context) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Gisteren werkte** ik thuis. | Yesterday I worked at home. |
| **Vorige week woonden** wij in een hotel. | Last week we stayed in a hotel. |
| **Toen** ik jong **was**, **woonde** ik in Engeland. | When I was young, I lived in England. |
| **Vroeger maakte** mijn moeder altijd soep. | In the past my mother always made soup. |

**Toen ik jong was** uses **was** from **zijn**. That is irregular; you learn full irregular past in [Lesson 19](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/19-simple-past-irregular). For now, learn **toen** + past as a fixed storytelling pattern.

### Present vs past: when to use which

| Situation | Tense | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Now, habit, general truth | Present | Ik **werk** in Utrecht. |
| Finished time in the past | Past | **Gisteren werkte** ik thuis. |
| **Gisteren**, **vorige week**, **toen** | Past | **Vorig jaar reisden** wij naar België. |
| **Vandaag**, **nu**, **elke dag** | Present | **Nu woon** ik in Den Haag. |

Spoken Dutch often uses the perfect tense (**Ik heb gewerkt**) instead of **Ik werkte**. Both mean _I worked_. At A1, **werkte** is clear and common in stories and writing. You learn the perfect tense in A2.

#### werken (to work) — past: werkte

You know **werken** from [Lesson 1](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/01-daily-routines). Stem **werk** ends in **k** → **te**:

| Pronoun | werken (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | werkte | I worked |
| jij / je | werkte | you worked |
| u | werkte | you worked (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | werkte | he / she worked |
| wij / we | werkten | we worked |
| jullie | werkten | you worked (plural) |
| zij / ze | werkten | they worked |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werkte** gisteren thuis. | I worked at home yesterday. |
| **Werkte** je **gisterenavond**? | Did you work yesterday evening? |
| Hij **werkte** tot laat. | He worked until late. |
| Wij **werkten** samen aan het project. | We worked together on the project. |
| Ik **werkte gisteren niet**. | I did not work yesterday. |

#### wonen (to live) — past: woonde

From [Lesson 13](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/13-housing). Stem **woon** ends in **n** → **de**:

| Pronoun | wonen (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | woonde | I lived |
| jij / je | woonde | you lived |
| u | woonde | you lived (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | woonde | he / she lived |
| wij / we | woonden | we lived |
| jullie | woonden | you lived (plural) |
| zij / ze | woonden | they lived |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **woonde** in Rotterdam. | I lived in Rotterdam. |
| **Woonde** je **vroeger** in Amsterdam? | Did you used to live in Amsterdam? |
| Zij **woonde** in een klein appartement. | She lived in a small flat. |
| Wij **woonden** **vorig jaar** bij mijn ouders. | We lived with my parents last year. |
| Wij **woonden niet** in Nederland. | We did not live in the Netherlands. |

#### maken (to make) — past: maakte

From [Lesson 12](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1/12-more-present-verbs). Stem **maak** ends in **k** → **te**:

| Pronoun | maken (past) | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | maakte | I made |
| jij / je | maakte | you made |
| u | maakte | you made (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | maakte | he / she made |
| wij / we | maakten | we made |
| jullie | maakten | you made (plural) |
| zij / ze | maakten | they made |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **maakte** soep. | I made soup. |
| **Maakte** je koffie **vanochtend**? | Did you make coffee this morning? |
| Hij **maakte** een fout. | He made a mistake. |
| Wij **maakten** pasta **gisterenavond**. | We made pasta yesterday evening. |
| Zij **maakten geen** ruzie. | They did not argue. (literally: make no quarrel) |

### Tier B: high frequency regular past (grouped table)

These verbs follow the same **te/de** rules. Learn the Tier A set first, then add these.

| Pronoun | luisteren | wachten | gebruiken | leren | antwoorden |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik / jij / hij | luisterde | wachtte | gebruikte | leerde | antwoordde |
| wij / jullie / zij | luisterden | wachtten | gebruikten | leerden | antwoordden |

| Verb | Example 1 | Example 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **luisteren** | Ik **luisterde** naar muziek. <br /> _I listened to music._ | **Luisterde** je naar de docent? <br /> _Did you listen to the teacher?_ |
| **wachten** | Wij **wachtten** op de bus. <br /> _We waited for the bus._ | **Wachtte** je lang? <br /> _Did you wait long?_ |
| **gebruiken** | Hij **gebruikte** de computer. <br /> _He used the computer._ | **Gebruikte** je een kaartje? <br /> _Did you use a ticket?_ |
| **leren** | Zij **leerde** Nederlands. <br /> _She learned Dutch._ | **Leerden** jullie veel? <br /> _Did you learn a lot?_ |
| **antwoorden** | Ik **antwoordde** op de vraag. <br /> _I answered the question._ | **Antwoordde** hij snel? <br /> _Did he answer quickly?_ |

### Preview: irregular past next

**Zijn**, **hebben**, **gaan**, **komen**, **doen**, and **eten** do **not** follow **te/de**. Examples you will learn next:

| Present | Past | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik ben | ik **was** | I was |
| ik heb | ik **had** | I had |
| ik ga | ik **ging** | I went |
| ik kom | ik **kwam** | I came |
| ik doe | ik **deed** | I did |
| ik eet | ik **at** | I ate |

For now, stick to regular **te/de** verbs. Mix in **was** only in fixed phrases like **Toen ik jong was**.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  In **werkte**, **maakte**, and **woonde**, stress stays on the stem: **WERK-te**, **MAAK-te**, **WOON-de**. The plural **en** is a schwa: **werk-ten**. Say **ik werkte gisteren** in one smooth rhythm. See [Sentence Intonation](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm).
</Callout>

    >
    > **Tom:** Nice! Yesterday morning I walked in the park. After that I made lunch.
    >

    >
    > **Tom:** Yesterday evening I listened to music. This morning I worked early again.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Did you work a lot last week too?

    >

    >
