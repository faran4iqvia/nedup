## Introduction

You have completed all eleven A0 lessons, from greetings to telling time on a Dutch clock. This chapter brings everything together: a topic by topic summary, a combined vocabulary reference, a mixed test, and a clear picture of what you can do at A0 level. Take your time, say answers aloud, and treat mistakes as signposts for which lessons to revisit.

## Summary by Topic

### Greetings and introductions

### Numbers, money, and time

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 3, 7, and 11</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    You count from **nul** to **honderd**, form compound numbers (**vijfentwintig**), ask prices with **Hoeveel kost het?**, and tell time with the Dutch **half** rule (**half drie** = 2:30). You know **kwart over**, **kwart voor**, **om** + time, and **van ... tot ...**.

    **Key patterns:** Ik ben twintig jaar. · Het kost vijf euro. · Het is half zeven. · Om kwart over acht.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Core verbs: zijn and hebben

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 4 and 5</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **Zijn:** ik ben, jij bent, hij is, wij zijn. **Hebben:** ik heb, jij hebt, hij heeft, wij hebben. Age uses **zijn**; hunger and cold use **hebben** (**ik heb honger**, **ik heb het koud**). Questions flip word order; **t** drops before **jij** but not before **u**.

    **Key patterns:** Hoe oud ben je? · Ik heb een fiets. · Woon jij hier?
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Questions, calendar, and place

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 6, 8, and 9</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Question words: **wie, wat, waar, wanneer, hoe, waarom**. Word order: **Waar woon je?** Days and months are lowercase: **op vrijdag**, **in mei**. **Wonen** + **in** for where you live; **komen uit** for origin.

    **Key patterns:** Waar kom je vandaan? · Op zaterdag, twaalf mei. · Ik woon in Utrecht.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

### Food, drink, and daily life

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lessons 10 and 11</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    You order with **Ik wil graag ...** or **Een koffie, alstublieft**, ask for **de rekening**, and name common food and drinks. You tell time for meals and appointments and know **op tijd**, **te laat**, and **te vroeg**.

    **Key patterns:** Mag ik de menukaart? · Het diner is om zeven uur. · Kom op tijd!
  </CardContent>

## What You Can Do at A0 (CEFR)

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Can do statements</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    At A0 level, you can:

    - **Understand and use** familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases aimed at meeting concrete needs
    - **Introduce yourself** and others, ask and answer questions about personal details such as name, age, and where you live
    - **Interact** in a simple way provided the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help
    - **Recognise** familiar words and very basic signs, menus, prices, and timetables
    - **Fill in** simple forms with personal details such as name, nationality, and address

    **You are ready for A1 when** you can pass most of this test (roughly 80% or more) and hold a slow, simple conversation introducing yourself, ordering food, and making a basic appointment. If several sections felt hard, revisit those lessons before starting A1.

    **Next step:** [Phase 1 A1 lessons](/notes/phase-1-a0-a1/a1) begin with daily routines and expand everything you built here.
