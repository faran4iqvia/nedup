## Introduction

Ten lessons, six verbs, 200 words. Before you cross into A1, this chapter pulls everything together: a topic by topic summary of what you learned, your complete A0 vocabulary in one place, and a 30 question test with a real reading passage and a real translation. Take the test without looking anything up first, then use your mistakes as a map: every question links back to a lesson you can revisit. There is no passing grade posted on the wall here, but if you get about 24 of 30 right, you are more than ready for A1.

Three threads ran through the whole level. First, **the disappearing t**: any verb directly before jij or je loses its t, but never before u. Second, **the register line**: jij, doei, alsjeblieft and dank je wel with friends; u, tot ziens, alstublieft and dank u wel with strangers and officials. Third, **the verb shape**: one singular person gets a short form, every group gets the infinitive. Carry these three into A1 and half the level is already familiar.

You also collected the great Dutch particles along the way: **nou**, **hoor**, **toch** and **dan**. You are not required to produce them yet, but you should hear them now in every real conversation around you.

## Answer Key

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Check your answers</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    1. **ben, ben.** Ik ben Anna en ik ben tweeëntwintig jaar.
    2. **heeft, hebben.** Hij takes heeft, the group wij takes hebben.
    3. **Spreek.** The t drops before jij.
    4. **wil.** The modal rebel: zij wil, no t. From Lesson 10.
    5. **Bent.** With u the t always stays: Bent u meneer De Vries?
    6. **komt, woont.** Stem plus t for Emma and zij.
    7. **op, in.** Op dinsdag, in september.
    8. **Heb jij een pen?** Verb first, t gone.
    9. **Waar woont zij?** Question word, verb, subject.
    10. **Hoe oud bent u?** is correct. The disappearing t applies only to jij and je, never to u.
    11. **veertien, achtendertig, zesenzeventig.**
    12. **sleutel.** The others are drinks.
    13. **Please** when asking, and **here you are** when handing something over.
    14. **dank je wel** (informal) and **dank u wel** (formal).
    15. **donderdag** comes after woensdag, **juli** comes after juni.
    16. **Gefeliciteerd!**
    17. **expensive, cheap, tasty, tired.**
    18. **huis.** The letters h, u, i, s, using the letter names from Lesson 2.
    19. Emma is **vijfentwintig**, twenty five years old.
    20. She comes from **Engeland** and lives in **Amsterdam** now.
    21. **Engels en een beetje Nederlands**, English and a little Dutch.
    22. The cheese costs **six euros**, and Emma thinks that is **cheap**.
    23. Her birthday is on **dertig mei**, the thirtieth of May.
    24. The party is **thuis**, at home.
    25. **Goedemorgen! Mag ik een thee, alstublieft?**
    26. **Hij heeft een nieuwe telefoon, maar het is duur.** (De telefoon is duur is also fine.)
    27. **Waar kom jij vandaan? Ik kom uit Amerika.** (Waar komt u vandaan is equally correct.)
    28. **Wij zijn moe en wij willen de rekening.** (Wij willen graag de rekening is even better manners.)
    29. **Mijn verjaardag is op eenentwintig maart.**
    30. Good afternoon! I am Mr De Vries. I come from Belgium, but I live in Utrecht now. I am sixty eight years old. I have a house, a car and two bikes. On Tuesday and Thursday I am at home. The coffee here is tasty and cheap: one euro fifty. May I have a coffee? Thank you! Goodbye!
