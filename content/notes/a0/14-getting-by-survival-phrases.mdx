## Introduction

Every beginner reaches the same moment: a Dutch person says something, it is too fast, and you freeze. The difference between a conversation that dies and one that keeps going is a handful of **survival phrases**. This lesson gives you the exact sentences to ask someone to repeat, to slow down, to explain a word, or to switch to English — politely and without panic. These are the most-used sentences in your first months in the Netherlands, so learn them until they come out automatically.

## Quick Review

You have already met a few of these. In [Lesson 2](/notes/a0/02-alphabet-and-spelling) you learned **Ik begrijp het niet** (I do not understand), **Hoe spel je dat?** (How do you spell that?), and **Langzaam, alsjeblieft** (Slowly, please). In [Lesson 13](/notes/a0/13-nationalities-and-languages) you learned **Spreek jij Engels?** and **Ik spreek …**. This lesson adds the formal **Spreekt u …?** pattern and brings everything together into a complete toolkit.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The dummy word het

In English you can just say "I don't understand." In Dutch you almost always need the little word **het** (it) as an object:

| English | Dutch |
| --- | --- |
| I don't understand. | Ik begrijp **het** niet. |
| I don't get it. | Ik snap **het** niet. |
| I don't know. | Ik weet **het** niet. |

Leaving out **het** sounds unfinished to a Dutch ear, so keep it in.

### kunnen: kunt u versus kun je

To ask politely, use the verb **kunnen** (can). You saw its cousin **mag ik** in [Lesson 10](/notes/a0/10-ordering-food-and-drink). For now, learn just two forms:

| Form | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Kunt u …?** | formal, with strangers and officials | Kunt u dat herhalen? |
| **Kun je …?** | informal, with friends | Kun je dat herhalen? |

Both mean "Can you …?" The verb at the end stays in its full form: **herhalen**, **praten**, **opschrijven**.

### When you do not understand

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik begrijp het niet. | I do not understand. |
| Ik snap het niet. | I don't get it. |
| Sorry? / Pardon? | Sorry? / Pardon? |
| Wat zei u? | What did you say? (formal) |
| Ik versta u niet. | I can't make out what you are saying. |

### Asking someone to repeat or slow down

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Kunt u dat herhalen? | Can you repeat that? |
| Nog een keer, alstublieft. | One more time, please. |
| Kunt u langzamer praten? | Can you speak more slowly? |
| Niet zo snel, alstublieft. | Not so fast, please. |
| Langzaam, alstublieft. | Slowly, please. |

### Asking about a word

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wat betekent dat? | What does that mean? |
| Wat betekent "gezellig"? | What does "gezellig" mean? |
| Hoe zeg je … in het Nederlands? | How do you say … in Dutch? |
| Hoe schrijf je dat? | How do you write that? |
| Kunt u dat opschrijven? | Can you write that down? |

### Buying time and switching languages

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Een momentje, alstublieft. | One moment, please. |
| Even denken … | Let me think … |
| Spreekt u Engels? | Do you speak English? |
| Ik spreek nog niet zo goed Nederlands. | I don't speak Dutch very well yet. |
| Ik leer Nederlands. | I am learning Dutch. |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  **begrijpen**, **snappen**, **herhalen**, and **betekenen** are all regular verbs. The polite **Kunt u …?** is the formal form of **kunnen**, a modal verb you will meet fully in A1. For now, treat **Kunt u …?** and **Kun je …?** as fixed openers. See [Question Formation](/notes/grammar/05-question-formation) for how these questions are built.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Alstublieft** is a mouthful — say it as three quick beats: **als-tu-blieft**. **Herhalen** has the soft Dutch **h** at the start of both halves. Practise **Ik begrijp het niet** until it is one smooth phrase, because you will need it fast and often.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping het after begrijp</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik begrijp niet.

    ✅ Ik begrijp **het** niet.

    English drops the object, Dutch does not. The same goes for **Ik weet het niet** and **Ik snap het niet**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying Kunt jij or Kan u</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Kunt jij dat herhalen? · ❌ Kan u dat herhalen?

    ✅ **Kun** jij dat herhalen? (informal) · ✅ **Kunt u** dat herhalen? (formal)

    Pair the forms correctly: **kunt** goes with **u**, **kun** goes with **jij**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Barking a bare command</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Herhaal! (sounds like an order)

    ✅ Kunt u dat herhalen? / Nog een keer, alstublieft.

    A question with **kunt u** plus **alstublieft** is polite. A bare imperative to a stranger sounds rude in Dutch.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using langzaam when you mean slower</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Kunt u langzaam praten? (grammatically fine, but "slow", not "slower")

    ✅ Kunt u **langzamer** praten?

    **Langzamer** means _more slowly_ — that is what you actually want when someone is already talking fast. **Langzaam, alstublieft** on its own is also fine as a quick request.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Learning Tips

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Memorise three, not thirty</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    You do not need every phrase at once. Drill just three until they are automatic: **Ik begrijp het niet**, **Kunt u dat herhalen?**, and **Kunt u langzamer praten?**. Those three keep almost any conversation alive.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Keep them on your phone</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Save your survival phrases as a note on your phone. Before a phone call, an appointment, or a visit to the **gemeente**, read them once. Knowing you have the words removes half the fear.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">At the bakery, lost for a second</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>A customer keeps the conversation alive without switching fully to English.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Bakker:** Goedemorgen! Wilt u er ook nog een tompoucetje bij?
    >
    > **Klant:** Sorry, ik begrijp het niet. Kunt u dat herhalen?
    >
    > **Bakker:** Natuurlijk. Wilt u er iets zoets bij?
    >
    > **Klant:** Kunt u langzamer praten, alstublieft? Ik leer Nederlands.
    >
    > **Bakker:** Geen probleem. Wilt … u … koekje … erbij?
    >
    > **Klant:** Ah, een koekje! Wat betekent "erbij"?
    >
    > **Bakker:** "Erbij" betekent: samen, extra.
    >
    > **Klant:** Nu snap ik het. Ja, graag, een koekje erbij!
    >
    > **Bakker:** Prima. Alstublieft, en een fijne dag!

    **In English:**

    > **Baker:** Good morning! Would you like a little pastry with it too?
    >
    > **Customer:** Sorry, I don't understand. Can you repeat that?
    >
    > **Baker:** Of course. Would you like something sweet with it?
    >
    > **Customer:** Can you speak more slowly, please? I'm learning Dutch.
    >
    > **Baker:** No problem. Would … you … like … a biscuit … with it?
    >
    > **Customer:** Ah, a biscuit! What does "erbij" mean?
    >
    > **Baker:** "Erbij" means: together, extra.
    >
    > **Customer:** Now I get it. Yes please, a biscuit with it!
    >
    > **Baker:** Great. Here you go, and have a nice day!

    Notice how the customer never gives up: **Kunt u dat herhalen?**, **Kunt u langzamer praten?**, and **Wat betekent …?** turn a confusing moment into a successful order.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
