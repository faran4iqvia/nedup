## Introduction

You can count to honderd, ask **wanneer**, and use **vandaag** and **morgen**. Now you learn the calendar: days of the week, months, seasons, and how Dutch people say the date. This is how you make plans, understand appointments, and answer when something happens beyond just "today" or "tomorrow."

## Quick Review

In [Lesson 6](/notes/a0/06-basic-questions) you learned **wanneer**, **vandaag**, **morgen**, **gisteren**, and **vanavond**. In [Lesson 7](/notes/a0/07-numbers-21-100-and-money) you practised compound numbers like **drieëndertig**, which you need for dates and years. Today those pieces come together on the calendar.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Days of the week

Dutch days are not capitalised unless they start a sentence. The week starts on **maandag** (Monday) in Dutch planning culture, not Sunday.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| maandag | Monday |
| dinsdag | Tuesday |
| woensdag | Wednesday |
| donderdag | Thursday |
| vrijdag | Friday |
| zaterdag | Saturday |
| zondag | Sunday |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het is maandag vandaag. | It is Monday today. |
| Tot vrijdag! | See you Friday! |
| Op zaterdag heb ik tijd. | On Saturday I have time. |

Use **op** before a day when something happens on that day: **op maandag**, **op vrijdag**.

### Months of the year

Months are also written lowercase in Dutch.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| januari | January |
| februari | February |
| maart | March |
| april | April |
| mei | May |
| juni | June |
| juli | July |
| augustus | August |
| september | September |
| oktober | October |
| november | November |
| december | December |

Watch out: **maart** (March) looks like **maandag** (Monday). They are different words.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Mijn verjaardag is in mei. | My birthday is in May. |
| In december is het koud. | In December it is cold. |
| De cursus begint in september. | The course starts in September. |

Use **in** before a month: **in januari**, **in juli**.

### Seasons

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de lente | spring |
| de zomer | summer |
| de herfst | autumn |
| de winter | winter |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| In de zomer is het warm. | In summer it is warm. |
| Ik hou van de lente. | I love spring. |

### Saying the date

Dutch usually says the date as: **day + month**, without "of" in simple speech.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het is vijf maart. | It is the fifth of March. |
| Vandaag is het twaalf oktober. | Today is the twelfth of October. |
| Mijn verjaardag is drie juli. | My birthday is the third of July. |

For emphasis or formal speech, Dutch also uses **de + ordinal + maand**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de vijfde van maart | the fifth of March |
| de eerste van januari | the first of January |

Ordinal endings at A0 level: add **e** to the number for most dates: **eerste** (1st), **tweede** (2nd), **derde** (3rd), then **vierde**, **vijfde**, etc.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Wanneer ben je jarig? | When is your birthday? |
| Op de achttiende van april. | On the eighteenth of April. |
| Welke dag is het vandaag? | What day is it today? |
| Het is woensdag, vijf juni. | It is Wednesday, the fifth of June. |

### Years

Years use the compound number pattern from Lesson 7:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| tweeduizend vierentwintig | 2024 |
| negentiennegentig | 1999 |
| Ik ben geboren in negentienachtennegentig. | I was born in 1998. |

You will learn **geboren** (born) properly later. For now, recognise it in dates of birth.

### Asking about days and dates

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Welke dag is het vandaag? | What day is it today? |
| Welke datum is het? | What is the date? |
| Wanneer is de les? | When is the lesson? |
| Op welke dag? | On which day? |
| In welke maand? | In which month? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De les is op donderdag. | The lesson is on Thursday. |
| Het feest is in augustus. | The party is in August. |
| Wanneer ben je jarig? | When is your birthday? |
| Op zaterdag, twaalf mei. | On Saturday, the twelfth of May. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Woensdag** and **donderdag** have that Dutch **w** sound (closer to English v). **Augustus** stresses the second syllable: au**gus**tus. Say the days of the week in order every morning this week until they feel automatic. See [Consonant Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds) for **w** and [Word Stress](/notes/pronunciation/03-word-stress) for month names.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Capitalising days and months</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Maandag, Januari

    ✅ maandag, januari

    English capitalises days and months. Dutch does not, unless they start a sentence.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using English month day order</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Het is maart vijf. (mirroring March fifth)

    ✅ Het is vijf maart.

    Dutch puts the day first, then the month: **vijf maart**, not maart vijf.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Confusing maart and maandag</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ De les is op maart. (trying to say Monday)

    ✅ De les is op maandag.

    **Maart** is March (the month). **Maandag** is Monday (the day). Easy to mix up because both start with "maan."
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting op before days</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik heb tijd vrijdag.

    ✅ Ik heb tijd op vrijdag.

    When something happens on a specific day, Dutch usually needs **op**: **op vrijdag**, **op zaterdag**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Learning Tips

  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Start every morning with de dag</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>

  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Link months to seasons</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Group months in your head: **december, januari, februari** = winter. **juni, juli, augustus** = zomer. When you know the season, the month order sticks more naturally.
  </CardContent>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Planning a birthday dinner</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Tom and Anna check dates and days.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Tom:** Hoi Anna! Wanneer ben je jarig?
    >
    > **Anna:** Op zaterdag, twaalf mei. En jij?
    >
    > **Tom:** Mijn verjaardag is in maart. Op de achttiende van maart.
    >
    > **Anna:** Leuk! Wanneer is het feest?
    >
    > **Tom:** Op zaterdag, twintig maart. Om zeven uur.
    >
    > **Anna:** Zaterdag, twintig maart. Prima! Welke dag is het vandaag?
    >
    > **Tom:** Vandaag is het vrijdag, vijf maart.
    >
    > **Anna:** Dan is het feest over twee weken. Ik heb tijd op zaterdag!
    >
    > **Tom:** Super. Tot zaterdag dan?
    >
    > **Anna:** Ja! Tot zaterdag, twintig maart!

    **In English:**

    > **Tom:** Hi Anna! When is your birthday?
    >
    > **Anna:** On Saturday, the twelfth of May. And you?
    >
    > **Tom:** My birthday is in March. On the eighteenth of March.
    >
    > **Anna:** Nice! When is the party?
    >
    > **Tom:** On Saturday, the twentieth of March. At seven o'clock.
    >
    > **Anna:** Saturday the twentieth of March. Great! What day is it today?
    >
    > **Tom:** Today is Friday, the fifth of March.
    >
    > **Anna:** So the party is in two weeks. I have time on Saturday!
    >
    > **Tom:** Great. See you Saturday then?
    >
    > **Anna:** Yes! See you Saturday, the twentieth of March!

    **Over twee weken** means in two weeks. **Om zeven uur** means at seven o'clock. You will learn telling time fully in [Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
