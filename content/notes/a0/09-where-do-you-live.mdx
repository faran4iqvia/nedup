## Introduction

Someone has asked your name, your age, and maybe your birthday. The next question in a real Dutch conversation is almost always about where you live or where you come from. This lesson teaches the verb **wonen** (to live), the phrase **ik kom uit** (I come from), and the place words you need to describe your home, your city, and your country.

## Quick Review

In [Lesson 6](/notes/a0/06-basic-questions) you learned **waar**, **hier**, **daar**, and question words like **waarom**. Today you add **stad**, **land**, and **adres**. In [Lesson 8](/notes/a0/08-days-months-and-dates) you used **op** and **in** for days and months: **op vrijdag**, **in mei**. Today **in** returns for cities and countries: **in Amsterdam**, **in Nederland**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The verb wonen (to live)

| Pronoun | Form | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | woon | I live |
| jij / je | woont | you live |
| u | woont | you live (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | woont | he / she / it lives |
| wij / we | wonen | we live |
| jullie | wonen | you live (plural) |
| zij / ze | wonen | they live |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik woon in Utrecht. | I live in Utrecht. |
| Waar woon je? | Where do you live? |
| Wij wonen in Nederland. | We live in the Netherlands. |
| Zij woont in België. | She lives in Belgium. |

Questions follow the same pattern as **zijn** and **hebben**:

| Statement | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Jij woont in Amsterdam. | Woon jij in Amsterdam? |
| Hij woont hier. | Woont hij hier? |
| U woont in Rotterdam. | Woont u in Rotterdam? |

The **t** drops with **jij**: **Woon jij**, not Woont jij. With **u**, keep the **t**: **Woont u**.

### in + city or country

Use **in** when you live inside a city or country:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik woon in Amsterdam. | I live in Amsterdam. |
| Ik woon in Londen. | I live in London. |
| Ik woon in Nederland. | I live in the Netherlands. |
| Ik woon in Engeland. | I live in England. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Waar woon je? | Where do you live? |
| Ik woon in Den Haag. | I live in The Hague. |
| Woon jij ook hier? | Do you live here too? |
| Nee, ik woon in Brussel. | No, I live in Brussels. |

### ik kom uit (I come from)

**Wonen** says where you live now. **Komen uit** says where you are originally from:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik kom uit Engeland. | I come from England. / I am from England. |
| Waar kom je vandaan? | Where are you from? |
| Ik kom uit India, maar ik woon in Amsterdam. | I come from India, but I live in Amsterdam. |
| Kom jij uit Nederland? | Are you from the Netherlands? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Waar kom je vandaan? | Where do you come from? |
| Ik kom uit Schotland. | I come from Scotland. |
| Hij komt uit Turkije. | He comes from Turkey. |

**Komt** is the hij/zij form of **komen** (to come). You will learn the full verb later.

### Address and home words

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| het huis | house / home |
| het appartement | apartment |
| de flat | flat / apartment |
| de straat | street |
| het huisnummer | house number |
| de postcode | postal code |
| de buurt | neighbourhood |
| de stad | city |
| het dorp | village |
| het land | country |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik woon in een flat. | I live in a flat. |
| Mijn straat heet Kerkstraat. | My street is called Church Street. |
| Het huisnummer is twaalf. | The house number is twelve. |
| Het is een leuke buurt. | It is a nice neighbourhood. |

### Useful country and city names

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Nederland | the Netherlands |
| België | Belgium |
| Duitsland | Germany |
| Frankrijk | France |
| Engeland | England |
| Amsterdam | Amsterdam |
| Rotterdam | Rotterdam |

| Den Haag | The Hague |

<Callout title="Grammar Focus">
  **wonen** and **komen** follow the same present-tense rules as **zijn** and **hebben**: **t** drops before **jij** (**Woon jij**), stays with **u** (**Woont u**). For the full conjugation pattern and more regular verbs, see [Present Tense: Regular Verbs](/notes/grammar/04-present-tense-regular) in the Grammar Track.
</Callout>

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Nederland** has that Dutch **d** and **r** together. **België** ends with a soft **ë** sound. City names like **Utrecht** and **Groningen** appear constantly on Dutch trains. Listen for them and repeat.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using zijn instead of wonen for where you live</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik ben in Amsterdam. (when meaning "I live in Amsterdam")

    ✅ Ik woon in Amsterdam.

    **Ik ben in Amsterdam** means "I am in Amsterdam" right now. For your home city, use **woon**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using naar instead of in</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik woon naar Rotterdam.

    ✅ Ik woon in Rotterdam.

    **Naar** means towards or to (movement). **In** means inside or in (location). You live **in** a city, not **to** a city.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Mixing up woon and kom uit</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik woon uit Engeland.

    ✅ Ik kom uit Engeland.

    **Wonen** pairs with **in** for place of residence. **Komen uit** is the fixed phrase for origin.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying Woont jij in questions</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Woont jij in Utrecht?

    ✅ Woon jij in Utrecht?

    The **t** drops before **jij**, just like **Ben jij** and **Heb jij**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">New neighbours meet</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Two people chat in the stairwell of their building.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Sanne:** Hallo! Ik ben Sanne. Woon jij hier ook?
    >
    > **Raj:** Hoi Sanne! Ja, ik woon hier. Ik ben Raj. Aangenaam!
    >
    > **Sanne:** Aangenaam. Waar kom je vandaan?
    >
    > **Raj:** Ik kom uit India, maar ik woon al drie jaar in Nederland. En jij?
    >
    > **Sanne:** Ik kom uit België. Ik woon nu in Rotterdam, in deze flat.
    >
    > **Raj:** Leuk! Welke straat woonde je in België?
    >
    > **Sanne:** In Brussel, in de Leopoldstraat. En jij? Waar woon je precies?
    >
    > **Raj:** Op de derde verdieping, appartement twaalf. Een lekkere buurt, hoor!
    >
    > **Sanne:** Ja, ik vind het hier ook fijn. Nou, tot ziens!
    >
    > **Raj:** Tot ziens, Sanne!

    **In English:**

    > **Sanne:** Hello! I am Sanne. Do you live here too?
    >
    > **Raj:** Hi Sanne! Yes, I live here. I am Raj. Nice to meet you!
    >
    > **Sanne:** Nice to meet you. Where are you from?
    >
    > **Raj:** I come from India, but I have lived in the Netherlands for three years already. And you?
    >
    > **Sanne:** I come from Belgium. I now live in Rotterdam, in this flat.
    >
    > **Raj:** Nice! Which street did you live on in Belgium?
    >
    > **Sanne:** In Brussels, on Leopold Street. And you? Where exactly do you live?
    >
    > **Raj:** On the third floor, apartment twelve. A nice neighbourhood, really!
    >
    > **Sanne:** Yes, I like it here too. Well, goodbye!
    >
    > **Raj:** Goodbye, Sanne!

    **Verdieping** means floor. **Precies** means exactly. **Vind** comes from **vinden** (to find/think). **Fijn** means fine or pleasant.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
