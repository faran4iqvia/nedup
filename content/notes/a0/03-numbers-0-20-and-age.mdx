## Introduction

You can greet someone, say your name, and spell it. The next question Dutch people almost always ask is your age, or they need a number for something simple: how many coffees, how many people, which floor. This lesson gives you every number from **nul** to **twintig**, the question **Hoeveel?** (how many), and **Hoe oud ben je?** (how old are you?). Numbers are some of the most useful words in any language, and in Dutch they follow clear patterns once you pass ten.

## Quick Review

In [Lesson 1](/notes/a0/01-greetings-and-introductions) you learned **ik ben**, **ik heet**, and **Hoe heet je?**, including the question pattern where the verb comes first: **Ben jij Tom?** In [Lesson 2](/notes/a0/02-alphabet-and-spelling) you met **naam**, **spellen**, and **Hoe spel je dat?** Today you reuse **ik ben** and the same verb first question shape for age: **Hoe oud ben je?**

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Numbers 0 to 10

Start with zero through ten. These are the building blocks for everything else.

| Number | Dutch | Pronunciation tip |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | nul | like "nool" |
| 1 | één | like "ane" with a long ay |
| 2 | twee | like "tway" |
| 3 | drie | like "dree" |
| 4 | vier | like "veer" |
| 5 | vijf | ends in an **f** sound, not English v |
| 6 | zes | like "zes" |
| 7 | zeven | like "ZAY ven" |
| 8 | acht | guttural ch, like **acht** in **achternaam** from [Lesson 2](/notes/a0/02-alphabet-and-spelling) |
| 9 | negen | like "NAY gen" |
| 10 | tien | like "teen" |

**Één** vs **een**: Dutch uses **een** for "a" or "an" (a book) and **één** when you mean the number one. In speech the stress makes the difference. When counting, people often stress **één**.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb één broer. | I have one brother. |
| Een koffie, alsjeblieft. | A coffee, please. |
| Één, twee, drie, vier, vijf. | One, two, three, four, five. |

### Numbers 11 to 20

Eleven and twelve are irregular, just like in English. From thirteen onward, Dutch follows a clear pattern: **unit + tien**.

| Number | Dutch | Pattern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 11 | elf | irregular |
| 12 | twaalf | irregular |
| 13 | dertien | drie + tien |
| 14 | veertien | vier + tien |
| 15 | vijftien | vijf + tien |
| 16 | zestien | zes + tien |
| 17 | zeventien | zeven + tien |
| 18 | achttien | acht + tien |
| 19 | negentien | negen + tien |
| 20 | twintig | irregular base, but very common |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik woon op de derde verdieping. Nee, de dertiende! | I live on the third floor. No, the thirteenth! |
| Er zijn twintig mensen. | There are twenty people. |
| Twaalf plus acht is twintig. | Twelve plus eight is twenty. |

### Asking how many

Use **Hoeveel?** to ask about quantity.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoeveel? | How many? |
| Hoeveel is dat? | How much is that? (price, later lesson) |
| Hoeveel mensen? | How many people? |
| Hoeveel koffies? | How many coffees? |

The answer often starts with **Er zijn** (there are) or just the number plus noun:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoeveel broers heb je? | How many brothers do you have? |
| Ik heb twee broers. | I have two brothers. |
| Hoeveel zijn er? | How many are there? |
| Vijf. | Five. |

You will learn **heb** properly in [Lesson 5](/notes/a0/05-hebben-and-everyday-things). For now, recognise the pattern.

### Saying your age

To ask someone's age:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoe oud ben je? | How old are you? |
| Hoe oud ben jij? | How old are you? (emphasis) |
| En jij? | And you? |

To answer, use **ik ben** plus the number plus **jaar**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik ben twintig jaar. | I am twenty. |
| Ik ben zestien jaar oud. | I am sixteen years old. |
| Ik ben negen jaar. | I am nine. |

The word **jaar** means year. **Oud** means old. You can say **Ik ben twintig** alone in casual speech, but **jaar** is clearer for learners.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoe oud is hij? | How old is he? |
| Hij is dertien jaar. | He is thirteen. |
| Ben jij achttien? | Are you eighteen? |
| Ja, ik ben achttien. | Yes, I am eighteen. |

Notice **Hoe oud ben je?** uses the same verb first pattern as **Ben jij Tom?** from Lesson 1.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  Three numbers trip up English speakers: **vijf** (ends in **f**), **acht** (uses the Dutch ch sound), and **twaalf** (the Dutch **aa** is long). Listen carefully to native speakers and practise with [Vowel Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) and [Consonant Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using hebben for age</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik heb twintig jaar.

    ✅ Ik ben twintig jaar.

    English says "I am twenty" but also "I have twenty years" in some contexts. Dutch age always uses **zijn** (ik ben), never **hebben**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Asking "how years are you?"</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Hoe jaar ben je?

    ✅ Hoe oud ben je?

    English speakers translate word for word from "how old." Dutch asks **hoe oud** (how old), not **hoe jaar**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Pronouncing vijf with an English v</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ "vijv" (English v at the end)

    ✅ "vijf" (ends in f)

    Dutch **v** at the end of a word often sounds like **f**. Vijf, twaalf, and elf all end with a sharp consonant, not a buzzing v.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting the teen pattern after twelve</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ tiendrie for 13

    ✅ dertien

    After **twaalf**, Dutch reverses the order: unit first, then **tien**. Drie + tien = **dertien**, not tiendrie.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">At the language café</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Two learners meet and compare ages.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Lisa:** Hoi! Ik heet Lisa. En jij?
    >
    > **Mark:** Hoi Lisa, ik ben Mark. Aangenaam!
    >
    > **Lisa:** Aangenaam. Hoe oud ben je?
    >
    > **Mark:** Ik ben negentien jaar. En jij?
    >
    > **Lisa:** Ik ben twintig. Hoeveel mensen zijn hier?
    >
    > **Mark:** Ik zie vijftien mensen, denk ik.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Wow, vijftien! Nou, leuk je te ontmoeten, Mark.
    >
    > **Mark:** Jij ook! Doei!

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** Hi! My name is Lisa. And you?
    >
    > **Mark:** Hi Lisa, I am Mark. Nice to meet you!
    >
    > **Lisa:** Nice to meet you. How old are you?
    >
    > **Mark:** I am nineteen. And you?
    >
    > **Lisa:** I am twenty. How many people are here?
    >
    > **Mark:** I see fifteen people, I think.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Wow, fifteen! Well, nice to meet you, Mark.
    >
    > **Mark:** You too! Bye!

    You already know **hoi**, **ik heet**, **aangenaam**, **en jij**, and **doei** from Lesson 1. **Denk ik** means "I think." You will learn **denken** later.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
