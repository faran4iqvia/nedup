## Introduction

You have been meeting Dutch nouns since [Lesson 5](/notes/a0/05-hebben-and-everyday-things): **een fiets**, **een hond**, **het geld**. Every Dutch noun is either **de** or **het**. English has no equivalent, so English speakers often skip the article or guess wrong. This short primer gives you the habit that prevents months of mistakes: **always learn the article with the noun**, as one chunk. You do not need full grammar rules yet. That comes in [A1 Lesson 6: Food and Drink](/notes/a1/06-food-and-drink) and the [Grammar Track: Articles](/notes/grammar/09-articles-de-het).

## Quick Review

From [Lesson 10](/notes/a0/10-ordering-food-and-drink) you already order **de koffie** and **het brood** as fixed phrases. From [Lesson 9](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live): **de stad**, **het land**. ## Grammar and Language Explanation

### One rule: noun + article = one word

| ❌ Learn this | ✅ Learn this |
| --- | --- |
| brood | **het** brood |
| fiets | **de** fiets |
| koffie | **de** koffie |

When you write vocabulary, always include **de** or **het**. When you speak, the article is not optional the way "a" often is in English.

### Two articles, three forms in speech

| Article | Used for | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **de** | most nouns; all plurals | **de** koffie, **de** appels |
| **het** | many neuter nouns | **het** brood, **het** water |
| **een** | a / an (indefinite) | **een** broodje (a roll) |

Plural nouns always use **de**, even if the singular was **het**:

| Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- |
| **het** brood | **de** broden |
| **het** ei | **de** eieren |
| **de** appel | **de** appels |

### Memory tricks (not perfect, but useful at A0)

| Pattern | Usually | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Words ending in **ing** | **de** | **de** rekening, **de** bestelling, **de** meeting |
| Diminutives **je** / **tje** / **pje** | **het** | **het** brood**je**, **het** meis**je** |
| People and jobs | **de** | **de** man, **de** docent, **de** moeder |
| Languages and metals | **het** | **het** Nederlands, **het** goud |
| Two syllables, start with **ge** | often **het** | **het** geld, **het** gebouw |

These tricks help at the start. Many exceptions exist. When in doubt, check a dictionary and learn the chunk.

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Saying de brood or het fiets</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ de brood · ✅ **het** brood

    ❌ het fiets · ✅ **de** fiets

    English has one "the", so learners pick de or het at random. Learn each noun with its article from day one.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping the article in Dutch</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik drink koffie. (when you mean coffee in general at the table)

    ✅ Ik drink **de** koffie. / ✅ Ik drink koffie. (bare noun can work for substances)

    At A0, safest pattern: use the phrases from lessons (**Ik wil graag een koffie**) until A1 clarifies **de/het** with more nouns.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using English logic for new words</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ het rekening (because "it" is neuter in English)

    ✅ **de** rekening

    Dutch gender does not match English. **Rekening** ends in **ing** → almost always **de**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Wrong plural article</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **het** eieren

    ✅ **de** eieren

    Every plural noun takes **de**, including words that were **het** in the singular (**het** ei → **de** eieren).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Learning Tips

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Colour code your word list</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Highlight **de** words in one colour and **het** words in another in your notes. Your eye learns the pattern faster than your brain guesses.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Say the article aloud every time</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Not "brood" but "**het** brood". Not "koffie" but "**de** koffie". Ten repetitions a day beats reading a grammar table once.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">At the bakery</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Lisa buys bread and pays.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Lisa:** Goedemorgen! Ik wil **het** brood en **de** kaas, alsjeblieft.
    >
    > **Bakker:** **De** kaas is op. Alleen **het** brood vandaag.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Prima. Hoeveel kost **het** brood?
    >
    > **Bakker:** **Het** brood kost drie euro vijftig.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Alstublieft. Dank u wel!

    **In English:**

    > **Lisa:** Good morning! I would like the bread and the cheese, please.
    >
    > **Baker:** The cheese is gone. Only bread today.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Fine. How much does the bread cost?
    >
    > **Baker:** The bread costs three euros fifty.
    >
    > **Lisa:** Here you are. Thank you!

    Notice **het brood** three times. The article repeats because Dutch uses it more consistently than English uses "the".
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
