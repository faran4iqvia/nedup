## Introduction

You can introduce yourself, count, ask questions, and say where you live. Now you use Dutch in one of the most satisfying real situations: ordering coffee, lunch, or dinner. Dutch cafés and restaurants are everywhere, and even a short order in Dutch earns instant goodwill. You also meet **Hoe laat is het?** and **om … uur** for meal times and reservations — the full clock rules (including the tricky **half** rule) come in [Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock).

## Quick Review

In [Lesson 1](/notes/a0/01-greetings-and-introductions) you greeted people and used **u** for formal address. In [Lesson 7](/notes/a0/07-numbers-21-100-and-money) you asked **Hoeveel kost het?** and talked about paying. In [Lesson 9](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live) you invited someone for coffee. Today those pieces meet at the table, and you learn **alsjeblieft**, **alstublieft**, and **graag** for polite orders.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Ordering politely

Dutch has several polite ways to order. All are common:

| Dutch | English | Formality |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Een koffie, alstublieft. | A coffee, please. | polite |
| Ik wil graag een thee. | I would like a tea. | neutral polite |
| Mag ik een broodje kaas? | May I have a cheese sandwich? | polite question |
| Kan ik een biertje krijgen? | Can I get a beer? | informal café |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Goedemiddag. Een cappuccino, alstublieft. | Good afternoon. A cappuccino, please. |
| Ik wil graag de soep. | I would like the soup. |
| Mag ik de rekening? | May I have the bill? |
| Nog iets te drinken? | Something else to drink? |

**Graag** softens a request and means gladly or please. **Mag ik** is very polite. In casual cafés, **een koffie** alone with a smile works fine.

### A first look at wil, kan, and mag

You will learn full modal verb tables in [A1 Lesson 12](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs). For now, recognise these chunks:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** graag … | I would like … |
| **Mag** ik …? | May I …? |
| **Kan** ik … **krijgen**? | Can I get …? |

The main verb stays at the end: **Ik wil graag een koffie drinken** (I would like to drink a coffee). In cafés, the short form **Ik wil graag een koffie** is enough.

### Food and drink words

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| de koffie | coffee |
| de thee | tea |
| het water | water |
| de melk | milk |
| het sap | juice |
| het brood | bread |
| de kaas | cheese |
| het broodje | sandwich / roll |
| het fruit | fruit |
| de groente | vegetables |
| het vlees | meat |
| de vis | fish |
| het bier | beer |
| de wijn | wine |
| de soep | soup |
| de salade | salad |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik drink koffie. | I drink coffee. |
| Zij eet brood met kaas. | She eats bread with cheese. |
| Wij bestellen twee biertjes. | We order two beers. |
| Het eten is lekker. | The food is tasty. |

**Eten** (to eat) and **drinken** (to drink) are core verbs you will learn fully soon. For now:

| Pronoun | eten | drinken |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | eet | drink |
| jij | eet | drinkt |
| hij/zij | eet | drinkt |
| wij | eten | drinken |

### At the restaurant

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| het restaurant | restaurant |
| het café | café |
| de menukaart / het menu | menu |
| de ober / de serveerster | waiter / waitress |
| bestellen | to order |
| de rekening | the bill |
| de fooi | tip |
| Smakelijk! / Eet smakelijk! | Enjoy your meal! |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Mag ik de menukaart? | May I see the menu? |
| Wat raadt u aan? | What do you recommend? |
| De rekening, alstublieft. | The bill, please. |
| Het was erg lekker. | It was very tasty. |

### Telling time on the hour

Reservations and meeting friends need a time. At A0, focus on the hour and **om** (at):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hoe laat is het? | What time is it? |
| Het is twee uur. | It is two o'clock. |
| Het is twaalf uur. | It is twelve o'clock. |
| Om zeven uur. | At seven o'clock. |
| Hoe laat bent u er? | What time will you be there? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Om acht uur ontbijt. | Breakfast at eight o'clock. |
| De lunch is om twaalf uur. | Lunch is at twelve o'clock. |
| Het diner is om zes uur. | Dinner is at six o'clock. |

**Half drie**, **kwart over**, and the rest of the clock come in [Lesson 11](/notes/a0/11-telling-time-and-reading-the-clock). For now, **om zeven uur** and **Hoe laat is het?** are enough to book a table.

Meal times:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| het ontbijt | breakfast |
| de lunch | lunch |
| het diner | dinner |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus">
  **Graag** has that Dutch **g** and a long **aa**. **Broodje** has a double **o**. Practise ordering aloud: **Ik wil graag een koffie en een broodje kaas, alstublieft.** See [Consonant Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds) for **g** and [Vowel Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) for long **aa**.
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting alstublieft or graag when ordering</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Geef koffie. (sounds like a command)

    ✅ Een koffie, alstublieft. / Ik wil graag een koffie.

    In cafés and restaurants, **alstublieft** or **graag** turns a blunt order into a polite one.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Asking for the menu with verkeerde woorden</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Mag ik het brief? (invented translation of menu)

    ✅ Mag ik de menukaart?

    **Menukaart** or **menu** is the word for menu. **Brief** means letter or mail.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Using nemen only without alstublieft in formal places</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik neem koffie. (blunt in a formal restaurant)

    ✅ Een koffie, alstublieft. / Ik wil graag een koffie.

    **Ik neem** (I will take) is fine among friends. In polite settings, add **graag** or **alstublieft**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Forgetting de and het with food words</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ Ik drink koffie. (missing article in learning mode)

    ✅ **Ik drink de koffie.** / **Een koffie, alstublieft.** (In cafés, **koffie** without **de** is common in speech — but learn chunks with the article: **de koffie**, **het brood**, **de kaas**.)
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Lunch at a terrace café</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Tom orders lunch on a sunny afternoon in Utrecht.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Ober:** Goedemiddag! Wat mag het zijn?
    >
    > **Tom:** Goedemiddag. Mag ik de menukaart, alstublieft?
    >
    > **Ober:** Natuurlijk. Hier.
    >
    > **Tom:** Dank u. Ik wil graag een broodje kaas en een kop thee.
    >
    > **Ober:** En om te drinken? Water?
    >
    > **Tom:** Nee, alleen thee. Hoeveel kost dat?
    >
    > **Ober:** Dat is zes euro vijftig.
    >
    > **Tom:** Prima. Hoe laat is het nu?
    >
    > **Ober:** Het is twee uur.
    >
    > **Tom:** Ah, ik heb haast om drie uur. Alstublieft, de rekening.
    >
    > **Ober:** Natuurlijk. Alstublieft, uw bonnetje.
    >
    > **Tom:** Dank u wel. Het was lekker. Tot ziens!
    >
    > **Ober:** Tot ziens! Fijne dag!

    **In English:**

    > **Waiter:** Good afternoon! What can I get you?
    >
    > **Tom:** Good afternoon. May I have the menu, please?
    >
    > **Waiter:** Of course. Here you are.
    >
    > **Tom:** Thank you. I would like a cheese sandwich and a cup of tea.
    >
    > **Waiter:** And to drink? Water?
    >
    > **Tom:** No, just tea. How much is that?
    >
    > **Waiter:** That is six euros fifty.
    >
    > **Tom:** Great. What time is it now?
    >
    > **Waiter:** It is two o'clock.
    >
    > **Tom:** Ah, I am in a hurry by three o'clock. The bill, please.
    >
    > **Waiter:** Of course. Here is your receipt.
    >
    > **Tom:** Thank you very much. It was tasty. Goodbye!
    >
    > **Waiter:** Goodbye! Have a nice day!

    **Wat mag het zijn?** is a classic Dutch service phrase. **Natuurlijk** means of course. **Om te drinken** means to drink.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
