## Introduction

Dutch indirect objects show **who receives** the direct object. Main lesson: [A2 Lesson 15](/notes/a2/15-indirect-objects).

## Quick Review

From [A2 Lesson 15](/notes/a2/15-indirect-objects): **Ik geef hem het boek** · **me/mij**, **je/jou**, **hem**, **haar**.

<Callout title="Remember from…" type="info">
  Remember from [Grammar 01](/notes/grammar/01-personal-pronouns): unstressed **me/je** vs stressed **mij/jou** in short phrases.
</Callout>

## Explanation

### Roles

| Role | Question | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Indirect object | to/for whom? | Tom in **geef Tom het boek** |
| Direct object | what? | het boek |

### Two nouns: IO before DO

**Ik geef de man het boek.**

### Two pronouns: DO before IO

**Ik geef het hem.** · **Geef het me.**

| Wrong | Right |
| --- | --- |
| geef me het | **geef het me** |

### Unstressed vs stressed

| Unstressed | Stressed / after preposition |
| --- | --- |
| me | mij / **aan mij** |
| je | jou / **aan jou** |

### aan + person

**Ik geef het boek aan hem.** = **Ik geef hem het boek.**

### hen / hun / ze

| Form | Role |
| --- | --- |
| **hen** | direct (them) |
| **hun** | indirect (to them) |
| **ze** | both (spoken) |

### Subordinate clauses

Object pronouns before verb: **omdat hij het me geeft**.

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Order |
| --- | --- |
| Two nouns | IO + DO |
| Two pronouns | DO + IO |
| After aan | mij, jou, hem, haar |
