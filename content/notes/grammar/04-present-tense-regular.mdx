## Introduction

Regular verbs share one pattern: remove **-en** from the infinitive, add endings (**ik werk**, **jij werkt**, **wij werken**). You already use this in **Ik woon in Utrecht** and **Waar werk je?** Main lessons: [A0 Lesson 9](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live) · [A1 Lesson 12: More Present Verbs](/notes/a1/12-more-present-verbs).

## Quick Review

From [A0 Lesson 9](/notes/a0/09-where-do-you-live) you say **Ik woon in …** and **Woon je in …?** Regular verbs use stem + endings (**werk**, **werkt**, **werken**).

## Explanation

Regular verbs: remove **-en**, add endings.

| Person | Ending | werken | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ik | stem | werk | I work |
| jij / u | stem + t | werkt | you work |
| hij / zij / het | stem + t | werkt | he/she/it works |
| wij / jullie / zij | infinitive | werken | we/you/they work |

**t-drop:** verb before **je** loses **t**: **Werk** je vandaag?

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk** in Rotterdam. | I work in Rotterdam. |
| **Maak** je koffie? | Do you make coffee? |
| Zij **spreekt** Nederlands. | She speaks Dutch. |
| Wij **wonen** in Utrecht. | We live in Utrecht. |
| Ik **loop** naar huis. | I walk home. |

  </CardContent>
