## Welcome to the Grammar Track 💡

This is the **reference shelf**, not a second course. Follow the **A0 → A1 → A2 → B1** lesson path first. Open a grammar file only when a lesson's **Grammar Focus** box points you here, or when you want extra tables and drills on one topic.

### Track bands

The grammar track has **three bands**. What you see on each page depends on which band you are in:

| Band | Files | What to expect on each page |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **A0–A2 core** | **01–30** | Full **classroom loop**: Quick Review, **Remember from…** callout, **Part A** (closed) + **Part B** (say aloud), partial answer keys |
| **B1 core grammar** | **31–42** | Full **classroom loop** like 01–30: Quick Review, **Remember from…**, **Part A** + **Part B**, partial answer keys |
| **Phrase & register** | **43–58** | **Chunks, tone, and context** — not new verb paradigms. **Part A** drills + **Part B: Say chunks aloud** on every page |

**A0–A2 core (01–30)** covers pronouns, _zijn_, _hebben_, present tense, questions, word order, negation, articles, possessives, plurals, reflexive and separable verbs, past tenses, modals, comparatives, prepositions, relative clauses, _om te_, _want_ / _omdat_, _er_, conditionals, and passive intro.

**B1 core grammar (31–42)** covers extended subordinates (_hoewel_, _terwijl_, _nadat_, …), advanced relatives and _waar_ + preposition, future with _zullen_, _zou_ politeness, hypotheticals and conditional past, passives with _worden_ and _zijn_, indirect speech, _te_ + infinitive, and the word-order masterclass.

**Phrase & register (43–58)** covers opinions, debate, news, formal writing, work, education, society, feelings, culture, storytelling, diminutives, particles, idioms, fast speech, practical Netherlands, and the B1 capstone ([58](/notes/grammar/58-bringing-it-together)).

### How to use this track

**Worked example:** You miss item 10 on [Grammar Review and Practice](/notes/grammar/review-and-practice) (**omdat** vs **want**).

1. Check the answer: **Ik blijf thuis, omdat ik ziek ben.** (verb last)
2. Open [Connector Choice](/notes/grammar/connector-choice) → **want vs omdat** table.
3. Drill [26: Subordinate want and omdat](/notes/grammar/26-subordinate-want-omdat) Part A.
4. Say aloud: one **want** sentence and one **omdat** sentence with the same reason.

Same loop for tense doubts → [Tense and Register Choice](/notes/grammar/tense-and-register-choice) → one topic file → three spoken sentences.
