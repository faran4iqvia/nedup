## Welcome to the Grammar Track 💡

This is the **reference shelf**, not a second course. Follow the **A0 → A1 → A2 → B1** lesson path first. Open a grammar file only when a lesson's **Grammar Focus** box points you here, or when you want extra tables and drills on one topic.

**A0 to A2 (files 01 to 30):**

- Pronouns, _zijn_, _hebben_, and the present tense
- Questions, word order, and negation
- Articles (_de_ / _het_), possessives, demonstratives, plurals, and adjectives
- Reflexive and separable verbs
- Past tenses: simple past, perfect tense, weak and strong verbs
- Modal verbs, comparatives, and prepositions
- Complex sentences: relative clauses, _om te_, _want_ / _omdat_
- _Er_ constructions, conditionals, and the passive voice

**B1 (files 31 to 58):**

- Extended subordinate clauses: _hoewel_, _terwijl_, _nadat_, _voordat_, _totdat_
- Advanced relatives and _waar_ + preposition
- Future with _zullen_, politeness with _zou_, hypotheticals and conditional past
- Passives with _worden_ and _zijn_, indirect speech, _te_ + infinitive
- Word order masterclass and verb clusters
- Topic references for opinions, news, formal writing, work, society, culture, idioms, fast speech, and practical Netherlands

Each grammar file follows the same layout:

1. **Introduction** with a link to the matching main lesson
2. **Explanation** with Dutch and English tables
3. **Common Mistakes for English Speakers**
4. **Quick Reference**
5. **Practice Exercises** and **Answer Key**
6. **Related Main Lessons** for cross links
