## Introduction

Dutch passive focuses on what happens to something (**wordt** + participle). Spoken Dutch often stays active; news and rules use passive. Process vs result (**wordt** vs **is**) → [Tense and Register Choice](/notes/grammar/tense-and-register-choice). Main lesson: [A2 Lesson 21](/notes/a2/21-passive-intro).

## Explanation

### Formula

**subject + worden + past participle**

| Active | Passive | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mila schrijft de brief. | De brief **wordt geschreven**. | The letter is being written. |

### Spoken vs written

| Register | Passive use | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Spoken** | less common; active voice preferred | **Mila schrijft de brief.** | Mila is writing the letter. |
| **News, reports, formal** | passive frequent | **De brief wordt geschreven.** | The letter is being written. |
| **Signs, rules** | **Er wordt** … | **Er wordt hier niet gerookt.** | No smoking here. |

Use passive when the **action** matters more than **who** did it (news, procedures). In conversation, Dutch speakers often keep the active voice unless the agent is unknown.

### worden conjugation

| | present | past |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | word | werd |
| hij/zij/het | wordt | werd |
| wij/zij | worden | werden |

### Questions

**Wordt** de vergadering **verplaatst**?

### Er wordt …

**Er wordt hier niet gerookt.** · **Er wordt gewerkt.**

### door + agent (preview)

**De brief wordt door Mila geschreven.**

### worden vs zijn

| Type | Dutch | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **worden** (process) | De soep **wordt** gemaakt. | The soup is being made. |
| **zijn** (result) | De soep **is** gemaakt. | The soup has been made. |

### Subordinate clause

… **omdat** de vergadering **verplaatst wordt**.

## Quick Reference

| Tense | Form | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Present | wordt / worden + participle | **De brief wordt geschreven.** | The letter is being written. |
| Past | werd / werden + participle | **De brief werd geschreven.** | The letter was written. |
| Impersonal | **Er wordt** + participle | **Er wordt gewerkt.** | People are working. |
