## Introduction

A2 taught **die** and **dat** with verb final order. B1 adds **wie** (people + preposition), **waar** (places and things), **wat** / **hetgeen** (whole clauses or abstract reference), and longer nested relatives. Main lesson: [B1 Lesson 2](/notes/b1/02-relative-clauses). Full **waar** + preposition tables live in [B1 Lesson 3](/notes/b1/03-waar-plus-preposition).

## Explanation

### Review: die and dat

| Antecedent | Pronoun | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| de word (sg. + pl.) | **die** | de collega **die** … |
| het word (sg.) | **dat** | het rapport **dat** … |

Verb last: **de man die aardig is**, **het boek dat ik lees**.

### wie: people, especially with a preposition

When the relative clause needs a preposition about a **person**, Dutch uses **preposition + wie**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De collega met wie** ik **werk** | The colleague I work with |
| **De klant aan wie** ik **mailde** | The client I emailed |
| **De manager voor wie** ik **werk** | The manager I work for |
| **Het team waarmee** zij **samenwerkt** | The team she collaborates with |

Spoken Dutch also allows **die** + preposition at the end: **de collega die ik mee werk** (less formal).

### waar: places and things

**Waar** replaces **die/dat** when the antecedent is a **place** or when a **thing** combines with a preposition in more formal Dutch:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De stad waar** ik **woon** | The city where I live |
| **Het kantoor waar** ik **werk** | The office where I work |
| **Het punt waar** wij **het over hebben** | The point we are talking about |

Spoken: **het punt dat wij het over hebben**. Written: **het punt waarover wij praten** (see Lesson 3).

### wat and hetgeen

| Form | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **alles wat** | everything that | **Alles wat** hij **zegt**, **geloof** ik. |
| **iets wat** | something that | **Iets wat** mij **opviel** … |
| **niets wat** | nothing that | **Niets wat** hij **deed**, **hielp**. |
| **hetgeen** | that which (formal) | **Hetgeen** hij **voorstelde**, **werd** **afgewezen**. |

**Hetgeen** is formal and common in reports and news. **Wat** after **alles / iets / niets** is everyday.

### degene die / diegene die

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Degene die** eerst **komt**, **wint**. | The one who arrives first wins. |
| **Diegene die** dat **heeft** **gezegd**, **moet** **uitleggen**. | The one who said that must explain. |

Use when you refer to a person without repeating a noun.

### Nested relatives

Dutch can stack relatives:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De man die** de vrouw **die** daar **staat** **kent** | The man who knows the woman who is standing there |
| **Het rapport dat** de cijfers **bevat die** wij **nodig** **hebben** | The report that contains the figures we need |

Read from the **inner** clause outward. Each relative keeps verb final.

### Restrictive vs non restrictive

| Type | Commas? | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Restrictive (essential) | Usually no | **De collega die** naast mij **zit** is nieuw. |
| Non restrictive (extra info) | Often yes | **Mijn baas, die** in Utrecht **woont**, **komt** morgen. |

In Dutch, comma use is less strict than in English, but non restrictive relatives in writing often get commas around the clause.

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

❌ **De man wie** ik ken

✅ **De man die** ik ken · **De man met wie** ik werk

**Wie** appears after a **preposition**, not as a simple subject relative for **de man**.

❌ **De stad die** ik woon

✅ **De stad waar** ik woon

Places take **waar**, not **die**.

❌ **Alles die** hij zegt

✅ **Alles wat** hij zegt

After **alles / iets / niets**, use **wat**.

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Pronoun |
| --- | --- |
| de noun, no prep. | **die** |
| het noun, no prep. | **dat** |
| person + prep. | **prep. + wie** |
| place | **waar** |
| alles / iets / niets | **wat** |
| formal whole clause | **hetgeen** |
