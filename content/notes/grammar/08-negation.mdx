## Introduction

Dutch does not use **niet** for everything. You choose between **geen** and **niet**, and **niet** has a fixed place in the sentence depending on what you deny. Main lesson: [A1 Lesson 3: Negation](/notes/a1/03-negation).

### Before this page

You should be comfortable with:

- [Grammar 07: Word Order](/notes/grammar/07-word-order-svo) (verb in position 2)
- [Grammar 03: _hebben_](/notes/grammar/03-verb-hebben) (**Ik heb geen tijd**)

If word order is still shaky, read [07](/notes/grammar/07-word-order-svo) first. Negation sits **inside** the V2 frame; it does not change it.

## Quick Review

From A0 you already say **Ik heb geen tijd** and **Ik weet het niet**. From [A1 Lesson 2](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo) you placed **niet** before the infinitive: **Ik wil vandaag niet werken**. This page makes those patterns explicit.

## Explanation

### Two words, two jobs

| Word | Negates | English equivalent |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **geen** | nouns without **de**, **het**, **mijn**, **die**, and so on | no / not a / not any |
| **niet** | verbs, adjectives, adverbs, definite nouns, whole sentences | not |

### Decision: geen or niet?

| Are you denying a noun? | Is the noun indefinite (no the/my/this)? | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Yes | Yes | **geen** |
| Yes | No (has **de**, **het**, **mijn**, **die**, and so on) | **niet** after that noun phrase |
| No (denying verb, adjective, or sentence) | · | **niet** |

| Dutch | English | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik heb **geen** fiets. | I do not have a bicycle. | indefinite noun |
| Ik drink **geen** koffie. | I do not drink coffee. | general noun, no article |
| Ik drink **de** koffie **niet**. | I do not drink the coffee. | definite noun → **niet** |
| Ik heb mijn sleutels **niet**. | I do not have my keys. | possessive = definite → **niet** |
| Ik werk **niet** vandaag. | I am not working today. | denying the action |
| Zij is **niet** moe. | She is not tired. | denying adjective |

### Geen in detail

**Geen** replaces **een** or the zero article.

| Affirmative | Negative with geen | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik heb **een** fiets. | Ik heb **geen** fiets. | I do not have a bicycle. |
| Wij hebben **tijd**. | Wij hebben **geen** tijd. | We have no time. |
| Dat is **een** probleem. | Dat is **geen** probleem. | That is not a problem. |

**Geen** also works with **geen idee**, **geen probleem**, **geen honger** (with **hebben**, not **zijn**).

When the noun already has **de**, **het**, **mijn**, **die**, or **deze**, use **niet**, not **geen**:

| Wrong | Correct | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ❌ Ik heb geen mijn telefoon. | ✅ Ik heb mijn telefoon **niet**. | I do not have my phone. |
| ❌ Ik eet geen de kaas. | ✅ Ik eet de kaas **niet**. | I do not eat the cheese. |

### Where does niet go?

**Rule 1: Denying the whole action → niet at the end** (default)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik werk vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. |
| Lisa kookt **niet**. | Lisa is not cooking. |

**Rule 2: Denying one word → niet right before that word**

| Dutch | English | What is denied |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik ben **niet** moe. | I am not tired. | adjective |
| Zij werkt **niet** hard. | She does not work hard. | adverb |
| Ik werk **niet** vandaag. | I am not working today. | time (emphasis on today) |
| Hij eet **niet** thuis. | He does not eat at home. | place |

| Dutch | English | Nuance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik werk vandaag **niet**. | I am not working today. | default: denying today's work |
| Ik werk **niet** vandaag. | I am not working **today**. | emphasis on **vandaag** |

**Rule 3: Definite noun phrase → niet after that phrase**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik heb mijn telefoon **niet**. | I do not have my phone. |
| Ik eet de soep **niet**. | I do not eat the soup. |

**Rule 4: Two verbs → niet before the verb at the end**

When position 2 has one verb and the infinitive or participle sits at the end, **niet** goes **before** the end verb:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik wil vandaag **niet** werken. | I do not want to work today. |
| Hij kan morgen **niet** komen. | He cannot come tomorrow. |
| Zij moet **niet** sporten. | She must not exercise. |
| Ik heb gisteren **niet** gewerkt. | I did not work yesterday. |

| Position 2 | Middle | End |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik **wil** | vandaag **niet** | **werken** |
| Ik **heb** | gisteren **niet** | **gewerkt** |
| Ik **sta** | om zeven uur **niet** | **op** |

For separable verbs, **niet** goes before the prefix at the end:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik sta **niet** op. | I do not get up. |
| Ik sta om zeven uur **niet** op. | I do not get up at seven o'clock. |

See [Grammar 15: Separable Verbs](/notes/grammar/15-separable-verbs) and [Grammar 07](/notes/grammar/07-word-order-svo).

**Rule 5: Inversion and questions → same rules, verb still in position 2**

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Vandaag** werk ik **niet**. | Today I do not work. |
| **Morgen** wil ik **niet** sporten. | Tomorrow I do not want to exercise. |
| Werk je vandaag **niet**? | Are you not working today? |
| Heb je **geen** tijd? | Do you have no time? |

Negation does not change V2. Only **niet** placement inside the sentence needs care.

### Negation in past and perfect

Same **niet** rules apply. The auxiliary (**heb**, **ben**, **wil**, **kan**) stays in position 2:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **heb** gisteren **niet** gewerkt. | I did not work yesterday. |
| Ik **ben** **niet** ziek geweest. | I have not been ill. |
| Ik **wil** vandaag **niet** werken. | I do not want to work today. |

### Side by side

| Situation | geen | niet |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik heb \_\_\_ fiets. | **geen** | · |
| Ik heb mijn fiets \_\_\_. | · | **niet** |
| Ik ben \_\_\_ moe. | · | **niet** |
| Ik werk vandaag \_\_\_. | · | **niet** |
| Ik drink \_\_\_ koffie. (general) | **geen** | · |
| Ik drink de koffie \_\_\_. | · | **niet** |

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Summary |
| --- | --- |
| Indefinite noun | **geen** |
| Definite noun / possessive | **niet** after the phrase |
| Verb / adjective / whole action | **niet** (default: end of sentence) |
| Two verbs | **niet** before infinitive or participle at end |
| Separable | **niet** before prefix at end |
