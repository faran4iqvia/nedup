## Introduction

A2 taught **omdat**, **want**, **dat**, and **als**. B1 adds subordinate connectors for **contrast**, **simultaneous time**, and **sequence**: **hoewel**, **terwijl**, **nadat**, **voordat**, **totdat**. Main lesson: [B1 Lesson 1](/notes/b1/01-conjunctions-overview).

## Explanation

### One rule for all bijzinnen

After these words, the **conjugated verb goes to the end** of the subordinate clause, same as **omdat** and **dat**:

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **hoewel** | although | **Hoewel** ik moe **ben**, **werk** ik door. |
| **terwijl** | while | **Terwijl** ik **kook**, **luister** ik naar muziek. |
| **nadat** | after | **Nadat** ik **heb** gegeten, **ga** ik **wandelen**. |
| **voordat** | before | **Voordat** ik **ga**, **bel** ik je. |
| **totdat** | until | **Ik wacht**, **totdat** hij **komt**. |

### hoewel: contrast

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Hoewel** het **regent**, **ga** ik **toch** **naar** buiten. | Although it is raining, I am going outside anyway. |
| Ik **ga** **toch**, **hoewel** ik **moe** **ben**. | I am going anyway, although I am tired. |

**Toch** often appears in the main clause after **hoewel** to stress _still / anyway_.

### terwijl: two actions at the same time

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Terwijl** zij **werkt**, **kook** ik **het** **eten**. | While she works, I cook the food. |
| **Terwijl** hij **belde**, **las** ik **een** **boek**. | While he was calling, I was reading a book. |

**Terwijl** = simultaneous. For _when_ in the past narrative, use **toen** (A2).

### nadat and voordat: sequence

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Nadat** wij **hebben** **afgesproken**, **stuur** ik **een** **mail**. | After we have agreed, I send an email. |
| **Voordat** de **vergadering** **begint**, **lees** ik **de** **notities**. | Before the meeting starts, I read the notes. |

With completed prior actions, **nadat** often takes **perfect tense** in the subordinate clause: **nadat ik heb gewerkt**.

### Subordinate first: main clause still V2

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Hoewel** ik **ziek** **ben**, **moet** ik **vandaag** **werken**. | Although I am sick, I have to work today. |
| **Nadat** het **is** **gestopt** **met** **regenen**, **gaan** wij **naar** buiten. | After it has stopped raining, we go outside. |

### Overview: A2 + B1 connectors

| Type | Words |
| --- | --- |
| Reason | **omdat**, **want**, **doordat** |
| Contrast | **hoewel**, **ofschoon** (formal) |
| Time (simultaneous) | **terwijl** |
| Time (sequence) | **nadat**, **voordat**, **totdat**, **zodra** |
| Condition | **als**, **tenzij** |
| Opinion / content | **dat**, **of** |

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Terwijl with wrong progressive</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Terwijl ik ben aan het koken…** (broken order) · ✅ **Terwijl ik kook…** / **Terwijl ik aan het koken ben…** Natural progressive or simple present.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Nadat perfect in wrong order</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Nadat we gegeten hebben…** (spoken OK, written careful) · ✅ **Nadat we hebben gegeten, gaan we wandelen.** Auxiliary before participle at clause end.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Tenzij treated like want</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>

  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Answer Key

1. **Hoewel ik moe ben, ga ik door.**
2. **Terwijl zij werkt, wacht ik.**
3. **Nadat we hebben gegeten, gaan we wandelen.**
4. **Voordat hij komt, bel ik hem.**
