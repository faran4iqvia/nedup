## Introduction

Grammar in Dutch is **one system** built step by step. This chapter helps you **find gaps** and **drill patterns** without rereading every lesson. Use it:

- After **A1**, **A2**, or **B1** review tests when something felt shaky
- Before a real conversation, exam, or job interview in Dutch
- Once a month as maintenance if you already finished B1

Work **on paper or aloud**. For each mistake, open the linked grammar file and do three fresh examples.

## Where you stand

You do not need perfect scores on every part. **A0 to A1** should feel mostly automatic. **A2** should be usable in stories and opinions. **B1** parts matter for independence in the Netherlands.

After this review:

- Retry weak topics in the [Grammar Track](/notes/grammar) (single files only)
- Take the [B1 Review and Test](/notes/b1/review-and-test) if you have finished B1 lessons
- Run [Pronunciation Review and Practice](/notes/pronunciation/review-and-practice) so grammar reaches your mouth
- Keep a **taalroutine**: ten minutes of Dutch daily beats one long cram session
