## Introduction

Dutch is not "backwards." It follows one rule in almost every main clause: **the conjugated verb goes in position 2** (the **V2** rule). Main lesson: [A1 Lesson 2: Word Order](/notes/a1/02-word-order-svo).

## Quick Review

In A0 you asked **Waar woon je?** (verb first). In statements you say **Ik woon in Utrecht** (subject first). The verb is always the **second** element in a normal main clause.

## Explanation

### The V2 train

Think of the sentence as a train with three slots:

| Position | What goes here | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | First element (often the subject) | **Ik** / **Vandaag** / **Waar** |
| 2 | Conjugated verb (always here) | **werk** / **woon** |
| 3+ | Everything else | **in Rotterdam** / **ik thuis** |

| Dutch | Position breakdown | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ik** **werk** in Rotterdam. | ik → werk → rest | I work in Rotterdam. |
| **Lisa** **woont** in Utrecht. | Lisa → woont → rest | Lisa lives in Utrecht. |
| **Wij** **eten** om zes uur. | wij → eten → rest | We eat at six o'clock. |

The label **SVO** (Subject, Verb, Object) is common in English books. The more precise Dutch label is **V2**: the **verb is always second**, even when the subject is not first.

### Standard order: subject first

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werk** vandaag. | I work today. |
| Ik **koop** brood **in de winkel**. | I buy bread in the shop. |
| Hij **doet** vandaag niets. | He is doing nothing today. |

Safe default at A1: **subject + verb + object**, then **time**, then **place**.

### Inversion: something else in position 1

When **time**, **place**, or another word starts the sentence, the **subject moves after the verb**. The verb **stays** in position 2.

| Normal | Inverted | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik werk vandaag. | **Vandaag** werk **ik**. | Today I work. |
| Hij woont in Utrecht. | **In Utrecht** woont **hij**. | In Utrecht he lives. |
| Wij sporten op zaterdag. | **Op zaterdag** sporten **wij**. | On Saturday we exercise. |

❌ **Morgen ik ga werken.** · ✅ **Morgen ga ik werken.**

### The verb slot stays the same in every tense

Only the **form** of the verb changes. The **position** does not.

| Tense | Position 2 | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Present | present form | Ik **werk** vandaag. |
| Simple past | past form | Gisteren **werkte** ik. |
| Perfect | **hebben/zijn** | Ik **heb** gisteren gewerkt. |
| Modal | modal verb | Ik **wil** vandaag **werken**. |

The second verb piece (infinitive, participle, separable prefix) goes to the **end**:

| Dutch | English | Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik **sta** om zeven uur **op**. | I get up at seven. | separable: **op** at end |
| Ik **wil** vanavond **koken**. | I want to cook tonight. | infinitive at end |
| Ik **heb** gisteren **gewerkt**. | I worked yesterday. | participle at end |

Full rules: [Grammar 15: Separable Verbs](/notes/grammar/15-separable-verbs) · [Grammar 18: Perfect Tense](/notes/grammar/18-perfect-tense) · [Grammar 06: Modal Verbs](/notes/grammar/06-modal-verbs-intro).

### Subordinate clauses: verb last (preview)

After **omdat**, **dat**, **als**, and similar words, the conjugated verb moves to the **end** of that clause:

| Main clause (V2) | Subordinate (verb last) |
| --- | --- |
| Ik blijf thuis, **omdat** ik ziek **ben**. | verb **ben** at end |
| Ik denk **dat** hij morgen **komt**. | verb **komt** at end |

This is the next step on the word order spine. Full reference: [Grammar 26: want and omdat](/notes/grammar/26-subordinate-want-omdat) · [Grammar 42: Word Order Masterclass](/notes/grammar/42-word-order-masterclass) (B1 clusters).

### Word order spine (where this file fits)

1. **V2** in main clauses (this page)
2. **Inversion** when position 1 is not the subject
3. **Verb at end** with modals, separables, perfect
4. **Verb last** in subordinate clauses
5. **Verb clusters** at B1 ([42](/notes/grammar/42-word-order-masterclass))

## Quick Reference

| Rule | Summary |
| --- | --- |
| Main clause | verb in position 2 |
| Inversion | fronted word + verb + subject |
| Two verbs | boss verb in 2, other verb at end |
| Subordinate | verb(s) at end of clause |
