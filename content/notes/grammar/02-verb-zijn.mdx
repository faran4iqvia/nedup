## Introduction

**_zijn_** is Dutch "to be" for identity, location, age, and descriptions: **Ik ben Anna**, **Wij zijn in Amsterdam**, **Het is koud**. English uses "to be" for hunger too · Dutch uses **_hebben_** instead (**Ik heb honger**). Main lesson: [A0 Lesson 4: Pronouns and _zijn_](/notes/a0/04-pronouns-and-zijn).

## Quick Review

From [A0 Lesson 4](/notes/a0/04-pronouns-and-zijn) you conjugate **ben**, **bent**, **is**, and **zijn** for introductions and location. Hunger and thirst use **hebben**, not **zijn**.

<Callout title="Remember from…" type="info">
  Remember from [Grammar 01: Personal Pronouns](/notes/grammar/01-personal-pronouns): every **zijn** sentence needs a subject (**Ik ben …**, not **Ben moe.**).
</Callout>

## Explanation

**zijn** is irregular in the present tense.

| Pronoun | zijn | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | ben | I am |
| jij / je | bent | you are |
| u | bent | you are (formal) |
| hij / zij / het | is | he / she / it is |
| wij / jullie / zij | zijn | we / you / they are |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben** twintig jaar. | I am twenty years old. |
| **Ben** je klaar? | Are you ready? |
| Het **is** koud. | It is cold. |
| Wij **zijn** in Amsterdam. | We are in Amsterdam. |
| U **bent** welkom. | You are welcome. |

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">

    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Wrong form with u</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **U is welkom** · ✅ **U bent welkom.** Formal **u** takes **bent**, not **is**.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Ben jij vs Bent jij in questions</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    In questions, **t** drops before **jij**: ❌ **Bent jij klaar?** · ✅ **Ben jij klaar?** (informal)
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
