## Explanation

**Yes/no questions:** verb first, then subject.

| Statement | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Jij bent Anna. | **Ben** jij Anna? |
| Hij woont hier. | **Woont** hij hier? |
| Wij hebben tijd. | **Hebben** jullie tijd? |

**Wh-questions:** question word + verb + subject.

| Word | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| wie | who | **Wie** ben jij? |
| wat | what | **Wat** doe je? |
| waar | where | **Waar** woon je? |
| wanneer | when | **Wanneer** kom je? |
| hoe | how | **Hoe** heet je? |
| waarom | why | **Waarom** niet? |

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Dutch has no **do**: ❌ **Do you work today?** · ✅ **Werk jij vandaag?** — verb to front, no extra word.
  </CardContent>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Question word order unchanged</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Waar jij woont?** · ✅ **Waar woon je?** In yes/no and **wa-** questions, verb still comes early (V2).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Hoeveel vs Hoe veel</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **Hoeveel** (one word) = how many/much: **Hoeveel kost het?** · **Hoe oud ben je?** uses **Hoe** + adjective.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
