## Introduction

English often keeps **do/does** in questions; Dutch **inverts** subject and verb (**Werk je?**) or fronts a question word (**Waar woon je?**). Main lesson: [A0 Lesson 6: Basic Questions](/notes/a0/06-basic-questions).

## Quick Review

From [A0 Lesson 6](/notes/a0/06-basic-questions) you invert for yes/no (**Ben je …?**) and front **waar**, **wanneer**, **wie**, **hoeveel**.

<Callout title="Remember from…" type="info">
  Remember from [Grammar 04: Present Tense](/notes/grammar/04-present-tense-regular): inversion swaps subject and verb · the verb stays conjugated.
</Callout>

## Explanation

**Yes/no questions:** verb first, then subject.

| Statement | Question | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jij bent Anna. | **Ben** jij Anna? | You are Anna. → Are you Anna? |
| Hij woont hier. | **Woont** hij hier? | He lives here. → Does he live here? |
| Wij hebben tijd. | **Hebben** jullie tijd? | We have time. → Do you have time? |

**Wh-questions:** question word + verb + subject.

| Word | Meaning | Dutch example | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| wie | who | **Wie** ben jij? | Who are you? |
| wat | what | **Wat** doe je? | What are you doing? |
| waar | where | **Waar** woon je? | Where do you live? |
| wanneer | when | **Wanneer** kom je? | When are you coming? |
| hoe | how | **Hoe** heet je? | What is your name? |
| waarom | why | **Waarom** niet? | Why not? |
