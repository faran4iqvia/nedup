## Introduction

High-frequency verbs have **irregular** past forms with vowel change, not **-te/-de**: **ging**, **kwam**, **was**, **had**, **zag**. Same register split as regular past: perfect in speech, simple past in narrative writing. Main lesson: [A1 Lesson 19: Simple Past Irregular](/notes/a1/19-simple-past-irregular).

Not sure which past, passive, or future form? See [Tense and Register Choice](/notes/grammar/tense-and-register-choice).

## Explanation

High-frequency irregular past forms.

| Infinitive | ik / hij | wij |
| --- | --- | --- |
| zijn | was | waren |
| hebben | had | hadden |
| gaan | ging | gingen |
| komen | kwam | kwamen |
| doen | deed | deden |
| eten | at | aten |
| kunnen | kon | konden |
| moeten | moest | moesten |
| willen | wilde / wou | wilden |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **was** ziek. | I was ill. |
| **Ging** je naar huis? | Did you go home? |
| Wij **aten** pasta. | We ate pasta. |
| Hij **had** geen tijd. | He had no time. |

### Spoken vs written

| Register | Typical choice | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Spoken** | perfect | **Ik ben gegaan.** / **Ik heb gegeten.** | I went. / I ate. |
| **Written narrative** | simple past | **Ik ging naar huis.** / **Toen at ik.** | I went home. / Then I ate. |
| **Stories, news** | simple past common | **Er was eens …** | Once upon a time … |

Strong verbs appear often in stories (**ging**, **kwam**, **zag**). In chat, the perfect is usually more natural for a finished event.
