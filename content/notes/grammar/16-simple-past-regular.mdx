## Introduction

The **simple past** (imperfect) marks past events with **-te** or **-de** on regular verbs: **Ik werkte**, **Wij maakten**. It is common in **written** stories and formal text; **spoken** Dutch often prefers the perfect (**Ik heb gewerkt**). Main lesson: [A1 Lesson 18: Simple Past Regular](/notes/a1/18-simple-past-regular).

Not sure which past, passive, or future form? See [Tense and Register Choice](/notes/grammar/tense-and-register-choice).

## Explanation

Past of regular verbs: stem + **te** or **de** + plural **-n**.

| Rule | Ending | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| stem ends in t, k, f, s, ch, p | -te | werkte, maakte |
| otherwise | -de | woonde, speelde |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **werkte** gisteren thuis. | I worked at home yesterday. |
| **Woonde** je in Amsterdam? | Did you live in Amsterdam? |
| Wij **maakten** soep. | We made soup. |
| Hij **woonde** in Leiden. | He lived in Leiden. |

### Spoken vs written

| Register | Typical tense | Example | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Spoken** (everyday) | perfect | **Ik heb gisteren gewerkt.** | I worked yesterday. |
| **Written** (story, report) | simple past | **Gisteren werkte ik thuis.** | Yesterday I worked at home. |
| **Either** | both OK | **Woonde je in Amsterdam?** / **Heb je in Amsterdam gewoond?** | Did you live in Amsterdam? |

At A1/A2, if you tell a friend what you did yesterday, default to **Ik heb … ge-…**. Use **-te/-de** when you read or write narratives. See [Grammar 18: Perfect Tense](/notes/grammar/18-perfect-tense).
