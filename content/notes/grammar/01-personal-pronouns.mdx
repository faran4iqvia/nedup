## Introduction

Full reference for **personal pronouns** (stressed/unstressed and formal **u**). Main lesson: [A0 Lesson 4: Pronouns and _zijn_](/notes/a0/04-pronouns-and-zijn).

## Explanation

Dutch personal pronouns include stressed and unstressed forms (**jij/je**, **zij/ze**) and formal **u**.

| Pronoun | English |
| --- | --- |
| ik | I |
| jij / je | you (informal) |
| u | you (formal) |
| hij | he |
| zij / ze | she |
| het | it |
| wij / we | we |
| jullie | you (plural) |
| zij / ze | they |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** ben Anna. | I am Anna. |
| **Jij** bent mijn vriend. | You are my friend. |
| **Hij** is docent. | He is a teacher. |
| **Zij** woont in Utrecht. | She lives in Utrecht. |
| **Wij** zijn studenten. | We are students. |
| **Jullie** zijn op tijd. | You are on time. |
| **Zij** komen uit België. | They come from Belgium. |

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

  <CardContent>
    English has one "you". Dutch splits **jij/je** (informal) and **u** (formal). ❌ **Hoe gaat het met je?** to an official · ✅ **Hoe gaat het met u?**
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Mixing up zij (she) and zij (they)</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Same spelling, different verb: **Zij is** (she is) vs **Zij zijn** (they are). Context and the verb form disambiguate.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Dropping pronouns like English</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    ❌ **Ben moe.** (too telegraphic) · ✅ **Ik ben moe.** Dutch usually keeps the subject pronoun.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

## Practice Exercises

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Drills</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    1. Fill in: \_\_\_\_\_\_ ben Anna. (**ik**)
    2. Fill in: Waar woont \_\_\_\_\_\_? (formal you) → **u**
    3. Choose **zij** (she) or **zij** (they): **\_\_\_\_\_\_ zijn op tijd.** → **Zij**
    4. Translate: We are students. → **Wij zijn studenten.**
    5. Fix: **Ben moe.** → **Ik ben moe.**
    6. Say aloud: greet with **ik**, **jij**, and **u** in one sentence each.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
