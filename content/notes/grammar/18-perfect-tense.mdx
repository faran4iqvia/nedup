## Introduction

The perfect tense talks about completed actions in the past. You build it in clear steps: pick **hebben** or **zijn**, form the participle, then place both in the sentence. Main lesson: [A2 Lesson 2: Perfect Tense Intro](/notes/a2/02-perfect-tense-intro).

Not sure which past, passive, or future form? See [Tense and Register Choice](/notes/grammar/tense-and-register-choice).

## Quick Review

You already say **Ik heb gewerkt** and **Ik ben gegaan**. The perfect is **hebben/zijn** (position 2) + **past participle** (end).

## Explanation

### Step 1: The formula

| Component | Role | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **hebben** or **zijn** (conjugated) | auxiliary in position 2 | **heb** / **ben** / **is** |
| **past participle** | main verb at end | **gewerkt** / **gegaan** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **heb** gisteren **gewerkt**. | I worked yesterday. |
| Zij **is** naar huis **gegaan**. | She went home. |
| Wij **hebben** pizza **gegeten**. | We ate pizza. |

### Step 2: Build the past participle

**Regular verbs:** **ge-** + stem + **-t** or **-d**

| Infinitive | Stem | Participle | English |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| werken | werk | **gewerkt** | worked |
| kopen | koop | **gekocht** | bought |
| praten | praat | **gepraat** | talked |

**Irregular:** memorize (**gegaan**, **gedaan**, **gezien**, **gehad**, and so on).

**Separable verbs:** **ge-** goes **between** prefix and stem:

| Infinitive | Participle | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| opstaan | **op**ge**staan** | got up |
| uitgaan | **uit**ge**gaan** | went out |
| meebrengen | **mee**ge**bracht** | brought along |

See [Grammar 15](/notes/grammar/15-separable-verbs).

**Verbs with inseparable prefix** (be-, ver-, ont-, ge-, er-, her-): **no ge-**

| Infinitive | Participle | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| begrijpen | **begrepen** | understood |
| vertellen | **verteld** | told |

### Step 3: Hebben or zijn? (decision tree)

```
Does the verb describe movement from A to B?
  (gaan, komen, lopen, rijden, vliegen, reizen, vertrekken, aankomen)
  → YES → zijn
  → NO ↓

Does the verb describe a change of state?
  (worden, zijn in past: geworden, gebleven, gestorven, gegroeid)
  → YES → zijn
  → NO ↓

All other verbs (werken, eten, kopen, zien, doen, hebben, …)
  → hebben
```

| Auxiliary | Typical verbs | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zijn** | movement A → B | Ik **ben** naar Amsterdam **gegaan**. |
| **zijn** | change of state | Hij **is** ziek **geworden**. |
| **hebben** | everything else | Ik **heb** pizza **gegeten**. |

**Common zijn verbs to memorize:** **gaan**, **komen**, **lopen**, **rijden**, **fietsen**, **vliegen**, **reizen**, **vertrekken**, **aankomen**, **vallen**, **blijven**, **worden**, **zijn** (geweest).

**Trick:** If you can ask **Waarheen?** (where to?) with the verb, it is often **zijn**.

| Dutch | English | Why zijn |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ik ben thuis **gekomen**. | I came home. | movement |
| Wij zijn laat **aangekomen**. | We arrived late. | movement |
| Het is koud **geworden**. | It has become cold. | change of state |

### Step 4: Put it in the sentence

Same V2 frame as present tense. **Hebben/zijn** in position 2; participle at the end.

| Dutch | Position breakdown |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **heb** gisteren **gewerkt**. | ik → heb → gisteren → gewerkt |
| Gisteren **heb** ik **gewerkt**. | inversion: gisteren → heb → ik → gewerkt |

**With separable:** prefix stays with participle at end:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben** om zeven uur **opgestaan**. | I got up at seven. |
| Hij **heeft** de deur **opengedaan**. | He opened the door. |

**With modal in perfect** (advanced): double infinitive at end → [Grammar 20](/notes/grammar/20-modal-verbs-depth).

### Spoken vs written

| Register | When to use perfect | When simple past appears |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Spoken** | default for "I did X yesterday" | less common in casual chat |
| **Written** | reports, emails (completed actions) | novels, **toen** stories, news background |
| **Tip** | **Ik heb gisteren gewerkt** | **Gisteren werkte ik** / **Toen ging ik** |

Both are grammatically past. Choose perfect for everyday telling; choose simple past when you write a sequence (**Toen …, toen …**) or read formal Dutch. See [Grammar 16](/notes/grammar/16-simple-past-regular) and [52: Storytelling](/notes/grammar/52-storytelling-past-tenses).

## Quick Reference

| Step | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Conjugate **hebben** or **zijn** |
| 2 | Form participle (**ge-** + stem + **-t/-d**, or irregular) |
| 3 | Movement or change of state? → **zijn**; else **hebben** |
| 4 | Auxiliary in position 2, participle at end |
