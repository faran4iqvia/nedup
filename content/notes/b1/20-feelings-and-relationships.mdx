## Introduction

You can discuss politics and work in Dutch. Personal talk needs **feelings** and **relationships**: **Ik ben teleurgesteld**, **Ik ben verliefd**, **Wij hebben ruzie gehad**, **Laten we verzoenen**. Dutch uses **zijn**, **hebben**, and **zich voelen** for emotions in different patterns. After this lesson you can support a friend, talk about love and friendship, and handle conflict with B1 nuance.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Three ways to express feelings

Dutch emotions use **zijn**, **hebben**, or **zich voelen**:

| Pattern | Dutch | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zijn** + adjective | **Ik** **ben** **blij**. | I am happy. |
| **hebben** + noun | **Ik** **heb** **spijt**. | I am sorry. / I regret it. |
| **zich** **voelen** + adj | **Ik** **voel** me **moe**. | I feel tired. |
| **worden** + adjective | **Ik** **word** **boos**. | I am getting angry. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **ben** **verdrietig** **over** **het** **nieuws**. | I am sad about the news. |
| **Ik** **heb** **medelijden** **met** **jou**. | I feel sorry for you. |
| **Voel** jij je **opgelucht**? | Do you feel relieved? |
| **Hij** **wordt** **gekwetst** **door** **die** **opmerking**. | He is hurt by that remark. |

**Physical** states use **hebben**: **Ik heb het koud**. **Emotions** usually use **zijn** or **zich voelen**: **Ik ben boos**, **Ik voel me gespannen**.

### Core emotion adjectives

| Dutch | English | Typical trigger |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **blij** | happy / glad | good news |
| **verdrietig** | sad | loss, bad news |
| **boos** | angry | unfair treatment |
| **teleurgesteld** | disappointed | unmet expectation |
| **verliefd** | in love | romantic feeling |
| **eenzaam** | lonely | isolation |
| **trots** | proud | achievement |
| **bang** / **angstig** | afraid / anxious | danger, uncertainty |
| **gespannen** | tense | stress, conflict |
| **opgewonden** | excited | anticipation |
| **gefrustreerd** | frustrated | blocked goals |
| **gekwetst** | hurt (feelings) | harsh words |
| **beledigd** | offended | insult |
| **schuldig** | guilty | own mistake |
| **jaloers** | jealous | rivalry |
| **opgelucht** | relieved | tension gone |
| **kwetsbaar** | vulnerable | openness, risk |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **ben** **teleurgesteld** **in** **het** **resultaat**. | I am disappointed in the result. |
| **Zij** **is** **trots** **op** **haar** **team**. | She is proud of her team. |
| **Wij** **zijn** **opgewonden** **over** **het** **reisplan**. | We are excited about the travel plan. |

| **De** **vreugde** **was** **groot**. | The joy was great. |

### Love, friendship, and trust

You know **de relatie** and **de partner** from A1. B1 adds:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **de** **vriendschap** | friendship |
| **de** **liefdesrelatie** | romantic relationship |
| **de** **liefde** | love |
| **liefhebben** | to love (people) |
| **verliefd** **zijn** **op** | to be in love with |
| **vertrouwen** | trust |
| **wantrouwen** | distrust |
| **de** **affectie** | affection |
| **de** **intimiteit** | intimacy |
| **de** **band** | bond / connection |
| **de** **hechtheid** | closeness / attachment |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **ben** **verliefd** **op** **hem**. | I am in love with him. |
| **Onze** **vriendschap** **is** **hecht**. | Our friendship is close. |
| **Vertrouwen** **is** **belangrijk** **in** **een** **relatie**. | Trust is important in a relationship. |
| **Zij** **daten** **al** **een** **jaar**. | They have been dating for a year. |

**Houden van** (A1) is broader liking. **Liefhebben** is deeper love for people.

### Supporting someone

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **steunen** | to support |
| **troosten** | to comfort |
| **troost** **bieden** | to offer comfort |
| **begrip** **hebben** **voor** | to understand / sympathise with |
| **begripvol** | understanding |
| **empathisch** | empathetic |
| **de** **empathie** | empathy |
| **medelijden** **hebben** **met** | to feel sorry for |
| **zorgzaam** | caring |
| **liefdevol** | loving / affectionate |
| **aandacht** **geven** | to give attention |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **maak** me **zorgen** **om** **jou**. | I worry about you. |
| **Ik** **maak** me **druk** **om** **het** **examen**. | I am worried about the exam. |
| **Heb** **je** **begrip** **voor** **mijn** **situatie**? | Do you understand my situation? |
| **Ik** **ben** **er** **voor** **je**. | I am here for you. |

From [B1 Lesson 5](/notes/b1/05-zou-politeness-and-advice): soft support with **Zou je willen praten?**

### Conflict, ruzie, and verzoenen

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **de** **ruzie** | quarrel / argument |
| **ruziën** | to quarrel |
| **het** **conflict** | conflict |
| **verzoenen** | to reconcile |
| **excuses** **aanbieden** | to apologise |
| **Het** **spijt** me. | I am sorry. |
| **grenzen** **stellen** | to set boundaries |
| **de** **grens** | boundary / limit |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wij** **hebben** **ruzie** **gehad** **over** **geld**. | We had a quarrel about money. |
| **Kunnen** **wij** **verzoenen**? | Can we make up? |
| **Ik** **bied** **mijn** **excuses** **aan**. | I apologise. |
| **Ik** **moet** **grenzen** **stellen** **in** **deze** **relatie**. | I need to set boundaries in this relationship. |

From [B1 Lesson 14](/notes/b1/14-agreeing-and-debating): **Ik begrijp je punt, maar ik ben gekwetst**. **Laten we het uitpraten**.

### Life stages in relationships

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **daten** | to date |
| **verloofd** | engaged |
| **getrouwd** | married (A1) |
| **de** **echtscheiding** | divorce |
| **echtscheiden** | to divorce |
| **uit** **elkaar** **gaan** | to break up / separate |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zij** **zijn** **verloofd**. | They are engaged. |
| **Na** **de** **echtscheiding** **blijven** **zij** **vrienden**. | After the divorce they remain friends. |
| **Wij** **zijn** **uit** **elkaar** **gegaan**. | We broke up. |

Use **de echtscheiding** for divorce. **Scheiding** alone often means separation in grammar contexts.

### Expressing feelings with nuance

Combine B1 opinion and debate chunks with emotions:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **heb** **gemengde** **gevoelens** **over** **de** **relatie**. | I have mixed feelings about the relationship. |
| **Het** **lijkt** me **dat** **jij** **gekwetst** **bent**. | It seems to me that you are hurt. |
| **Naar** **mijn** **mening** **moeten** **wij** **openhartig** **praten**. | In my opinion we should talk openly. |
| **Ik** **ben** **niet** **boos**, **maar** **wel** **teleurgesteld**. | I am not angry, but I am disappointed. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **voel** me **kwetsbaar** **als** ik **dit** **vertel**. | I feel vulnerable when I tell this. |
| **Openhartig** **zijn** **is** **moeilijk**, **maar** **nodig**. | Being open is difficult but necessary. |
| **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **mee** **eens**. | I agree with that. |

### Physical affection

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **omhelzen** | to hug |

Dutch affection language is often understated. **Ik ben er voor je** and **begrip hebben voor** are very common.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Stress **ver-LIEFD**, **teleur-ge-STELD**, **ge-KWETST**. In **Ik maak me zorgen om jou**, link **zorgen-om**. Practise: **Ik ben teleurgesteld, maar ik wil verzoenen.** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

    **om** + person. **over** + thing/situation is common.

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Na een ruzie</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam talk through hurt feelings and try to reconcile.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** **Ik** **voel** me **gekwetst** **na** **gisteren**. **Ik** **was** **teleurgesteld** **dat** **je** **niet** **belde**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Het** **spijt** me. **Ik** **maak** me **druk** **om** **werk**, **maar** **ik** **heb** **begrip** **nodig** **van** **jou** **ook**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ik** **ben** **niet** **boos**, **maar** **wel** **verdrietig**. **Onze** **vriendschap** **is** **me** **veel** **waard**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Dat** **voel** ik **ook**. **Ik** **wil** **niet** **ruziën**. **Kunnen** **wij** **verzoenen**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**, **als** **wij** **openhartig** **praten**. **Ik** **heb** **gemengde** **gevoelens**, **maar** **vertrouwen** **is** **belangrijk**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Ik** **bied** **mijn** **excuses** **aan**. **Ik** **ben** **er** **voor** **je**. **Zullen** **wij** **vanavond** **praten**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Graag**. **Dan** **voel** ik me **misschien** **weer** **opgelucht**.

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** I feel hurt after yesterday. I was disappointed that you did not call.
    >
    > **Sam:** I am sorry. I am worried about work, but I need understanding from you too.
    >
    > **Mila:** I am not angry, but I am sad. Our friendship means a lot to me.
    >
    > **Sam:** I feel that too. I do not want to quarrel. Can we make up?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, if we talk openly. I have mixed feelings, but trust is important.
    >
    > **Sam:** I apologise. I am here for you. Shall we talk tonight?
    >
    > **Mila:** Gladly. Then I may feel relieved again.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
