## Introduction

You can already discuss opinions and debate in Dutch. Now you read **real news**: headlines on NOS, short items in **de Volkskrant** or **AD**, and updates on your phone. Dutch news uses patterns you know (**volgens**, **dat** clauses, passive) but packages them in **short, dense** text. After this lesson you can skim a kop, read the first alinea for facts, spot **feitelijk** vs **meningsmatig** content, and summarise a story in your own words.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### What Dutch news looks like at B1

You do not need every word to understand the **kernpunt**. A typical short item has:

| Part | Dutch label | Your job |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Title | **de kop** / **nieuwskop** | What happened? |
| First paragraph | **de lead** / **openingszin** | Who, where, when? |
| Body | **de alinea** | Details, quotes, context |
| Context box | **achtergrond** | Why it matters |
| Reactions | **reacties** | Who responds? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **lees** **eerst** **de** **kop**. | I read the headline first. |
| **Daarna** **scan** ik **de** **eerste** **alinea**. | Then I scan the first paragraph. |
| **Tot** **slot** **vul** ik **achtergrond** **in** **als** **ik** **tijd** **heb**. | Finally I fill in background if I have time. |

### Headline grammar

Headlines are **compact**. Articles (**de**, **het**) often disappear:

| Headline | Expanded feel |
| --- | --- |
| **Regering** **presenteert** **nieuw** **klimaatplan** | De regering presenteert een nieuw klimaatplan. |
| **Files** **raken** **Randstad** **zwaar** **getroffen** | Files raken de Randstad zwaar. |
| **Minister** **reageert** **op** **kritiek** | De minister reageert op kritiek. |

Three headline habits:

| Habit | Example | Note for English readers |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Historical present** | **Minister** **zegt** **dat** … | Present verb, past event |
| **Past participle as adjective** | **Zwaar** **getroffen** **gebied** | Hit hard area |
| **Verb first** in some alerts | **Komt** **er** **extra** **geld**? | Question headline |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De** **krant** **schrijft** **kort**; **de** **kop** **is** **niet** **een** **volledige** **zin**. | The paper writes briefly; the headline is not a full sentence. |
| **In** **het** **nieuws** **gebruik** je **vaak** **de** **tegenwoordige** **tijd** **voor** **gisteren**. | In the news you often use the present tense for yesterday. |
| **De** **kopregel** **vangt** **de** **nieuwswaarde** **in** **weinig** **woorden**. | The headline captures the newsworthiness in few words. |

### volgens and naming your source

You met **volgens** in [B1 Lesson 10](/notes/b1/10-indirect-speech). In news it introduces **who claims what**:

| Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- |
| **Volgens** + source | **Volgens** **de** **krant** **is** **het** **akkoord** **rond**. |
| **Volgens** **berichten** | **Volgens** **berichten** **wordt** **de** **wet** **vandaag** **besproken**. |
| **Volgens** **de** **woordvoerder** | **Volgens** **de** **woordvoerder** **klopt** **het** **rapport**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Volgens** **het** **persbureau** **zijn** **er** **geen** **gewonden**. | According to the press agency there are no injuries. |
| **Volgens** **de** **redactie** **is** **dit** **het** **belangrijkste** **nieuws** **van** **vandaag**. | According to the editorial team this is today's most important news. |
| **De** **bronvermelding** **staat** **onderaan** **het** **artikel**. | The source citation is at the bottom of the article. |

**Volgens** is **not** a reporting verb. Pair it with a normal sentence, or use **melden** / **berichten**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De** **krant** **meldt** **dat** **de** **prijzen** **stijgen**. | The paper reports that prices are rising. |
| **Er** **wordt** **bericht** **dat** **de** **vergadering** **is** **uitgesteld**. | It is reported that the meeting is postponed. |
| **NOS** **bericht** **dat** **het** **stormt** **aan** **de** **kust**. | NOS reports that it is storming on the coast. |

### Reporting verbs and dat clauses

News reuses reporting verbs from [B1 Lesson 10](/notes/b1/10-indirect-speech):

| Verb | Typical news use |
| --- | --- |
| **melden** | neutral report |
| **bevestigen** | official confirmation |
| **ontkennen** | denial |
| **aankondigen** | announcement |
| **bekendmaken** | public announcement |
| **verklaren** | formal statement |
| **berichten** | to report (media style) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De** **minister** **bevestigt** **dat** **de** **maatregel** **ingaat**. | The minister confirms the measure takes effect. |
| **Het** **bedrijf** **ontkent** **dat** **er** **data** **zijn** **gelekt**. | The company denies that data has been leaked. |
| **Er** **wordt** **gemeld** **dat** **de** **trein** **vertraging** **heeft**. | It is reported that the train is delayed. |

Remember: **dat** + verb last. **Volgens de krant is het waar** (main clause) vs **De krant meldt dat het waar is** (subordinate).

### Passive and er in news

News loves **passive** because the **actor** is not always the focus:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De** **wet** **wordt** **vandaag** **in** **de** **Tweede** **Kamer** **besproken**. | The law is being discussed in parliament today. |
| **Het** **rapport** **is** **gisteravond** **gepubliceerd**. | The report was published last night. |
| **Er** **worden** **nieuwe** **maatregelen** **voorbereid**. | New measures are being prepared. |
| **Er** **is** **een** **incident** **gemeld** **bij** **het** **station**. | An incident has been reported at the station. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wie** **doet** **het**? **Soms** **staat** **het** **niet** **in** **de** **kop**. | Who does it? Sometimes it is not in the headline. |
| **Passief** **helpt** **je** **het** **feitelijke** **deel** **snel** **te** **zien**. | Passive helps you see the factual part quickly. |

### Feitelijk vs meningsmatig

| Type | Signal words | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Feitelijk** | numbers, dates, **volgens**, **bevestigt** | **De** **temperatuur** **was** **38** **graden**. |
| **Meningsmatig** | **volgens mij**, **commentator**, **analyse** | **De** **commentator** **vindt** **het** **plan** **riskant**. |
| **Reportage** | story, scene, **verslaggever** on location | **De** **verslaggever** **beschrijft** **de** **drukte** **op** **het** **station**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Feitelijk** **nieuws** **zegt** **wat** **er** **is** **gebeurd**. | Factual news says what happened. |
| **Meningsmatig** **nieuws** **zegt** **wat** **iemand** **ervan** **vindt**. | Opinion based news says what someone thinks of it. |
| **Een** **nieuwsanalyse** **legt** **de** **achtergrond** **uit**. | A news analysis explains the background. |

### Sample article: read in four steps

Read this short item the way Dutch readers do:

**Kop:** **Overheid** **kondigt** **nieuw** **thuiswerkbeleid** **aan**

**Lead:** **De** **overheid** **heeft** **vandaag** **een** **nieuw** **thuiswerkbeleid** **bekendgemaakt**. **Volgens** **de** **woordvoerder** **moeten** **ministeries** **minstens** **twee** **dagen** **per** **week** **op** **kantoor** **werken**.

**Body:** **Er** **wordt** **gemeld** **dat** **de** **maatregel** **volgende** **maand** **ingaat**. **Hoewel** **vakbonden** **reageren** **kritisch**, **bevestigt** **de** **minister** **dat** **er** **overleg** **komt**. **In** **de** **achtergrond** **speelt** **de** **discussie** **over** **productiviteit** **en** **werkplekken** **in** **de** **Randstad**.

**Reading steps:**

| Step | Question | Answer here |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1. Kop | What is announced? | New work from home policy |
| 2. Lead | Who says what? | Government; spokesperson: two days in office |
| 3. Body | When + contrast? | Next month; unions critical, minister promises talks |
| 4. Summary | One sentence | Government announces hybrid policy; unions push back |

### Following news day to day

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **volg** **het** **nieuws** **via** **een** **nieuwsapp**. | I follow the news via a news app. |
| **Ik** **krijg** **een** **nieuwsupdate** **om** **acht** **uur**. | I get a news update at eight o'clock. |
| **Het** **journaal** **om** **acht** **uur** **is** **actueel**. | The eight o'clock bulletin is current affairs. |
| **De** **nieuwsfeed** **toont** **het** **laatste** **nieuws** **bovenaan**. | The news feed shows the latest news at the top. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  In **volgens de krant**, link **volgens-de**. Stress **VOL-gens**. In **er wordt gemeld**, the **t** in **wordt** often softens in fast speech. Practise: **Volgens berichten wordt de wet vandaag besproken.** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech) and [Regional Variation](/notes/pronunciation/06-regional-variation) for broadcast Dutch.
</Callout>

    >

    > **Mila:** Have you seen the latest news? The headline was: Government announces new work from home policy.
    >
    > **Sam:** No, I do not always follow the news. What is the key point?
    >

    >
    > **Sam:** Is that factual or opinion based?
    >

    >
    > **Sam:** I partly agree with that. According to reports unions react critically. The minister does confirm that talks will follow.
    >
    > **Mila:** Exactly. I read the headline first, then the lead, then the background. That way you grasp the news context quickly.
    >
    > **Sam:** Good idea. Tonight I will watch the bulletin. Then we can talk it through.
