## Introduction

You already use **dat** clauses from A2 and B1: **Ik denk dat het regent**, **Omdat hij ziek is, blijft hij thuis**. **Indirect speech** reports what someone **said, asked, or promised** without quoting: **Mila zegt dat zij morgen komt**, **Sam vraagt of ik klaar ben**, **Hij zei dat hij zou helpen**. After this lesson you can relay meetings, emails, and conversations the way Dutch speakers do at work and in the media.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Direct vs indirect speech

| Direct (quote) | Indirect (report) |
| --- | --- |
| Mila **zegt**: "**Ik** **kom** **morgen**." | Mila **zegt** **dat** **zij** **morgen** **komt**. |
| Sam **vraagt**: "**Ben** **jij** **klaar**?" | Sam **vraagt** **of** **ik** **klaar** **ben**. |
| De **baas** **belooft**: "**Wij** **zullen** **betalen**." | De **baas** **belooft** **dat** **wij** **zullen** **betalen**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zij** **vertelt** **dat** **zij** **volgende** **week** **begint**. | She says she starts next week. |
| **Hij** **meldt** **dat** **het** **rapport** **is** **afgerond**. | He reports that the report is completed. |
| **Ik** **hoor** **dat** **de** **vergadering** **wordt** **verplaatst**. | I hear the meeting is being rescheduled. |

### Reporting verbs

| Verb | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zeggen** | general say | **Hij** **zegt** **dat** … |
| **vertellen** | tell | **Zij** **vertelt** **me** **dat** … |
| **vragen** | ask | **Hij** **vraagt** **of** … |
| **bevestigen** | confirm | **Zij** **bevestigt** **dat** … |
| **ontkennen** | deny | **Hij** **ontkent** **dat** … |
| **melden** | report | **IT** **meldt** **dat** … |
| **beloven** | promise | **Zij** **belooft** **dat** … |
| **waarschuwen** | warn | **Hij** **waarschuwt** **dat** … |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Mila** **bevestigt** **dat** **zij** **morgen** **komt**. | Mila confirms that she is coming tomorrow. |
| **Sam** **ontkent** **dat** **er** **fouten** **zijn** **gemaakt**. | Sam denies that mistakes have been made. |
| **De** **woordvoerder** **meldt** **dat** **de** **wijziging** **is** **doorgevoerd**. | The spokesperson reports that the change has been implemented. |

### dat + verb last

Indirect statements use **dat** + subordinate clause. The **verb goes last**:

| Direct | Indirect |
| --- | --- |
| "**Ik** **woon** **in** **Utrecht**." | **Hij** **zegt** **dat** hij **in** **Utrecht** **woont**. |
| "**Wij** **zijn** **klaar**." | **Zij** **zegt** **dat** **wij** **klaar** **zijn**. |
| "**Het** **rapport** **is** **afgerond**." | **Hij** **meldt** **dat** **het** **rapport** **is** **afgerond**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zij** **vertelt** **dat** **zij** **morgen** **niet** **kan**. | She says she cannot tomorrow. |
| **Hij** **zegt** **dat** **hij** **gisteren** **heeft** **gewerkt**. | He says he worked yesterday. |
| **Wij** **horen** **dat** **de** **audit** **volgende** **week** **wordt** **uitgevoerd**. | We hear the audit is being carried out next week. |

### Tense: Dutch usually does not backshift

**Important for English speakers:** Dutch often **keeps** the same tense in indirect speech. You do **not** automatically change present to past.

| Direct (present) | Indirect (still present) |
| --- | --- |
| "**Ik** **ben** **ziek**." | **Hij** **zegt** **dat** hij **ziek** **is**. |
| "**Het** **gaat** **goed**." | **Zij** **zegt** **dat** **het** **goed** **gaat**. |

| Direct (past) | Indirect (still past) |
| --- | --- |
| "**Ik** **kwam** **gisteren**." | **Hij** **zei** **dat** hij **gisteren** **kwam**. |
| "**Wij** **waren** **te** **laat**." | **Zij** **zei** **dat** **wij** **te** **laat** **waren**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Mila** **zegt** **dat** **zij** **nu** **druk** **is**. | Mila says she is busy now. |
| **Sam** **vertelde** **dat** **hij** **gisteren** **thuis** **werkte**. | Sam said he worked from home yesterday. |

The **reporting verb** can be past (**zei**) while the **reported clause** stays present (**is**) if the content is still true.

### zou / zouden for reported future

When the direct speech uses **zullen** for the future, indirect speech often uses **zou / zouden**:

| Direct | Indirect |
| --- | --- |
| "**Ik** **zal** **komen**." | **Hij** **zei** **dat** hij **zou** **komen**. |
| "**Wij** **zullen** **betalen**." | **Zij** **zei** **dat** **wij** **zouden** **betalen**. |
| "**Het** **zal** **morgen** **klaar** **zijn**." | **Hij** **zei** **dat** **het** **morgen** **klaar** **zou** **zijn**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zij** **beloofde** **dat** **zij** **zou** **helpen**. | She promised she would help. |
| **Hij** **zei** **dat** **de** **wijziging** **zou** **worden** **doorgevoerd**. | He said the change would be implemented. |
| **Wij** **dachten** **dat** **het** **zou** **regenen**. | We thought it would rain. |

From [B1 Lesson 5](/notes/b1/05-zou-politeness-and-advice): **zou** inside **dat** with verb last.

### Pronoun and possessive shifts

When you report, shift pronouns to match the new perspective:

| Direct (speaker = Mila) | Indirect (about Mila) |
| --- | --- |
| "**Ik** **kom**." | **Zij** **zegt** **dat** **zij** **komt**. |
| "**Mijn** **rapport** **is** **klaar**." | **Zij** **zegt** **dat** **haar** **rapport** **klaar** **is**. |
| "**Kun** **jij** **me** **helpen**?" | **Zij** **vraagt** **of** **ik** **haar** **kan** **helpen**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Hij** **zegt** **dat** **hij** **morgen** **met** **zijn** **team** **overlegt**. | He says he consults with his team tomorrow. |
| **Zij** **vertelt** **ons** **dat** **zij** **haar** **werk** **heeft** **afgerond**. | She tells us she has completed her work. |

### Yes/no questions: of

Direct questions with inversion become **of** + **statement** order (verb last):

| Direct | Indirect |
| --- | --- |
| "**Kom** **jij** **morgen**?" | **Hij** **vraagt** **of** **ik** **morgen** **kom**. |
| "**Is** **het** **rapport** **klaar**?" | **Zij** **vraagt** **of** **het** **rapport** **klaar** **is**. |
| "**Heb** **jij** **tijd**?" | **Hij** **vraagt** **of** **ik** **tijd** **heb**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Sam** **vraagt** **of** **wij** **morgen** **kunnen** **meetingen**. | Sam asks whether we can meet tomorrow. |
| **Mila** **vraagt** **of** **het** **project** **al** **is** **afgerond**. | Mila asks whether the project is already completed. |

No inversion after **of**: not **of kom ik**, but **of ik kom**.

### WH-questions: question word + statement order

| Direct | Indirect |
| --- | --- |
| "**Waar** **woon** **jij**?" | **Hij** **vraagt** **waar** **ik** **woon**. |
| "**Wanneer** **begint** **het**?" | **Zij** **vraagt** **wanneer** **het** **begint**. |
| "**Waarom** **ben** **jij** **te** **laat**?" | **Hij** **vraagt** **waarom** **ik** **te** **laat** **ben**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Hij** **vraagt** **wanneer** **de** **vergadering** **begint**. | He asks when the meeting starts. |
| **Zij** **vraagt** **waarom** **de** **export** **nog** **niet** **is** **vrijgegeven**. | She asks why the export has not been released yet. |

### Commands and requests

| Type | Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **dat** + **moeten** | reported order | **Hij** **zei** **dat** **ik** **moest** **wachten**. |
| **vragen** + **om** **te** | reported request | **Zij** **vroeg** **me** **om** **te** **bellen**. |
| polite **zou** | soft request | **Hij** **zei** **dat** **ik** **het** **zou** **kunnen** **proberen**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **De** **baas** **zei** **dat** **wij** **voor** **vrijdag** **moesten** **indienen**. | The boss said we had to submit before Friday. |
| **Zij** **vroeg** **ons** **om** **de** **data** **te** **controleren**. | She asked us to check the data. |
| **Hij** **raadde** **aan** **dat** **wij** **zouden** **wachten**. | He advised that we should wait. |

### Present vs past reporting verb

| Reporting verb | Feel | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **zegt** / **vraagt** | still relevant now | **Hij** **zegt** **dat** **hij** **komt**. |
| **zei** / **vroeg** | reported in the past | **Hij** **zei** **dat** **hij** **kwam**. |

Both are common. The **reported clause** tense follows meaning, not automatic English style backshift.

### volgens, naar verluidt, and sources

| Expression | Use | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **volgens** + person | according to | **Volgens** **Mila** **is** **het** **afgerond**. |
| **naar** **verluidt** | allegedly / reportedly | **Naar** **verluidt** **wordt** **de** **deal** **getekend**. |
| **Hij** **beweerde** **dat** … | he claimed | **Hij** **beweerde** **dat** **het** **niet** **klopte**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Volgens** **de** **woordvoerder** **is** **de** **audit** **afgerond**. | According to the spokesperson, the audit is completed. |
| **Naar** **verluidt** **zijn** **er** **wijzigingen** **aangekondigd**. | Reportedly, changes have been announced. |

### Combining with B1 grammar

| Topic | Indirect example |
| --- | --- |
| B1 L8 **worden** passive | **Zij** **meldt** **dat** **de** **regel** **wordt** **ingevoerd**. |
| B1 L9 **zijn** passive | **Hij** **bevestigt** **dat** **alles** **is** **geverifieerd**. |
| B1 L6 **als** unreal | **Zij** **zei** **dat** **zij** **zou** **komen** **als** **zij** **tijd** **had**. |
| B1 L2 **die/dat** | **Het** **nieuws** **dat** **hij** **aankondigde**, **kwam** **onverwacht**. |

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

    > **Sam:** What did the boss actually say?

    > **Mila:** She said we have to submit before Friday. She promised she would help with the review.
    >
    > **Sam:** Did she ask if we can meet tomorrow?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, and I said we can tomorrow. She also asked when the report is ready.
    >
    > **Sam:** And the spokesperson?
    >
    > **Mila:** According to him the audit is completed. He reports that the change has been implemented. Reportedly an announcement will be made tomorrow.
    >
    > **Sam:** Did he claim there are no problems?
    >
    > **Mila:** He denied that mistakes have been made, but finance remarked that the figures are inconsistent. In response to the rumour he said everything is under control.
    >
    > **Sam:** Clear. Can you render exactly what she said about the deadline?
    >
    > **Mila:** She stressed that it stays confidential until the announcement. She asked us not to pass anything on to external parties.
  </CardContent>
