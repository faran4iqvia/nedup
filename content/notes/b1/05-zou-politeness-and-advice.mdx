## Introduction

You met **zou** and **zouden** as past forms of **zullen** in [A2 Lesson 4](/notes/a2/04-modals-in-depth), and **Zou u â€¦ kunnen?** in [A2 Lesson 19](/notes/a2/19-appointments). At B1, **zou** becomes a politeness tool: **Zou je me kunnen helpen?** sounds softer than **Kun je me helpen?**, **Ik zou dat niet doen** gives advice without ordering, and **Dat zou kunnen** hedges an opinion. After this lesson you can request, advise, and disagree in Dutch the way native speakers do in offices, shops, and everyday conversation.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### zou / zouden: forms and core idea

| Pronoun | Form | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | **zou** | Ik **zou** **graag** **komen**. |
| jij / je / u / hij / zij / het | **zou** | **Zou** je **kunnen** **helpen**? |
| wij / jullie / zij | **zouden** | Wij **zouden** **graag** **meewerken**. |

**Zou** adds **distance**: less direct, more polite or tentative. It is not only past tense. Think _would_ in English for requests and advice.

### Polite requests: Zou je â€¦? / Zou u â€¦?

The standard polite pattern: **zou** + subject + **kunnen / willen / mogen** + infinitive:

| Direct | Polite with **zou** |
| --- | --- |
| **Kun** je me **helpen**? | **Zou** je me **kunnen** **helpen**? |
| **Wil** je dit **bevestigen**? | **Zou** je dit **willen** **bevestigen**? |
| **Mag** ik **even** **binnenkomen**? | **Zou** ik **even** **mogen** **binnenkomen**? |
| **Kunt** u **terugbellen**? | **Zou** u **kunnen** **terugbellen**? |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zou** je de **presentatie** **kunnen** **delen**? | Could you share the presentation? |
| **Zou** u **morgen** **tijd** **hebben** **voor** een **gesprek**? | Would you have time tomorrow for a conversation? |
| **Zou** je **even** **naar** dit **rapport** **kunnen** **kijken**? | Could you take a quick look at this report? |
| **Zou** je me **kunnen** **zeggen** **waar** het **kantoor** **zit**? | Could you tell me where the office is? |

**Word order:** **Zou** + subject + objects/other elements + **kunnen/willen/mogen** + main infinitive **last**.

Informal: **Zou je â€¦?** Â· Formal with **u**: **Zou u â€¦?**

### Wishes and preferences: zou graag

From [A1 Lesson 7](/notes/a1/07-at-the-restaurant): **Ik zou graag** is more polite than **Ik wil graag**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zou** **graag** **willen** **reserveren**. | I would like to reserve. |
| Wij **zouden** **graag** **een** **afspraak** **maken**. | We would like to make an appointment. |
| Ik **zou** **graag** **meewerken** **aan** het **project**. | I would like to collaborate on the project. |
| **Zou** je **misschien** **koffie** **willen**? | Would you perhaps like coffee? |

**Zouden graag** for **wij / jullie / zij**. Add **misschien** or **eens** for extra softness.

### Giving advice: Ik zou â€¦

**Ik zou** + infinitive gives advice without sounding bossy:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zou** **meer** **pauze** **nemen**. | I would take more breaks. |
| Ik **zou** **dat** **niet** **doen**. | I would not do that. |
| Ik **zou** **eerst** **de** **cijfers** **controleren**. | I would check the figures first. |
| **Zou** je **niet** **eerder** **beginnen**? | Would you not start earlier? |

| Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- |
| **Als** ik jou **was**, **zou** ik â€¦ | **Als** ik jou **was**, **zou** ik **rustiger** **praten**. |
| Ik **zou** **zeggen** **dat** â€¦ | Ik **zou** **zeggen** **dat** wij **moeten** **wachten**. |
| Je **zou** **kunnen** **proberen** â€¦ | Je **zou** **kunnen** **proberen** **het** **anders** **te** **formuleren**. |

Full hypothetical **als** + past (**Als ik rijk was, zou ik reizen**) comes in [Lesson 6](/notes/b1/06-hypotheticals-with-als). Here, use **Als ik jou was, zou ik â€¦** as a fixed advice chunk.

### Hedging opinions: dat zou kunnen

When you are not fully sure, **zou** softens the claim:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dat **zou** **kunnen**. | That could be. |
| Dat **zou** **best** **kunnen**. | That could well be. |
| Dat **zou** **wel** **eens** **kunnen** **kloppen**. | That might just be correct. |
| Ik **zou** **denken** **dat** â€¦ | I would think that â€¦ |
| Het **zou** **kunnen** **dat** hij **ziek** **is**. | It could be that he is sick. |

| Strong | Hedged with **zou** |
| --- | --- |
| Dat **is** **fout**. | Dat **zou** **kunnen**, **maar** ik **ben** **niet** **zeker**. |
| Hij **komt** **niet**. | Hij **zou** **wel** **eens** **niet** **kunnen** **komen**. |
| Dit **werkt** **niet**. | Dit **zou** **niet** **goed** **kunnen** **werken**. |

### Warnings and gentle criticism

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zou** **niet** **te** **hard** **gaan**. | I would not push too hard. |
| **Zou** je **niet** **vergeten** **te** **mailen**? | You would not forget to email, would you? |
| Dat **zou** ik **niet** **aanraden**. | I would not recommend that. |
| Je **zou** **voorzichtiger** **kunnen** **zijn**. | You could be more cautious. |

**Zou je niet â€¦?** can be a soft reminder, not only a real question.

### zou for reported plans (preview)

**Zou** also appears when reporting what someone **was going to** do:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hij **zei** **dat** hij **zou** **komen**. | He said he would come. |
| Zij **beloofde** **dat** ze **zou** **helpen**. | She promised she would help. |
| Wij **dachten** **dat** het **zou** **regenen**. | We thought it would rain. |

Full indirect speech comes in [B1 Lesson 10](/notes/b1/10-indirect-speech). For now, notice **zou** inside **dat** clauses with verb **last**.

### Direct vs polite: when to use zou

| Situation | Direct | Polite with **zou** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Colleague you know well | **Kun** je **helpen**? | **Zou** je **kunnen** **helpen**? |
| Client / manager | **Kunt** u **bellen**? | **Zou** u **kunnen** **bellen**? |
| Advice to a friend | **Doe** **dat** **niet**! | Ik **zou** **dat** **niet** **doen**. |
| Uncertain opinion | Dat **is** **zo**. | Dat **zou** **kunnen**. |

Dutch is often direct ([A2 Lesson 18](/notes/a2/18-culture-traditions)), but **zou** is the standard tool when you need **respect**, **distance**, or **softness**.

### zou in subordinate clauses

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **vroeg** **of** hij **zou** **kunnen** **helpen**. | I asked if he could help. |
| **Hoewel** het **moeilijk** **zou** **worden**, **gingen** wij **door**. | Although it would become difficult, we continued. |
| **Voordat** ik **belde**, **wist** ik **niet** **of** zij **zou** **kunnen**. | Before I called, I did not know if she could. |

Verb **last** in the subordinate clause: **of hij zou kunnen helpen**.

### Combining with B1 grammar

| Topic | Example with **zou** |
| --- | --- |
| B1 L1 **hoewel** | **Hoewel** ik **zou** **willen** **komen**, **kan** ik **niet**. |
| B1 L2 **met wie** | **De collega** **met wie** ik **zou** **willen** **werken**, **vertrekt**. |
| B1 L3 **waarover** | **Het onderwerp** **waarover** wij **zouden** **praten**, **is** **gevoelig**. |
| B1 L4 future | Ik **ga** **morgen** **werken**, **maar** ik **zou** **vandaag** **willen** **voorbereiden**. |

  **Zou** is often short and unstressed: **zou-ik**, **zou-u**. Stress the main verb: **ZOU** je **KUNNEN** helpen? Practise polite chains: **Zou u dat kunnen uitleggen, alstublieft?** See [Intonation and Rhythm](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm).

    >
    > **Mila:** **Begrijpelijk**. **Ik** **zou** **zeggen** **dat** het **al** **tactvol** **is**. **Misschien** **zou** je **de** **eerste** **zin** **kunnen** **afzwakken**.

    > **Sam:** **Zou** je **een** **suggestie** **kunnen** **geven**? **Ik** **waardeer** je **terugkoppeling**.

    >
    > **Sam:** **Goede** **tip**. **Zou** je **me** **ook** **kunnen** **adviseren** **over** de **afsluiting**? **Ik** **zou** **graag** **iets** **hoffelijks** **willen**, **zonder** **te** **formeel** **te** **worden**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Zou** **Met** **vriendelijke** **groet** **niet** **volstaan**? **Als** ik **jou** **was**, **zou** ik **dat** **nemen**. **En** **zou** je **niet** **vergeten** **te** **bedanken** **voor** de **samenwerking**?

    **In English:**

    > **Sam:** Mila, could you take a quick look at my email? I hesitate a bit.
    >
    > **Mila:** Of course. What would you like to know exactly?
    >
    > **Sam:** Whether the tone is appropriate. I would not want to come across as too blunt, but not too vague either.
    >
    > **Mila:** Understandable. I would say it is already tactful. Perhaps you could soften the first sentence.
    >
    > **Sam:** Could you give a suggestion? I appreciate your feedback.
    >
    > **Mila:** Sure. Instead of _This does not work_, I would write: _This might not work well_. That could well help.
    >
    > **Sam:** Good tip. Could you also advise me on the closing? I would like something courteous without becoming too formal.
    >
    > **Mila:** Would _Kind regards_ not suffice? If I were you, I would use that. And you would not forget to thank them for the collaboration?
