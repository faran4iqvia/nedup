## Introduction

At A2 you agreed and disagreed with **Daar ben ik het mee eens** and **Dat zie ik anders**. B1 Lesson 13 added nuance in how strongly you state a view. This lesson adds **how you respond to someone else's view**: partial agreement, polite pushback, structured debate, and closing a discussion without bad blood. After this lesson you can take part in Dutch meetings, panel talks, and dinner table debates the way native speakers do: firm when needed, respectful always.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### The agreement spectrum

Dutch disagreement is often **graded**, not binary.

| Level | Dutch | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Full agree | **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **mee** **eens**. | I agree with that. |
| Partial | **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **deels** **mee** **eens**. | I partly agree with that. |
| Soft no | **Ik** **begrijp** je **punt**, **maar** … | I understand your point, but … |
| Clear no | **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **niet** **mee** **eens**. | I do not agree with that. |
| Different view | **Ik** **zie** **dat** **anders**. | I see that differently. |
| Close peacefully | **Laten** **we** **het** **oneens** **zijn**. | Let's agree to disagree. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **volledig** **mee** **eens**. | I fully agree with that. |
| **Gedeeltelijk** **eens**, **maar** **niet** **helemaal**. | Partially in agreement, but not completely. |
| **Wij** **hoeven** **het** **niet** **eens** **te** **zijn**. | We do not have to agree. |
| **Wij** **kunnen** **respectvol** **van** **mening** **verschillen**. | We can respectfully differ in opinion. |

**Mee** points back to the earlier statement. The fixed chunk is **het mee eens**, never **het eens mee**.

### Daar ben ik het (deels) mee eens

Use **daar** when you react to something just said:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **A:** **Ik** **denk** **dat** **wij** **moeten** **wachten**. | I think we should wait. |
| **B:** **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **mee** **eens**. | I agree with that. |
| **B:** **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **deels** **mee** **eens**. | I partly agree with that. |
| **B:** **Tot** **op** **zekere** **hoogte** **ben** ik **het** **eens**. | To a certain extent I agree. |

Combine with B1 nuance from [Lesson 13](/notes/b1/13-nuanced-opinions):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Het** **lijkt** me **dat** **jij** **gelijk** **hebt**, **en** **daar** **ben** ik **het** **deels** **mee** **eens**. | It seems to me you are right, and I partly agree with that. |
| **Naar** **mijn** **mening** **klopt** **het** **rapport**, **maar** **daar** **ben** ik **het** **niet** **volledig** **mee** **eens**. | In my opinion the report is correct, but I do not fully agree with that. |

### Ik zie dat anders

**Ik zie dat anders** is a standard, polite disagreement. **Dat** = the idea or claim just discussed.

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **zie** **dat** **anders**. | I see that differently. |
| **Ik** **zie** **het** **probleem** **anders**. | I see the problem differently. |
| **Mijn** **waarneming** **is** **anders**. | My observation is different. |
| **Dat** **is** **een** **ander** **standpunt**, **en** **dat** **respecteer** ik. | That is a different position, and I respect that. |

Stronger but still acceptable in debate:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **niet** **mee** **eens**. | I do not agree with that. |
| **Ik** **kan** me **daar** **niet** **in** **vinden**. | I cannot accept that. |
| **Ik** **sluit** me **daar** **niet** **bij** **aan**. | I do not align with that. |

Softer:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **begrijp** je **punt**, **maar** **ik** **zie** **dat** **anders**. | I understand your point, but I see that differently. |
| **Je** **hebt** **een** **goed** **punt**, **maar** **ik** **ben** **het** **niet** **eens**. | You make a good point, but I do not agree. |

### Laten we het oneens zijn

When debate stalls, Dutch speakers often **close with respect**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Laten** **we** **het** **oneens** **zijn**. | Let's agree to disagree. |
| **Wij** **hoeven** **het** **niet** **eens** **te** **zijn**. | We do not have to agree. |
| **Het** **meningsverschil** **blijft**, **en** **dat** **is** **oké**. | The difference of opinion remains, and that is okay. |
| **Laten** **we** **het** **hier** **bij** **laten**. | Let's leave it at that. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Wij** **debatteren** **verder** **niet**; **laten** **we** **het** **oneens** **zijn**. | We will not debate further; let's agree to disagree. |
| **Ik** **respecteer** je **standpunt**, **maar** **ik** **blijf** **het** **oneens**. | I respect your position, but I remain in disagreement. |

### Structuring a debate: two sides and counterpoints

From [B1 Lesson 1](/notes/b1/01-conjunctions-overview) you know **enerzijds** / **anderzijds**. In spoken debate, **aan de ene kant** / **aan de andere kant** is very common:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Aan** **de** **ene** **kant** **winnen** **wij** **tijd**; **aan** **de** **andere** **kant** **nemen** **wij** **een** **risico**. | On one hand we gain time; on the other hand we take a risk. |
| **Enerzijds** **klopt** **het** **argument**; **anderzijds** **mis** **ik** **data**. | On one hand the argument holds; on the other hand I miss data. |
| **Echter** **blijft** **het** **risico** **groot**. | However, the risk remains large. |

Introduce objections and replies:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Het** **bezwaar** **is** **dat** … | The objection is that … |
| **Het** **tegenvoorstel** **is** **dat** … | The counterproposal is that … |
| **Ter** **verdediging** **van** **het** **plan** … | In defence of the plan … |
| **Het** **weerwoord** **is** **dat** … | The rejoinder is that … |

From [B1 Lesson 10](/notes/b1/10-indirect-speech): reporting debate moves with **dat** + verb last: **Hij** **zei** **dat** **het** **tegenvoorstel** **haalbaar** **was**.

### Reacting on the floor

In meetings and panels, ask for the floor politely:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Mag** ik **daar** **op** **reageren**? | May I respond to that? |
| **Als** ik **mag** **tussenbeide** **komen** … | If I may interject … |
| **Ik** **wil** **daar** **op** **ingaan**. | I want to address that. |
| **Mag** ik **het** **woord**? | May I speak? |

Chair language:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik** **geef** **het** **woord** **aan** **Mila**. | I give the floor to Mila. |
| **Mila** **voert** **het** **woord**. | Mila has the floor. |
| **Laten** **we** **niet** **onderbreken**. | Let's not interrupt. |

Wrap up:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Samenvattend** **zijn** **wij** **het** **deels** **eens**. | In summary we partly agree. |
| **Kortom**: **wij** **kiezen** **voor** **kwaliteit**. | In short: we choose quality. |
| **Tot** **slot** **wil** ik **benadrukken** **dat** … | Finally I want to emphasise that … |
| **Laten** **we** **het** **debat** **afronden**. | Let's wrap up the debate. |

### Seeking consensus without forcing agreement

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Kunnen** **wij** **tot** **overeenstemming** **komen**? | Can we reach agreement? |
| **Laten** **we** **de** **middenweg** **kiezen**. | Let's choose the middle ground. |
| **Constructief** **debatteren** **betekent** **luisteren**. | Constructive debating means listening. |
| **Wij** **gaan** **de** **discussie** **aan**, **niet** **de** **confrontatie**. | We enter the discussion, not the confrontation. |

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  In **daar ben ik het mee eens**, link **mee-eens** smoothly. Stress **EENS** at the end. In **laten we het oneens zijn**, stress **oneens**. Practise: **Daar ben ik het deels mee eens, maar ik zie dat anders.** See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Example Conversation

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Teamoverleg over thuiswerken</CardTitle>
    <CardDescription>Mila and Sam debate how many days the team should work from home.</CardDescription>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    **In Dutch:**

    > **Mila:** **Ik** **denk** **dat** **wij** **minstens** **drie** **dagen** **per** **week** **thuis** **moeten** **werken**. **Aan** **de** **ene** **kant** **winnen** **wij** **tijd**; **aan** **de** **andere** **kant** **mis** **ik** **soms** **het** **contact** **op** **kantoor**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **deels** **mee** **eens**. **Het** **lijkt** me **dat** **twee** **dagen** **thuis** **genoeg** **is**. **Ik** **zie** **dat** **anders** **dan** **jij**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Mag** ik **daar** **op** **reageren**? **Ter** **verdediging** **van** **drie** **dagen**: **het** **team** **werkt** **constructief**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Ik** **begrijp** je **punt**, **maar** **het** **tegenvoorstel** **is** **twee** **dagen** **plus** **één** **vrije** **middag**. **Echter** **wil** ik **geen** **harde** **confrontatie**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Je** **hebt** **een** **goed** **punt**. **Tot** **op** **zekere** **hoogte** **kan** ik me **daarin** **vinden**. **Het** **meningsverschil** **blijft** **klein**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Met** **alle** **respect**: **ik** **ben** **niet** **overtuigd** **van** **drie** **dagen**. **Laten** **we** **de** **middenweg** **kiezen**: **twee** **vaste** **dagen** **thuis** **en** **één** **flexibele**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Daar** **ben** ik **het** **mee** **eens**. **Samenvattend**: **wij** **debatteren** **verder** **niet**; **laten** **we** **het** **debat** **afronden** **en** **het** **uitpraten** **met** **het** **team**.

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** I think we should work from home at least three days a week. On one hand we gain time; on the other hand I sometimes miss contact at the office.
    >
    > **Sam:** I partly agree with that. It seems to me two days at home is enough. I see that differently than you.
    >
    > **Mila:** May I respond to that? In defence of three days: the team works constructively.
    >
    > **Sam:** I understand your point, but the counterproposal is two days plus one free afternoon. However I do not want a harsh confrontation.
    >
    > **Mila:** You make a good point. To a certain extent I can accept that. The difference of opinion remains small.
    >
    > **Sam:** With all due respect: I am not convinced about three days. Let's choose the middle ground: two fixed days at home and one flexible day.
    >
    > **Mila:** I agree with that. In summary: we will not debate further; let's wrap up the debate and talk it through with the team.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
