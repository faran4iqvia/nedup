## Introduction

At A2 you learned **om te** for purpose: **Ik ga naar de cursus om Nederlands te leren**, **te moe om te gaan**. B1 opens the wider world of **te** + infinitive: **Dit is moeilijk te begrijpen**, **Hij vertrok zonder te groeten**, **Door de data te controleren voorkom je fouten**, **Zij belooft te komen**, **In plaats van te wachten, bel ik**. After this lesson you can read and write formal Dutch that uses **te** naturally in instructions, reports, and everyday speech.

## Quick Review

From [A2 Lesson 13](/notes/a2/13-om-te): **om te** + infinitive; **te** between prefix and stem in separables (**om op te staan**); **te** + adjective + **om te** (_too … to_). From [B1 Lesson 10](/notes/b1/10-indirect-speech): **Hij vroeg me om te bellen**; **Zij belooft te komen** (without **dat**). From [A1 Lesson 21](/notes/a1/21-separable-verbs): separable prefix + stem. Today you master **te** beyond **om te**.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Three main uses of te at B1

| Use | Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Purpose (A2) | **om** + **te** + infinitive | **om** **te** **leren** |
| Adjective (B1) | **adj** + **te** + infinitive | **moeilijk** **te** **begrijpen** |
| Manner / means (B1) | **zonder** / **door** / **in plaats van** + **te** | **zonder** **te** **vragen** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **kom** **om** **te** **helpen**. | I am coming to help. |
| Dit **is** **lastig** **te** **vinden**. | This is difficult to find. |
| **Door** **te** **oefenen** **word** je **beter**. | By practising you get better. |

### om te review (purpose)

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **bel** je **om** een afspraak **te** maken. | I am calling you to make an appointment. |
| **Wat** **doe** je **om** gezond **te** blijven? | What do you do to stay healthy? |
| Zij **komt** **om** **te** **helpen**. | She is coming to help. |

**Om te** answers **why** or **what for**. Do not drop **te**: not **om leren**, but **om te leren**.

### Adjective + te + infinitive (no om)

When an adjective describes how **easy, hard, nice, or impossible** something is **to do**, Dutch uses **te** directly after the adjective, **without** **om**:

| Pattern | Example |
| --- | --- |
| **adj** + **te** + infinitive | een **moeilijk** **te** **begrijpen** **tekst** |
| Het **is** + **adj** + **te** + infinitive | Het **is** **makkelijk** **te** **gebruiken**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Dit **rapport** **is** **lastig** **te** **lezen**. | This report is difficult to read. |
| De **instructie** **is** **eenvoudig** **te** **volgen**. | The instruction is easy to follow. |
| Het **systeem** **is** **niet** **moeilijk** **te** **configureren**. | The system is not difficult to configure. |

Compare:

- **te** moe **om te** gaan = _too tired to go_ (A2)
- **moeilijk te** begrijpen = _difficult to understand_ (B1, no **om**)

### zonder te: without doing

**Zonder te** + infinitive = **without** doing something:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Hij **vertrok** **zonder** **te** **groeten**. | He left without greeting. |
| **Zonder** **te** **vragen** **nam** zij **het** **over**. | Without asking, she took it over. |
| **Zonder** **de** **data** **te** **controleren**, **kun** je **niet** **indienen**. | Without checking the data, you cannot submit. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zonder** **te** **af te spreken**, **werkt** dat **niet**. | Without agreeing in advance, that does not work. |
| Hij **ging** **weg** **zonder** **te** **betalen**. | He left without paying. |

### door te: by doing

**Door te** + infinitive = **by** doing something (means or method):

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Door** **hard** **te** **werken** **slaagde** hij. | By working hard he succeeded. |
| **Door** **de** **data** **te** **controleren**, **voorkom** je **fouten**. | By checking the data, you prevent mistakes. |
| **Door** **vroeg** **te** **beginnen**, **ben** je **op** **tijd** **klaar**. | By starting early, you are done on time. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Door** **samen** **te** **werken**, **bereiken** wij **meer**. | By working together, we achieve more. |
| **Door** **de** **richtlijn** **te** **volgen**, **blijf** je **conform**. | By following the guideline, you stay compliant. |

### in plaats van te: instead of

From [B1 Lesson 1](/notes/b1/01-conjunctions-overview): **in plaats van** + noun. With a verb, add **te**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **In plaats van** **te** **wachten**, **bel** ik **direct**. | Instead of waiting, I call immediately. |
| **In plaats van** **alles** **te** **herhalen**, **stuur** ik **een** **samenvatting**. | Instead of repeating everything, I send a summary. |
| **In plaats van** **te** **klagen**, **zoek** ik **een** **oplossing**. | Instead of complaining, I look for a solution. |

### Verbs + te + infinitive

Many verbs take a second verb with **te** instead of a bare infinitive:

| Verb | Example |
| --- | --- |
| **beginnen** | Ik **begin** **te** **begrijpen**. |
| **proberen** | Wij **proberen** **te** **helpen**. |
| **beloven** | Zij **belooft** **te** **komen**. |
| **weigeren** | Hij **weigert** **te** **tekenen**. |
| **vergeten** | Ik **ben** **vergeten** **te** **bellen**. |
| **hopen** | Ik **hoop** **te** **winnen**. |
| **durven** | **Durft** je **te** **vragen**? |
| **blijven** | Zij **blijft** **te** **werken**. |
| **ophouden** | Hij **houdt** **op** **te** **klagen**. |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zij** **belooft** **te** **helpen**. | She promises to help. |
| **Hij** **zei** **te** **willen** **komen**. | He said he wanted to come. |
| **Wij** **proberen** **de** **fout** **te** **voorkomen**. | We try to prevent the error. |

From [B1 Lesson 10](/notes/b1/10-indirect-speech): **Hij vroeg me om te bellen** (with **om**) vs **Hij beloofde te komen** (with **te** only). Both are correct.

### alvorens te and voorafgaand

Formal Dutch uses **alvorens te** (_before_) and **voorafgaand** patterns:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Alvorens** **te** **indienen**, **moet** je **controleren**. | Before submitting, you must check. |
| **Voorafgaand** **aan** de **audit** **moet** alles **worden** **gedocumenteerd**. | Prior to the audit, everything must be documented. |
| **Lees** **de** **richtlijn** **alvorens** **te** **beginnen**. | Read the guideline before starting. |

### Separable verbs: te in the middle (all patterns)

In **every** **te** construction, separable verbs split: **prefix + te + stem**:

| Infinitive | te form |
| --- | --- |
| opstaan | op **te** staan |
| afspreken | af **te** spreken |
| doornemen | door **te** nemen |
| nalopen | na **te** lopen |
| uitvoeren | uit **te** voeren |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zonder** **af** **te** **spreken**, **gaan** wij **niet** **door**. | Without agreeing, we do not continue. |
| Het **is** **moeilijk** **door** **te** **nemen** **in** één **dag**. | It is difficult to go through in one day. |
| Ik **probeer** **op** **tijd** **te** **komen**. | I try to arrive on time. |

### om te vs te after adjectives: decision guide

| Question | Use |
| --- | --- |
| Purpose / why? | **om te** |
| Too … to? | **te** + adj + **om te** |
| Easy/hard/nice to do? | adj + **te** (no **om**) |
| Without / by / instead of? | **zonder/door/in plaats van te** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ben** **te** **moe** **om** **te** **gaan**. | I am too tired to go. |
| Het **is** **lastig** **te** **begrijpen**. | It is difficult to understand. |
| Ik **ga** **om** **te** **rusten**. | I am going to rest. |

### Combining with B1 grammar

| Topic | te example |
| --- | --- |
| B1 L8 passive | **Het** **is** **makkelijk** **te** **misverstaan** **wanneer** **de** **regel** **wordt** **ingevoerd**. |
| B1 L10 indirect | **Hij** **vroeg** **me** **om** **te** **controleren**; **zij** **beloofde** **te** **helpen**. |
| B1 L1 **hoewel** | **Hoewel** het **moeilijk** **is** **te** **vinden**, **proberen** wij **door** **te** **zoeken**. |

### Preview: word order masterclass (Lesson 12)

**Te** clusters sit at the **end** of clauses with other verbs: **Ik denk dat het moeilijk is te vinden**. Full verb cluster rules come in [Lesson 12](/notes/b1/12-word-order-masterclass).

    > **Sam:** **Wat** **als** ik **iets** **verzuim** **te** **melden**?

    >
    > **Sam:** **Is** de **nieuwe** **methode** **al** **toegepast**?
    >
    > **Mila:** **Ja**, **in** **de** **voorbereidingsfase**. **De** **implementatiefase** **begint** **volgende** **week**. **De** **uitvoerder** **belooft** **te** **helpen** **bij** **de** **synchronisatie**.
    >
    > **Sam:** **Goed**. **Ik** **hoop** **het** **doel** **te** **behalen** **zonder** **te** **talmen**.
    >
    > **Mila:** **Precies**. **Voorafgaand** **aan** **de** **oplevering** **moet** **alles** **gereed** **zijn**.

    **In English:**

    > **Mila:** Read the guideline before starting. The process is not difficult to follow, but the documentation is hard to find.
    >
    > **Sam:** Do I have to go through everything?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes. By using the checklist, you prevent mistakes. Without checking, you cannot submit.
    >
    > **Sam:** What if I fail to report something?
    >
    > **Mila:** Report it immediately. Instead of waiting, call IT. We try to avoid problems by coordinating early.
    >
    > **Sam:** Has the new method already been applied?
    >
    > **Mila:** Yes, in the preparation phase. The implementation phase starts next week. The executor promises to help with synchronisation.
    >
    > **Sam:** Good. I hope to achieve the goal without dawdling.
    >
    > **Mila:** Exactly. Prior to delivery, everything must be ready.
