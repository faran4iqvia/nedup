## Introduction

You have reached the last **content** lesson of B1. You know **bijzinnen** and passives, **zou** and conditionals, formal letters and meetings, culture and idioms, fast speech strategies, and **practical Netherlands**. **Bringing it together** means using all of it in one flow: listen, speak, read, and write in the right **register**, with the grammar you built from A0 upward. After this lesson you can plan **integratieoefening**, judge your **B1-gereedheid**, and move confidently to the [B1 Review and Test](/notes/b1/review-and-test).

### Register: who are you talking to?

**Register** is how formal or informal your Dutch sounds:

| Context | Register | Signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Friends, family | informal | **je/jij**, particles, idioms |
| Colleagues, neighbours | neutral | mix of **je** and polite chunks |
| Employer, official letter | formal | **u**, **zou**, **Geachte**, full forms |
| News, debate | structured | connectors, passive, **volgens** |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **de** **stijlregister** | style register |
| **de** **dagelijkse** **spraak** | everyday speech |
| **de** **formele** **schrijfstijl** | formal writing style |
| **de** **taalfunctie** | language function (purpose) |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Bij** **de** **gemeente** **gebruik** **ik** **u**. | At the municipality I use **u**. |
| **Met** **vrienden** **zeg** **ik** **het** **maakt** **niet** **uit**. | With friends I say it does not matter. |
| **In** **een** **sollicitatiebrief** **schrijf** **ik** **formeel**. | In a job application letter I write formally. |
| **Op** **het** **nieuws** **hoor** **ik** **passief** **en** **hoewel**. | On the news I hear passive and **hoewel**. |

From [B1 Lesson 21](/notes/b1/21-dutch-culture-and-directness): Dutch **directness** is not rudeness. From [B1 Lesson 24](/notes/b1/24-particles-masterclass): **hoor**, **even**, **toch** colour informal speech. From [B1 Lesson 25](/notes/b1/25-idioms-and-expressions): **Geen zorgen**, **Dat klopt**.

<Callout title="Pronunciation Focus" type="info">
  Integration means **switching register** with your mouth, not only your words. Practise one formal chain (**Zou u … alstublieft**) and one informal chain (**Geen zorgen, hoor**). See [Regional Variation](/notes/pronunciation/06-regional-variation) and [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>

## Common Mistakes for English Speakers

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Mixing registers in one conversation</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    English speakers often stay informal too long. At **de gemeente** or in a formal email: **u**, **Zou u …?**, **Met vriendelijke groet**. With friends: **je**. Plan which register before you speak.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Grammar in isolation, not in flow</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    B1 means combining patterns: **Hoewel ik moe ben, ga ik toch naar de vergadering.** Not separate drills for **hoewel** and **toch** that never meet in one sentence.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Skipping receptive skills</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    If you only write grammar exercises, speaking lags. Balance **luisteren** (podcast shadowing), **lezen** (news), **schrijven** (one email/week), **spreken** (one scenario aloud/week).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
