## Introduction

Dutch does not form the future the way English does with one _will_ for everything. You choose between **gaan** + infinitive for concrete near plans (**Ik ga morgen presenteren**), **zullen** for promises, predictions, and formal announcements (**Ik zal je de slides sturen**), and often the **present tense** for timetables (**De vergadering begint om tien uur**). After this lesson you can plan, promise, predict, and read schedules in natural Dutch without overusing **zullen** where **gaan** or present tense fits better.

## Grammar and Language Explanation

### Three ways to talk about the future

| Strategy | Pattern | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Gaan** + infinitive | Ik **ga** morgen **werken** | near future, concrete intention |
| **Zullen** + infinitive | Ik **zal** je **bellen** | promise, prediction, formal tone |
| **Present tense** | De trein **vertrekt** om acht uur | schedules, programs, timetables |

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** vanavond **studeren**. | I am going to study tonight. |
| Ik **zal** je **morgen** **antwoorden**. | I will answer you tomorrow. |
| Het **concert** **begint** om **acht** uur. | The concert starts at eight o'clock. |
| **Zullen** we **volgende** **week** **afspreken**? | Shall we meet next week? |

### zullen: full conjugation and core uses

| Pronoun | Form | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ik | **zal** | Ik **zal** **komen**. |
| jij / je | **zult** / **zal** | **Zul** jij **meedenken**? |
| u | **zult** / **zal** | U **zult** **spoedig** **horen**. |
| hij / zij / het | **zal** | Hij **zal** **helpen**. |
| wij / jullie / zij | **zullen** | Wij **zullen** **doorpakken**. |

#### Promises and commitments

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **zal** de **mail** **vandaag** **nog** **sturen**. | I will send the email today still. |
| **Dat** **zal** ik **niet** **vergeten**. | I will not forget that. |
| Wij **zullen** **zorgen** **dat** het **op** **tijd** **is**. | We will make sure it is on time. |
| Ik **beloof** het, ik **zal** **komen**. | I promise, I will come. |

#### Suggestions: Zullen we …?

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Zullen** we **even** **bellen**? | Shall we call for a moment? |
| **Zullen** we **de** **planning** **doornemen**? | Shall we go through the planning? |
| **Zullen** jullie **morgen** **eerder** **beginnen**? | Will you start earlier tomorrow? |
| **Zal** ik **de** **agenda** **sturen**? | Shall I send the agenda? |

**Zal ik …?** is a polite **offer**: _Shall I …?_

#### Predictions and expectations

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Het **zal** **morgen** **regenen**. | It will rain tomorrow. |
| De **prijzen** **zullen** **stijgen**. | Prices will rise. |
| Hij **zal** **wel** **te** **laat** **komen**. | He will probably arrive late. |
| Dat **zal** **wel** **meevallen**. | That will probably be fine. |

**Zal / zullen + wel** softens a guess: _probably, I suppose_.

#### Formal and written Dutch

In reports, news, and official texts, **zullen** marks future events:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De **minister** **zal** **morgen** **spreken**. | The minister will speak tomorrow. |
| Het **onderzoek** **zal** **drie** **maanden** **duren**. | The research will take three months. |
| De **wijziging** **zal** **per** **1** **januari** **ingaan**. | The change will take effect on 1 January. |

### gaan + infinitive: the everyday near future

**Gaan** points to something **about to happen** or **already decided**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** **straks** **lunchen**. | I am going to have lunch shortly. |
| Wij **gaan** **volgende** **maand** **verhuizen**. | We are moving next month. |
| Het **gaat** **hard** **regenen**. | It is going to rain hard. |
| **Ga** je **mee** **naar** de **borrel**? | Are you coming along to drinks? |

**Het gaat** + infinitive / weather: impersonal **going to** for visible or immediate outcomes (**Het gaat sneeuwen**).

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| **Kijk**, het **gaat** **onweren**. | Look, it is going to storm. |
| Er **gaat** **veel** **veranderen**. | A lot is going to change. |
| Zij **gaan** **niet** **meewerken** **aan** dit **plan**. | They are not going to collaborate on this plan. |

Do **not** stack **zullen** and **gaan** for one verb: ❌ **Ik zal morgen gaan werken** → ✅ **Ik ga morgen werken** or **Ik zal morgen werken**.

### Present tense for schedules (presens futurum)

When a time is **fixed on a program**, Dutch uses **present tense**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| De **vergadering** **begint** om **tien** uur. | The meeting starts at ten o'clock. |
| Het **project** **loopt** **tot** **december**. | The project runs until December. |
| De **trein** **vertrekt** om **8:15**. | The train departs at 8:15. |
| Het **rapport** **komt** **volgende** **week** **uit**. | The report comes out next week. |

This sounds natural in Dutch. English often uses _will_ here; Dutch prefers **present**.

### Choosing gaan, zullen, or present

| Situation | Best choice | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Personal plan tonight | **gaan** | Ik **ga** **vanavond** **koken**. |
| Promise to a colleague | **zullen** | Ik **zal** het **voor** **vrijdag** **af** **hebben**. |
| Train timetable | **present** | De **intercity** **vertrekt** om **negen** uur. |
| Suggestion to a group | **zullen** | **Zullen** we **pauze** **nemen**? |
| Weather you can see coming | **gaan** | Het **gaat** **regenen**. |
| Press release / formal | **zullen** | De **CEO** **zal** **antwoorden**. |

### Time expressions with the future

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **straks** | soon / in a bit | Ik **bel** je **straks**. |
| **binnenkort** | soon | Het **verschijnt** **binnenkort**. |
| **morgen / overmorgen** | tomorrow / day after | Wij **gaan** **overmorgen** **beginnen**. |
| **volgende week** | next week | **Zullen** we **volgende** **week** **evalueren**? |
| **aanstaande** + day | coming (on) | **Aanstaande** **maandag** **start** het **project**. |
| **over** + time | in X time | **Over** **twee** **weken** **zijn** wij **klaar**. |

### zullen in subordinate clauses

From [A2 Lesson 5](/notes/a2/05-opinions): in **dat** clauses, the verb goes **last**:

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **denk** **dat** hij **zal** **komen**. | I think he will come. |
| **Voordat** ik **ga**, **zal** ik **je** **bellen**. | Before I leave, I will call you. |
| **Hoewel** het **moeilijk** **zal** **worden**, **gaan** wij **door**. | Although it will become difficult, we continue. |

Note: **Voordat** clause keeps its own verb order; the main clause can use **zal** in position 2.

### Negative future

| Dutch | English |
| --- | --- |
| Ik **ga** **niet** **meewerken**. | I am not going to collaborate. |
| Ik **zal** **niet** **te** **laat** **komen**. | I will not come late. |
| De **shop** **opent** **morgen** **niet**. | The shop does not open tomorrow. |

**Niet** before the infinitive with modals and **gaan**: **Ik zal niet vergeten**.

### Combining with earlier grammar

| Topic | Future example |
| --- | --- |
| B1 L1 **hoewel** | **Hoewel** wij **volgende** **week** **gaan** **presenteren**, **is** er **nog** **werk**. |
| B1 L2 **die/dat** | **Het** **plan** **dat** wij **morgen** **gaan** **bespreken**, **staat** **online**. |
| B1 L3 **waarover** | **Het** **onderwerp** **waarover** wij **zullen** **stemmen**, **is** **gevoelig**. |
| A2 L4 **moeten** | Ik **moet** **morgen** **vroeg** **beginnen**, **dus** ik **ga** **nu** **naar** **bed**. |

  In **Zullen we afspreken?**, stress **Zul** clearly. In fast promises, **zal** is short: _ik-zal-wel-kijken_. Drill five **gaan** and five **zullen** sentences without pausing before the infinitive. See [Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech).
</Callout>
