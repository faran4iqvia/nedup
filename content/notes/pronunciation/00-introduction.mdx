## Welcome to the Pronunciation Track 📣

This is the **ear-and-mouth shelf**, not a second course. Follow the **A0 → A1 → A2 → B1** lesson path first. Open a pronunciation file when a lesson's **Pronunciation Focus** box points you here, or when people understand your grammar but not your sounds.

Dutch vowels, the throat **g**, and word stress matter more than a perfect accent. If those three are clear, you will be understood even when grammar wobbles.

### Track bands

The pronunciation track has **four bands**. Match the band to your main-course level:

| Band | Files | What to expect on each page |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **A0 sounds** | **01–03** | Vowel length, **g/ch**, **r**, **v/w**, word stress, compounds |
| **A1 rhythm** | **04** | Statement fall, yes/no rise, wh-questions, early subordinate rhythm |
| **A2 flow** | **05** | Linking, schwa, reductions, sentence chains from longer grammar |

Use both.

### How to study

**Daily minimum:** five minutes aloud beats one long session monthly. Every pronunciation page assumes you **say** the examples.

**Worked example:** People confuse **huis** and **hout**.

1. Open [01: Vowel Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) → minimal pair **huis** · **hout**.
2. Say the pair ten times slowly, then in **Ik zie het huis**.
3. Record yourself. Compare vowel length on **ui** vs **ou**.
4. Revisit when you learn new **ui** words in main lessons.

Same loop for stress (**VER-staan** vs ver**STAAN**) → [03](/notes/pronunciation/03-word-stress) → mark stress on five new words before you memorise meaning.

Same loop for fast dialogues → [05](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech) → read one A2 dialogue for **flow only**, no pauses before **dat** or **omdat**.

**External audio:** [Forvo](https://forvo.com/languages/nl/) for single words · [Jeugdjournaal](https://jeugdjournaal.nl) for clear broadcast Dutch · shadow any lesson dialogue half a beat behind the speaker.

**Hub page:** [Listening and Register Choice](/notes/pronunciation/listening-and-register-choice) when you are unsure which file fits your symptom.
