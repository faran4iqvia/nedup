## Introduction

This Pronunciation Track lesson supports the main course. Study alongside **A0 Lessons 1–3**. Say every example word aloud at least twice.

## Explanation

Dutch vowels that differ most from English:

| Spelling | Sound | Tips | Example words |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| aa | open ah | mouth wide | **naam**, **laat**, **graag** |
| ee / ie | ay / ee | tight lips for ie | **week**, **niet**, **zien** |
| oo | oh | round lips | **brood**, **groot**, **hoop** |
| eu | urh | round lips, tongue forward | **neus**, **keuken**, **zeur** |
| ui | ow-ih | start with aa, glide to ie | **huis**, **buiten**, **zuipen** |
| ij / ei | ay (tight) | like English "say" but shorter | **jij**, **mijn**, **tijd** |

**Short vs long:** Dutch spelling shows length. **Man** (short a) vs **maan** (long aa). **Pen** vs **peen**.
