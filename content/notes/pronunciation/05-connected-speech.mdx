## Introduction

In slow classroom Dutch, every word sounds separate: **Ik ben op kantoor**. In real speech, words **link**, **shorten**, and **merge**. This lesson belongs to the A2 stage of the Pronunciation Track. ## Quick Review

**de**, **het**, **een** shorten in fast speech. Link across boundaries: **is het**, **dat ik**, **op een**. Do not pause before **dat** / **omdat**.

## Explanation

### Unstressed function words shrink

Small words (**de**, **het**, **een**, **je**, **te**, **van**) are usually **unstressed**. They shorten in fast speech:

| Written | Often sounds like | In context |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **de** | duh / very short | op **de** tafel → op-de-tafel |
| **het** | ut / ht | in **het** huis → in-ut-huis |
| **een** | un / n | **een** kop koffie → un kop koffie |
| **je** | juh / schwa | **Geef** je me dat? |
| **te** | tuh | om **te** werken → om-t-werken |

You do **not** need to drop them when you speak. But you **must** recognise them when natives blur them together.

### Linking across word boundaries

Dutch links the last sound of one word to the first sound of the next when both are consonants or vowels:

| Written | Linked feel | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **is het** | i-shet | is it |
| **op een** | o-peen | on a |
| **aan het** | aan-tet | busy doing (progressive) |
| **dat ik** | da-tik | that I |
| **met een** | me-teen | with a |
| **in de** | in-de | in the |

**Ik ben aan het werken** often sounds like one flowing phrase: **ik-ben-aan-t-werken**.

### Final consonants before another consonant

At natural speed, final consonants can soften or disappear before another consonant starts:

| Written | Fast speech note |
| --- | --- |
| **het is goed** | **is** stays clear; **het** very short |
| **niet waar** | **niet** + **waar** link: **nie-waar** |
| **op het plein** | **op-ut-plein** |
| **bij de ingang** | **bij-de-ingang** as one rhythm unit |

Do not drop sounds in your own speech until you are comfortable. **Listen** for them first.

### Common reductions in Randstad Dutch

These appear constantly in Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht, and Den Haag:

| Full form | Reduced / casual | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **dat** | **'t** / da | **'t** is goed |
| **niet** | **nie** (informal) | **Ik kom nie.** |
| **gewoon** | **gewoan** | **Gewoan doen.** |
| **even** | **eve** | **Wacht eve.** |
| **misschien** | **missche** | **Missche morgen.** |
| **waarschijnlijk** | **waarschijnlek** | **Waarschijnlek wel.** |

These are **normal**, not sloppy. You will hear them in [A2 Lesson 18](/notes/a2/18-culture-traditions) dialogues with particles like **hoor** and **zeg**.

### Sentence chains from A2 grammar

Long A2 sentences are good connected-speech practice:

| Sentence | What to practise |
| --- | --- |
| **Ik denk dat het morgen regent.** | Link **denk-dat**, verb **regent** at end without pause |
| **Omdat ik tijd heb, kom ik.** | No break after comma; **omdat-ik** flows |
| **Er liggen er drie boeken op tafel.** | Keep both **er** sounds distinct but quick |
| **De brief wordt morgen geschreven.** | **wordt-morgen** links; stress on **brief** and **schreven** |
| **Als ik tijd heb, bel ik je.** | **Als-ik**, **bel-ik-je** as one rhythm |

From [Word Stress](/notes/pronunciation/03-word-stress): content words stay strong; function words shrink.

## Common mistakes for English speakers

| Mistake | Fix |
| --- | --- |
| Pausing before **dat**, **omdat**, **als** | Link into the next word: **denk-dat**, **omdat-ik** |
| Pronouncing every **e** in **de**, **het**, **een** | Reduce to schwa in fast speech |
| Reading word-by-word from the page | Practise **chains** of 4–6 words without breaks |
| Adding English stress on function words | Stress content words: **VER-gadering**, not **de VER-gadering** |
| Ignoring **'t**, **'k**, **'m** in captions | They are normal; learn the full form, accept the reduction |

## Answer Key

| Chain | Linked feel |
| --- | --- |
| **is het** | i-shet |
| **dat ik** | da-tik |
| **op het plein** | op-ut-plein |
| **Ik denk dat …** | **denk-dat** flows; verb at end without pause |

## Common listening mistakes

| You hear | You might think | Actually |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **'t is goed** | _it is good_ (unclear subject) | **dat** / **het** is reduced |
| **nie** | a new word | **niet** informal |
| **op-ut-plein** | missing words | **op het plein** linked |
| **da-ik** | one word | **dat ik** |

When listening fails, ask: **which small words could be hidden here?** (**de**, **het**, **een**, **dat**, **niet**).
