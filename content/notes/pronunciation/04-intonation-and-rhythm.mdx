## Introduction

Dutch intonation tells listeners whether you are asking, stating, or continuing a thought. Flat English rhythm on Dutch sentences sounds uncertain or robotic. This lesson belongs to the A1 stage of the Pronunciation Track. Study it alongside [A1 Lesson 1](/notes/a1/01-daily-routines) onward and revisit when you build longer B1 sentences.

Read short chains **without** pausing between every word.

## Quick Review

Statements **fall** at the end. Yes/no questions **rise**. Wh-questions stress the question word, then fall.

## Explanation

### Statements (flat fall)

Neutral statements use a **steady fall** at the end:

| Pattern | Example | Rhythm |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Subject · Verb · Rest | **Ik werk vandaag.** | slight fall on **werk** |
| Inversion | **Vandaag werk ik.** | pause feel after **Vandaag**, stress **werk** |
| Chain | **Ik sta op. Ik douche. Ik ontbijt.** | three equal units |

Do not rise at the end of a statement (English habit).

### Yes/no questions (rise at end)

| Type | Example | Intonation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Yes/no | **Werk je vandaag?** | rise on **vandaag?** |
| Modal | **Kun je me helpen?** | rise at end |
| **zou** polite | **Zou je dat kunnen doen?** | rise, but softer |

### Wh-questions (fall after question word)

| Question | Stress |
| --- | --- |
| **WAAR woon je?** | high on **waar**, fall after |
| **WANNEER kom je?** | **wanneer** clear, then fall |
| **HOE gaat het?** | **hoe** stressed |

### Subordinate clauses (B1)

Long sentences need **breath groups**, not random pauses:

| Structure | Rhythm tip |
| --- | --- |
| **Hoofdzin** | one group: **Ik GA naar huis** |
| **Bijzin** first | **HOE-wel ik MOE ben**, comma, **GA ik TOCH** |
| **dat** clause | no pause before final verb: **…dat hij MORgen KOMT** |
| List | short pauses at commas only |

From [B1 Lesson 1](/notes/b1/01-conjunctions-overview): **HOE-wel ik MOE ben**, **GA ik TOCH**.

From [B1 Lesson 12](/notes/b1/12-word-order-masterclass): do **not** pause inside the verb cluster at the end.

### Emotion and particles

| Signal | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **hoor**, **toch** | light, unstressed tag |
| **Geen zorgen** | calm fall |
| Surprise | higher start, still Dutch fall |
| Direct opinion | clear stress on opinion word |

Particles do not usually carry sentence stress.

## Common mistakes for English speakers

| Mistake | Fix |
| --- | --- |
| Rising every statement | fall at end in statements |
| Pause before **dat** verb | link into final verb |
| Flat questions | rise on yes/no questions |
| Same rhythm for **hoofdzin** and **bijzin** | front load contrast in **hoewel** clauses |
| Stressing **hoor** | particle stays light |
