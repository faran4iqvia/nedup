## Introduction

Many pronunciation problems are **routing problems**: you reopen the wrong file or drill single sounds when the real issue is **linking** or **register**. This hub answers **which lesson** to open first. Use the symptom table below; then drill the linked file.

Core files: [01: Vowel Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds) · [02: Consonant Sounds](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds) · [03: Word Stress](/notes/pronunciation/03-word-stress) · [04: Intonation and Rhythm](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm) · [05: Connected Speech](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech) · [06: Regional Variation](/notes/pronunciation/06-regional-variation).

## Quick Review

**Wrong vowel** → [01](/notes/pronunciation/01-vowel-sounds). **English g or r** → [02](/notes/pronunciation/02-consonant-sounds). **Wrong syllable loud** → [03](/notes/pronunciation/03-word-stress). **Flat or rising statements** → [04](/notes/pronunciation/04-intonation-and-rhythm). **Words blur at speed** → [05](/notes/pronunciation/05-connected-speech) + [B1 L26](/notes/b1/26-fast-speech-strategies). **News / street / Flemish** → [06](/notes/pronunciation/06-regional-variation).

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">Treating **nie** as wrong Dutch</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Casual **nie** for **niet** is normal Randstad speech, not an error. Learn the full form, recognise the reduction. See [06: Regional Variation](/notes/pronunciation/06-regional-variation).
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>

<ShadCnCard className="my-4">
  <CardHeader>
    <CardTitle className="text-lg">One accent as the only correct Dutch</CardTitle>
  </CardHeader>

  <CardContent>
    Clear vowels, stress, and linking matter more than copying Amsterdam street talk. News Dutch and Flemish variants are still Dutch. Aim for **clear** standard Dutch, not one city.
  </CardContent>
</ShadCnCard>
